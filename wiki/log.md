## [2026-07-12] ingest | Wise psychological interventions (Walton 2014) + Program evaluation (Sechrest & Figueredo 1993)

- **Sources:** `.raw/Walton - 2014 - The New Science of Wise Psychological Interventions.md` → [[Walton-2014-Wise-Psychological-Interventions]] (*Current Directions in Psychological Science* 23(1):73–82); `.raw/Input/Sechrest and Figueredo - 1993 - Program Evaluation.pdf` → [[Sechrest-Figueredo-1993-Program-Evaluation]] (*Annual Review of Psychology* 44:645–674). Hybrid-LYT filing: sources→`wiki/sources/`, concepts→`wiki/concepts/`, entities→`wiki/entities/`.
- **Note on Sechrest source:** the pre-existing `.raw/…Program Evaluation.md` was an empty page-marker stub (bad extraction). Ingested from the scanned PDF in `.raw/Input/` via pdftoppm + tesseract OCR (32 pages). The `.raw/` `.md` and PDF were left untouched (immutable).
- **Substance (Walton 2014):** names **[[Wise Interventions]]** — brief, psychologically precise, field-experimental interventions. Three properties: (1) psychologically precise (well-founded theory in a brief exercise; inaccurate theory misfires — e.g. mishandled [[Growth Mindset]]); (2) target **[[Recursive Processes (Wise Interventions)|recursive processes]]** ("movie not snapshot"; largest effects most distal; proximal outcomes carry effects forward); (3) **context-dependent, not silver bullets**. Direct (reading/writing) vs indirect (new-experience) interventions; the Schulz nursing-home reversal. Exemplars: [[Social Belonging Intervention]], [[Values Affirmation]], growth mindset, [[Saying-Is-Believing]]. Term traces via [[Claude Steele]]'s "wise schooling" ([[Stereotype Threat]]).
- **Substance (Sechrest & Figueredo 1993):** an **[[Ecological Paradigm in Evaluation|ecological]]** reading of program evaluation — **[[Ecological Paradigm in Evaluation|Newtonian vs Darwinian]]** paradigms (the I₁→D₁→O₁→U₁ path-model critique), a concurrent industrial→postindustrial social revolution changing the evaluator's niche, **[[Evaluation Is Not a Field]]**, a skeptical read of the qualitative movement (discovery vs justification; PUSH/Excel case), **[[Five Fundamental Issues of Evaluation]]** (Shadish et al.), **[[Formative vs Summative Evaluation]]** drift, **[[Prospective Evaluation]]**, **[[Strength and Integrity of Treatment]]**, and a case for multivariate methods (common-factor modeling; sequential canonical analysis; "rule causes *in*, not out").
- **Cross-source link:** both are about *intervention* — Walton at the micro/psychological grain (change one process precisely), Sechrest & Figueredo at the macro/program grain (evaluate whole social programs ecologically). Both foreground **strong theory** (Walton: precise psychological theory; S&F: theory can compensate for weak method) and **external validity in real-world context**.

- **Pages updated:** [[Stereotype Threat]] (wise-interventions remedy section), [[Theory-Driven Evaluation]] (linked S&F + Five Fundamental Issues + Rossi), [[Learning & Development MOC]] (new "Wise psychological interventions" section), [[index]], [[hot]].
- **Addresses:** c-001237–c-001260.
- **Key insight:** precision (Walton) and ecological realism (S&F) are two faces of the same lesson — interventions work only when a *specific, well-founded theory* meets the *real recursive/multi-determined context*, not when the intervention is merely large or the method merely rigorous.

## [2026-07-11] ingest | Selection & work-analysis trio — work analysis for selection (García-Izquierdo 2015) + TJA vs CM (Sanchez & Levine 2009) + Uniform Guidelines (EEOC 1978)

- **Sources:** `.raw/García-Izquierdo et al. - 2015 - Work analysis for personnel selection.md` → [[Garcia-Izquierdo-et-al-2015-work-analysis-personnel-selection]] (chapter, Nikolaou & Oostrom Eds., Routledge); `.raw/Sanchez and Levine - 2009 ...md` → [[Sanchez-Levine-2009-competency-modeling-vs-job-analysis]] (*HRMR* 19:53–63); `.raw/Equal Employment Opportunity Commission - 1978 - Uniform Guidelines ...md` → [[EEOC-1978-uniform-guidelines-employee-selection]] (43 FR 38295; 29 CFR 1607 / 41 CFR 60-3). Hybrid-LYT convention: sources→`wiki/sources/`, concepts→`wiki/concepts/`, entities→`wiki/entities/`, new cluster gets a MOC.
- **Substance (García-Izquierdo):** work analysis (WA) = systematic gathering of activities + KSAOs → job description + job specification; feeds predictor & criterion development. Task- vs worker-oriented orientations (both needed; selection → worker orientation). Methods: observation/interview + job-task inventories, F-JAS, PIC, PPRF, PAQ, O*NET/ISCO-08. **Reliability must mean agreement, not mere consistency** (interrater r≈.90 can hide divergent means; Morgeson & Campion's 16 social/cognitive inaccuracy sources). Person-work fit shifting job→org→market fit. WA is the EEO safeguard: documents KSAO–task job-relatedness.
- **Substance (Sanchez & Levine):** six-dimension TJA-vs-CM contrast — purpose (describe vs influence), view (object vs role/enacted), focus (job vs org), time (past vs future), performance level (typical vs maximum), measurement (latent trait vs clinical judgment). CM raises *situation strength* via behavioral themes; competencies are fuzzy multi-construct composites → global/clinical judgments, NOT construct-valid latent traits (so internal-consistency reliability is the wrong metric; interrater agreement isn't an accuracy index). TJA & CM **supplement** each other; strategic JA = strategy *formulation*, CM = *execution* (SJA can't replace CM). To satisfy Uniform Guidelines job-relatedness, supplement CM with TJA.
- **Substance (EEOC):** joint federal framework (EEOC/CSC/DOL/DOJ) for using selection procedures under Title VII + EO 11246. No adverse impact → no validity study required; adverse impact = discrimination unless validated. Four-fifths (80%) rule flags adverse impact; "bottom line" for total process; suitable less-adverse alternatives. Three validity strategies (criterion-related §14B / content §14C / construct §14D), each resting on a **job analysis** (§14A). No assumption of validity (§9); disparate treatment (§11) distinct from validation; transportability/borrowing (§7); ranking needs stronger evidence than pass/fail (§5G); fairness/differential prediction (§14B(8)).
- **Cross-source connection:** all three orbit **job analysis as the hub** — García-Izquierdo supplies the methods & psychometrics, Sanchez & Levine the TJA/CM theory (and the explicit tie to the Guidelines' job-relatedness), EEOC the legal mandate that makes JA non-optional as a validity foundation. The Guidelines' three validity strategies map onto the vault's [[Validity Typology]] and [[binning-barrett-1989-validity-personnel-decisions|Binning-Barrett framework]] (noted as the scientific parent, with critique re: validity generalization).

## [2026-07-11] ingest | L&D trio — informal learning (Cerasoli 2018) + experience-based leadership development (McCall 2010) + coactive vicarious learning (Myers 2018)

- **Sources:** `.raw/Cerasoli et al. - 2018 ...` → [[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis]] (*J. Bus. Psychol.* 33:203–230); `.raw/McCall - 2010 - Recasting Leadership Development.md` → [[mccall-2010-recasting-leadership-development]] (*Ind. Organ. Psychol.* 3:3–19); `.raw/Myers - 2018 - Coactive Vicarious Learning ...` → [[myers-2018-coactive-vicarious-learning]] (*AMR* 43(4):610–634). Hybrid-LYT convention: sources→`wiki/sources/`, concepts→`wiki/concepts/`, entities→`wiki/entities/`.
- **Substance (Cerasoli):** first meta-analysis of [[Informal Learning Behaviors (ILBs)]] (k=49, N=55,514, 376 ESs). Defines ILBs = non-curricular, self-directed, intentional, field-based learning behaviors (vs formal learning behaviors). Three-level antecedent framework. **Support** the strongest antecedent (ρ=.32; formal/tangible .38 ≥ informal .30 ≈ people .31); job/task .21 (autonomy .31, resources .30); individual predispositions .27 (learning motives .33); opportunities .04 (equivocal); demographics near-zero. **Outcomes:** attitudes ρ=.29, knowledge/skill .41, performance .42. Rigor did not moderate.
- **Substance (McCall):** experience — not genetics/training/business-school — is the primary source of learning to lead. Seven "sure bets" (leadership learned from experience; challenging experiences teach; [[Developmental Experiences|challenge]] + feedback + reflection; nonlinear/dynamic → [[Derailment]] risk). Barrier isn't the [[Knowing-Doing Gap]] but executives' short-term results-drive + "you have it or you don't" beliefs (turning challenge into *testing* not developing) + misunderstood true cost. Remedies: embed development in strategy, use business initiatives not HR processes, create a "wise counselor" role, focus on learning *from* experience, and shift to **mastery** of the [[Five Demands of Leadership]]. Central polemic: [[Competency Models vs Experience (Leadership Development)|competence, not competencies]]. Provocation: "selection is ultimately the key to development."
- **Substance (Myers):** theory paper reframing vicarious learning from intrapersonal one-way ([[Independent Vicarious Learning (IVL)]]) to relational/coconstructed ([[Coactive Vicarious Learning (CVL)]]). Integrates experiential learning (Kolb) + symbolic interactionism (Mead). Three multiplicative interactional elements: **experience + analysis + support**. Antecedent contexts (relational/structural/individual, P1–P3) and developmental consequences (individual knowledge P4; individual capacity — transactive memory, perspective-taking, self-efficacy — P5; relational capacity — shared mental models, trust, affective commitment — P6), which loop back. Motivated by tacit, ambiguous knowledge work where the "lesson" can't be deduced by observation alone.
- **Cross-source connection:** the three map on-the-job learning at complementary grains — Cerasoli gives the *behavior-level* meta-analytic evidence (ILBs pay off), McCall the *experience-level* leadership-development thesis (experience is primary; reflection the bottleneck), Myers the *interaction-level* mechanism for one channel of it (social/vicarious learning done relationally). Notably, CVL is exactly the *social/vicarious learning that Cerasoli et al. bracket out* of ILBs — the two are complementary halves. All three converge on **[[Learning from Experience]]** (upgraded seed→developing) and reflection/support as the enabling conditions.

Cerasoli]], [[Jamie S. Donsbach]], [[Karin A. Orvis]], [[Christopher G. Myers]]); 3 sources. **Updated:** [[George M. Alliger]], [[Scott I. Tannenbaum]], [[John E. Mathieu]], [[Morgan McCall]], [[Albert Bandura]], [[Linda Argote]], [[Enactive and Vicarious Learning]], [[Learning & Development MOC]], [[index]]. **Addresses c-001167–c-001178.**
