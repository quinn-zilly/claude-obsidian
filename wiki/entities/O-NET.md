# O*NET (Occupational Information Network)

US Department of Labor occupational database that replaced the **Dictionary of Occupational Titles (DOT)**. Dynamic system covering 800+ occupations, regularly updated (Peterson et al. 1999). Built on **Functional Job Analysis**.

## Content Model — 6 descriptor sets
1. Worker characteristics *(worker-oriented)*
2. Worker requirements *(worker-oriented)*
3. Experience requirements *(worker-oriented)*
4. Occupational requirements *(job-oriented)*
5. Occupation-specific requirements *(job-oriented)*
6. Workforce characteristics *(job-oriented; links to external labor-market data)*

Tools: O*NET OnLine, O*NET Questionnaires. Sanchez & Levine (2009) note it attempts a **universal occupational language** but uses psychological jargon ("deductive reasoning," "speed of closure") that end-users find hard to parse.
