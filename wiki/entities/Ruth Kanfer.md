# Ruth Kanfer

Nav: [[index]]

I/O psychologist. School of Psychology, Georgia Institute of Technology. Leading figure in work motivation theory, resource allocation, and self-regulation at work.

## Key Contributions

- Developed the content–context–process meta-framework for work motivation (Kanfer, Chen & Pritchard 2008)
- Resource allocation model of motivation and skill acquisition (Kanfer & Ackerman 1989) — integrates cognitive ability and motivational processes; shows task complexity × goal type interactions
- Motivation traits and skills framework (Kanfer & Heggestad 1997) — approach/avoidance distinction
- Aging, adult development, and work motivation (Kanfer & Ackerman 2004)
- Motivation to work in later adulthood (Kanfer, Beier & Ackerman 2013)

## Major Works

- Kanfer & Ackerman (1989) — resource allocation model, *Journal of Applied Psychology*
- Kanfer, Chen & Pritchard (2008) — tripartite meta-framework
- [[wiki/sources/kanfer-frese-johnson-2017-work-motivation|Kanfer, Frese & Johnson (2017)]] — century of progress review, *Journal of Applied Psychology*
