# Marcus Munafò

Marcus R. Munafò is a psychologist and meta-scientist at the University of Bristol, best known as lead author of the "Manifesto for Reproducible Science" (2017) and as chair of the CHDI Foundation Independent Statistical Standing Committee.

## Affiliation

- University of Bristol, MRC Integrative Epidemiology Unit; School of Experimental Psychology
- UK Centre for Tobacco and Alcohol Studies (UKCTAS)

## Key Contributions

- **[[munafo-2017-manifesto|"A Manifesto for Reproducible Science" (2017, with Nosek, Bishop, Button, Chambers, et al.)]]** — *Nature Human Behaviour* — Five-category reform program for reproducible science; published in the inaugural volume of the journal
- Research on genetic epidemiology, tobacco and alcohol addiction, and cognitive neuroscience
- Methodological work on statistical power, effect size estimation, and research waste in biomedical science

## Significance

Munafò brings a biomedical epidemiology perspective to the reproducibility reform movement — his unit's focus on large-scale causal inference (Mendelian randomization, longitudinal cohort studies) gives him credibility across medicine and psychology. The Manifesto's multi-author format (10 authors from 9 institutions) was a deliberate coalition-building strategy.
