# David Chan

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

Organizational psychologist (Michigan State University; National University of Singapore). Author of the influential [[chan-1998-composition-models-typology|1998 *Journal of Applied Psychology* article]] proposing a **[[Chan's Typology of Composition Models|typology of composition models]]** — five ideal-type forms (additive, direct consensus, referent-shift consensus, dispersion, process) that specify how a construct at a lower level functionally relates to "the same" construct at a higher level.

Credits [[Steve W. J. Kozlowski]] with introducing him to multilevel research. His **[[Referent-Shift Consensus]]** and **dispersion** models are widely cited and are explicitly incorporated into [[kozlowski-klein-2000-multilevel-approach|Kozlowski & Klein (2000)]]. Also works on person-organization fit (his "cognitive misfit" construct is a worked example of a cross-level *target* construct).
