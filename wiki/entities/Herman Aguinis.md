# Herman Aguinis

Management scholar at **The George Washington University** (School of Business). Prolific methodologist and theorist on performance management, research methods, and evidence-based management; frequent writer on parsimony and theory-building quality ("theorrhea," illusory theoretical contributions).

## In this wiki

- Co-author (with [[Jason D. Marshall]] and [[Jose R. Beltran]]) of the [[marshall-aguinis-beltran-2024-theories-of-performance|2024 *AMA Annals* integrative review]] that produced the [[CORE Model of Performance]].
- Co-author (with [[Wayne F. Cascio]]) of [[cascio-aguinis-2011-criteria-concepts-measurement-evaluation|*Applied Psychology in Human Resource Management* (2011), Ch. 4 on criteria]].
- Co-author (with [[Jason R. Pierce]]) of [[pierce-aguinis-2013-tmgt-effect|Pierce & Aguinis (2013)]], proposing the [[Too-Much-of-a-Good-Thing Effect]] meta-theory. A second worked [[Meta-Theory (Management)|meta-theory]] in his corpus, alongside the CORE model.
