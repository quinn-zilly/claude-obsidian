# UK Reproducibility Network (UKRN)

The first national [[Reproducibility Networks|Reproducibility Network]] — a consortium of UK researchers across universities working with institutions, government, and funders to drive rigorous, transparent research.

## Significance

- Provides a **unified, consistent voice** for open science practices across UK research institutions.
- Its coordinated advocacy secured a **£4.5M strategic investment from Research England** to roll out open science training across 18 institutions.
- Contributes to parliamentary inquiries, helping embed open science into UK research culture top-down as well as bottom-up.
- Served as the template for the dozen-plus reproducibility networks now operating internationally.

Co-founded by [[Marcus Munafò]] (also Associate Pro Vice-Chancellor for Research Culture at the University of Bristol), who authored Box 1 of [[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0|Thibault et al. (2023)]].
