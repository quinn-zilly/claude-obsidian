# Daniel M. Wegner

**Daniel Merton Wegner** (1948-2013) was an American social psychologist and the originator of transactive memory theory. He proposed the concept of [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory]] in 1985, describing how people in close relationships collectively encode, store, and retrieve knowledge across one another's minds.

**Affiliation:** John Lindsley Professor of Psychology in Memory of William James at Harvard University (from 2000); earlier at Trinity University, Texas (1974-1990) and the University of Virginia (1990-2000). Fellow of the AAAS and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.

**Beyond TMS:** also known for work on mental control (ironic process theory), conscious will, and action identification.

In [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] he is cited as the original theorist of TMS; the Wegner et al. (1991) questionnaire is listed among established measures for dyads.
