# Eric Uhlmann

## Identity

Social/organizational psychologist. Professor, Organizational Behaviour Area, INSEAD (Singapore campus). Email: eric.luis.uhlmann@gmail.com.

## Research Focus

- Crowdsourcing in science; metascience
- Moral judgment and implicit cognition
- Research methodology and reform
- Bias in scientific evaluation

## Key Works

- [[uhlmann-2019-crowdsourcing-science|Uhlmann et al. (2019)]] — *Perspectives on Psychological Science* — Scientific Utopia III; crowdsourcing as complement to standard scientific model
- Silberzahn et al. (2018) — many analysts, one dataset; 29 teams, radical effect-size dispersion for the same research question
- Landy et al. (2018) — crowdsourcing study designs; same hypothesis, multiple independent operationalizations

## Collaborators

- [[Brian Nosek]] — UVA / Center for Open Science; co-author on Utopia III and multiple crowd-science projects
- Raphael Silberzahn — co-PI on many-analysts project
- Charles Ebersole, Christopher Chartier — crowd-science methodology collaborators

## Theoretical Contributions

Led the argument that **horizontal distribution** of research ownership, resources, and expertise is a viable and complementary model to the standard vertically integrated small-team approach. Empirically demonstrated that different analyst teams produce radically different conclusions from the same data — showing analytic flexibility is a structural (not individual failure) problem.

## Connections

- [[Crowdsourcing Science]] — primary architect of Utopia III framework
- [[Motivated Reasoning]] / [[P-hacking]] — Silberzahn et al. demonstrates that analytic flexibility at scale produces genuine uncertainty, not just individual error
