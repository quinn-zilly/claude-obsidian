# Blake E. Ashforth

Organizational behavior scholar. At the time of the 1989 paper, affiliated with **Concordia University**. Co-author with [[Fred Mael]] of [[ashforth-mael-1989-social-identity-theory-organization|"Social Identity Theory and the Organization"]] (AMR 1989), the paper that brought [[Social Identity Theory]] into the organizational behavior mainstream and reframed organizational identification as a perceptual "perception of oneness."

Work spans organizational socialization, identity and identification, role transitions, and "dirty work" / occupational identity. His earlier work on climate formation (Ashforth 1985) is cited within the 1989 paper.
