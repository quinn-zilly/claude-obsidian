# John Ioannidis

John P. A. Ioannidis is an epidemiologist, meta-scientist, and statistician at Stanford University, best known for his 2005 paper "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False," which catalyzed the meta-science movement.

## Affiliation

- Stanford University: Meta-Research Innovation Center at Stanford (METRICS); Stanford Prevention Research Center; Department of Medicine; Department of Statistics

## Key Contributions

- **"Why Most Published Research Findings Are False" (2005)** — *PLOS Medicine* — Mathematical argument that given low prior probabilities, low statistical power, and publication bias, the majority of published results in many fields are false positives. Most-cited PLOS article.
- **[[munafo-2017-manifesto]]** — co-author
- **METRICS** (Meta-Research Innovation Center at Stanford) — co-founded to advance evidence about research practices
- Extensive work on clinical trial methodology, reporting bias, and systematic reviews

## Significance

Ioannidis is arguably the most prominent meta-scientist globally. His 2005 paper gave the replication crisis movement a rigorous theoretical grounding before large empirical replication projects (like [[open-science-collaboration-2015]]) provided the empirical evidence. His METRICS center institutionalizes meta-science as a legitimate research program.

## Relationship to Open Science Movement

Ioannidis's approach is more theoretical and epidemiological than Nosek's institutional reform approach, but they converge: both argue that the current incentive structure produces a systematically biased literature, and both advocate structural reforms.
