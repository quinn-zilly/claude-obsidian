# Jeff W. Johnson

> [!note] Disambiguation
> **Not** [[Russell Johnson]] (the LMX / self-regulation researcher). This is **Jeff W. Johnson**, I/O psychologist specializing in the personality–job-performance link and synthetic validation.

I/O psychologist known for modeling *how* personality determines individual job performance.

Central contributions:

- **Johnson (2003)** performance model: extended Campbell (1990) and Motowidlo, Borman & Schmit (1997) by adding **work habits** as a fourth direct determinant (alongside knowledge, skill, motivation) and treating personality as an **indirect** determinant. See [[Personality–Performance Process Models]].
- **Johnson & Hezlett (2008)** general integrative model of personality's influence on work.
- Leading advocate for **[[Synthetic Validity|synthetic validation]]** and a shared predictor/criterion database (Johnson et al. 2010, "*Validation is like motor oil: synthetic is better*").
- Proposed a multilevel taxonomy of performance dimensions for building a synthetic-validity database.
