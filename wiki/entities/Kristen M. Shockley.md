# Kristen M. Shockley

Industrial-organizational psychologist (University of Georgia) specializing in the **work–family interface**, flexible work, and meta-analytic methods. Her research repeatedly probes whether taken-for-granted work–family beliefs survive rigorous quantitative synthesis — including the flexibility-and-telecommuting literature and gender differences in conflict.

## In this vault
- **[[shockley-et-al-2017-gender-work-family-conflict|Shockley et al. (2017)]]** — meta-analysis disentangling gender and work–family conflict; finds near-null gender differences (*JAP* monograph).
