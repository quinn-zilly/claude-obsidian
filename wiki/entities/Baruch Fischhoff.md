# Baruch Fischhoff

Department of Engineering and Public Policy + Institute for Politics and Strategy, Carnegie Mellon University. One of the founding figures of behavioral decision research / decision science.

## Affiliations

- Carnegie Mellon University (primary)
- Society for Risk Analysis (co-founder, 1981)

## Key Contributions

- Co-developer of **psychometric risk perception paradigm** (dread + uncertainty dimensions; Slovic, Fischhoff, Lichtenstein)
- **Decision-Making Competence (DMC)** individual-difference measure — validated against CEDAR longitudinal data
- Early work on **calibration training** (Lichtenstein & Fischhoff 1980: 200-judgment intensive feedback generalized)
- Applied decision science to **FDA drug benefit-risk framework**, **intelligence analysis** (Lanir & Kahneman 2006), climate change communication (from Carter admin)
- **Mental models** methodology for studying what people know about risky processes (HIV/AIDS, pandemic, radon)
- **Adolescent decision making**: analysis-first approach challenging folk claims about teen invulnerability
