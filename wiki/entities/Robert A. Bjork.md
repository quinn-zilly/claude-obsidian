# Robert A. Bjork

Robert A. Bjork is a cognitive psychologist at UCLA best known for developing the **New Theory of Disuse** (with Elizabeth Ligon Bjork) and for popularizing the concept of **desirable difficulties** in learning research.

## Key Contributions

- **[[New Theory of Disuse]]** (Bjork & Bjork, 1992): storage strength vs. retrieval strength as dissociable memory properties
- **[[Desirable Difficulties]]**: the counterintuitive principle that conditions making learning harder in the short term often produce better long-term retention
- Foundational work on the [[Testing Effect]], [[Spacing Effect]], and [[Judgment of Learning]]

## Affiliation

Distinguished Research Professor, Department of Psychology, UCLA.
