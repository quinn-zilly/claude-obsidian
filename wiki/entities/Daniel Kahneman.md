# Daniel Kahneman

Israeli-American psychologist and behavioral economist (1934–2024). Professor at Hebrew University of Jerusalem (where the 1974 work originated), later Princeton University.

With [[Amos Tversky]], co-founded the [[Heuristics and Biases Program]]. Awarded the **Nobel Prize in Economics in 2002** (alongside Vernon Smith) for integrating psychological insights into economics — the committee explicitly noted the award would have been shared with Tversky.

Author of *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011), which popularized System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs. System 2 (slow, deliberate) thinking for a general audience.

**Key contribution in this wiki:** Tversky & Kahneman (1974) — the founding empirical document of the heuristics-and-biases research program. See [[tversky-kahneman-1974-heuristics-biases]].
