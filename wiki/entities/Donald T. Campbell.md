# Donald T. Campbell

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

Psychologist and methodologist (1916–1996), founder of the modern tradition of [[Quasi-Experimental Design]] and the [[Validity Typology]]. His **internal vs. external validity** distinction (Campbell 1957; Campbell & Stanley 1963, *Experimental and Quasi-Experimental Designs for Research*) was later split by [[Thomas D. Cook|Cook]] & Campbell (1979) into the four validity types, and carried forward by [[William R. Shadish|Shadish]], Cook & Campbell (2002), on which he is the third author.

## Contribution here

Originated much of the vocabulary these chapters use: the [[Threats to Validity|threats-to-validity]] catalogue, the [[Nonequivalent Control Group Design]], the recurrent institutional cycle design, cohort controls, and the Campbell & Erlebacher (1970) critique of [[Matching (Quasi-Experiments)|matching]] under regression to the mean. His maxim — make alternative causes implausible by design — is the backbone of quasi-experimentation.
