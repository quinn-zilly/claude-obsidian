# John W. Meyer

American sociologist, professor (emeritus) at Stanford University. A founder of the **new institutionalism** in organizational sociology and of world-society / world-polity theory.

## Key Contributions

- Co-author with [[Brian Rowan]] of the foundational [[meyer-rowan-1977-institutionalized-organizations|"Formal Structure as Myth and Ceremony"]] (*AJS* 1977), which recast formal structure as ceremonial conformity to [[Rationalized Myth|rationalized myths]] rather than a tool for efficiency.
- Developed **world-society theory**: nation-states and organizations worldwide adopt strikingly similar models because of a shared global institutional culture.
- His work, alongside [[meyer-rowan-1977-institutionalized-organizations]], set up the field-level theory of [[Institutional Isomorphism]] later formalized by [[Paul DiMaggio]] and [[Walter Powell]].

## Institutional Affiliation
