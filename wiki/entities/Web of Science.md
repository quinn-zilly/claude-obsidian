# Web of Science

Bibliometric database (WoS), owned by Clarivate Analytics. Descends from the Science Citation Index created by Eugene Garfield (1955), which selectively indexed only the most-cited periodicals because manual indexing of all ~50,000 journals was infeasible.

For decades the main — often only — large-scale bibliometric data source. Its **selective, citation-based inclusion** produces a documented Western / English-language bias (Mongeon & Paul-Hus 2016): lower coverage of journals from non-English-speaking and developing countries than Scopus or [[Dimensions]].

In [[basson-et-al-2022-oa-dimensions-wos]]: indexed ~8.05M pubs (2015–2019, with affiliation), document types articles + reviews, OA status obtained by linking to [[Unpaywall]]. Reports lower overall OA (43.4% vs Dimensions' 46.6%) and far lower OA for the global South. Notably higher in **green-only** OA, reflecting the self-archiving culture of the well-represented Western countries.
