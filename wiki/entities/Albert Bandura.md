# Albert Bandura

Nav: [[index]] | [[Social Cognitive Theory]]

Stanford psychologist (1925–2021), originator of **[[Social Cognitive Theory]]** and the construct of **[[Self-Efficacy (Goal Setting)|perceived self-efficacy]]**. Developed social learning theory (observational learning / modeling; the Bobo doll studies), then reframed it as social cognitive theory built on **[[Triadic Reciprocal Causation]]** and **[[Human Agency]]**.

## Key contributions in the wiki

- **[[bandura-2001-social-cognitive-theory-agentic|Social Cognitive Theory: An Agentic Perspective (2001)]]** — the agentic synthesis: four core features of agency + three [[Modes of Human Agency|modes of agency]].
- **Self-efficacy** — task-specific belief in one's capabilities; the pivotal mechanism of agency. Four sources: mastery experience, vicarious experience, verbal persuasion, physiological/affective states (elaborated in [[schunk-usher-2012-social-cognitive-theory-motivation|Schunk & Usher 2012]]).
- **[[Collective Efficacy]]** — extended efficacy to the group level.
- **[[Moral Disengagement]]** — mechanisms by which moral self-sanctions are selectively disengaged.

Frequently cited across the vault's motivation and learning cluster (see [[Self-Efficacy (Goal Setting)]], [[Learning & Development MOC]]). His one-way social-learning account of vicarious learning is the foundation extended by [[myers-2018-coactive-vicarious-learning|Myers (2018)]] into relational [[Coactive Vicarious Learning (CVL)]].
