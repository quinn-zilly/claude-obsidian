# Alvin Gouldner

Sociologist (1920–1980); author of "The Norm of Reciprocity: A Preliminary Statement" (*American Sociological Review*, 1960), the foundational analysis of reciprocity in [[Social Exchange Theory|social exchange]].

## Key Ideas

- Argued the **[[Norm of Reciprocity|norm of reciprocity]]** is a **universal moral principle**: people should help (and refrain from injuring) those who have helped them.
- Distinguished **three senses of reciprocity**: (1) interdependent transactional exchange, (2) a folk belief, and (3) a moral norm (the "ought" quality separating norm from folk belief).
- His interdisciplinary review is the reference point [[cropanzano-mitchell-2005-social-exchange-theory|Cropanzano & Mitchell (2005)]] use to organize reciprocity rules.
