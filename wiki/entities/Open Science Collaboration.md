# Open Science Collaboration

The Open Science Collaboration (OSC) is a distributed collective of researchers who conducted the landmark 2015 replication study of 100 psychology findings, published as the single-author entity "Open Science Collaboration" in *Science*.

## Formation

The OSC was organized starting in November 2011 by [[Brian Nosek]] and colleagues to address the lack of systematic empirical evidence about the reproducibility of psychological science. The project was coordinated through the [[Center for Open Science]] and hosted on the [[Open Science Framework]] (https://osf.io/ezcuj).

## Membership

270 contributing researchers from universities worldwide. Authors are listed alphabetically in the 2015 paper. Notable members include [[Etienne LeBel]], [[Frank Bosco]], and Brian Nosek (corresponding author).

## Key Output

- **[[open-science-collaboration-2015]]** — "Estimating the Reproducibility of Psychological Science," *Science* 349, aac4716 (2015) — 100 replications of psychology studies from three 2008 journals; 36% replicated by significance criterion.

## Significance

The OSC represents the first large-scale, transparent, systematic empirical test of reproducibility in psychology. Its collective authorship model — hundreds of researchers sharing credit for one paper — was itself an open science innovation. The project demonstrated that large-scale collaborative science is feasible in psychology without centralized funding.
