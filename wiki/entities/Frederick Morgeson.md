# Frederick Morgeson

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work Design (Concept)]]

IO psychologist; Michigan State University. Primary contemporary theorist of work design. Developed WDQ with Humphrey. Co-authored Humphrey, Nahrgang & Morgeson (2007) meta-analysis. Extended work design framework to include social, contextual, and knowledge characteristics beyond classic JCM.

## Key Contributions

- **WDQ** ([[morgeson-humphrey-2006-work-design-questionnaire|Morgeson & Humphrey 2006]]): 18-category / 21-factor comprehensive measure
- **Morgeson & Campion (2002):** longitudinal quasi-experiment resolving motivational–mechanistic tradeoffs
- **Morgeson & Campion (2003):** handbook chapter (precursor to 2012 chapter)
- **Humphrey, Nahrgang & Morgeson (2007):** definitive meta-analysis of work design
- **Morgeson & Humphrey (2008):** expanded framework with social + contextual characteristics
- **Nahrgang, Morgeson & Hofmann (2011):** JD-R meta-analysis including safety outcomes

## Other work in this vault

- Lead author of the [[Morgeson et al. (2007) Panel Critique|Morgeson et al. (2007) panel critique]] questioning personality tests in selection — rebutted by [[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al. (2007)]].
- **Morgeson & Campion (1997):** the 16-source framework of social/cognitive **inaccuracy in job analysis** — the cardinal accuracy reference discussed in [[Sackett-Walmsley-Laczo-2013-job-and-work-analysis|Sackett et al. (2013)]]. Also the Morgeson & Dierdorff journal-count compilation cited by [[Sanchez-Levine-2012-rise-fall-job-analysis|Sanchez & Levine (2012)]] to document job analysis's decline in top outlets.
