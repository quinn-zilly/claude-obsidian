# Melissa K. Carsten

Nav: [[index]] | [[Leadership MOC]]

Management scholar (Winthrop University at the time of the 2014 review). Known for empirical work on **follower role orientations** and **co-production beliefs** (Carsten et al. 2010; Carsten & Uhl-Bien 2012, 2013).

## Key Contributions

- Co-author, **[[uhl-bien-et-al-2014-followership-theory-review|Followership Theory: A Review and Research Agenda]]** (2014).
- First formal empirical investigation of followers' own views of followership (Carsten et al. 2010) — distinguishing passive vs. proactive follower schemas enacted contingent on climate; foundational to [[Co-Production of Leadership]].
