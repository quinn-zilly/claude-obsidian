# Mina Beigi

## Identity

Organizational behavior / careers researcher. Associate Professor, University of Southampton (UK). Email: m.beigi@soton.ac.uk.

## Research Focus

- Career success among minority groups (women, racial/ethnic minorities, persons with disabilities, LGBTQ+)
- Diversity, equity, and inclusion in organizations
- Subjective career success; identity at work
- Systematic review methods in management

## Key Works

- [[beigi-2025-career-success-minority|Beigi et al. (2025)]] — *Journal of Management* — systematic review, complex visibility framework, four-group career success analysis; 337 articles

## Collaborators

- [[Melika Shirmohammadi]] — University of Houston; co-author on 2025 JOM piece

## Theoretical Contributions

Introduced **complex visibility** construct (three-state model: hypervisibility, invisibility, managed visibility) as mechanism linking minority status to career success disparities. Integrated resource management (Spurk et al.) with "bodies out of place" (Puwar) to build a cross-group explanatory framework.

- [[On-the-Job Development]] — OJD resource denial as structural mechanism for career gaps
- [[Outsider-Within Status]] — Collins (1986) anchor for insider-position / outsider-experience tension
