# Adriaan de Groot

Dutch psychologist and chess master (1914–2006). His *Thought and Choice in Chess* (1946/1978) is the founding empirical study of chess expertise: world-class players did *not* search more deeply than club players but **accessed the best move during initial perception** — implying pattern-based recall from memory rather than exhaustive search. This finding seeded [[William G. Chase|Chase]] & [[Herbert A. Simon|Simon]]'s (1973) theory and, through it, the whole [[Expert Performance (Ericsson Definition)|expert-performance]] program reviewed in [[ericsson-lehmann-1996-expert-exceptional-performance|Ericsson & Lehmann (1996)]].
