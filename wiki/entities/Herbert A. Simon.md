# Herbert A. Simon

Polymath (1916–2001), Carnegie Mellon; Nobel laureate in Economics (1978), Turing Award (1975). Foundational to cognitive science, AI, and the study of **bounded rationality** and problem solving (Newell & Simon, *Human Problem Solving*, 1972).

Relevant here for the [[William G. Chase|Chase]] & Simon (1973) theory of chess expertise: superior performance as knowledge-based [[Chunking|pattern retrieval]] within general information-processing limits. The account seeded modern expertise research even as [[ericsson-lehmann-1996-expert-exceptional-performance|Ericsson & Lehmann (1996)]] extended and partly overturned its pure-STM chunking model via [[Long-Term Working Memory]].
