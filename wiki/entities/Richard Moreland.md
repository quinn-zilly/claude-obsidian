# Richard Moreland

**Richard L. Moreland** (1951-2017) was a social psychologist and Professor Emeritus of Psychology at the University of Pittsburgh, known for research on small-group behavior and group socialization, much of it in long-running collaboration with John Levine (jointly awarded the 2012 Joseph E. McGrath Award for Lifetime Achievement in the Study of Groups).

**TMS contribution:** his laboratory studies of group-versus-individual training - notably with [[Linda Argote]] and Diane Liang (Liang, Moreland & Argote, 1995) - showed that groups trained *together* recalled assembly procedures better and produced higher-quality output than groups trained individually, an effect mediated by the development of a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]]. This is among the most influential demonstrations that group training builds TMS.

In [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] he is cited for the group-dynamics perspective on TMS.
