# Robert Karasek

Nav: [[index]] | [[Job Demands-Control Model]]

Originator of the **[[Job Demands-Control Model]]** (Karasek, 1979, *Administrative Science Quarterly*), one of the most influential frameworks linking work design to mental and physical health. Argued that job strain is greatest when demands are high and control (decision latitude) is low, and that control buffers demands.

With Töres Theorell extended the model to include social support and developed it into a broader program on stress, productivity, and the reconstruction of working life (Karasek & Theorell, 1990, *Healthy Work*). His tradition underpins much of the epidemiological work design literature (e.g. Whitehall, Whitehall II).
