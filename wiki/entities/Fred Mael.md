# Fred Mael

Organizational psychologist. At the time of the 1989 paper, affiliated with **Wayne State University**. Co-author with [[Blake E. Ashforth]] of [[ashforth-mael-1989-social-identity-theory-organization|"Social Identity Theory and the Organization"]] (AMR 1989).

His 1988 dissertation (Wayne State) developed the **6-item organizational identification scale** (e.g., "This organization's successes are my successes") and used confirmatory factor analysis against the OCQ to show identification and [[Organizational Commitment|commitment]] are empirically distinct (two-factor AGFI .825 vs single-factor .780). He also found that perceived **distinctiveness** and **prestige** of a religious college predicted alumni identification, and that identification predicted donating, recruiting, and attendance. The Mael scale became the standard measure in the organizational identification literature.
