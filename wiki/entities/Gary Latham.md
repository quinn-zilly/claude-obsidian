# Gary P. Latham

Nav: [[index]]

Industrial–organizational psychologist. Joseph L. Rotman School of Business, University of Toronto. Co-developer (with [[Edwin Locke]]) of [[Goal-Setting Theory]].

## Key Contributions

- Extensive field research validating GST (logging, trucking, government, engineering)
- Developed the [[Situational Interview]] (Latham et al. 1980) — highest-validity selection interview
- Demonstrated participation-in-goal-setting = primarily cognitive (not motivational) benefit
- Self-management training for attendance (Frayne & Latham 1987) — application of F.H. Kanfer methodology
- Co-developed [[High-Performance Cycle]]
- Collaborative resolution of Erez–Latham dispute on participative vs. assigned goals (Latham, Erez & Locke 1988)
- Proximal goals on complex tasks (Latham & Seijts 1999)

## Major Works

- Latham & Baldes (1975) — truck driver goal-setting field study ($250k savings)
- Latham & Kinne (1974) — logging goal-setting
- Latham, Saari, Pursell & Campion (1980) — situational interview
- [[frayne-latham-1987-self-management-attendance|Frayne & Latham (1987)]] — self-management attendance training (*JAP* 72(3))
- Locke & Latham (1990) — *A Theory of Goal Setting and Task Performance*
- [[wiki/sources/locke-latham-2002-goal-setting-theory|Locke & Latham (2002)]] — 35-year summary, *American Psychologist*
