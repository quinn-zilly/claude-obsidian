# Eric Trist

Nav: [[index]] | [[Sociotechnical Systems Theory]]

Organizational psychologist at the Tavistock Institute; co-founder of **[[Sociotechnical Systems Theory]]**. With Ken Bamforth, conducted the landmark Longwall coal-mining studies (Trist & Bamforth, 1951, *Human Relations*) showing that mechanizing work without regard to its social organization caused conflict and productivity loss — and that self-managing miner groups completing whole tasks alleviated the damage.

This work established the principle of **joint optimization** of social and technical systems and seeded the development of [[Autonomous Work Groups]] (Trist, Susman & Brown, 1977). The largest cluster in [[parker-morgeson-johns-2017-100-years-work-design|Parker et al. (2017)]] traces to this tradition.
