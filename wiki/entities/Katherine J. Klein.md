# Katherine J. Klein

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

Organizational scholar (University of Pennsylvania, Wharton; earlier University of Maryland) and co-architect of the **multilevel paradigm**. Co-editor with [[Steve W. J. Kozlowski]] of *Multilevel Theory, Research, and Methods in Organizations* (2000).

## Contributions

Co-author of the foundational Chapter 1 ([[kozlowski-klein-2000-multilevel-approach|Kozlowski & Klein 2000]]) and lead author of the accessible practitioner essay ([[klein-kozlowski-2000-micro-to-meso|Klein & Kozlowski 2000]]) that walks a hypothetical researcher ("Dr. Faust") through the four choices of multilevel research. Earlier work with Dansereau & Hall (1994, *AMR*) — "Levels issues in theory development, data collection, and analysis" — established the **[[Cross-Level Models|levels framework]]** (including [[Cross-Level Models#Frog-pond models|frog-pond models]]) that the 2000 synthesis extends. Klein, Conn, Smith & Sorra empirically showed that survey-item **referent** wording (self vs. unit) shifts within-group agreement.
