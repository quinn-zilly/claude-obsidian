# Roger Giner-Sorolla

Social psychologist at the **University of Kent** (Keynes College, Canterbury, UK). Research focuses on moral psychology, emotion, and social judgment. Known in open science circles for his 2012 *Perspectives on Psychological Science* essay diagnosing the publication bottleneck as the root cause of psychology's replication crisis.

## Key Contribution

[[giner-sorolla-2012-science-or-art|Giner-Sorolla (2012)]] argues that aesthetic criteria — statistical perfection, narrative conformity, novelty — distort scientific judgment because of an information-economic bottleneck: too many researchers, too few publication slots. This is distinct from (and arguably more fundamental than) fraud or p-value misuse per se. The 1970s crisis solutions (multi-study papers, meta-analysis) failed because they never addressed the bottleneck itself.
