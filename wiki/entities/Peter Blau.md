# Peter Blau

Sociologist (1918–2002); author of *Exchange and Power in Social Life* (1964), the foundational statement of **[[Social Exchange Theory|social exchange]]** that organizational research draws on most.

## Key Ideas

- **Economic vs. social exchange** — social exchange entails **unspecified, diffuse future obligations**; "the nature of the return cannot be bargained." Only social exchange engenders **trust, gratitude, and personal obligation**; economic exchange does not.
- Identified **trust** as a defining outcome of favorable social exchanges.
- Treated social and economic exchange as **types of transactions** (Cropanzano & Mitchell note he was ambiguous about whether they're also types of *relationships*).

His distinction is the backbone of "contemporary social exchange theory" in the [[Organizational Justice|justice]] literature and of the exchange-quality mediators (trust, [[Organizational Commitment|commitment]], POS, LMX).
