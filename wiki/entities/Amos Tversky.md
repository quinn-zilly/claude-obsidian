# Amos Tversky

Israeli cognitive and mathematical psychologist (1937–1996). Professor at Hebrew University of Jerusalem (where the 1974 paper originated) and later Stanford University.

With [[Daniel Kahneman]], co-founded the [[Heuristics and Biases Program]] — the systematic empirical study of cognitive biases in judgment under uncertainty. Their collaboration produced some of the most cited work in the social sciences.

**Key contribution in this wiki:** Tversky & Kahneman (1974) — "Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases" — the foundational paper identifying [[Representativeness Heuristic]], [[Availability Heuristic]], and [[Anchoring and Adjustment]] as the three core heuristics of probabilistic judgment. See [[tversky-kahneman-1974-heuristics-biases]].

**Note:** Tversky died in 1996; Kahneman was awarded the Nobel Prize in Economics in 2002, which the committee explicitly acknowledged would have been shared with Tversky had he lived.
