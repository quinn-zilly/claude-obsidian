# Brenton M. Wiernik

Brenton M. Wiernik is a quantitative/personnel psychologist (University of South Florida at time of writing) known for psychometric meta-analysis and work on personality, careers, and research methods. Lead author of the PBCO meta-analysis that reframed protean/boundaryless orientations as a single **proactive career orientation** factor.

## Key work in vault
- [[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal (2019)]]
- [[campbell-wiernik-2015-modeling-assessment-work-performance|Campbell & Wiernik (2015)]] — co-authored the Annual Review synthesis behind the [[Campbell Model of Job Performance]]
- [[wiernik-et-al-2021-meta-analytic-cpa|Wiernik et al. (2021)]] — lead author; developed [[Criterion Profile Analysis|MACPA]], extending CPA to meta-analytic data (`configural` R package)
