# Kyle Lewis

> [!note] This page was previously filed under "Kristina Lewis" (a mis-transcription in an earlier ingest). Renamed 2026-06-09; "Kristina Lewis" retained as an alias so older links still resolve.

**Kyle Lewis** is an organizational scholar best known for developing the most widely adopted field measure of transactive memory systems.

**Key work:** *"Measuring transactive memory systems in the field: Scale development and validation"* (Lewis, 2003, *Journal of Applied Psychology*, 88(4), 587-604). The paper introduced a **15-item self-report scale** with three subscales - specialization, [[Task Credibility|credibility]], and [[Task Coordination|coordination]] - validated across a lab sample (124 teams) and field samples of MBA consulting and technology-company teams, establishing convergent, criterion-related, and discriminant validity.

In [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]], Lewis (2003) is singled out as the **most widely adopted** TMS measure, and the review recommends standardizing on it to accumulate comparable findings.
