# Dimensions

Bibliometric database launched in 2018 by Digital Science. The broadest large-scale indexer of scientific documents.

Distinctive design: inclusion keyed on a **single criterion — presence of a DOI** — populated largely from Crossref, rather than selective criteria (citations, reputation) as in [[Web of Science]] or Scopus. This yields much wider coverage, especially of smaller national journals and Southern OA platforms (AJOL, AmeliCA, SciELO).

Trade-offs (per [[basson-et-al-2022-oa-dimensions-wos]]):
- Classifies **all** journal documents as "articles" — no document-type disaggregation, so meeting abstracts/editorials cannot be filtered out.
- Higher rate of missing affiliation metadata; standardized via the GRID identifier system + ORCID, historically thin for Africa, South America, Central Asia.
- Ships Unpaywall OA status in its data.

Because of its breadth, Dimensions reports more OA than WoS, especially outside North America and Europe — see [[Geographic Bias in Indexing]].
