# Frank Bosco

Frank A. Bosco is an organizational psychology and research methods scholar best known as the lead developer of metaBUS, a large-scale curated database of applied psychology research findings.

## Affiliation

Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU), School of Business, Richmond, Virginia.

## Key Contributions

- **[[bosco-2020-metabus|"Advancing Meta-Analysis With metaBUS" (2020)]]** — *AMPPS* — Introduced metaBUS to the broader academic community; described the database's hierarchical taxonomy, search modes, and open-science implications
- Development and maintenance of metaBUS (metabus.org): 1M+ coded bivariate relationships from 27 journals (1980–2018)
- Research on methodological best practices in meta-analysis; effect size computation

## Research Focus

Bosco's program aims to make cumulative science practical and reproducible in applied psychology — particularly I/O (industrial-organizational) psychology, which has rich databases of bivariate findings but limited infrastructure for systematic synthesis.

## Notable

Frank Bosco also appears as a contributor in [[open-science-collaboration-2015]] (as one of 270 contributing authors) — connecting his open science work in databases to the broader replication reform movement.
