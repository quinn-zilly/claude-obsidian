# Steve W. J. Kozlowski

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

Organizational psychologist (Michigan State University) and a principal architect of the **multilevel paradigm** in organizational science. With [[Katherine J. Klein]], co-edited *Multilevel Theory, Research, and Methods in Organizations* (2000) and co-authored its foundational Chapter 1 ([[kozlowski-klein-2000-multilevel-approach|Kozlowski & Klein 2000]]) and the companion *Organizational Research Methods* essay ([[klein-kozlowski-2000-micro-to-meso|Klein & Kozlowski 2000]]).

## Contributions

Central to the theory of **[[Emergence (Organizational)|emergence]]** and the **[[Composition vs Compilation|composition/compilation]]** distinction — arguing that bottom-up emergent phenomena are not all isomorphic and shared, and elaborating **compilation** (configural, discontinuous) forms neglected in the literature. Advanced the **[[Global, Shared, and Configural Unit Properties|typology of unit-level constructs]]** and the treatment of **time** in multilevel models ([[Entrainment]], differential time scales). Also a major team-effectiveness and team-training theorist; appears across the vault's team cluster (e.g., team compilation, [[Collective Efficacy]], regulation).

Mentor to [[David Chan]] (Chan credits Kozlowski for "introducing me to multilevel research").
