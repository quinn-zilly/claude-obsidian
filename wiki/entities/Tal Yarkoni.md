# Tal Yarkoni

Psychologist and computational scientist at the University of Texas at Austin (at time of 2017 publication). Key contributor to quantitative methodology reform in psychology, psychoinformatics, and the application of machine learning to behavioral science.

## Key Contributions

- **Yarkoni & Westfall (2017):** "Choosing Prediction Over Explanation in Psychology" — foundational argument for importing machine learning concepts (cross-validation, regularization, overfitting) into psychological research; see [[yarkoni-westfall-2017-prediction-vs-explanation]]
- Psychoinformatics: intersection of psychological and computing sciences (Yarkoni, 2012)
- Large-scale personality and language use analysis (Yarkoni, 2010): 100,000-word blog analysis; personality correlates of word use
- Critiques of inflated fMRI correlations in small samples (Yarkoni, 2009)
- Work on Big Data definitions and limitations in psychology (Yarkoni, 2014)
- Python/R tutorials for machine learning in psychology: http://github.com/tyarkoni/PPS2016

## Affiliation
