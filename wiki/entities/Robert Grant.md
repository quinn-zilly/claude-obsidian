# Robert Grant

Robert M. Grant is a British-American strategist and the author of one of the foundational papers in the knowledge-based view of the firm. He is best known for articulating the firm as primarily an institution for integrating specialist knowledge.

## Affiliation

Georgetown University (McDonough School of Business), Washington D.C.

## Key Contributions

- **[[grant-1996-knowledge-based-theory|"Toward a Knowledge-Based Theory of the Firm" (1996)]]** — SMJ Special Issue. Identified four knowledge integration mechanisms (rules/directives, sequencing, routines, group problem-solving) and the concept of **common knowledge** as the foundation for integration without homogenization.
- *Contemporary Strategy Analysis* (textbook, multiple editions) — one of the most widely adopted MBA strategy textbooks

## Relationship to KBV

Grant's 1996 paper differs from [[Jay Barney]]'s resource-based view by focusing not on *whether* resources are valuable but on *how* firms integrate specialist knowledge to produce goods and services. Grant's contribution is more process-oriented and micro-foundational than RBV.
