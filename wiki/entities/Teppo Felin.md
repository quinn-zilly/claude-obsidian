# Teppo Felin

Teppo Felin is a strategic management and organizational theory scholar known for his work on microfoundations of organizational capabilities and epistemology of social science.

## Affiliation

University of Oxford (Saïd Business School); previously Brigham Young University (BYU) at time of writing the 2013 paper with Barney.

## Key Contributions

- **[[barney-felin-2013-microfoundations|"What Are Microfoundations?" (2013, with Jay Barney)]]** — AMP — Conceptual clarification of microfoundations; additive vs. emergent aggregation
- Work on the epistemology of strategic management: how do organizations "see" opportunities?
- Agent-based modeling applications to strategic theory

## Research Focus

Felin's broader program interrogates the causal mechanisms linking individual cognition and action to organizational outcomes — the microfoundations problem applied to perception, search, and learning. His work connects philosophy of mind, cognitive science, and organizational theory.
