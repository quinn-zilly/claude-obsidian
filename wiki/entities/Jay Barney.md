# Jay Barney

Jay B. Barney is a strategic management scholar and the principal architect of the **resource-based view (RBV)** of the firm. His 1991 paper "Firm Resources and Sustained Competitive Advantage" is among the most cited articles in management research.

## Affiliation

University of Utah (David Eccles School of Business), Salt Lake City.

## Key Contributions

- **"Firm Resources and Sustained Competitive Advantage" (1991)** — *Journal of Management* — Articulated the VRIN framework: resources must be Valuable, Rare, Imperfectly Imitable, and Non-substitutable to generate sustained competitive advantage.
- **[[barney-felin-2013-microfoundations|"What Are Microfoundations?" (2013, with Teppo Felin)]]** — *Academy of Management Perspectives* — Provided conceptual clarity on microfoundational analysis; distinguished additive from emergent aggregation.

## Relationship to Knowledge-Based View

Barney's RBV is the parent framework for the [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm]]. Grant (1996) extends RBV by making knowledge the central resource type. Barney & Felin (2013) extend it further by demanding that RBV theorists specify the individual-level mechanisms underlying collective-level capabilities.
