# Center for Open Science

The Center for Open Science (COS) is a non-profit organization based in Charlottesville, Virginia, with a mission to increase openness, integrity, and reproducibility of scientific research.

## Founding and Leadership

- Founded 2013 by [[Brian Nosek]] and Jeff Spies
- Funded primarily by the Laura and John Arnold Foundation
- Executive director: Brian Nosek

## Key Products and Initiatives

| Product/Initiative | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **[[Open Science Framework]] (OSF)** | Free, open platform for research project management, preregistration, and data sharing |
| **Registered Reports** | Publishing format with in-principle acceptance before results; >100 journals adopted by 2018 |
| **Open Science Badges** | Open data / open materials / preregistration badges for journals; Psychological Science adoption showed 10× increase in open data sharing |
| **Transparency and Openness Promotion (TOP) Guidelines** | Journal policy framework; 757+ journal signatories as of 2016 |
| **Preregistration Challenge** | Incentive program (awards, education) for completing Registered Reports |

## Major Projects

- **[[open-science-collaboration-2015|Reproducibility Project: Psychology]]** — 100-study replication initiative coordinated through COS; produced the Science 2015 paper
- **Reproducibility Project: Cancer Biology** — analogous replication effort in cancer biology

## Significance

COS operates at the intersection of technology (OSF infrastructure), policy (TOP guidelines), and culture change (badges, incentives). It is the organizational spine of the open science reform movement — providing both the tools and the advocacy for systemic change.
