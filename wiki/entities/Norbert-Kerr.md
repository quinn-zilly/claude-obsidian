# Norbert Kerr

Social psychologist. Faculty at Michigan State University. Best known for coining the term **HARKing** (Hypothesizing After the Results are Known) in a 1998 paper that became foundational to the open science and replication crisis literature.

## Key Contribution

[[kerr-1998-harking|Kerr 1998]] — defined HARKing, distinguished three forms, identified cognitive and social mechanisms enabling it, and proposed reforms (preregistration, confirmatory/exploratory separation, registered reports) that the open science movement later institutionalized.
