# Leaetta M. Hough

I/O psychologist, principal of **The Dunnette Group, Ltd.** (St. Paul, MN), and a leading authority on personality in work settings. A key member of the **Project A** personality research team (with John Kamp and Bruce Barge) that established the criterion-related validity of personality measures by summarizing validities **by construct**.

Central contributions:

- Champion of **[[Facet-Level Personality Measurement|facet-level measurement]]** — Hough (1992) showed lower-order facets of the same Big Five factor correlate very differently with performance ("*The 'Big Five' personality variables — construct confusion: description versus prediction*").
- Originated the **[[Nomological-Web Clustering]]** approach to personality structure (Hough & Ones 2001).
- Argued five factors are too few; at least seven needed (Hough 1997).
- Longtime critic of over-reliance on broad Big Five factors; co-author of the "beyond the Big Five" agenda.

Frequent collaborators: [[Deniz S. Ones]], [[Frederick L. Oswald]], [[Jeff W. Johnson]], Adrian Furnham, Stephan Dilchert.
