# Murphy & Cleveland

Kevin R. Murphy and Jeanette N. Cleveland, authors of *Understanding Performance Appraisal: Social, Organizational, and Goal-Oriented Perspectives* (1995).

Key contribution relevant to the wiki:
- Reframed appraisal as a **motivated act embedded in a social context** rather than a pure measurement task. Their **four competing rater goals** — task, interpersonal, strategic, and internalized — explain why accuracy is frequently *not* the driver of a rating, and why managers rationally inflate and retrofit ratings ([[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]; [[Three Assumptions of Performance Rating]]).
