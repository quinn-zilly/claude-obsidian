# Frederick Herzberg

Nav: [[index]] | [[Job Characteristics Model]]

Psychologist who developed **Motivator-Hygiene (Two-Factor) Theory** (Herzberg, Mausner & Snyderman, 1959; Herzberg, 1966). Through interviews with accountants and engineers, concluded that only intrinsic work factors (recognition, achievement) are truly motivational, while hygiene factors (pay, conditions) merely prevent dissatisfaction.

Though the specific theory was largely discredited (Locke & Henne, 1986), it was historically pivotal: it explicitly proposed that **intrinsic job features drive motivation**, sparking the job enrichment movement and stimulating the research stream that culminated in the [[Job Characteristics Model]] (Hackman & Oldham). His "One More Time: How Do You Motivate Employees?" remains a much-reprinted *Harvard Business Review* classic.
