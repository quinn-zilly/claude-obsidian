Last ingest: 2026-06-29 — **[[ashforth-mael-1989-social-identity-theory-organization|Ashforth & Mael (1989)]]**, *Social Identity Theory and the Organization* (AMR). Imported **[[Social Identity Theory|SIT]]** (Tajfel & Turner) into OB and redefined **[[Social Identification|organizational identification]]** as a *purely perceptual* "**perception of oneness** with a group" (the answer to "Who am I?") — surgically separated from [[Organizational Commitment|commitment]], internalization ("I am" vs "I believe"), loyalty (affect), and effort (behavior). Mael's (1988) 6-item identification scale ("this org's successes are my successes") beats the OCQ in a two-factor CFA (AGFI .825 vs .780) → constructs are differentiable (see new **contradiction callout** on [[Organizational Commitment]]). Antecedents: group **distinctiveness**, **prestige**, **out-group salience**, group-formation factors. Consequences: identity-congruent activity, institutional support, **[[Self-Categorization and Self-Stereotyping|self-stereotyping]]** (→ depersonalization, conformity), + reinforces own antecedents. Biggest contribution = the **[[Psychological Group]]**: identification with a collectivity can arise *without* interpersonal cohesion/similarity/interaction ([[Minimal Group Paradigm|mere categorization suffices]]), reversing the "interpersonal regard aggregates up" logic. Applied to **socialization** (socialization→identification→internalization indirect path; investiture/divestiture; [[Routinization of Charisma]], AA vs NCA), **role conflict** (identity = amalgam of loosely-coupled identities; resolved by ordering/separating/buffering not integrating; Challenger "engineering hat/management hat"), and **intergroup relations** ([[In-Group Bias and Intergroup Comparison]]; conflict without objective causes; legitimacy mitigates). New: 7 concepts + 2 entities ([[Blake E. Ashforth]], [[Fred Mael]]) + 1 source (c-000734–743). Updated [[Organizational Commitment]], [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]], [[index]].

Prior ingest: 2026-06-15 — **[[parker-vandenbroeck-holman-2017-work-design-influences|Parker, Van den Broeck & Holman (2017)]]**, *Work Design Influences* (AMR Annals). The **antecedents-side companion** to the [[parker-morgeson-johns-2017-100-years-work-design|100-years review]]: flips work design from *independent* to **dependent** variable and asks what *causes* work design. The [[Work Design Influences Framework]] = four levels (higher-level external [global/national/occupational] → organizational → work-group → individual) × three mechanisms each (direct; indirect via managers' AMO; indirect via employees' AMO). Proximal engine = the [[Ability-Motivation-Opportunity Model|AMO model]] (motivation + KSAs + opportunity-incl-power) for both managers (formal decisions) and employees (informal [[Job Crafting|crafting]]/i-deals). Most higher-level effects are **mediated, not deterministic**; top-down dominates but bottom-up is real-but-slow. Evidence strongest for **organizational factors** (strategy/[[Strategic HRM|SHRM]], operational uncertainty, technology [SBTC/RBTC], org design) and **national institutions/[[Institutional Regimes & Employment Regime Theory|regimes]]** (the "Nordic advantage"); weakest for global/national-culture/occupational. Bridges work-design cluster to [[Institutional & Strategy MOC|institutional theory & strategy]] via [[Institutional Isomorphism|isomorphism]]. New: 4 concepts (2 stubs) + 2 entities ([[Anja Van den Broeck]], [[David Holman]]) + 1 source (c-000727–733). Updated [[Sharon Parker]], [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]], [[index]].

Prior ingest: 2026-06-12 — [[parker-morgeson-johns-2017-100-years-work-design|Parker, Morgeson & Johns (2017)]], the **JAP centennial review of work design**. Scientific mapping of 5,708 articles → **five historically-anchored clusters**: sociotechnical/AWG, JCM, job demands-control, job demands-resources, role theory. Central argument: clusters reflect **history, not topic similarity**, so cross-cluster **integration** is the field's biggest opportunity (interdisciplinary, elaborated/WDQ, relational, learning-development perspectives + a multilevel theory). JAP scorecard: only 6.4% of work-design articles but ~19% of citations — most-cited outlet by 2x. Work design influences economics/epidemiology/HBR/policy, usually without the academic label. Future agenda is **proactive + dynamic + multilevel**: ask what role work design plays in achieving outcomes and enabling adaptation to automation. Prior ingest: 2026-06-12 — [[morgeson-humphrey-2006-work-design-questionnaire|Morgeson & Humphrey (2006)]]: backfilled the **primary WDQ development paper** (the [[Work Design Questionnaire|WDQ]] concept + the 2007 meta-analysis were already in the vault, but this foundational 2006 source had no note). 107 work-characteristic terms → **18 categories**, but the best CFA is a **21-factor** model (autonomy → 3 facets, interdependence → 2 directions; CFI .91). N=540 incumbents / 243 jobs; avg α ≈ .87 (only ergonomics < .70); scales converge with archival DOT/O*NET. Key theory payoff: **task** characteristics raise satisfaction *without* raising training/compensation cost; only **knowledge** characteristics drive those costs → a route around the classic motivational trade-off; **social support** incrementally predicts satisfaction and is *negatively* tied to training cost. Meta + work-design cluster only; no new entities.
