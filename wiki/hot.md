## Key Insights from This Batch

**Knowledge Management sub-domain:**
- Grant (1996) extends existing KM pages with micro-level integration mechanics: four coordination mechanisms (rules/directives, sequencing, routines, group problem-solving) and the *common knowledge* concept that maps directly onto [[Transactive Memory Systems]]
- Barney & Felin (2013) provide vocabulary for the micro-macro gap identified in [[zahra-2020-knowledge-integration]]: additive vs. emergent aggregation; microfoundations are not reductionism

**Open Science sub-domain (new):**
- [[open-science-collaboration-2015]]: 36% replication rate in psychology; replication effects half the magnitude of originals; cognitive > social psychology
- [[nosek-2018-preregistration]]: preregistration separates confirmatory (prediction) from exploratory (description) research; garden of forking paths; [[Registered Reports]] as strongest form
- [[lebel-2018-credibility-framework]]: four credibility dimensions — transparency, analytic reproducibility, analytic robustness, effect replicability; replication taxonomy from exact to conceptual
- [[munafo-2017-manifesto]]: five reform categories (methods, reporting, reproducibility, evaluation, incentives); badges 10× data sharing; >40 journals adopted Registered Reports
- **[[nosek-2012-scientific-utopia-ii]]** (NEW): core insight: [[Conflict of Interest in Science]] is not character flaw but ordinary human psychology under misaligned incentives; publishing rewards novelty+positive results; accuracy is abstract/distal. Solutions: [[Preregistration]] (separates confirmatory from exploratory), [[Open Workflow]] (makes research visible, enables accountability), [[Paradigm-Driven Research]] (reuses methods, reduces waste), PLoS ONE model (judge soundness not importance)

**Semantic Web sub-domain (new):**
- [[lehmann-2015-dbpedia]]: DBpedia extracts 400M+ structured facts from 111 Wikipedia editions; LOD cloud hub; used in IBM Watson, NLP pipelines
- DBpedia/LOD connects to KM domain: codification strategy, common knowledge, tacit→explicit conversion at scale

## Cross-Domain Connections (NEW 2026-05-21, Utopia III batch)

- **[[Crowdsourcing Science]] (open science) ↔ [[Conflict of Interest in Science]] / [[Motivated Reasoning]] (open science):** crowdsourcing is a structural fix — when many analysts analyze same data, no individual's career depends on outcome; dilutes the incentive misalignment that produces motivated reasoning
- **[[Vertical Integration (Science)]] (open science) ↔ [[Institutional Isomorphism]] (inst. theory):** the standard model is a normatively isomorphic structure; same lab template reproduced across academia via training, journal conventions, and career incentives; crowdsourcing is anti-isomorphic reform
- **[[Matthew Effect (Science)]] (open science) ↔ [[Complex Visibility]] (DEI) ↔ [[Institutional Isomorphism]] (inst. theory):** three-way convergence — normative isomorphism reproduces elite-institution advantage (Matthew effect) and somatic norm (complex visibility) via the same mechanisms (credential filters, professional networks, mimetic hiring)
- **[[Crowdsourcing Science]] (open science) ↔ [[Knowledge Transfer]] / [[Organizational Learning]] (org KM):** Silberzahn round-robin feedback = visible inter-analyst knowledge transfer; crowd science externalizes tacit analytic knowledge; Many Labs = distributed organizational learning at science scale
- **[[Many Labs]] (open science) ↔ [[Microfoundations]] (org KM):** both expose that aggregate outcomes (replication rate / firm capability) depend critically on individual-level variation (analyst choices / individual cognition) — same macro question, radically different micro implementations
- **[[Horizontal Distribution (Science)]] ↔ [[Paradigm-Driven Research]] (open science):** both advocate systematic variation over wholesale reinvention; [[Paradigm-Driven Research]] suggests reusing established procedures; [[Horizontal Distribution (Science)]] distributes that systematic variation across many labs

## Cross-Domain Connections (NEW 2026-05-21, Beigi batch)

- **[[Complex Visibility]] (DEI) ↔ [[Learning Agility]] (leadership dev):** managed visibility is a cognitive tax that crowds out developmental risk-taking; psychological safety (suppressed by hypervisibility) is prerequisite for agile learning from experience
- **[[Complex Visibility]] (DEI) ↔ [[Motivated Reasoning]] (open science):** both describe ordinary cognition (not malice) producing systematic bias; majority-group managers don't see exclusion behavior as biased for same reason researchers don't see p-hacking as dishonest — motivated cognition under misaligned incentives
- **[[Complex Visibility]] (DEI) ↔ [[Institutional Isomorphism]] (inst. theory):** normative isomorphism transmits "somatic norm" (who looks like a leader) across organizations; complex visibility is a structural feature of entire organizational fields, not individual bad actors
- **[[Invisibility (Career)]] (DEI) ↔ [[Knowledge Transfer]] / [[Organizational Learning]] (org KM):** voice suppression and contribution non-attribution = epistemic resource loss; diversity in organizations is a knowledge asset destroyed by invisibility mechanisms
- **[[Outsider-Within Status]] (DEI) ↔ [[Microfoundations]] (org KM):** outsider-within perspective is the individual-level epistemic resource that microfoundations theories of competitive advantage can't model without addressing identity dynamics
- **[[Glass Cliff]] (DEI) ↔ [[Developmental Experiences]] (leadership dev):** glass cliff is the dark-side inversion of turnaround/stretch assignment OJD — high developmental potential, but no support, and disproportionate blame attribution on failure
- **[[Subjective Career Success]] (DEI) ↔ [[On-the-Job Development]] (leadership dev):** authenticity dimension of subjective career success = prerequisite for OJD yield; managed visibility burden means minority employees cannot fully access OJD even when formally offered the same assignments

## Cross-Domain Connections (NEW 2026-05-21, Yost batch)

- **[[On-the-Job Development]] (leadership dev) ↔ [[Knowledge Transfer]] (org KM):** OJD is primarily tacit knowledge transfer via experience; same mechanisms (socialization, personnel movement, apprenticeship) apply
- **[[Learning Agility]] (leadership dev) ↔ [[Organizational Learning]] (org KM):** individual learning agility aggregates to org learning capacity; Argote's "latent context" enables or inhibits individual-level extraction
- **[[Developmental Experiences]] (leadership dev) ↔ [[Learning Curves]] (org KM):** experience accumulation = learning curve descent; OJD theory specifies *which* experiences produce non-linear jumps
- **[[70-20-10 Rule]] (leadership dev) ↔ [[Tacit vs Explicit Knowledge]] (org KM):** ratio maps onto knowledge types — 70% experience = tacit acquisition; 10% training = explicit/codified; relationship-based 20% = tacit via socialization
- **[[Institutional Isomorphism]] (inst. theory) ↔ [[On-the-Job Development]] (leadership dev):** normative isomorphism in HR profession = convergent adoption of same OJD frameworks across firms; Yost & Plunkett's catalysts spread via professional community (mimetic + normative)
- **[[Knowing-Doing Gap]] (implicit in Yost & Plunkett) ↔ [[Motivated Reasoning]] (open science):** both describe systematically not acting on known best practices; different mechanisms (organizational inertia vs. incentive misalignment), similar structure

- **NEW: [[Institutional Isomorphism]] ↔ [[Knowledge Transfer]] (KM):** normative isomorphism is a mechanism of inter-organizational tacit knowledge transfer via personnel flows and professional communities; mimetic isomorphism is vicarious organizational learning
- **NEW: [[Motivated Reasoning]] (open science) ↔ [[Conflict of Interest in Science]] ↔ [[Institutional Isomorphism]]:** explains HOW incentive misalignment causes systemic bias — motivated reasoning is the psychological mechanism; happens unconsciously under normal conditions when situation is complex + information ambiguous + multiple defensible actions exist
