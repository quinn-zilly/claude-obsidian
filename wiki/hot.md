## Key Insights

**KM:** Grant 1996 — 4 coordination mechanisms (rules, sequencing, routines, group problem-solving); *common knowledge* → [[Transactive Memory Systems]]. Barney & Felin 2013 — additive vs. emergent aggregation; microfoundations ≠ reductionism.

**Open Science:** OSC 2015: 36% replication rate; effects half magnitude. Nosek 2018: preregistration separates confirmatory/exploratory; garden of forking paths; [[Registered Reports]] = strongest form. LeBel 2018: 4 credibility dims (transparency, analytic reproducibility, analytic robustness, effect replicability). Munafò 2017: 5 reform categories; badges → 10× data sharing. Utopia II: [[Conflict of Interest in Science]] = ordinary psychology under misaligned incentives; solutions: [[Preregistration]], [[Open Workflow]], [[Paradigm-Driven Research]], PLoS ONE model. **Giner-Sorolla 2012:** [[Publication Bottleneck]] = root cause; too many researchers / too few slots → Anna Karenina conjunction rules → [[Aesthetic Standards in Science]] (3 criteria: statistical perfection, narrative conformity, novelty) → [[P-hacking]], [[HARKing]], replication suppression. 1970s solutions failed because they never touched bottleneck. Fix: open bottleneck (carrots: Nosek & Bar-Anan online structure; sticks: mandatory dissemination as IRB condition).

**Semantic Web:** DBpedia — 400M+ facts from 111 Wikipedia editions; LOD hub; IBM Watson; connects to KM codification strategy.

## Cross-Domain Connections

- Publication Bottleneck ↔ Crowdsourcing Science (Uhlmann): both diagnose vertical integration as structurally unsustainable; crowdsourcing is one bottleneck-opening reform
