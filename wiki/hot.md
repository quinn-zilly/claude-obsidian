- **(NEW) Simmons et al. 2011 (False-Positive Psychology, Psychological Science 22(11)):** 5p created — [[simmons-et-al-2011-false-positive-psychology]], [[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]], [[Joseph P. Simmons]], [[Leif D. Nelson]], [[Uri Simonsohn]]. Updated: [[P-hacking]], [[Questionable Research Practices]], [[Preregistration]], [[Open Science MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: founding methods paper of the [[Replication Crisis]] reform wing; coins **[[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]]** = the many defensible, ex-post analytic decisions (which DVs, which covariates, whether to exclude obs, when to stop collecting, which conditions to compare) that researchers — via [[Motivated Reasoning]], not fraud — pick to land on p≤.05. SIMULATIONS (15k/scenario, 2-cond, 20/cell baseline) of 4 common RDFs, FP rate at p<.05: two correlated DVs 9.5%; optional stopping (+10 obs, re-test) 7.7%; flexible gender covariate 11.7%; drop 1 of 3 conditions 12.6%; **all four combined = 60.7%** (more likely than not to false-positive). Estimates conservative (omit many other RDFs). Optional stopping alone tested every obs from n=10 ≈ 22%. DEMO ("chronological rejuvenation," everything real): Study 1 "Hot Potato"(Wiggles) makes undergrads FEEL older, F(1,27)=5.06 p=.033; Study 2 "When I'm Sixty-Four"(Beatles) makes them chronologically YOUNGER by birthdate, F(1,17)=4.92 p=.040 — necessarily false, "worked" only via undisclosed father's-age covariate (without it p=.33), optional stopping (~every 10 ppts, no ex-ante rule), n<20/cell, + long redacted measure list (Table 3). SOLUTION = disclosure not new stats: **6 author requirements** (1: decide+report termination rule before collecting; 2: ≥20 obs/cell or justify; 3: list ALL variables; 4: report ALL conditions incl failed; 5: report results with excluded obs included; 6: report results without each covariate) + **4 reviewer guidelines** (enforce; tolerate messy/imperfect results, distrust perfect underpowered ones; require robustness across arbitrary choices; demand exact replication if justifications weak). Turns sins-of-omission into sins-of-commission → realigns incentives. REJECTED non-solutions: Bonferroni alpha-correction (which/how many RDFs? introduces new ones); Bayesian (INCREASES RDFs via new analyses + prior choices); conceptual replications (don't bind analytic choices — misleading; it's what authors did); posting data/materials (too costly to audit real-time; redaction extends to data). CROSS-LINKS: [[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]] = raw material, [[P-hacking]] = exploiting it at analysis stage, [[HARKing]] = at write-up stage, [[Questionable Research Practices]] = the umbrella; Requirement 1 = [[Preregistration]] in embryo. Authors = Data Colada founders / p-curve developers.

Sugimoto]], [[Vincent Larivière]], [[Dimensions]], [[Web of Science]], [[Unpaywall]], [[Bibliometric Database Coverage]], [[OA Type Classification]], [[Geographic Bias in Indexing]]. Updated: [[Open Access]], [[Open Science MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: OA prevalence is DATABASE-DEPENDENT — same world, different number per data source. Compared WoS vs [[Dimensions]] for all journal pubs 2015–2019 w/ first-author affiliation; OA status from [[Unpaywall]] ([[OA Type Classification|5-cat]]: gold/green-only/hybrid/bronze/closed, one cat per paper, journal-OA prioritized). Corpus: WoS 8.05M (articles+reviews), Dimensions 10.74M (labels ALL journal docs "articles" — incl meeting abstracts, can't filter). HEADLINE: overall OA WoS 43.4% vs Dimensions 46.6%. REGIONAL gap (Dimensions−WoS): developed (NA, Europe/C.Asia) ≈ equal; South Asia +57.9%, LAC +36.6%, MENA +33.5%, Sub-Saharan Africa +12.4%. BY TYPE: gold = most abundant OA in every region EXCEPT N.America (green-only in WoS / bronze in Dimensions); gold much higher in Dimensions for S.Asia/LAC/MENA; **green-only consistently higher in WoS** (West self-archiving culture — mandates + repos like PubMed); **bronze higher in Dimensions everywhere**; hybrid <10% all regions, higher in WoS for NA(+24.4%)/Europe. COUNTRY level (Fig4): most countries higher %OA in Dimensions, esp Asia + global South. WHY ([[Geographic Bias in Indexing]]): [[Web of Science]] = citation-selective, Western/English bias (Mongeon & Paul-Hus 2016); its journals mostly ALSO in Dimensions → shared core agrees; the EXTRA journals Dimensions adds (smaller national journals via DOI/Crossref inclusion; Southern OA platforms AJOL/AmeliCA/SciELO) are disproportionately OA. Bronze surplus = non-DOAJ free-to-read, messy metadata → can't get gold; some maybe predatory or excluded doc types. CAVEATS: Dimensions higher missing-affiliation rate (GRID+ORCID standardization, historically thin for Africa/S.America/C.Asia → country studies constrained); no doc-type disaggregation → can't fully rule out doc-type confound driving OA gap. POLICY: prefer inclusive databases for measuring OA beyond NA/Europe; Western-biased DBs understate Southern OA uptake → distort mandate/funding/APC-waiver debates. CROSS-LINKS: adds measurement layer to [[Open Access]] (refines gold/green binary → [[OA Type Classification]]); pairs w/ [[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0]] (OA stuck ~50%, cost shifted to APCs); [[Vincent Larivière]]+[[Cassidy R. Sugimoto]] = same authors as Piwowar 2018 / Measuring Research. Filed under [[Open Science MOC]].
- **(NEW) Dwivedi et al. 2019 (Re-examining UTAUT, Information Systems Frontiers):** 8p created — [[dwivedi-2019-utaut-revision|Dwivedi et al. (2019)]], [[UTAUT]], [[Revised UTAUT Model]], [[Attitude as Mediator]], [[MASEM]], [[Technology Acceptance Model]], [[Facilitating Conditions]], [[Yogesh Dwivedi]], [[Viswanath Venkatesh]]. Updated: [[Culture & IT MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: critical review + [[MASEM]] of [[UTAUT]] (Venkatesh et al. 2003 = 4 constructs PE/EE/SI/FC + 4 moderators gender/age/experience/voluntariness; synthesized 8 adoption models; ~70% var in BI). THREE CRITIQUES: (1) moderators rarely apply / mostly dropped by studies / meaningless under mandated adoption; (2) UTAUT omits FC→BI (assumes EE absorbs it) but studies find FC predicts BI even with EE; (3) THE INDIVIDUAL IS MISSING — no individual-characteristics construct, esp [[Attitude as Mediator|attitude]]. [[Revised UTAUT Model]]: drop moderators → "basic UTAUT"; add FC→BI; add attitude as mediator. METHOD: meta-analysis 1600 obs / 21 relationships / 7 constructs / 162 studies (96 journal, 49 conf, 16 diss, 1 chapter); correct for measurement+sampling error → pooled matrix; path analysis AMOS 21, min N=4319 (validated w/ harmonic+avg); drop paths CR<1.96, add emergent paths MI≥10 + theory. EMERGENT MODEL added AT→UB (MI 138.47), FC→AT (MI 122.63), SI→AT (MI 59.17). FINDINGS: attitude partially mediates all 4 exogenous→BI, direct AT→BI (0.37) AND direct AT→UB (intention→usage often weak). Exogenous load STRONGER on attitude than intention (PE→AT 0.47 vs PE→BI 0.11). R²: BI 38→45%, UB 21→27%, AT 55%; RMSEA 0.111→0.041 (excellent). IMPLICATIONS: reinstate attitude in UTAUT; theorize missing direct effects (esp FC→BI); moderators context-dependent not universal; managers shape attitude directly (clean design, communicate capabilities, training, help desks, champions/forums). LIMITS: only Pearson-convertible studies; imputed missing reliabilities/SDs; 4 moderators not modelled (primary studies didn't report) so original UTAUT not tested in full. KEY: parsimony-vs-explanation — attitude (dropped for elegance) is where tech attributes + contextual factors converge before intention/behaviour. CROSS-LINKS: filed under [[Culture & IT MOC]] (IT adoption cluster); [[Technology Acceptance Model]] = TAM ancestor that kept attitude.
- **(NEW) Roberts et al. 2012 (Absorptive Capacity and IS Research, MIS Quarterly):** 9p created — [[roberts-2012-absorptive-capacity-is-review]], [[Absorptive Capacity]], [[Dynamic Capabilities]], [[Exploration vs Exploitation]], [[Nicholas Roberts]], [[Pamela Galluch]], [[Michael Dinger]], [[Varun Grover]], [[Wesley Cohen]], [[Daniel Levinthal]]. Updated: [[Organizational Learning]], [[Knowledge Transfer]], [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm]], [[Shaker Zahra]], [[Knowledge Management MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: review of [[Absorptive Capacity]] (Cohen & Levinthal 1990 = identify/assimilate/transform/apply external knowledge) across 98 IS articles (1990-2008, 7 journals). CENTRAL CONFUSION = ASSET vs CAPABILITY: asset view = stock of prior related knowledge (proxied R&D intensity/patents, static); capability view = routines to absorb (substantive) or to reconfigure substantive capabilities ([[Dynamic Capabilities|dynamic]]). Predominant theory = CAPABILITY not asset; prior knowledge "necessary but not sufficient" (Lane et al. 2006). FOUR ASSUMPTIONS: depends on prior related knowledge → domain-specific; depends on members but > their sum → firm-specific; path-dependent → biased toward [[Exploration vs Exploitation|exploitation]] (Kodak missed digital). CONSTRUCT-SPACE finding: inconsistent (25 asset/35 capability/38 none), multilevel (65% org, 26% individual), 9 direct measures ALL survey/perceptual. NETWORK-SPACE = 4 THEMES (role of IT in parens): business-IT knowledge (nominal), [[Knowledge Transfer]] (nominal; recipient AC throttles transfer, recursive), IT assimilation (proxy; related knowledge + knowledge diversity lower knowledge barriers), IT business value (tool; AC × IT capabilities synergy → advantage). FOUR LIMITATIONS+GUIDELINES: (1) conceptualize as capability not asset; (2) treat as COLLECTIVE construct (≠ individual; use individual-learning theory for individual qs); (3) measure as multidimensional capability, domain-specific (not define-as-capability-measure-as-asset); (4) name the IT ARTIFACT (Orlikowski & Iacono 2001 "nominal view"). FRAMEWORK (Fig 3, 4 propositions): bounded rationality (Simon 1976) caps AC; IT expands it; but IT alone insufficient (Mata et al. 1995) → COMPLEMENTARITIES/SYNERGY (Milgrom & Roberts 1995). Pair Wade & Hulland (2004) IT capabilities with org capabilities: P1 outside-in IT × coordination → IDENTIFY; P2 spanning IT × coordination + P3 spanning IT × socialization → ASSIMILATE/TRANSFORM; P4 inside-out IT × socialization → APPLY. Coordination caps = job rotation, cross-functional interfaces; socialization caps = cohesion, shared language, shared goals. OPEN Qs: AC's role in IT innovation beyond assimilation; do IT-AC synergies become RIGIDITIES; AC as digital capability; digitized vs non-digitized knowledge absorption. CROSS-LINKS: nests in [[Organizational Learning]] (construct vs broad concept); sharpens [[Knowledge Transfer]] (Szulanski 1996; Ko et al. 2005); backdrop = [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm]]; [[Shaker Zahra]] = Zahra & George 2002 reconceptualization (potential vs realized AC).
- **(NEW) Leidner & Kayworth 2006 (Culture in IS Research, MIS Quarterly):** 7p created — [[leidner-kayworth-2006-culture-is-review]], [[Dorothy Leidner]], [[Timothy Kayworth]], [[IT-Culture Conflict]], [[Cultural Fit]], [[IT Culture]], [[Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions]], [[Culture & IT MOC]]. Updated: [[Home MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: review of 82 IT-culture studies (51 national / 31 org) merging two previously separate streams under a VALUES-BASED lens (Schein: assumptions→values→artifacts). SIX THEMES: (1) culture & ISD; (2) culture, IT adoption & diffusion — fit is dominant construct; (3) culture, IT use & outcomes — same IT used differently across cultures; (4) culture, IT mgmt & strategy; (5) IT's impact on culture (under-studied; mostly treated as stable); (6) IT culture = values a group attributes to IT (IT not value-neutral). CENTRAL THEORY = IT-CULTURE CONFLICT, three types: SYSTEM (group values ↔ specific-IT embedded values), CONTRIBUTION (group values ↔ general IT values; theorized via Bourdieu capital/distinction), VISION (general IT values ↔ specific-IT embedded values). 5 proposition-sets (P1a-d system, P2a-d contribution, P3a-d vision, P4 managers reduce all conflict via shared IT values, P5 conflict resolution → cultural change over time, starting subunit→national). KEY REFRAME: culture predicts TIMING/breadth/objective of adoption, not whether. KAARST-BROWN 5 IT cultural patterns (fearful/controlled/revered/demystified/integrated) = assumptions-based alt. >60% national studies used [[Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions]]; uncertainty avoidance most-used, generally dampens adoption (contradicted by Galliers 1998). CROSS-LINKS: shares author [[Dorothy Leidner]] with [[alavi-leidner-2001-knowledge-management]]; Theme 3 connects to [[Knowledge Management]] (values → KM success).
