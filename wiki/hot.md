Last ingest: 2026-06-30 — **[[ericsson-lehmann-1996-expert-exceptional-performance|Ericsson & Lehmann (1996)]]**, *Expert and Exceptional Performance* (*Annual Review of Psychology*). Organizing thesis = **[[Maximal Adaptation to Task Constraints|expert performance is acquired maximal adaptation to task constraints]]**, not innate talent. Highest levels appear only after the **[[10-Year Rule|~10-year rule]]** of daily **[[Deliberate Practice]]** (coach-designed, feedback-driven, effortful, ~4h/day sustainable cap) — raw experience predicts performance only weakly. **[[Talent vs Deliberate Practice|Talent debunked]]**: physiology (heart, capillaries, muscle fiber, ballet turnout, perfect pitch) is largely training-induced/reversible; IQ→performance correlation decays to non-sig after ~5 yrs; innate domain-specific capacity's role "small, possibly negligible" — **height** the lone exception. Mechanisms circumvent generic limits: **anticipation from advance cues** beats reaction-time floors (cricket/tennis/typing eye-hand span); **[[Skilled Memory Theory]] → [[Long-Term Working Memory]]** (Chase & Ericsson digit spans 82/100+; chess blindfold) expands functional WM in-domain and **subsumes** the classic Chase & Simon **[[Chunking]]** account (whose pure-STM claim was overturned). Contrary to the automatization myth, expertise stays mediated by active planning/reasoning. **[[Expert Performance (Ericsson Definition)|Restrictive definition]]** (reproducible superior performance on representative tasks) dissociates **expertise from experience** → converges with the JDM cluster: experience without feedback-rich practice ≠ valid judgment (see convergence callout on [[Mechanical vs Clinical Prediction]]; Dawes/Faust/Meehl). New: 8 concepts + 5 entities ([[K. Anders Ericsson]], [[Andreas C. Lehmann]], [[Herbert A. Simon]], [[William G. Chase]], [[Adriaan de Groot]]) + 1 source (c-000773–786). Updated [[Learning & Development MOC]] (new expertise/deliberate-practice section), [[Mechanical vs Clinical Prediction]], [[index]]. Candidate next: Ericsson, Krampe & Tesch-Römer (1993) primary deliberate-practice paper; Macnamara et al. (2014) critique.

Prior: 2026-06-30 — **batch of 3 forming a lens-model / personnel-selection cluster** around the Brunswik [[Lens Model]]. **[[karelaia-hogarth-2008-lens-model-meta-analysis|Karelaia & Hogarth (2008)]]** (*Psych Bulletin*) = 5-decade meta-analysis of 249 lens-model environments (~303k judgments): mean achievement $r_a=.56$, matching $G=.80$, consistency $R_s=.80$, residual $C=.04$; three conclusions — people judge fairly well, learn best from **[[Cue Feedback Types|task-information feedback]]** (not outcome feedback), and **[[Bootstrapping (Improper Linear Models)|a linear model of the judge beats the judge]]** ($GR_e−r_a≈.10$) by stripping inconsistency; expertise does *not* raise accuracy. **[[viswesvaran-schmidt-ones-2005-general-factor-job-performance|Viswesvaran, Schmidt & Ones (2005)]]** (*JAP*) = after netting out **[[Halo Error|halo]]** + 3 error sources, a real **[[General Factor in Job Performance|general factor (~60% variance)]]** survives; halo inflates interdimension correlations +33% (supervisor) / +63% (peer) — the asymmetry (inflates within-rater, deflates between-rater) is what lets them isolate it; peer & supervisory ratings construct-equivalent. **[[kuncel-et-al-2013-mechanical-vs-clinical|Kuncel et al. (2013)]]** (*JAP*) = **[[Mechanical vs Clinical Prediction|mechanical (formula) data combination beats clinical (holistic)]]** by >50% for job performance (.44 vs .28), even when experts had more info; framed via the lens model ($R_s<1$, $G<1$, $C≈0$). Unifying thread = **[[Judgmental Achievement and Consistency|consistency, not knowledge]]**: judges roughly know the cue weights but apply them noisily; bootstrapping/mechanical combination recovers the loss. [[Deniz S. Ones]] bridges both IO papers; Kuncel cites Karelaia & Hogarth for $C≈0$; [[Frank L. Schmidt]]'s psychometric meta-analysis is the shared engine. New: 7 concepts + 6 entities ([[Robin Hogarth]], [[Natalia Karelaia]], [[Chockalingam Viswesvaran]], [[Frank L. Schmidt]], [[Deniz S. Ones]], [[Nathan R. Kuncel]]) + 3 sources (c-000756–771). Updated [[Lens Model]], [[Policy Capturing]], [[index]]. Candidate next: a **JDM / Selection MOC**.

Prior: 2026-06-29 — **[[lefkowitz-2008-values-organizational-psychology|Lefkowitz (2008)]]** (*JOB*) + **[[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct|APA Ethics Code (2017)]]**, a paired ethics/values ingest. Lefkowitz's thesis: "the fault lies not in our ethics, but in our **values**." I/O psychologists behave ethically but the field's *institutional values* — encoded in the [[Scientist-Practitioner Model|S-P model]] — have drifted to corporate-economic goals, away from psychology's humanist tradition. Five indictments: (1) S-P replaces humanism with business goals; (2) overly [[Scientism in Social Science|"scientistic"]] / false "value-free" claim; (3) ethical-but-**amoral** (avoids normative questions, e.g. [[Distributive Justice]]); (4) [[Pro-Management Bias]] (EAW, SHRM); (5) [[Means-Ends Inversion|means over ends]] (Munsterberg "hired hand" → Baritz *Servants of Power*). Remedy = expand into the **[[Scientist-Practitioner-Humanist (S-P-H) Model]]** ("Good Science–Good Practice–**Good Works**"), measured against the **[[APA Ethics Code]]** preamble's mandate to serve "individuals, organizations, **and** society." The Code itself is three-layered: aspirational [[Five General Principles (APA)]] (A Beneficence, B Fidelity, C Integrity, D Justice, E Respect) + enforceable Standards (see [[Aspirational vs Enforceable Standards]]). New: 8 concepts + 2 entities ([[Joel Lefkowitz]], [[American Psychological Association]]) + 2 sources (c-000744–755). Updated [[Institutional & Strategy MOC]] (new professional-ethics cluster), [[index]].

Prior: 2026-06-29 — **[[ashforth-mael-1989-social-identity-theory-organization|Ashforth & Mael (1989)]]**, *Social Identity Theory and the Organization* (AMR). Imported **[[Social Identity Theory|SIT]]** (Tajfel & Turner) into OB and redefined **[[Social Identification|organizational identification]]** as a *purely perceptual* "**perception of oneness** with a group" (the answer to "Who am I?") — surgically separated from [[Organizational Commitment|commitment]], internalization ("I am" vs "I believe"), loyalty (affect), and effort (behavior). Mael's (1988) 6-item identification scale ("this org's successes are my successes") beats the OCQ in a two-factor CFA (AGFI .825 vs .780) → constructs are differentiable (see new **contradiction callout** on [[Organizational Commitment]]). Antecedents: group **distinctiveness**, **prestige**, **out-group salience**, group-formation factors. Consequences: identity-congruent activity, institutional support, **[[Self-Categorization and Self-Stereotyping|self-stereotyping]]** (→ depersonalization, conformity), + reinforces own antecedents. Biggest contribution = the **[[Psychological Group]]**: identification with a collectivity can arise *without* interpersonal cohesion/similarity/interaction ([[Minimal Group Paradigm|mere categorization suffices]]), reversing the "interpersonal regard aggregates up" logic. Applied to **socialization** (socialization→identification→internalization indirect path; investiture/divestiture; [[Routinization of Charisma]], AA vs NCA), **role conflict** (identity = amalgam of loosely-coupled identities; resolved by ordering/separating/buffering not integrating; Challenger "engineering hat/management hat"), and **intergroup relations** ([[In-Group Bias and Intergroup Comparison]]; conflict without objective causes; legitimacy mitigates). New: 7 concepts + 2 entities ([[Blake E. Ashforth]], [[Fred Mael]]) + 1 source (c-000734–743). Updated [[Organizational Commitment]], [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]], [[index]].

Prior ingest: 2026-06-15 — **[[parker-vandenbroeck-holman-2017-work-design-influences|Parker, Van den Broeck & Holman (2017)]]**, *Work Design Influences* (AMR Annals). The **antecedents-side companion** to the [[parker-morgeson-johns-2017-100-years-work-design|100-years review]]: flips work design from *independent* to **dependent** variable and asks what *causes* work design. The [[Work Design Influences Framework]] = four levels (higher-level external [global/national/occupational] → organizational → work-group → individual) × three mechanisms each (direct; indirect via managers' AMO; indirect via employees' AMO). Proximal engine = the [[Ability-Motivation-Opportunity Model|AMO model]] (motivation + KSAs + opportunity-incl-power) for both managers (formal decisions) and employees (informal [[Job Crafting|crafting]]/i-deals). Most higher-level effects are **mediated, not deterministic**; top-down dominates but bottom-up is real-but-slow. Evidence strongest for **organizational factors** (strategy/[[Strategic HRM|SHRM]], operational uncertainty, technology [SBTC/RBTC], org design) and **national institutions/[[Institutional Regimes & Employment Regime Theory|regimes]]** (the "Nordic advantage"); weakest for global/national-culture/occupational. Bridges work-design cluster to [[Institutional & Strategy MOC|institutional theory & strategy]] via [[Institutional Isomorphism|isomorphism]]. Prior ingest: 2026-06-12 — [[morgeson-humphrey-2006-work-design-questionnaire|Morgeson & Humphrey (2006)]]: backfilled the **primary WDQ development paper** (the [[Work Design Questionnaire|WDQ]] concept + the 2007 meta-analysis were already in the vault, but this foundational 2006 source had no note). 107 work-characteristic terms → **18 categories**, but the best CFA is a **21-factor** model (autonomy → 3 facets, interdependence → 2 directions; CFI .91). N=540 incumbents / 243 jobs; avg α ≈ .87 (only ergonomics < .70); scales converge with archival DOT/O*NET. Key theory payoff: **task** characteristics raise satisfaction *without* raising training/compensation cost; only **knowledge** characteristics drive those costs → a route around the classic motivational trade-off; **social support** incrementally predicts satisfaction and is *negatively* tied to training cost. Meta + work-design cluster only; no new entities.
