- **(NEW) Fanelli 2012 (Negative Results Are Disappearing from Most Disciplines and Countries, Scientometrics 90(3):891–904):** 2 sources + 4 concepts + 7 entities. Created — [[fanelli-2012-negative-results-disappearing]], [[soderberg-et-al-2021-registered-reports-quality]], [[Positive-Outcome Bias]], [[Publish or Perish]], [[In-Principle Acceptance]], [[Statcheck]], [[Daniele Fanelli]], [[Courtney K. Soderberg]], [[Timothy M. Errington]], [[Kevin M. Esterling]], [[Sarah R. Schiavone]], [[Julia Bottesini]], [[Felix Singleton Thorn]]. Updated — [[Publication Bias]], [[File-Drawer Problem]], [[Registered Reports]], [[Brian Nosek]], [[Simine Vazire]], [[Center for Open Science]], [[Open Science MOC]], [[index]]. CORE (FANELLI): single-coder content analysis of 4,656 papers that "test* the hypothes*", random-sampled from ESI/ISI (10,837 journals, 20 disciplines; *Science/Nature/PNAS* excluded), coded positive (full/partial support) vs negative (null/contrary), first hypothesis only. Coding BLIND to year+country (retrieved after) → trends not a coding artefact. Positive share: 70.2% (1990–91) → peak 88.6% (2005) → 85.9% (2007); odds +~6%/yr, survives all confounds (≈5%/yr maximal model). GRADIENT: positive freq rises physical→biological→social, applied>pure (= Fanelli 2010 "hierarchy of the sciences"); steepest growth Econ&Business, Clinical Med, Psych/Psychiatry, Pharma&Tox; Space Science only decliner. COUNTRY: US fewer positives than Asia (esp Japan) but MORE than Europe (esp UK) — editorial-bias story can't explain US>UK (both rich/English) → stronger US bias against negatives (backed by Steen 2011 US retractions). 3 causal candidates, only #3 fits: (1) safer/confirmable hypotheses; (2) rising power — UNSUPPORTED (power low everywhere, not growing); (3) negatives submitted/accepted less OR converted to positives via QRP/selection/fabrication ← best fit. WHY MATTERS: missing negatives inflate meta-analytic effects, waste replication, can spawn phantom fields (Ioannidis); worst where theory/method loose + replication rare. KEY: strongest positive growth came in fields (Clinical Med, Pharma, Mol Bio) where pub-bias remedies (trial registration, reporting guidelines, negative-results journals) had LONGEST history → those early fixes had NOT worked; problem worsening. This is the empirical baseline the RR reform later targets. CORE (SODERBERG): naturalistic quasi-experiment (no randomization; authors self-select RRs). 29 published RRs (psych+neuro, NOVEL research; replication RRs excluded — can't match in standard lit) from 102 RR-offering journals 2014–18, vs 57 comparison papers via two preregistered matching schemes (same journal+date / same lead-author+date). 353 reviewers (4.5% of 7,835 invites) each rated one RR + one control on 19 criteria (−4…+4), fixed order methods→results→abstract to isolate method eval from findings. Papers lightly redacted (removed "Registered Report"); full blinding infeasible w/o cutting rigour-relevant content; 54% still guessed RR correctly. Bayesian multilevel, partial pooling. RESULTS: RRs numerically beat comparisons on ALL 19 criteria (standard model never numerically won any); mean +0.46 (range 0.13–0.96). Biggest: methods rigour +0.99 [0.62,1.35], analysis rigour +0.96 [0.60,1.34], overall quality +0.66 [0.30,1.02]. INDISTINGUISHABLE (CrI spans 0): novelty +0.13 [−0.24,0.49], creativity +0.22 [−0.14,0.58], importance of findings — i.e. NO support for skeptics' fear (Baumeister/Cropley) that RR planning kills discovery. Held within- AND between-subjects, across reviewer familiarity, even among reviewers who believed RRs neutral/negative, even among those who FAILED to guess RR status (weaker, directionally intact). OBJECTIVE CODING: open data 86% (RR) vs 14–18%; open materials 59% vs 7–11%; prereg 59% vs 0–7%; larger median samples + more sample-size justifications; [[Statcheck]] errors rare+similar across groups; only 59% RRs had FINDABLE independent prereg (documentation gap). Speculated mechanism: authors design harder for [[In-Principle Acceptance]]; reviewers judge methods undistracted by results; review improves study pre-run. LIMITS: naturalistic (self-selection confound, matching mitigates); early-adoption effects; quality = peer PERCEPTION not replicability; 4.5% response. LINKS: Fanelli=disease, Soderberg=cure's report card; Soderberg cites Fanelli as ref 6; both reinforce [[Publication Bias]]→[[Registered Reports]] arc; from [[Center for Open Science]] ([[Brian Nosek]], [[Timothy M. Errington]]); [[Simine Vazire]] coding. NO contradictions w/ existing pages. Filed under [[Open Science MOC]].

- **(NEW) Simmons et al. 2011 (False-Positive Psychology, Psychological Science 22(11)):** 5p created — [[simmons-et-al-2011-false-positive-psychology]], [[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]], [[Joseph P. Simmons]], [[Leif D. Nelson]], [[Uri Simonsohn]]. Updated: [[P-hacking]], [[Questionable Research Practices]], [[Preregistration]], [[Open Science MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: founding methods paper of the [[Replication Crisis]] reform wing; coins **[[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]]** = the many defensible, ex-post analytic decisions (which DVs, which covariates, whether to exclude obs, when to stop collecting, which conditions to compare) that researchers — via [[Motivated Reasoning]], not fraud — pick to land on p≤.05. SIMULATIONS (15k/scenario, 2-cond, 20/cell baseline) of 4 common RDFs, FP rate at p<.05: two correlated DVs 9.5%; optional stopping (+10 obs, re-test) 7.7%; flexible gender covariate 11.7%; drop 1 of 3 conditions 12.6%; **all four combined = 60.7%** (more likely than not to false-positive). Estimates conservative (omit many other RDFs). Optional stopping alone tested every obs from n=10 ≈ 22%. DEMO ("chronological rejuvenation," everything real): Study 1 "Hot Potato"(Wiggles) makes undergrads FEEL older, F(1,27)=5.06 p=.033; Study 2 "When I'm Sixty-Four"(Beatles) makes them chronologically YOUNGER by birthdate, F(1,17)=4.92 p=.040 — necessarily false, "worked" only via undisclosed father's-age covariate (without it p=.33), optional stopping (~every 10 ppts, no ex-ante rule), n<20/cell, + long redacted measure list (Table 3). SOLUTION = disclosure not new stats: **6 author requirements** (1: decide+report termination rule before collecting; 2: ≥20 obs/cell or justify; 3: list ALL variables; 4: report ALL conditions incl failed; 5: report results with excluded obs included; 6: report results without each covariate) + **4 reviewer guidelines** (enforce; tolerate messy/imperfect results, distrust perfect underpowered ones; require robustness across arbitrary choices; demand exact replication if justifications weak). Turns sins-of-omission into sins-of-commission → realigns incentives. REJECTED non-solutions: Bonferroni alpha-correction (which/how many RDFs? introduces new ones); Bayesian (INCREASES RDFs via new analyses + prior choices); conceptual replications (don't bind analytic choices — misleading; it's what authors did); posting data/materials (too costly to audit real-time; redaction extends to data). CROSS-LINKS: [[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]] = raw material, [[P-hacking]] = exploiting it at analysis stage, [[HARKing]] = at write-up stage, [[Questionable Research Practices]] = the umbrella; Requirement 1 = [[Preregistration]] in embryo. Authors = Data Colada founders / p-curve developers.

- **(NEW) Dwivedi et al. 2019 (Re-examining UTAUT, Information Systems Frontiers):** 8p created — [[dwivedi-2019-utaut-revision|Dwivedi et al. (2019)]], [[UTAUT]], [[Revised UTAUT Model]], [[Attitude as Mediator]], [[MASEM]], [[Technology Acceptance Model]], [[Facilitating Conditions]], [[Yogesh Dwivedi]], [[Viswanath Venkatesh]]. Updated: [[Culture & IT MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: critical review + [[MASEM]] of [[UTAUT]] (Venkatesh et al. 2003 = 4 constructs PE/EE/SI/FC + 4 moderators gender/age/experience/voluntariness; synthesized 8 adoption models; ~70% var in BI). THREE CRITIQUES: (1) moderators rarely apply / mostly dropped by studies / meaningless under mandated adoption; (2) UTAUT omits FC→BI (assumes EE absorbs it) but studies find FC predicts BI even with EE; (3) THE INDIVIDUAL IS MISSING — no individual-characteristics construct, esp [[Attitude as Mediator|attitude]]. [[Revised UTAUT Model]]: drop moderators → "basic UTAUT"; add FC→BI; add attitude as mediator. METHOD: meta-analysis 1600 obs / 21 relationships / 7 constructs / 162 studies (96 journal, 49 conf, 16 diss, 1 chapter); correct for measurement+sampling error → pooled matrix; path analysis AMOS 21, min N=4319 (validated w/ harmonic+avg); drop paths CR<1.96, add emergent paths MI≥10 + theory. EMERGENT MODEL added AT→UB (MI 138.47), FC→AT (MI 122.63), SI→AT (MI 59.17). FINDINGS: attitude partially mediates all 4 exogenous→BI, direct AT→BI (0.37) AND direct AT→UB (intention→usage often weak). Exogenous load STRONGER on attitude than intention (PE→AT 0.47 vs PE→BI 0.11). R²: BI 38→45%, UB 21→27%, AT 55%; RMSEA 0.111→0.041 (excellent). IMPLICATIONS: reinstate attitude in UTAUT; theorize missing direct effects (esp FC→BI); moderators context-dependent not universal; managers shape attitude directly (clean design, communicate capabilities, training, help desks, champions/forums). LIMITS: only Pearson-convertible studies; imputed missing reliabilities/SDs; 4 moderators not modelled (primary studies didn't report) so original UTAUT not tested in full. KEY: parsimony-vs-explanation — attitude (dropped for elegance) is where tech attributes + contextual factors converge before intention/behaviour. - **(NEW) Roberts et al. 2012 (Absorptive Capacity and IS Research, MIS Quarterly):** 9p created — [[roberts-2012-absorptive-capacity-is-review]], [[Absorptive Capacity]], [[Dynamic Capabilities]], [[Exploration vs Exploitation]], [[Nicholas Roberts]], [[Pamela Galluch]], [[Michael Dinger]], [[Varun Grover]], [[Wesley Cohen]], [[Daniel Levinthal]]. Updated: [[Organizational Learning]], [[Knowledge Transfer]], [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm]], [[Shaker Zahra]], [[Knowledge Management MOC]], [[index]]. CORE: review of [[Absorptive Capacity]] (Cohen & Levinthal 1990 = identify/assimilate/transform/apply external knowledge) across 98 IS articles (1990-2008, 7 journals). CENTRAL CONFUSION = ASSET vs CAPABILITY: asset view = stock of prior related knowledge (proxied R&D intensity/patents, static); capability view = routines to absorb (substantive) or to reconfigure substantive capabilities ([[Dynamic Capabilities|dynamic]]). Predominant theory = CAPABILITY not asset; prior knowledge "necessary but not sufficient" (Lane et al. 2006). FOUR ASSUMPTIONS: depends on prior related knowledge → domain-specific; depends on members but > their sum → firm-specific; path-dependent → biased toward [[Exploration vs Exploitation|exploitation]] (Kodak missed digital). CONSTRUCT-SPACE finding: inconsistent (25 asset/35 capability/38 none), multilevel (65% org, 26% individual), 9 direct measures ALL survey/perceptual. NETWORK-SPACE = 4 THEMES (role of IT in parens): business-IT knowledge (nominal), [[Knowledge Transfer]] (nominal; recipient AC throttles transfer, recursive), IT assimilation (proxy; related knowledge + knowledge diversity lower knowledge barriers), IT business value (tool; AC × IT capabilities synergy → advantage). FOUR LIMITATIONS+GUIDELINES: (1) conceptualize as capability not asset; (2) treat as COLLECTIVE construct (≠ individual; use individual-learning theory for individual qs); (3) measure as multidimensional capability, domain-specific (not define-as-capability-measure-as-asset); (4) name the IT ARTIFACT (Orlikowski & Iacono 2001 "nominal view"). FRAMEWORK (Fig 3, 4 propositions): bounded rationality (Simon 1976) caps AC; IT expands it; but IT alone insufficient (Mata et al. 1995) → COMPLEMENTARITIES/SYNERGY (Milgrom & Roberts 1995). Pair Wade & Hulland (2004) IT capabilities with org capabilities: P1 outside-in IT × coordination → IDENTIFY; P2 spanning IT × coordination + P3 spanning IT × socialization → ASSIMILATE/TRANSFORM; P4 inside-out IT × socialization → APPLY. Coordination caps = job rotation, cross-functional interfaces; socialization caps = cohesion, shared language, shared goals. OPEN Qs: AC's role in IT innovation beyond assimilation; do IT-AC synergies become RIGIDITIES; AC as digital capability; digitized vs non-digitized knowledge absorption. CROSS-LINKS: nests in [[Organizational Learning]] (construct vs broad concept); sharpens [[Knowledge Transfer]] (Szulanski 1996; Ko et al. 2005); backdrop = [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm]]; [[Shaker Zahra]] = Zahra & George 2002 reconceptualization (potential vs realized AC).
