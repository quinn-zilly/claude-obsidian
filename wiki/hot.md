Last ingest: 2026-06-12 — [[parker-morgeson-johns-2017-100-years-work-design|Parker, Morgeson & Johns (2017)]], the **JAP centennial review of work design**. Scientific mapping of 5,708 articles → **five historically-anchored clusters**: sociotechnical/AWG, JCM, job demands-control, job demands-resources, role theory. Central argument: clusters reflect **history, not topic similarity**, so cross-cluster **integration** is the field's biggest opportunity (interdisciplinary, elaborated/WDQ, relational, learning-development perspectives + a multilevel theory). JAP scorecard: only 6.4% of work-design articles but ~19% of citations — most-cited outlet by 2x. Work design influences economics/epidemiology/HBR/policy, usually without the academic label. Future agenda is **proactive + dynamic + multilevel**: ask what role work design plays in achieving outcomes and enabling adaptation to automation. New pages: 1 source + 6 concepts ([[Job Demands-Resources Model]], [[Job Demands-Control Model]], [[Role Theory (Work Design)]], [[Autonomous Work Groups]], [[Relational Job Design]], [[Learning and Development Perspective on Work Design]]) + 7 entities ([[Sharon Parker]], [[Gary Johns]], [[Robert Karasek]], [[Evangelia Demerouti]], [[Adam Grant]], [[Frederick Herzberg]], [[Eric Trist]]). Prior ingest: 2026-06-12 — [[morgeson-humphrey-2006-work-design-questionnaire|Morgeson & Humphrey (2006)]]: backfilled the **primary WDQ development paper** (the [[Work Design Questionnaire|WDQ]] concept + the 2007 meta-analysis were already in the vault, but this foundational 2006 source had no note). 107 work-characteristic terms → **18 categories**, but the best CFA is a **21-factor** model (autonomy → 3 facets, interdependence → 2 directions; CFI .91). N=540 incumbents / 243 jobs; avg α ≈ .87 (only ergonomics < .70); scales converge with archival DOT/O*NET. Key theory payoff: **task** characteristics raise satisfaction *without* raising training/compensation cost; only **knowledge** characteristics drive those costs → a route around the classic motivational trade-off; **social support** incrementally predicts satisfaction and is *negatively* tied to training cost. Meta + work-design cluster only; no new entities.

Prior ingest: 2026-06-12 — batch: [[humphrey-nahrgang-morgeson-2007-work-design-meta-analysis|Humphrey, Nahrgang & Morgeson (2007)]] + [[oldham-fried-2016-job-design-past-present-future|Oldham & Fried (2016)]] into the existing work-design cluster. **Humphrey et al. (2007):** definitive meta-analysis (259 studies, N=219,625); 14 work characteristics explain ~43% of variance across 19 outcomes; social characteristics add up to **+40%** (org commitment) and work context up to **+16%** (stress) incremental variance beyond motivational ones → empirical case for the broad [[Work Design Questionnaire|WDQ]] taxonomy; first formal meta-analytic mediation test shows **experienced meaningfulness** is the dominant [[Job Characteristics Model|JCM]] mediator. **Oldham & Fried (2016):** JCT co-author's review — JCT verdict (strong on attitudes + meaningfulness, modest on behavior, mixed GNS/context moderators); field shift to employee-driven [[Job Crafting]] and dynamic person–job fit; eight future directions. New pages: 2 sources + [[Job Crafting]] + entities [[Stephen Humphrey]], [[Jennifer Nahrgang]], [[Greg R. Oldham]], [[Yitzhak Fried]], [[Amy Wrzesniewski]], [[J. Richard Hackman]]. MOC: added work/job-design sub-cluster to [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]].
