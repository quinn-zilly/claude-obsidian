Last ingest: 2026-07-06 — **[[noe-2023-needs-assessment|Noe (2023), *Employee Training and Development* 9e, Ch. 3: Needs Assessment]]** — the standard training textbook's canonical, prescriptive treatment. **Needs assessment** = determining whether training is necessary, for whom, and for what tasks; first step of instructional design. Three analyses: **[[Organizational Analysis (Training)]]** (strategy fit, manager/peer support, resources → buy-vs-build; usually first), **[[Person Analysis (Noe Model)]]** (gap analysis via person characteristics + **input/output/consequences/feedback**; drives [[Motivation to Learn]] + [[Readiness for Training]]), **[[Task Analysis (Training)]]** (four-step task→**KSAO** mapping; SMEs rate tasks on **importance/frequency/difficulty**; task-KSAO grid). Before any of it, **[[Root Cause Analysis (Training)]]** asks whether training is even the right solution (7 questions, 5 Whys, Fishbone, 4 Square) — pressure points don't automatically mean training. Methods (Table 3.2): observation, surveys, interviews, focus groups/crowdsourcing, documentation, technology, data reviews — use multiple. Prior ingest: 2026-07-06 — **two L&D-evaluation papers**. **[[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|Tannenbaum & Wolfson (2022)]]** (*Annu. Rev. Organ. Psychol. Organ. Behav.*) reviews **[[Informal Field-Based Learning (IFBL)]]** — "intentional, self-directed behavior aimed at learning new, work-oriented content outside a formal program" (70–90%+ of workplace learning is informal). Three IFBL behaviors: feedback/reflection, vicarious, experimentation. Core contribution = the **CAM-OS framework**: personal readiness (**C**apability, **A**wareness = "learning attunement", **M**otivation) + situational readiness (**O**pportunity, **S**upport). Opportunity has four dimensions (richness, slack [curvilinear], malleability, appropriateness). Real **dark side**: detrimental IFBL (safety, overreach, stress). Tools: Field Learning Needs Analysis + Red-Yellow-Green chart. [[Psychological Safety]] is the key support enabler. **[[alliger-janak-1989-kirkpatrick-levels-thirty-years-later|Alliger & Janak (1989)]]** (*Personnel Psychology*) = the founding critique of the **[[Kirkpatrick Four-Level Model]]**, testing three implicit assumptions (ascending info value, causal chain, positive manifold). From 12 studies / 26 correlations: **reactions–learning r ≈ .07**, reactions–behavior ≈ .05; Levels 2–4 modestly larger. Alternative model: reactions independent; learning → behavior/results; behavior ↔ results reciprocal. Consolidated on [[Kirkpatrick Level Intercorrelation Critique]]; direct predecessor to the already-ingested [[alliger-et-al-1997-training-criteria-meta-analysis|Alliger et al. (1997)]] augmented framework. New: 2 concepts + 2 entities ([[Mikhail A. Wolfson]], [[Elizabeth A. Janak]]) + 2 sources (c-000860–865). Updated [[Kirkpatrick Four-Level Model]], [[George M. Alliger]], [[Scott I. Tannenbaum]], [[Learning & Development MOC]], [[index]]. Candidate next: Wolfson et al. (2018) IFBL primary; Holton (1996) alternative evaluation model.

Prior ingest: 2026-07-06 — **batch of 4 foundational papers** forming a *social-cognitive-theory / self-efficacy + metacognition* spine, plus one org-identification meta. **[[bandura-2001-social-cognitive-theory-agentic|Bandura (2001)]]** (*Annual Review of Psychology*) = the agentic synthesis of **[[Social Cognitive Theory|SCT]]**: "to be an agent is to intentionally make things happen." Four core features of **[[Human Agency]]** (intentionality, [[Forethought and Anticipatory Self-Guidance|forethought]], self-reactiveness [+[[Moral Disengagement]]], self-reflectiveness); three **[[Modes of Human Agency]]** (personal/proxy/collective → [[Collective Efficacy]]); **[[Triadic Reciprocal Causation]]**; **[[Agentic Management of Fortuity]]**. Thesis: **efficacy beliefs are the foundation of agency**. **[[schunk-usher-2012-social-cognitive-theory-motivation|Schunk & Usher (2012)]]** operationalizes SCT for motivation: **[[Enactive and Vicarious Learning]]**, the four **[[Sources of Self-Efficacy]]** (mastery > vicarious/[[Social Comparison|social comparison]] > verbal persuasion > physiological, all *interpreted* not automatic), and self-efficacy → task choice/effort/persistence (Multon 1991; Stajkovic & Luthans 1998, 28% work-performance gain). **[[kruger-dunning-1999-unskilled-and-unaware|Kruger & Dunning (1999)]]** = the **[[Dunning-Kruger Effect]]**: the unskilled suffer a *dual burden* — errors + the metacognitive deficit to recognize them (skills that produce answers overlap with skills that evaluate them). Bottom quartile 12th→**62nd** percentile; top quartile *under*estimates via **false-consensus** (error about others, not self); **competence begets calibration** (Study 4 training). Connects to [[Metacognition and Self-Directed Learning]], [[Calibration (Judgment)]], [[Above-Average Effect]], [[Expert Performance (Ericsson Definition)|Ericsson]] novice-metacognition. **[[lee-park-koo-2015-organizational-identification-meta-analysis|Lee, Park & Koo (2015)]]** (*Psych Bulletin*) = meta-analysis positioning **[[Organizational Identification (Outcomes)|organizational identification]]** as an upstream **basis** in the **[[Attitude-Behavior Relations|Fishbein-Ajzen]]** model: OI → in-role ρ=**.27** (k=41), extra-role ρ=**.42** (k=53), general attitude β=.74, **direct effect on behavior beyond attitude**, **stronger in collectivistic cultures**; distinct from [[Organizational Commitment]] (lacks self-defining nature; refines [[Social Identification|Ashforth & Mael]]). Through-line: **self-efficacy/metacognition** (efficacy = foundation of agency → 4 sources drive motivation → DK deficit is the failure mode → competence recalibrates). New: 14 concepts + 8 entities ([[Albert Bandura]], [[Dale H. Schunk]], [[Ellen L. Usher]], [[Justin Kruger]], [[David Dunning]], [[Eun-Suk Lee]], [[Tae-Youn Park]], [[Bonjin Koo]]) + 4 sources (c-000832–857). Updated [[Social Cognitive Theory]] (seed→developing), [[Self-Efficacy (Goal Setting)]], [[Learning & Development MOC]], [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]], [[index]]. Candidate next: Bandura (1997) *Self-Efficacy* primary; Riketta (2005) OI meta for comparison; Ehrlinger et al. (2008) DK follow-up.

Prior: 2026-07-06 — **batch of 2 DEI meta-analyses (JAP)** forming a *diversity-ideology + bias-intervention* spine. **[[leslie-et-al-2020-diversity-ideologies-meta-analysis|Leslie, Bono, Kim & Beaver (2020)]]** (*JAP*) = random-effects meta-analysis (**k=296**, 167 samples) of four **[[Diversity Ideologies]]** × four intergroup outcomes. Disaggregates the identity-blind ("melting pot") category into **colorblindness / meritocracy / assimilation** vs. the identity-conscious ("salad bowl") **multiculturalism** — prior conflation (e.g., CoBRAS scale) caused mixed findings. Effects **diverge** across ideologies *and* outcomes: multiculturalism most consistently improves relations (prejudice −.32, discrimination −.22, stereotyping −.17, **policy support +.57**); **meritocracy** strongest on discrimination (−.48) but null on prejudice/stereotyping; **colorblindness** cuts stereotyping (−.19) but null on discrimination; **assimilation** worsens all bias. Only consistent identity-blind effect = **all three reduce diversity policy support**. Consistency premise: each ideology most affects the outcome matching its content. Refines the existing binary in **[[Multicultural vs. Colorblind Approaches]]** (added a nuance callout). **[[costa-2024-discrimination-interventions|Costa (2024)]]** (*JAP*) = meta-analysis (70 articles / 208 ES) via **[[Attitude Dimension Consistency]]** (principle of compatibility + **[[Tripartite Model of Attitudes|ABC/tripartite model]]**): interventions best shift an outcome when the **attitude dimension targeted matches the dimension measured** (holds for cognitive/behavioral, *not* affective). **[[Bias Intervention Taxonomy|Taxonomy]]**: passive education/bias-reminders **ineffective**; **inhibit-bias-manifestation** class strongest — **accountability g=.80**, **social-norm change g=.58** (structured evaluation .51, imagined contact .47). Overall workplace effect modest (g≈.27). Both papers converge on **content–outcome alignment** as the key to reconciling mixed DEI findings. New: 4 concepts ([[Diversity Ideologies]], [[Tripartite Model of Attitudes]], [[Attitude Dimension Consistency]], [[Bias Intervention Taxonomy]]) + 2 entities ([[Lisa M. Leslie]], [[Elaine Costa]]) + 2 sources (c-000824–831). Updated [[Multicultural vs. Colorblind Approaches]], [[Learning & Development MOC]] (new DEI cluster), [[index]]. Prior: 2026-07-03 — **batch of 3 foundational team papers** forming a *team effectiveness + adaptation* spine. **[[mathieu-et-al-2017-century-work-teams|Mathieu, Hollenbeck, van Knippenberg & Ilgen (2017)]]** (*JAP* centennial) = a century of work teams in JAP: JAP published **zero** team articles in its first 32 yrs, then a curvilinear surge from the 1990s (R²=.81); the field shifted from **individuals-within-teams** to the **team itself** and multiteam systems. Three unifying themes = **tasks/structure** (task **scope × complexity** [component/coordinative/dynamic; Wood 1986]; structure via **[[IPO–IMOI Framework|skill & authority differentiation]]**, Hollenbeck et al. 2012), **member composition**, and **processes/[[Emergent States (Teams)|emergent states]]** — dynamic & reciprocal, not a pipeline. Grounded in Hackman's model: teams are **semi-autonomous social systems**, and the five **[[Enabling Conditions for Team Effectiveness|enabling conditions]]** (real team, compelling direction, enabling structure, supportive context, expert coaching) *raise the likelihood* — not guarantee — of effectiveness. Prior: 2026-06-30 — **[[ericsson-lehmann-1996-expert-exceptional-performance|Ericsson & Lehmann (1996)]]**, *Expert and Exceptional Performance* (*Annual Review of Psychology*). Organizing thesis = **[[Maximal Adaptation to Task Constraints|expert performance is acquired maximal adaptation to task constraints]]**, not innate talent. Highest levels appear only after the **[[10-Year Rule|~10-year rule]]** of daily **[[Deliberate Practice]]** (coach-designed, feedback-driven, effortful, ~4h/day sustainable cap) — raw experience predicts performance only weakly. **[[Talent vs Deliberate Practice|Talent debunked]]**: physiology (heart, capillaries, muscle fiber, ballet turnout, perfect pitch) is largely training-induced/reversible; IQ→performance correlation decays to non-sig after ~5 yrs; innate domain-specific capacity's role "small, possibly negligible" — **height** the lone exception. Mechanisms circumvent generic limits: **anticipation from advance cues** beats reaction-time floors (cricket/tennis/typing eye-hand span); **[[Skilled Memory Theory]] → [[Long-Term Working Memory]]** (Chase & Ericsson digit spans 82/100+; chess blindfold) expands functional WM in-domain and **subsumes** the classic Chase & Simon **[[Chunking]]** account (whose pure-STM claim was overturned). Contrary to the automatization myth, expertise stays mediated by active planning/reasoning. **[[Expert Performance (Ericsson Definition)|Restrictive definition]]** (reproducible superior performance on representative tasks) dissociates **expertise from experience** → converges with the JDM cluster: experience without feedback-rich practice ≠ valid judgment (see convergence callout on [[Mechanical vs Clinical Prediction]]; Dawes/Faust/Meehl). New: 8 concepts + 5 entities ([[K. Anders Ericsson]], [[Andreas C. Lehmann]], [[Herbert A. Simon]], [[William G. Chase]], [[Adriaan de Groot]]) + 1 source (c-000773–786). Updated [[Learning & Development MOC]] (new expertise/deliberate-practice section), [[Mechanical vs Clinical Prediction]], [[index]]. Candidate next: Ericsson, Krampe & Tesch-Römer (1993) primary deliberate-practice paper; Macnamara et al. (2014) critique.

Prior: 2026-06-30 — **batch of 3 forming a lens-model / personnel-selection cluster** around the Brunswik [[Lens Model]]. **[[karelaia-hogarth-2008-lens-model-meta-analysis|Karelaia & Hogarth (2008)]]** (*Psych Bulletin*) = 5-decade meta-analysis of 249 lens-model environments (~303k judgments): mean achievement $r_a=.56$, matching $G=.80$, consistency $R_s=.80$, residual $C=.04$; three conclusions — people judge fairly well, learn best from **[[Cue Feedback Types|task-information feedback]]** (not outcome feedback), and **[[Bootstrapping (Improper Linear Models)|a linear model of the judge beats the judge]]** ($GR_e−r_a≈.10$) by stripping inconsistency; expertise does *not* raise accuracy. **[[viswesvaran-schmidt-ones-2005-general-factor-job-performance|Viswesvaran, Schmidt & Ones (2005)]]** (*JAP*) = after netting out **[[Halo Error|halo]]** + 3 error sources, a real **[[General Factor in Job Performance|general factor (~60% variance)]]** survives; halo inflates interdimension correlations +33% (supervisor) / +63% (peer) — the asymmetry (inflates within-rater, deflates between-rater) is what lets them isolate it; peer & supervisory ratings construct-equivalent. **[[kuncel-et-al-2013-mechanical-vs-clinical|Kuncel et al. Prior: 2026-06-29 — **[[lefkowitz-2008-values-organizational-psychology|Lefkowitz (2008)]]** (*JOB*) + **[[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct|APA Ethics Code (2017)]]**, a paired ethics/values ingest. Lefkowitz's thesis: "the fault lies not in our ethics, but in our **values**." I/O psychologists behave ethically but the field's *institutional values* — encoded in the [[Scientist-Practitioner Model|S-P model]] — have drifted to corporate-economic goals, away from psychology's humanist tradition. The Code itself is three-layered: aspirational [[Five General Principles (APA)]] (A Beneficence, B Fidelity, C Integrity, D Justice, E Respect) + enforceable Standards (see [[Aspirational vs Enforceable Standards]]). Prior: 2026-06-29 — **[[ashforth-mael-1989-social-identity-theory-organization|Ashforth & Mael (1989)]]**, *Social Identity Theory and the Organization* (AMR). Imported **[[Social Identity Theory|SIT]]** (Tajfel & Turner) into OB and redefined **[[Social Identification|organizational identification]]** as a *purely perceptual* "**perception of oneness** with a group" (the answer to "Who am I?") — surgically separated from [[Organizational Commitment|commitment]], internalization ("I am" vs "I believe"), loyalty (affect), and effort (behavior). Mael's (1988) 6-item identification scale ("this org's successes are my successes") beats the OCQ in a two-factor CFA (AGFI .825 vs .780) → constructs are differentiable (see new **contradiction callout** on [[Organizational Commitment]]). Antecedents: group **distinctiveness**, **prestige**, **out-group salience**, group-formation factors. Consequences: identity-congruent activity, institutional support, **[[Self-Categorization and Self-Stereotyping|self-stereotyping]]** (→ depersonalization, conformity), + reinforces own antecedents. Biggest contribution = the **[[Psychological Group]]**: identification with a collectivity can arise *without* interpersonal cohesion/similarity/interaction ([[Minimal Group Paradigm|mere categorization suffices]]), reversing the "interpersonal regard aggregates up" logic. Applied to **socialization** (socialization→identification→internalization indirect path; investiture/divestiture; [[Routinization of Charisma]], AA vs NCA), **role conflict** (identity = amalgam of loosely-coupled identities; resolved by ordering/separating/buffering not integrating; Challenger "engineering hat/management hat"), and **intergroup relations** ([[In-Group Bias and Intergroup Comparison]]; conflict without objective causes; legitimacy mitigates). New: 7 concepts + 2 entities ([[Blake E. Ashforth]], [[Fred Mael]]) + 1 source (c-000734–743). Updated [[Organizational Commitment]], [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]], [[index]].
