Prior ingest: 2026-07-07 — **[[pichler-2012-social-context-appraisal|Pichler (2012)]]** (*Human Resource Management*, 51(5):709–732) — first **meta-analysis of the social context of performance appraisal**, extending the vault's PM cluster into the social/relational side. Meta-analyzed 69 samples / 62 studies. **Core finding: [[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)|rater–ratee relationship quality]] is the strongest predictor of [[Appraisal Reactions|appraisal reactions]]** — composite Mr ≈ **.66**, above appraisal participation (.48) and rating favorability (.39). Relationship-quality subdimensions (overall RQ/LMX, supervisor trust, support, satisfaction) all ≈ **.60**. Via **MASEM**, tested three competing models of the appraisal process: **(1) independent antecedents** (RQ, participation, favorability parallel) → poor fit (CFI=.72, RMSEA=.33); **(2) instrumental** (RQ predicts reactions *only* through participation+ratings, no direct path) → poor fit (CFI=.69, RMSEA=.39); **(3) [[Relational Model of Appraisal Reactions|relational/relationship-driven]]** (RQ antecedent to session characteristics **and** a *direct* path to reactions) → **good fit** (χ²(1)=1.93 p=.16, CFI=.99, RMSEA=.09, SRMR=.04, R²=.49). Model 3 wins → the appraisal process is **largely relational, not merely instrumental** — but instrumentality (participation, favorable outcomes) still matters; Model 3 in essence *integrates* both instrumental and relational propositions of [[Procedural Justice]] (Thibaut & Walker instrumental **vs** Lind & Tyler group-value/[[Voice Effect|voice]]). Theory move: integrates [[Social Exchange Theory]] with procedural justice; **reverses** the usual org-SET ordering (there treatment/rewards → exchange quality; in appraisal, exchange *quality* → favorable treatment → reactions). Robustness: RQ→reactions link **NOT moderated** by rating favorability (disconfirms H1) or participation (RQ2) — both n.s. **Practical:** ratees react positively regardless of rating favorability when they have a good supervisor relationship; interventions/rater-training should target supportive, participatory feedback + relationship quality, not just accuracy. **Limits:** cross-sectional percept–percept (inflation risk, no causal/process inference → calls for longitudinal); composite reactions variable (no differential prediction); scope = supervisor-driven *administrative* reviews only (not [[360-Degree Feedback|360]]/developmental). New: **4 concepts** ([[Appraisal Reactions]], [[Procedural Justice]], [[Voice Effect]], [[Relational Model of Appraisal Reactions]]) + **1 entity** ([[Shaun Pichler]]) + **1 source** (c-001076–c-001081). Updated [[Performance Appraisal]], [[Social Exchange Theory]], [[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)]], [[Performance Management MOC]], [[Social Exchange & Organizational Justice MOC]], [[index]]. Prior ingest: 2026-07-07 — **measurement-foundations trio (reliability + CPA primary + validity inference)**. (1) **[[revelle-condon-2019-reliability-alpha-to-omega|Revelle & Condon (2019)]]** (*Psychological Assessment*) — the α-to-ω tutorial. **[[Reliability]]** = a **variance ratio** (signal/total) and a *joint* property of test AND sample, not the test alone; reliable variance mixes **Trait+State+specific**, so **there is no single reliability** — design picks what you isolate (internal consistency vs dependability [short retest] vs stability [long retest] vs inter-rater). Two problems with **[[Coefficient Alpha|α]]**: it equals reliability only under **tau-equivalence**, and it is *not* an internal-consistency/unidimensionality index (just #items × mean inter-item r). Report model-based **[[Coefficient Omega|omega]]**: $\omega_h$ (single-general-factor saturation) + $\omega_t$ (total reliable variance ≈ **glb**), via bifactor/Schmid–Leiman (EFA positively biased at low saturation → prefer CFA). Ratings → **ICC1–3**; categorical → **Cohen's κ**. R `psych` (`omega`, `alpha`, `splitHalf`, `testRetest`, `ICC`, `cohen.kappa`). Prior: 2026-06-30 — **[[ericsson-lehmann-1996-expert-exceptional-performance|Ericsson & Lehmann (1996)]]**, *Expert and Exceptional Performance* (*Annual Review of Psychology*). Organizing thesis = **[[Maximal Adaptation to Task Constraints|expert performance is acquired maximal adaptation to task constraints]]**, not innate talent. Highest levels appear only after the **[[10-Year Rule|~10-year rule]]** of daily **[[Deliberate Practice]]** (coach-designed, feedback-driven, effortful, ~4h/day sustainable cap) — raw experience predicts performance only weakly. **[[Talent vs Deliberate Practice|Talent debunked]]**: physiology (heart, capillaries, muscle fiber, ballet turnout, perfect pitch) is largely training-induced/reversible; IQ→performance correlation decays to non-sig after ~5 yrs; innate domain-specific capacity's role "small, possibly negligible" — **height** the lone exception. Mechanisms circumvent generic limits: **anticipation from advance cues** beats reaction-time floors (cricket/tennis/typing eye-hand span); **[[Skilled Memory Theory]] → [[Long-Term Working Memory]]** (Chase & Ericsson digit spans 82/100+; chess blindfold) expands functional WM in-domain and **subsumes** the classic Chase & Simon **[[Chunking]]** account (whose pure-STM claim was overturned). Contrary to the automatization myth, expertise stays mediated by active planning/reasoning. **[[Expert Performance (Ericsson Definition)|Restrictive definition]]** (reproducible superior performance on representative tasks) dissociates **expertise from experience** → converges with the JDM cluster: experience without feedback-rich practice ≠ valid judgment (see convergence callout on [[Mechanical vs Clinical Prediction]]; Dawes/Faust/Meehl).
