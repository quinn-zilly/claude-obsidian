address: c-000481*Think Again* by Adam Grant - A book by an organizational psychologist that discusses how people can change their minds. Just a well-written book on an interesting topic, and the author draws on diverse examples from different domains. This is probably the easiest read on this list.
2. *Noise* by Daniel Kahneman, Olivier Sibony, and Cass Sunstein - Talks about how unreliability in human judgement creates inconsistency, or noise, in decision making. Very applicable to fields like insurance and law where individuals need to assess evidence to come to decisions across a number of cases. This is the densest read on this list.
3. *Nudge* by Richard Thaler and Cass Sunstein - One of my all time favorite books. Technically written by a behavioral economist, it discusses the science and application of behavioral nudges. A nudge is a subtle change in choice architecture which helps people make better decisions by guiding them toward personally beneficial options.
4. *Thinking Fast and Slow* by Daniel Kahneman - This is probably the most influential popular psychology book in the 21st century. Basically, it explains how we have two mental systems acting in parallel, a fast, unconscious system and slower, conscious system, which handle different problems for us. It describes the systematic errors our fast system makes, and the benefits of learning to use our slow processing system to double check the conclusions reached by the fast system.
