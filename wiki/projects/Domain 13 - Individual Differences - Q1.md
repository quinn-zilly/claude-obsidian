# Question

The "Big Five" have received increasing attention as dimensions important for selection. Briefly describe the "history" of the Big Five. Then, outline the arguments for and against the Big Five as a taxonomy of personality, especially as it relates to selection issues. Finally, briefly discuss the continuing debates regarding the use of personality measures in selection systems, being sure to indicate which side you are arguing should be adopted and why.

# Outline

## History of the Big Five

- Rooted in the lexical hypothesis: trait terms encoded in natural language index personality ([[Saucier-Srivastava-2015-structural-models-personality|Saucier & Srivastava, 2015]]).
    - Lexical work by Galton, Allport and Odbert, and Cattell was reduced to a five-factor structure (first recovered by Fiske; formalized by Tupes and Christal) and later popularized as the "Big Five" by Goldberg ([[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough, Oswald, & Ock, 2015]]).
- Organizational interest surged after Project A (U.S. Army), which summarized validity by construct rather than by measure ([[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough et al., 2015]]).
- Structure recovered via factor analysis; factors emerge in a radial hierarchy (1→2→3→5→6 factors), with one- to three-factor solutions most cross-culturally replicable ([[Saucier-Srivastava-2015-structural-models-personality|Saucier & Srivastava, 2015]]).

## Arguments For the Big Five as a Taxonomy (esp. selection)

- Parsimonious and robust: replicates across rating sources, some cultures, and rotation methods; the dominant working taxonomy ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]).
- Meaningful criterion-related validity: each trait predicts performance in the expected direction, and Conscientiousness is the strongest, most generalizable predictor (ρ ≈ .19 overall; [[zell-lesick-2022-big-five-performance-synthesis|Zell & Lesick, 2022]]).
- As a set, operational multiple correlations in the .30s–.40s across many criteria (overall job performance R = .27; [[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al., 2007]]).
- Small racial-ethnic subgroup differences relative to cognitive ability → lower adverse impact ([[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|Sackett et al., 2022]]; [[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al., 2007]]).
- Validity is context-dependent but predictable: it rises in weak situations and when the job activates the trait, so modest averages understate true validity ([[Judge-Zapata-2015-person-situation-revisited|Judge & Zapata, 2015]]; [[tett-et-al-2021-trait-activation-theory|Tett et al., 2021]]).

## Arguments Against the Big Five as a Taxonomy (esp. selection)

- Method-bound: factor solutions depend on which variables are entered ("prestructuring"), and the lexical Big Five is not universal across languages ([[Saucier-Srivastava-2015-structural-models-personality|Saucier & Srivastava, 2015]]; [[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough et al., 2015]]).
- Not comprehensive: omits constructs such as honesty-humility and the dark triad; HEXACO adds Honesty-Humility and explains 10–15% more variance in workplace delinquency ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]; [[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough et al., 2015]]).
- Broad factors obscure prediction: many lower-order facets out-predict their parent factor, and facets of the same factor can correlate oppositely with a criterion ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]).
    - Dominance vs. Sociability "win–win": a facet-level Dominance measure yields higher validity *and* lower adverse impact than broad Extraversion.
- Weak factor-level construct validity: facets cross-load, so structure is lattice-like rather than strictly hierarchical ([[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough et al., 2015]]).
- Aggregating to broad factors buries context specificity: associations vary by domain (C ≈ .28 academic vs. .20 job; [[zell-lesick-2022-big-five-performance-synthesis|Zell & Lesick, 2022]]).

## Continuing Debates: Personality Measures in Selection

- Core exchange: [[Morgeson et al. (2007) Panel Critique|Morgeson et al. (2007)]] (low validity + faking → reconsider) vs. [[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al. (2007)]] (meta-analytic validity holds; faking overstated).
- Validity magnitude: modern recalibration lowers absolute validities (Conscientiousness ρ .31 → .19) because range-restriction corrections were over-applied, though relative rankings hold ([[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|Sackett et al., 2022]]).
- Faking: real-applicant distortion does not appear to destroy criterion-related or construct validity, unlike lab directed-faking ([[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al., 2007]]; [[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]).
- Remedies exist: forced-choice and computer-adaptive formats, plus warnings with consequences, reduce faking without harming validity ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]).
- Contextualization improves prediction: situation strength and trait activation specify when traits predict ([[Judge-Zapata-2015-person-situation-revisited|Judge & Zapata, 2015]]; [[tett-et-al-2021-trait-activation-theory|Tett et al., 2021]]).

### Position A — Pro: Retain personality measures

- Adopt the [[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al. (2007)]] position: keep personality measures as validated supplements to cognitive ability.
- Even under conservative recalibration, the Big Five as a set and compound measures (integrity tests, ρ ≈ .40) predict performance and add incremental validity over cognitive ability ([[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al., 2007]]; [[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|Sackett et al., 2022]]).
- Small subgroup differences make them a diversity-friendly complement that eases the validity–adverse-impact tradeoff of cognitive-only batteries ([[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|Sackett et al., 2022]]).
- Faking is addressable through measurement design and is not corrosive of validity among real applicants ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]).
- The productive path is better construct–criterion matching (facets, trait activation), not removal ([[Judge-Zapata-2015-person-situation-revisited|Judge & Zapata, 2015]]; [[tett-et-al-2021-trait-activation-theory|Tett et al., 2021]]).

### Position B — Against: Reconsider personality measures

- Adopt the [[Morgeson et al. (2007) Panel Critique|Morgeson et al. (2007)]] position: reconsider routine reliance on off-the-shelf, broad self-report personality tests in high-stakes selection.
- Recalibrated validities are modest (C ρ ≈ .19; other factors ≈ .10) and fall below alternatives such as structured interviews (ρ = .42) ([[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|Sackett et al., 2022]]; [[zell-lesick-2022-big-five-performance-synthesis|Zell & Lesick, 2022]]).
- Broad factor scores obscure the facet- and context-specific relationships that carry validity, so generic batteries leave prediction on the table ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson, 2013]]; [[Judge-Zapata-2015-person-situation-revisited|Judge & Zapata, 2015]]).
- Faking remains a live concern for applicant self-reports, and the taxonomy is neither comprehensive nor cross-culturally universal ([[Morgeson et al. (2007) Panel Critique|Morgeson et al., 2007]]; [[Saucier-Srivastava-2015-structural-models-personality|Saucier & Srivastava, 2015]]).
- Defensible path is targeted use: job-specific facets and trait-activation measurement, or alternative methods, over default broad self-report ([[tett-et-al-2021-trait-activation-theory|Tett et al., 2021]]; [[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough et al., 2015]]).
