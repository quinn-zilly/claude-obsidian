# Question

- Based on theory and research, what are 7 job design dimensions that predict performance and employee engagement/ satisfaction? Given an example job, how would you build these elements into the job?

# Outline

## Model

Job Characteristics Model/WDQ ([[morgeson-humphrey-2006-work-design-questionnaire|Morgeson & Humphrey (2006) WDQ]]) identifies four broad work characteristics with a number of sub-dimensions.
- Task
- Knowledge
- Social
- Work Context

## Seven Design Elements

These seven design elements are strong predictors of performance, engagement, and satisfaction across a large number of studies and participants. [[humphrey-nahrgang-morgeson-2007-work-design-meta-analysis|Humphrey et al. 2007]]. 
- Task Variety: Degree to which a job requires employees to perform a wide range of tasks on the job.
- Skill Variety: Extent to which a job requires an individual to use a variety of different skills to complete the work.
- Task Significance: Degree to which a job influences the lives or work of others, whether inside or outside the organization.
- Autonomy: Extent to which a job allows freedom, independence, and discretion over work scheduling, decision making, and work methods.
- Feedback from job: Degree to which the job provides direct and clear information about the effectiveness of task performance.
- Feedback from others: Degree to which others in the organization provide information about performance.
- Social Support: Degree to which a job provides opportunities for advice and assistance from others.
