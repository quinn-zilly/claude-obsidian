# Q1 — Iterative PM redesign (Greystone, ~5,000 employees)

> Forced ranking + inconsistent annual narrative reviews; only iterative change permitted. Identify seven evidence-based elements, justify with theory/research, and address motivation/performance.

## Framing

[[Performance Management]] is the continuous set of activities (defining, appraising, developing) that aligns work with strategy; [[Performance Appraisal]] is the discrete formal evaluation within it ([[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy, 2017]]). A century of PA research shows rating formats matter little, the individual-to-firm link is unproven, and context matters enormously ([[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy, 2017]]). Given the iterative-only constraint, retain a formal appraisal but fix its weakest features and shift leverage toward day-to-day PM behavior ([[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]).

## Seven evidence-based elements

1. **Replace forced ranking with absolute, criterion-referenced standards.** [[Forced Distribution|Forced distribution]] assumes stable, comparable cross-rater true scores, but interrater reliability ≈ .50 and the top of a weak group may beat the bottom of a strong one → accuracy/fairness problems and eroded collaboration; use fell 49%→14% (2009–2011) ([[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy, 2017]]; [[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]).
2. **Anchor ratings to a behavioral, multidimensional performance model.** Separate task from citizenship/contextual and counterproductive behavior ([[campbell-wiernik-2015-modeling-assessment-work-performance|Campbell & Wiernik, 2015]]) to cut criterion deficiency and give inconsistent narratives a common referent.
3. **Add [[Frame-of-Reference Training|frame-of-reference (FOR) rater training]].** Error-avoidance training can reduce accuracy; FOR training is the dominant effective method for accuracy ([[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy, 2017]]), attacking the "raters can rate accurately" assumption and manager inconsistency ([[Three Assumptions of Performance Rating]]; [[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]).
4. **Set specific, difficult, aligned goals on shorter cycles.** Specific difficult goals beat "do your best" via direction, effort, persistence, strategy; moderated by commitment, self-efficacy, feedback ([[wiki/sources/locke-latham-2002-goal-setting-theory|Locke & Latham, 2002]]). [[Cascading Goals & SMART|Cascade]] from strategy but shorten annual→quarterly ([[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]).
5. **Institute frequent feedback and check-ins.** Goals + feedback > goals alone ([[wiki/sources/locke-latham-2002-goal-setting-theory|Locke & Latham, 2002]]); ongoing feedback combined with other reforms yields the largest impact ([[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]). Redistributes PM effort across the year.
6. **Build in employee voice and manager–employee relationship quality.** Relationship quality is the strongest correlate of [[Appraisal Reactions|appraisal reactions]] (composite Mr = .66), above participation or favorability; the process is [[Relational Model of Appraisal Reactions|relational, not merely instrumental]] ([[pichler-2012-social-context-appraisal|Pichler, 2012]]).
7. **Separate developmental from administrative purpose and evaluate the system.** Appraisals serve conflicting purposes and rater goals distort ratings ([[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy, 2017]]); decouple development from pay and evaluate PM against a multilevel [[PM Effectiveness Value Chains|criterion chain]] ([[schleicher-2019-pm-effectiveness-review|Schleicher et al., 2019]]). Retain ratings so information still reaches employees ([[Rating-less Reviews]]; [[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al., 2019]]).

## Motivation and performance

Three mechanisms reinforce each other. Goal setting is the proximal engine — specific, difficult, near-term goals with frequent feedback drive effort and raise self-efficacy via the high-performance cycle ([[wiki/sources/locke-latham-2002-goal-setting-theory|Locke & Latham, 2002]]). [[Self-Determination Theory]] explains the social-context changes: voice/coaching support autonomy, accurate feedback + a clear model support competence, supportive relationships support relatedness ([[wiki/sources/ryan-deci-2000-sdt|Ryan & Deci, 2000]]). Removing forced ranking eliminates a zero-sum structure that undermines relatedness, while relationship quality and voice produce the favorable reactions that predict motivation to improve ([[pichler-2012-social-context-appraisal|Pichler, 2012]]). These changes are a better bet than the status quo, though the individual-to-firm link remains unproven ([[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy, 2017]]; [[schleicher-2019-pm-effectiveness-review|Schleicher et al., 2019]]).
