## Instructions

Students will produce a (single-spaced) 3-5-page training and development checklist with at least 6 items for every week of class. Make sure you capture the high impact factors in the readings and lecture for each week.

The checklist should include a cover page, and a short introduction (write it in a way that it reads as an introduction to a guide that you will be handing off to another training professional so they can use it). You should write your checklist in enough detail so that someone unfamiliar with the class can understand it. For example, don’t include a checklist item that simply says, “Include adult development principles”. Instead write out the 5 principles of adult learning and briefly define them in a way that someone else could use the items to assess if the quality of their training module.

You should have at least 6 items per week. Include a wide variety of references (at least 4 per week!) to the class readings for each of your checklist items. For each item, include references feel free just this once in your life to write out only the first author not all of them. (First author, et al., year). You can also draw some of the principles from class lectures if desired. References to class slides should be noted as: (Yost, Class Lecture, date). Make sure you don’t rely on only a limited number of sources (e.g., One reading, class lectures). The checklist should provide evidence that you have integrated material from several class readings.
