# Question

How would you develop a research-based on-the-job learning system for employees in an organization that doesn’t include any formal training classes? Include how you could leverage job assignments, managers, and HR processes in your response.

# Outline

## Framing and Rationale
- 70–90% of competency acquisition occurs outside formal training, yet ~80% of learning research and investment target formal programs ([[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]; [[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|Tannenbaum & Wolfson, 2022]]). With no formal classes, engineer the *conditions* for on-the-job learning rather than deliver content.
- Design target = informal field-based learning (IFBL) / informal learning behaviors (ILBs): intentional, self-directed, field-based behaviors — feedback/reflection, vicarious learning, experimentation ([[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|T&W, 2022]]; [[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]). Intentionality distinguishes them from incidental learning.
- Defensible investment: ILBs relate to attitudes (ρ=.29), knowledge/skill (ρ=.41), performance (ρ=.42) ([[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]).

## Design Framework — Build Readiness (CAM-OS)
- Engagement = personal readiness (Capability, Awareness, Motivation) + situational readiness (Opportunity, Support); opportunity and support are the most actionable levers ([[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|T&W, 2022]]).
- Support is the strongest ILB antecedent (ρ=.32); tangible/formal rewards, autonomy, learning-rich tasks are the biggest drivers ([[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]).
- Begin with a Field Learning Needs Analysis (FLNA), the on-the-job analogue of a training needs analysis ([[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|T&W, 2022]]; needs-analysis logic per [[noe-2023-needs-assessment|Noe, 2023]]).

## Leverage Job Assignments (Opportunity)
- Competence is learned from experience; challenging experiences (stretch/first-time assignments, hardships) teach the most ([[mccall-2010-recasting-leadership-development|McCall, 2010]]).
- Publish a list of OJD experiences; use 70-20-10 in development planning ([[yost-plunkett-2010-ten-catalysts|Yost & Plunkett, 2010]]). Match needs to assignments — whoever controls assignments controls development ([[mccall-2010-recasting-leadership-development|McCall, 2010]]).
- Manage the dark side with a Red-Yellow-Green chart (do not / only-with-safety-net / free to trial-and-error) ([[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|T&W, 2022]]).

## Leverage Managers (Support + Vicarious Learning)
- Manager/peer support predicts engagement and transfer; behavior change is far larger when managers reinforce learning ([[bell-et-al-2017-100-years-training-development|Bell et al., 2017]]; [[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]).
- Managers add challenge + feedback/reflection; reflection is the bottleneck, and brief "what did you do / what did you learn" debriefs enrich learning ([[mccall-2010-recasting-leadership-development|McCall, 2010]]). Help capture the lessons ([[yost-plunkett-2010-ten-catalysts|Yost & Plunkett, 2010]]).
- Structure vicarious learning as *coactive* — expert and learner jointly process experience via shared experience, analysis, and support — necessary for tacit knowledge ([[myers-2018-coactive-vicarious-learning|Myers, 2018]]).

## Leverage HR Processes
- Reform HR to hardwire OJD rather than abandon it ([[yost-plunkett-2010-ten-catalysts|Yost & Plunkett, 2010]]), answering the "HR should step aside" argument ([[mccall-2010-recasting-leadership-development|McCall, 2010]]). Catalysts:
	- Publish a leadership/competency map (experiences, competencies, relationships, learning agility).
	- Require/audit development plans; separate goal-setting from development discussions.
	- Focus on transitions (high openness-to-learning) via a transition checklist.
	- Reward OJD; promote managers who develop people (tangible support = strongest ILB antecedent, [[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]).
- Use staffing/rewards/norms to build psychological safety and a learning climate ([[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|T&W, 2022]]).

## Embed Learning at the Organizational Level
- Individual learning is necessary but not sufficient; retain knowledge in supra-individual repositories (members, tools, routines/TMS) or it depreciates when people leave ([[argote-2011-org-learning-experience|Argote & Miron-Spektor, 2011]]; [[argote-2021-organizational-learning|Argote et al., 2021]]).
- Use HR/knowledge processes to retain and transfer learning, converting personal OJL into organizational capability ([[argote-2021-organizational-learning|Argote et al., 2021]]).

## Evaluation
- IFBL resists pre/post testing; use event-sampling, diary methods, longitudinal designs with baseline + repeated KSAO measures ([[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning|T&W, 2022]]).
- Measure antecedents (CAM-OS/support), behaviors (ILB engagement), and outcomes (attitudes, K/S, performance) ([[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis|Cerasoli et al., 2018]]).
