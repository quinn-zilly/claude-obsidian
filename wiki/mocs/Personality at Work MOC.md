# Personality at Work — Map of Content

> A Map of Content (MOC) links into a cluster of related notes. It is a navigation hub, not a container.

## Why this MOC exists

This cluster maps the use of **personality variables to predict and understand work behavior and performance** — how personality is *structured* (taxonomies), *measured*, *validated*, and *why it works* (process). The through-line: **prediction is high when predictor and criterion are conceptually matched**, and the field advances by refining *both* sides down to the facet level and linking them systematically. It also holds the live methodological tension between broad-factor synthesis and the "beyond the Big Five" agenda.

## Structure of personality (taxonomies)

- [[Five-Factor Model]] — the dominant Big Five / FFM taxonomy (lexical roots); useful but limiting
- [[HEXACO Model]] — six factors incl. Honesty-Humility; more comprehensive, still hierarchical
- [[Nomological-Web Clustering]] — non-hierarchical; cluster traits by shared relationship profiles
- [[Facet-Level Personality Measurement]] — narrow traits; facets often out-predict their factor
- [[Compound Traits (Personality)]] — integrity, service orientation; basic traits in different concentrations

## Validity, measurement & tensions

- [[Criterion-Related Validity of Personality]] — the matching principle; benchmark magnitudes
- [[Conscientiousness]] — the single best generalizable Big Five predictor of job performance; facets predict differentially
- [[Integrity Tests]] — the highest-validity compound trait; ~.41 for supervisory JP ratings
- [[Incremental Validity Over Cognitive Ability]] — personality's non-redundant gain over GMA
- [[Bandwidth-Fidelity Dilemma]] — broad vs narrow; a theory/practice tension, not a dichotomy
- [[Synthetic Validity]] — tailored predictor batteries from a shared predictor × criterion database
- [[Intentional Distortion (Faking)]] — faking rarely corrodes validity; warnings + consequences help
- [[Directed Faking vs Applicant Response Distortion]] — the lab-vs-applicant distinction; applicant distortion is overrated
- [[Second-Order Meta-Analysis]] — aggregating across meta-analyses (the method behind Zell & Lesick)

## Structure of personality (the "what are the units" question)

- [[Units Problem (Personality)]] — realist vs constructivist vs functionalist views of what traits *are*
- [[Lexical Hypothesis]] — deriving structure from the natural-language personality lexicon
- [[Personality Structure Hierarchy]] — radial hierarchy: Big One → Two → Three → Five → Six; robustness falls as factors rise

## Validity of selection procedures (the recalibration)

- [[Operational Validity (Selection)]] — the corrected predictor–performance correlation practitioners rank on
- [[Range Restriction Correction]] — the artifact correction that was systematically overapplied
- [[Predictive vs Concurrent Validation]] — the design distinction that drives the overcorrection
- [[Validity–Diversity Tradeoff]] — high-validity procedures often carry high adverse impact

## Why personality predicts (process)

- [[Personality–Performance Process Models]] — direct vs indirect determinants; motivational mediators
- [[Person–Situation Debate]] — the trait-vs-situation controversy interactionism resolves
- [[Trait Activation Theory]] — traits activated by valued situational cues
- [[Situation Strength]] — weak situations let personality predict more strongly
- [[Counterproductive Work Behavior]] — a key criterion (low C, low A, low H)

## Open questions / frontier

- Does refining to the **facet / nomological-web** level actually beat broad factors on *good* criteria — or does broad-factor synthesis (Zell & Lesick) already capture what matters? (the live tension in this cluster)
- Can a shared **synthetic-validity database** (Johnson et al. 2010; metaBUS) be built and sustained?
- Do **HEXACO** factors out-predict the Big Five once *contaminated* self-report criteria are replaced with real performance?
- Why are personality effects **context-specific** (job vs academic vs "other")? What moderators drive the domain differences?
- Missing primaries to ingest: Barrick, Mount & Judge (2001); Wilmot & Ones (2019) conscientiousness synthesis; Ones, Viswesvaran & Schmidt (1993) integrity meta-analysis; Tett & Burnett (2003) TAT primary; Judge et al. (2002) leadership.
