# Culture & IT — Map of Content

> A Map of Content (MOC) links into a cluster of related notes. It is a navigation hub, not a container.

## Why this MOC exists

This cluster maps how **culture** — national, organizational, and group — shapes the development, adoption, use, and management of information technology, and how IT in turn reshapes culture. Following these links answers: when does culture become *visible* (through conflict), what makes a technology "fit," and what values do groups attribute to IT itself?

## Core theory

- [[IT-Culture Conflict]] — system / contribution / vision conflict; the central theory of Leidner & Kayworth (2006)
- [[Cultural Fit]] — congruence between group values and values embedded in a system
- [[IT Culture]] — the values a group attributes to IT (IT values); Kaarst-Brown's five patterns

## Frameworks

- [[Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions]] — power distance, uncertainty avoidance, individualism–collectivism, masculinity–femininity

## Technology acceptance & adoption

- [[UTAUT]] — the dominant unified model of IS/IT acceptance (Venkatesh et al. 2003)
- [[Revised UTAUT Model]] — Dwivedi et al. (2019): attitude reinstated, moderators dropped
- [[Attitude as Mediator]] — the individual-characteristics construct UTAUT omitted
- [[Technology Acceptance Model]] — Davis (1989); ancestor of UTAUT
- [[Facilitating Conditions]] — infrastructure/support construct; contested FC→BI path
- [[MASEM]] — the meta-analytic SEM method used to test the revised model
