# Social Exchange & Organizational Justice — Map of Content

> A navigation hub for how fair treatment becomes reciprocated behavior at work.

## Why this MOC exists

This cluster maps the dominant explanation in organizational behavior for **why justice predicts behavior**: fair treatment is a symbolic resource that fosters a high-quality social exchange relationship, which employees reciprocate with citizenship and performance. Following these links answers: what is social exchange theory, how is justice structured and measured, and through which mediators (and which competing lens) does justice shape behavior?

## Core theory

- [[Social Exchange Theory]] — the three-pillar framework (rules, resources, relationships)
- [[Norm of Reciprocity]] — the operative exchange rule; Gouldner's three senses; exchange ideology
- [[Foa & Foa Resource Theory]] — what gets exchanged; particularism × concreteness
- [[Organizational Justice]] — perceived workplace fairness; justice vs. fairness
- [[Four Justice Dimensions]] — procedural, distributive, interpersonal, informational + their rules
- [[Justice Measurement Taxonomy]] — faceted/latent justice, overall/faceted fairness

## Exchange-quality mediators

- [[Perceived Organizational Support]] — organization-focused exchange indicator
- [[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)]] — supervisor-focused exchange indicator
- [[Organizational Commitment]] — commitment as repayment for support
- [[Organizational Citizenship Behavior]] — the exemplar reciprocative behavior (vs. CWB)

## Competing / complementary lens

- [[Affective Events Theory]] — the affect-based lens on justice
