# Recruitment — Map of Content

> A Map of Content (MOC) links into a cluster of related notes. It is a navigation hub, not a container.

## Why this MOC exists

This cluster maps how organizations **attract and acquire** talent — the prehire front end that feeds selection. Following these links answers: what predicts whether applicants are attracted and apply, how that pattern shifts across the stages of the funnel, why the same persuasion psychology (ELM, signaling) runs underneath, and which levers (fit, recruiter behavior, message, method) organizations can actually pull.

## Core concepts

- [[Employee Recruitment]] — the construct, its four-part definition, and the state of the field (no general theory)
- [[Applicant Attraction]] — the central prehire criterion; the seven predictor categories
- [[Recruitment Stages (Barber)]] — generate → maintain → influence job choice; stage as moderator

## Predictors & levers

- [[Perceived Fit (Recruitment)]] — the strongest predictor of attraction (the "first recruitment dollar")
- [[Recruiter Behaviors]] — second-largest incremental variance; personableness; early-stage lever
- [[Recruitment Message]] — amount, specificity, realism; web-site features; wording/belonging
- [[Recruitment Methods]] — referrals, web sites, job boards; realism vs individual-difference hypotheses
- [[Employee Referrals]] — the consistently superior method
- [[Realistic Job Preview]] — two-sided messaging → accurate expectations → self-selection

## Underlying theory

- [[Elaboration Likelihood Model]] — central vs peripheral processing; why stage changes what persuades
- [[Signaling Theory (Recruitment)]] — applicants infer unknown attributes from observable cues

## Open questions / frontier

- Can a **general theory of recruitment** integrating targeting, method, message, recruiters, and timing be built (Breaugh's standing challenge)?
- Do college-student findings generalize to experienced job seekers?
- Are the realism/prescreening mechanisms behind **referral** advantages real (untested on referrer data)?
- What predicts attraction/choice at the neglected **Stage 3** (influencing job choices)?
- Missing primaries to ingest: Chapman et al. (2005) recruiting meta-analysis; Barber (1998) *Recruiting Employees*; Rynes, Bretz & Gerhart (1991); Phillips (1998) RJP meta-analysis.
