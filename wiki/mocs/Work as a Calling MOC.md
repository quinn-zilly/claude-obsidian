# Work as a Calling — Map of Content

> A Map of Content (MOC) links into a cluster of related atomic notes. Notes live flat under `wiki/notes/`; this is a navigation hub.

## Why this MOC exists

This cluster serves the **meaning of work** question: when and why do people experience their work as more than a job or career? It organizes the calling literature around its central unsolved problem — the absence of a consensual definition — and the integrative frameworks proposed to resolve it (the calling/vocation distinction; the inner × outer requiredness 2×2 and its transcendent-calling apex).

## Core notes

- [[Work as a Calling]] — the construct, its history, the two definitional camps, and its correlates.
- [[Neoclassical Calling]] — the outside-in camp: destiny, duty, discovery.
- [[Modern Calling]] — the inside-out camp: passion, self-expression, self-fulfillment.
- [[Inner and Outer Requiredness]] — the orthogonal-dimensions framework (Maslow) that reconciles the two camps.
- [[Transcendent Calling]] — the high-inner/high-outer integration (Thompson & Bunderson).
- [[Vocation (Dik & Duffy)]] — the sibling construct: calling minus the external summons.
- [[Presence vs Search for Calling]] — the measurement / developmental-state distinction.
- [[Calling as Person-Job Fit]] — calling's incremental contribution and the discriminant-validity agenda.

## Open questions / frontier

- Does calling survive rigorous discriminant/incremental-validity tests against work importance, job involvement, occupational identification, Protestant work ethic, and duty orientation?
- Does calling predict **objective** (non-self-report) performance, and is it uniquely tied to resilience and rigidity?
- How do inner and outer requiredness take form among the working poor and across non-Western cultures?
- Missing primaries to ingest: Wrzesniewski et al. (1997); Bunderson & Thompson (2009) zookeeper study; Dobrow & Tosti-Kharas (2011); Duffy & Dik (2013) review.
