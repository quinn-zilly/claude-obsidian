# Motivation to Learn

Trainees' **desire to learn the content** of training programs. A central mediator in Noe's model: person characteristics + input + output + consequences + feedback all shape motivation to learn, which in turn drives learning and job performance. Empirically linked to knowledge gained, behavior change, and skill acquisition.

## Drivers

- **Person characteristics** — self-efficacy, awareness of training needs/career goals, attitude toward the content. (A student may grasp material yet learn little if they think the course is career-irrelevant.)
- **Environment** — situational constraints and social support can inhibit even a willing learner (the "loud roommate party" analogy).

## Levers Managers Can Pull

- Communicate the **personal value** of training (skills, career, on-the-job problems) — realistically; unmet expectations hurt.
- Give trainees **choice** of programs and **honor** it. Offering choice but not honoring it *undermines* motivation.
- Boost **self-efficacy**: frame training as improvement (not exposing incompetence), pre-inform about content, show peer success, give control-oriented feedback.

> [!example] Marriott welfare-to-work
> Personal characteristics (unreliable childcare, substance issues) drove non-completion → Marriott added selection standards (childcare, transport, housing) and drug testing.
