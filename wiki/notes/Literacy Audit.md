# Literacy Audit

A structured procedure to determine whether employees have the **basic skills** (reading, writing, computation) a job requires. Used in [[Person Analysis (Noe Model)]] because a diploma or degree does **not** guarantee basic skills, and trainees who lack them can't learn or transfer training.

## Six Steps (Noe, Table 3.4)

1. **Observe** employees to determine the basic skills the job needs (materials used, tasks, reading/writing/computation).
2. **Collect** all written/read materials and identify required computations (bills, memos, inventory lists, requisition sheets).
3. **Interview** employees on the basic skills they believe the job needs; assess the requirements yourself.
4. **Determine** whether employees have those skills; write a per-job description of reading/writing/computation demands.
5. **Develop or buy tests** tied to the job; have employees complete them.
6. **Compare** test results (step 5) to the required-skills description (step 4). A mismatch → a basic-skills problem exists.

Source: U.S. Dept. of Education / U.S. Dept. of Labor, *The Bottom Line: Basic Skills in the Workplace* (1988).

## Remedies When Reading Level Is Too High

Lower the reading level of materials (or use video/OJT); reassign via reading tests; provide remedial training; or redesign the job (costliest, least practical).
