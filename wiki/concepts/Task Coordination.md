# Task Coordination

Task coordination is one of the three behavioral indicators of a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]]: the ability of members to work together smoothly, sequence contributions, and integrate specialized knowledge without breakdown. Where [[Memory Differentiation]] and [[Knowledge Specialization]] describe the *structure* of distributed knowledge, coordination is the *transactive process* that puts that structure to use - directory updating, information allocation, and retrieval coordination.

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] stress that a functional TMS requires both specialization and coordination: knowing who knows what is useless if the group cannot route tasks and retrieve knowledge smoothly. Coordination is the dimension most visibly degraded by [[Member Turnover|membership change]] - when a member leaves, the directory survives but well-rehearsed coordination processes become less efficient.
