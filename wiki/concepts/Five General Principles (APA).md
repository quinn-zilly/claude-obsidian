# Five General Principles (APA)

The **aspirational** layer of the [[APA Ethics Code]] — ideals meant to guide and inspire, explicitly **not** a basis for sanctions (see [[Aspirational vs Enforceable Standards]]).

| Principle | Core commitment |
|---|---|
| **A — Beneficence and Nonmaleficence** | Benefit those you work with; do no harm; guard against misuse of professional influence. |
| **B — Fidelity and Responsibility** | Establish trust; uphold responsibility to society and community; manage conflicts of interest; contribute pro bono. |
| **C — Integrity** | Promote accuracy, honesty, truthfulness; no fraud, theft, or misrepresentation. |
| **D — Justice** | Fairness entitles all persons to access and benefit from psychology; guard against bias and overreach of competence. |
| **E — Respect for People's Rights and Dignity** | Dignity, privacy, confidentiality, self-determination; special safeguards for the vulnerable. |

Principles A (beneficence) and D (justice) map onto the **humanist tradition** that [[Joel Lefkowitz]] argues organizational psychology has under-emphasized relative to economic productivity values.
