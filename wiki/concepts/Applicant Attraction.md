# Applicant Attraction

The central prehire criterion in recruitment research: an applicant's **attitudes and intentions** toward an employment opportunity. [[uggerslev-et-al-2012-recruiting-through-stages|Uggerslev et al. (2012)]] aggregate three highly-correlated indices — **job/organizational attraction, job-pursuit intentions, and acceptance intentions** (meta-analytic r's ≈ .67–.85) — into a single applicant-attraction outcome, kept separate from actual **job-choice behavior**.

## Seven predictor categories (Barber 1998)

Job characteristics · organizational characteristics · [[Recruiter Behaviors|recruiter behaviors]] · recruitment-process characteristics · [[Perceived Fit (Recruitment)|perceived fit]] · perceived alternatives · hiring expectancies.

## Meta-analytic ranking (Uggerslev et al. 2012)

**Relative strength:** [[Perceived Fit (Recruitment)|perceived fit]] (largest, large effect) > organizational characteristics > job characteristics > recruiter/process/hiring-expectancies (medium) > perceived alternatives (**n.s.**). **Incremental variance** (model R² = .50): fit dominates (ΔR² ≈ .40); **recruiter behaviors** next; **job characteristics add ~no unique variance to attraction**.

> [!key-insight] Attraction ≠ job choice
> Fit is the strongest predictor of *attraction* but a **nonsignificant** predictor of actual *job choice*; **job characteristics** carry the most unique variance in job-choice decisions. The predictors that pull people into and through the funnel differ from those that clinch the final decision.
