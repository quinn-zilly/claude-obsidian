# Implicit Followership Theories

Nav: [[index]] | [[Leadership MOC]]

Cognitive structures — schemas — that people hold about the traits and behaviors characterizing followers. The follower-side parallel to [[Implicit Leadership Theories]], advanced by Sy (2010).

## Structure (Sy 2010)

IFTs are represented by a first-order **six-factor** structure — Industry, Enthusiasm, Good Citizen, Conformity, Insubordination, Incompetence — collapsing into a second-order **two-factor** structure:

- **Followership Prototype**: Industry, Enthusiasm, Good Citizen.
- **Followership Antiprototype**: Conformity, Insubordination, Incompetence.

## Leader vs. Follower IFTs

- **LIFTs** (leaders' IFTs): associated with relationship quality, liking, trust, and satisfaction; positive LIFTs → higher performance expectations, liking, and LMX → follower performance (naturally-occurring Pygmalion effects). Leaders with negative state/trait affect endorse more negative LIFTs.
- **FIFTs** (followers' IFTs): less studied, but LIFTs and FIFTs interact to affect relationship quality and follower performance.

Part of the **role-based** strand of [[Followership Theory]] (studying follower schemas as causal antecedents of leader attitudes and outcomes).
