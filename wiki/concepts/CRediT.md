# CRediT (Contributor Roles Taxonomy)

A standardized taxonomy of **14 contributor roles** (e.g., conceptualization, data curation, formal analysis, writing – review & editing), launched in 2014. It moves recognition from the binary notion of *authorship* toward granular *contributorship*.

## Why it matters for open science

CRediT makes the division of scientific labor visible, which:

- supports a shift toward **task specialization** and [[Big Team Science]] — a pillar of [[Open Science 2.0]];
- explicitly recognizes reproducibility-relevant work (data curation, validation) that traditional authorship ignores;
- yields a **dataset for [[Meta-Research]]** — researchers can study the research ecosystem and realign incentives (Larivière et al. 2021).

## Adoption

Dozens of major publishers have adopted it; some (e.g., PLOS) require a CRediT statement at submission. The US National Academy of Sciences' **TACS** (Transparency in Author Contributions in Science) lists journals committing to authorship standards, ORCID requirements, and the CRediT taxonomy.

> [!note] Limitation
> Per [[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0|Thibault et al. (2023)]], "authorship" still overshadows "contributorship" in practice — CRediT is a first step, not a finished reform.
