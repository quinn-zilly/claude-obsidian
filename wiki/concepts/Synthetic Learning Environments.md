# Synthetic Learning Environments

Synthetic learning environments (SLEs) are training contexts that simulate real-world conditions without exposing learners to the risks, costs, or consequences of actual performance settings. They range from low-fidelity role plays to high-fidelity flight simulators and immersive virtual reality environments.

## Key Properties

- **Fidelity**: degree to which the simulation replicates physical, functional, and psychological features of the real task environment
- **Scenario control**: instructors can manipulate difficulty, introduce rare events, and repeat critical scenarios
- **Safe failure**: learners can make mistakes without real-world consequences, supporting experiential learning

## Fidelity Paradox

Higher physical fidelity does not always produce better [[Transfer of Training]]. Psychological fidelity — the degree to which the learner's cognitive and motivational engagement matches the real task — often matters more.
