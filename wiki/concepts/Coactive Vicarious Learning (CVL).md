# Coactive Vicarious Learning (CVL)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Learning & Development MOC]]

**Coactive vicarious learning (CVL)** is *"a discursive learning process where individuals (a model and learner) intentionally share and jointly process a model's work experience(s) in interpersonal interactions to coconstruct an emergent, situated understanding of the experience(s)"* ([[myers-2018-coactive-vicarious-learning|Myers, 2018]]).

It reconceives [[Enactive and Vicarious Learning|vicarious learning]] as **relational and coconstructed** rather than intrapersonal and one-way. The model is an **active cocreator** — engaging the learner across the full experiential-learning cycle — not merely a source of an observable experience.

## Theoretical roots

Integrates **experiential learning** (Kolb — learning as meaning-making by transforming experience) with **symbolic interactionism** (Mead, Blumer — meaning is cocreated and continually reinterpreted through interpersonal interaction). Learning is defined as change in an individual's (or dyad's) **response repertoire**, which need not manifest as performance.

## The three interactional elements

A CVL interaction combines three **multiplicative** discursive components — low levels of any one inhibit learning:

1. **Experience** — exposure to the model's experience (shadowing, apprenticing, storytelling, in-person simulation), with interaction *during/after* rather than passive observation.
2. **Analysis** — probing questions/comments that let both parties evaluate, reinterpret, and compare understanding (enables second-order learning; a learner's question can prompt the model to revise their own interpretation).
3. **Support** — social-emotional statements that build trust/self-efficacy/relational capacity and indirectly enable fuller sharing and analysis.

## Antecedents & consequences

Engagement rises with **relational context** (relationship quality, positive affect, familiarity), **structural context** (proximity, helping/learning role structures, interdependent/low-time-pressure tasks), and **individual context** ([[Goal Orientation|learning goal orientation]], [[Motivation to Learn]], knowledge stocks/absorptive capacity, generalist knowledge). Consequences: increased individual knowledge; individual **capacity** ([[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory]], perspective-taking, self-efficacy); and dyadic **relational capacity** ([[Shared Mental Models]], trust, affective commitment) — which loop back to enable future CVL.

## Why it matters

Purely observational vicarious learning ([[Independent Vicarious Learning (IVL)]]) assumes the lesson is observable or codifiable. In **tacit, ambiguous knowledge work**, the lesson often can't be deduced without discussing the model's reasoning — so CVL adds "substantial nuance and clarity" for how people learn from one another in interdependent, knowledge-intensive organizations.
