# Campbell Model of Job Performance

The **eight-factor latent structure** of individual work performance (Campbell 2012; synthesized in [[campbell-wiernik-2015-modeling-assessment-work-performance|Campbell & Wiernik 2015]]). It is offered as a near-consensus, integrative description of performance **content** — the behaviors people do that advance organizational goals — asserted **invariant across organizational levels, functional specialties, industries, and cultures** (a "universal competency model").

## The Eight Factors

1. **Technical performance** — job-specific proficiency; includes core interpersonal/customer-service tasks. Varies by substantive area and complexity.
2. **Communication** — clear, organized, compelling conveyance of information; independent of subject-matter expertise; oral + written.
3. **Initiative, persistence, and effort** — observable extra effort (extra hours, volunteering, adverse conditions); Frese's "active performance."
4. **[[Counterproductive Work Behavior]] (CWB)** — controllable actions harming unit/org goals; two subfactors (CWB-O, CWB-I).
5. **Hierarchical leadership** — interpersonal influence in a hierarchy; six subfactors (consideration, initiating structure, goal emphasis, empowerment, training/coaching, modeling); *leadership ≠ management*.
6. **Hierarchical management** — generating/preserving/allocating resources; eight subfactors (decision-making, planning, coordination, monitoring, external representation, staffing, administration, commitment/compliance).
7. **Peer/team-member leadership** — Factor-5 behaviors enacted among peers; absorbs OCB personal-support behaviors.
8. **Peer/team-member management** — Factor-6 behaviors in the team context (planning, coordination, external representation, compliance).

## Structural Notes

- **Level of specificity is chosen, not fixed.** Higher-order factors (e.g., citizenship performance) and finer subfactors exist above/below the eight — but aggregating above the eight-factor level *loses information* (peer leadership vs. peer management shouldn't be merged).
- **[[General Factor in Job Performance|General factor]]** exists in the covariance matrix but is **formative** (a weighted composite), not a single reflective latent variable.
- **Compound performance domains** (green performance, IT performance) cut across factors around a shared goal.
- Analogy: like the Big Five in personality — a chosen middle level with higher-order factors and specific facets above and below.

## Practical Implication

Selection, training, appraisal, and reward systems should all align with this same latent structure; any comprehensive performance measure must cover all eight factors at some level of specificity.

## Relation to CORE

In the [[CORE Model of Performance]] (Marshall et al. 2024), this content model occupies the individual-level slice (KSAOs → Roles → Relationships). CORE abstracts above behavioral content; Campbell specifies it.
