# DEI Evaluation

Evaluation practice that integrates diversity, equity, and inclusion principles across all stages of evidence generation — design through dissemination.

## Core Claim

Inclusive evaluation = better quality evidence + better org outcomes (morale, PR, funder support). Diverse teams investigate program strengths and gaps more thoroughly.

## Three-Goal Framework

### 1. Engage Representative Stakeholders
- Periodic team reflection on systemic inequity drivers and personal biases
- Stakeholder feedback incorporated at design, enactment, and dissemination stages
- Participation made accessible: flexible timing (evenings/mornings), community locations

### 2. Ensure Data Quality and Validity
- Sample reflects full group of interest including subgroups
- Document analytic decisions; peer quality-control checks
- Watch for **reference bias** (social norms shaping responses) and **mischievous responders**
- Disaggregate analysis to detect differential program impact across groups

### 3. Communicate Across Differences
- Multiple modalities, formats, languages
- Disaggregated reporting by key characteristics (race, location, age)
- Honor cultural communication norms (e.g., visual/artistic formats for Native communities)

## Relationship to Evaluation Quality

DEI-informed evaluation is not just ethics — it improves validity:
- Unrepresentative samples = systematic bias in conclusions
- Single-language, single-modality surveys → missing data for non-dominant groups
- Colorblind analysis (aggregated only) hides differential effects → programs appear effective when they're not working for subgroups
