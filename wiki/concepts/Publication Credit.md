# Publication Credit

> [!note] Stub
> Seed page created to resolve inbound links from [[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct]]. Expand on next ethics ingest.

**Publication credit** (APA Ethics Code **Standard 8.12**) is the requirement that psychologists take responsibility and credit — including authorship credit — **only in proportion to their actual contribution** to a piece of work. Authorship must reflect substantive scientific or professional contribution, not merely institutional position or status.

Key provisions: principal authorship and other credits accurately reflect relative contributions regardless of relative status; a student is normally listed as principal author on any multiple-authored article substantially based on the student's dissertation. The standard targets **honorary/gift authorship** and the exploitation of junior contributors.

## Placement in the APA Code

- Sits in **Section 8, Research and Publication**, alongside informed consent to research, deception, debriefing, data sharing, and plagiarism.
- Expresses the aspirational principles of **Integrity** and **Justice**.
