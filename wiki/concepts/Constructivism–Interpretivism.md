# Constructivism–Interpretivism

The main alternative to the [[Positivism|"received view"]]. Against positivism's naïve realism, it holds a **relativist** position: multiple, apprehendable, equally valid realities, constructed in the mind of the individual rather than existing as a single external entity. Adopts a **hermeneutical** approach — meaning is hidden and surfaced through deep reflection, stimulated by interactive researcher–participant dialogue. The investigator and the object of investigation **co-construct** findings. Goals are [[Idiographic vs Nomothetic|idiographic]] and [[Etic vs Emic|emic]].

## Lineage

Traced to Kant's *Critique of Pure Reason* — perception derives not only from the senses but from the mind's organizing apparatus, so claims about nature can't be independent of the knower. Dilthey extended this, distinguishing *Naturwissenschaft* (natural science, aiming at explanation, *Erklären*) from *Geisteswissenschaft* (human science, aiming at understanding, *Verstehen*). The constructivist goal is to understand the **lived experience** (*Erlebnis*) from the point of view of those who live it. This paradigm is the primary anchor for **qualitative methods**.

## Stance on the five [[Philosophy of Science Parameters|parameters]]

- **Ontology**: relativism — multiple constructed realities; no single truth to unearth, no outside verification sought.
- **Epistemology**: transactional + subjectivist — meaning is captured only through dynamic researcher–participant interaction.
- **Axiology**: values bracketed, not eliminated; the researcher's own experience can be a resource for rapport and dialogue.
- **Rhetoric**: first-person, personalized; reflective journals, emotional impact disclosed.
- **Methodology**: naturalistic designs — lengthy in-depth interviews, theoretical sampling, evolving interview protocols.

Compare the more politically-engaged [[Critical–Ideological Paradigm]], which shares constructivism's constructed-reality stance but adds power and emancipation. [[Grounded Theory]] (in its Charmaz/Rennie form) is the canonical constructivist method in counseling psychology.
