# Relationships Motivation Theory (RMT)

Sixth and newest mini-theory of [[Self-Determination Theory]] (Deci & Ryan 2014). Extends SDT to close interpersonal relationships, arguing that high-quality relationships require not just [[Relatedness (SDT)]] satisfaction but also satisfaction of [[Autonomy (SDT)]] and [[Competence (SDT)]] within the relationship.

## Core Claims

- High-quality relationships are characterized by mutual autonomy support and need satisfaction
- Relationships in which one partner suppresses authentic self → lower well-being even with high closeness
- Both partners' need satisfaction within the relationship predicts relationship quality and individual well-being
- Relatedness alone is insufficient; autonomy within the relationship is crucial

## Empirical Status

**As of Ryan et al. (2022): zero meta-analyses exist for RMT** — identified as a major empirical gap in the SDT literature. The theory has individual empirical studies but lacks the meta-analytic consolidation achieved by other SDT mini-theories.

## Distinction from Relatedness Need

[[Relatedness (SDT)]] is a basic need (feeling connected to others). RMT is a theory about the conditions that make relationships themselves high-quality and need-satisfying — including the presence of autonomy support and authentic self-expression within the relationship.
