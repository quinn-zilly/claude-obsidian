# Cultural Fit (IT-culture)

## Definition

The degree of **congruence between a social group's values and the values embedded in a given information system**. High fit → favorable perceptions, adoption, and use; low fit → resistance, distortion, or non-adoption. It emerged across Leidner & Kayworth's review (2006) as the single most prominent construct linking culture to IT adoption and outcomes.

## Evidence cited in the review

- **Robey & Rodriguez-Diaz (1989)** — a US accounting IS deployed to Panama and Chile succeeded more in Panama, whose values fit the US headquarters more closely.
- **Cabrera et al. (2001)** — successful assimilation requires *either* the technology to fit the culture *or* the culture to be reshaped to the technology.
- **Loch et al. (2003)** — value similarity in technology between adopting and host countries predicts adoption level.
- **Robbins (2000)** — better integration of two subgroup cultures yielded better technology-performance outcomes.

## Relation to conflict

Fit is the inverse framing of [[IT-Culture Conflict|system conflict]]: low fit *is* high system conflict. Leidner & Kayworth note a limitation — prior fit work examined only specific applications, not the more general [[IT Culture|IT values]] a group holds. Compare the broader [[Person–Environment Fit]] concept.
