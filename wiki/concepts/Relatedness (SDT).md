# Relatedness (SDT)

One of three [[Basic Psychological Needs (SDT)]] in [[Self-Determination Theory]].

**Definition**: Need to feel belongingness and connectedness with others; to feel cared for. Roots in Baumeister & Leary (1995) need to belong; Reis (1994).

## Role in Intrinsic Motivation

Serves as **distal background condition** rather than primary driver. Secure attachment → more exploratory behavior (Frodi, Bridges & Grolnick 1985 — infant studies). Cold/uncaring teachers → ↓ intrinsic motivation (Ryan & Grolnick 1986). Ignored children in task context → very low intrinsic motivation (Anderson, Manoogian & Reznick 1976).

Note: Many intrinsically motivated behaviors occur in isolation — proximal relational support not always necessary, but a secure relational base matters.

## Role in Internalization

**Primary driver of initial internalization**. Extrinsically motivated behaviors are not typically interesting; people initially perform them because behaviors are valued by significant others they feel (or want to feel) connected to. Internalization more likely when ambient relatedness supports are present.

Ryan, Stiller & Lynch (1994): children who most fully internalized positive school-related behaviors were those who felt securely connected to and cared for by parents and teachers.

## Applied Contexts

- Nursing homes: autonomy + relatedness supports → greater well-being among residents (V. Kasser & Ryan, in press at 2000)
- Workplace: satisfaction of all three needs predicts performance + well-being (Baard, Deci & Ryan 1998)
