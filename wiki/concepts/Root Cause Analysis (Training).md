# Root Cause Analysis (Training)

The process of determining whether training is the **best or most likely solution** to a performance problem or gap. Central to needs assessment because pressure points that look like training needs often aren't. See [[Person Analysis (Noe Model)]].

## Four Methods (Noe)

1. **Seven questions** — is the problem important/costly? Do employees know how to perform (person)? Can they demonstrate it, or was training unused (input)? Were expectations clear / obstacles present (input)? Were positive consequences offered? Was feedback timely/specific? Were non-training solutions too expensive? → If only knowledge/skill is missing and everything else is fine, **train**. Otherwise, training likely isn't the fix.
2. **5 Whys** — repeatedly ask "why?" to trace root cause; check each cause for a knowledge/skill gap vs. other factors.
3. **Fishbone diagram** — structured brainstorming; considers machines, methods, materials, measurements, environment, people.
4. **4 Square method** — square split into motivation, resources, training, bad job fit; list contributing factors per theme. Reveals whether training alone is the issue.

## Critical-Issue Alignment

Training should be top priority when a **critical job issue**, **critical process issue**, and **critical business issue** are related — the training then has greater business impact and management support (Table 3.5, Rummler & Morrill "Results Chain").

> [!example] Papa Murphy's
> Root cause analysis → need for small, as-needed chunks → four-tier learning approach (new employee → shift supervisor → store manager → franchise owner).
