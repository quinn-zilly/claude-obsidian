# Threats to Validity

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

Specific, recurring reasons an inference may be partly or completely wrong. Each of the four [[Validity Typology|validity types]] carries its own threat list. Threat lists are **heuristic aids** — identified partly conceptually, partly from critical commentary on past experiments — not fixed or universally relevant.

## The three-question test

A threat is worth taking seriously only if:

1. **How** would it apply in this specific case?
2. Is it **plausible**, not merely possible?
3. Does it operate in the **same direction** as the observed effect (so it could explain the finding)?

## Ruling out: design over statistics

SCC's primary tool is **design control** (e.g. random assignment) that minimizes the number and plausibility of remaining threats — preferred over statistical adjustment, which requires full knowledge of how a threat operates and perfect measurement of it. Most studies use a mix, tilted toward design. Ruling out threats is a **falsificationist** enterprise, and inherits falsificationism's limits.

## The four threat lists

| Type | # | Threats |
|---|---|---|
| [[Statistical Conclusion Validity]] | 9 | power, violated assumptions, fishing, unreliable measures, restriction of range, unreliable implementation, extraneous variance, heterogeneity, inaccurate ES |
| [[Internal Validity]] | 9 | ambiguous precedence, selection, history, maturation, regression, attrition, testing, instrumentation, additive/interactive |
| [[Construct Validity (Causal Generalization)]] | 14 | inadequate explication, construct confounding, mono-operation, mono-method, confounding levels, treatment-sensitive factor structure, reactive self-report, reactivity, experimenter expectancies, novelty/disruption, compensatory equalization, compensatory rivalry, resentful demoralization, diffusion |
| [[External Validity]] | 5 | interactions with units / treatments / outcomes / settings, context-dependent mediation |
