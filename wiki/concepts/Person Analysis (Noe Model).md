# Person Analysis (Noe Model)

The leg of [[Training Needs Analysis]] that identifies **who** needs training and **whether** the performance gap is even a training issue. Often called a **gap analysis**: determining what is responsible for the difference between employees' current and expected performance.

## Three Jobs

1. Is the deficiency a lack of knowledge/skill/ability (training) or a motivation/work-design problem?
2. Who specifically needs training?
3. Are those employees **ready**? → [[Readiness for Training]]

## The Five Performance/Learning Factors

Noe's core model: person characteristics + **input, output, consequences, feedback** together determine performance and [[Motivation to Learn]].

| Factor | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Person characteristics** | Knowledge, skill, ability, attitudes; basic skills, cognitive ability, reading level, self-efficacy, awareness of needs, age/generation |
| **Input** | Instructions (what/how/when) + resources (equipment, time, budget); situational constraints; social support; opportunity to perform |
| **Output** | Performance standards — the level employees are expected to reach |
| **Consequences** | Incentives, rewards, and work-group norms for performing |
| **Feedback** | Information employees receive while performing — must be frequent, specific, detailed |

Interviews or questionnaires measure all five (e.g., package-delivery company's lead-driver coaching study: 14 lead drivers + 6 supervisors interviewed; obstacles clustered into lack of time, no privacy, reluctance to coach peers → became course content).

> [!key-insight] Only the knowledge/skill gap is fixable by training
> If input, output, consequences, or feedback are inadequate, training is likely the **wrong** solution. Faulty equipment → repair it; missing feedback → train the *manager* to give feedback, not the employee.
