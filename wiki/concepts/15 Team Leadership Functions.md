# 15 Team Leadership Functions

The taxonomy of team leadership behaviors derived by [[morgeson-et-al-2010-leadership-in-teams-functional|Morgeson, DeRue & Karam (2010)]] from **517 behavioral items** (inter-coder agreement 76% → 100%), organized by [[Functional Team Leadership Theory|team performance phase]]. Operationalized as the **Team Leadership Questionnaire (TLQ)**.

## Transition-phase functions (7)

1. **Compose team** — select members and configure the team.
2. **Define mission** — articulate purpose and direction.
3. **Establish expectations and goals** — set standards and objectives.
4. **Structure and plan** — organize work, roles, and process.
5. **Train and develop team** — build member and collective capability.
6. **Sensemaking** — interpret internal/external events for the team.
7. **Provide feedback** — give performance-relevant information.

## Action-phase functions (8)

8. **Monitor team** — track progress and performance.
9. **Manage team boundaries** — buffer and broker with outside groups.
10. **Challenge team** — push for higher performance / reconsider approaches.
11. **Perform team task** — contribute directly to the work.
12. **Solve problems** — diagnose and resolve obstacles.
13. **Provide resources** — secure what the team needs.
14. **Encourage team self-management** — build autonomous regulation.
15. **Support social climate** — maintain cohesion and interpersonal health.

> [!info] Source positioning
> Each function is rated for how well each [[Sources of Team Leadership (2x2)|source]] can perform it — good (+), better (++), best (+++). No single source is best-positioned for all 15; e.g., *manage team boundaries* favors external sources, *encourage self-management* favors internal ones.
