# Transfer of Training

Transfer of training refers to the degree to which knowledge, skills, and attitudes learned in a training context are applied and maintained on the job. It is the primary criterion for evaluating whether workplace training is effective.

## Types of Transfer

- **Near transfer**: applying learned skills to situations very similar to the training context
- **Far transfer**: applying learned principles to novel or dissimilar situations
- **Positive transfer**: training facilitates performance in the transfer context
- **Negative transfer**: training interferes with performance (e.g., habits from one context that are maladaptive in another)

## Transfer Problem

Research consistently shows that a large proportion of training investment does not transfer to job performance. Factors affecting transfer include:
- Similarity between training and job conditions
- Learner motivation and self-efficacy
- Supervisory support and organizational climate for transfer
- Opportunities to practice on the job
