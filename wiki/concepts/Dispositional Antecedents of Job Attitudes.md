# Dispositional Antecedents of Job Attitudes

The role of personality in job satisfaction was recognized earliest (Hoppock 1935) but fell out of favor in the 1970s–1980s. Revival came from three landmark studies.

## Landmark Revival Studies

**Staw & Ross (1985):** Job satisfaction is reasonably stable across time, even when changing employers or occupations. *Criticism:* didn't measure dispositions directly.

**Staw et al. (1986):** Affective disposition at ages 12–14 correlated **r = .34** (p < .05) with overall job satisfaction at ages 54–62. Unique longitudinal dataset with childhood personality ratings.

**Arvey et al. (1989):** Significant consistency in job satisfaction between 34 pairs of monozygotic twins reared apart. Establishes genetic component.

## Specific Dispositional Predictors

**Positive and Negative Affectivity (PA/NA):** Dispositional PA/NA predicts satisfaction over months even after controlling for job changes and occupational quality (Watson & Slack 1993).

**Big Five (Judge et al. 2002 meta-analysis):**
| Trait | rc with Job Satisfaction |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Neuroticism | −.29 |
| Extraversion | +.25 |
| Conscientiousness | +.26 |
| Agreeableness | (weaker) |
| Openness | (weaker) |

**[[Core Self-Evaluations]] (CSEs):** Most consistent predictor. Judge et al. (2008): when all three frameworks (Big Five, PA/NA, CSEs) compared, only CSEs/neuroticism factor **consistently** predicted satisfaction across studies. CSEs + neuroticism, extraversion, and conscientiousness together explain 36% of variance in self-reported satisfaction.

## State–Trait Integration

The disposition vs. situation debate is poorly posed — the real question is how situations contribute to trait expression and how traits contribute to reactions to situations (Magnusson 1999; Mischel & Shoda 1998).

Interactionist model: dispositions affect behavior *through* dispositionally-influenced perceptions of environments. High-CSE individuals:
1. Perceive the same environment more favorably
2. Actually select into better environments (objective job complexity mediates)
3. Set more self-concordant goals → higher attainment and satisfaction
