# Personality Dynamics (TAT)

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

Personality dynamics = "theory-based, testable processes mediating changes in an individual's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors in response to internal and external demands" (Tett & Fisher 2020). TAT extends from static trait–situation interactions to within-person variability over time.

## The 5×5 Matrix

Tett et al. (2021) apply **five TAT issues** (columns) to **five personality dynamics research lines** (rows). Each cell identifies where TAT can advance that domain.

### TAT Issues (Columns)
1. **Cross-situational consistency (CSC) vs. variability (CSV)** — behavior consistent only where situations are similarly trait-relevant
2. **Behavior/performance distinction** — trait expression ≠ performance; observers convert behavior to rating
3. **Interpersonal compatibility** — mutual trait activation = "chemistry"; people bring out each other's best/worst
4. **Dynamic interaction** — positive feedback loops (trait expression increases trait-relevant cues) and negative (decreases cues)
5. **Three-part motivational system** — intrinsic need satisfaction, knowledge of results, differential extrinsic rewards

### Research Lines (Rows)

#### 1. Dispositional Change
Traits targeting change: neuroticism (negative emotion volatility), adaptability, self-monitoring, functional flexibility, proactive personality, charisma.

- CSC/CSV: surface variability masks deep dispositional consistency in change-related traits
- Behavior/performance: change traits rely heavily on feedback (self-monitoring, flexibility)
- Compatibility: change-based traits generate especially dynamic interpersonal effects (two neurotics escalate; self-monitor feeds on every new interaction)
- Dynamic interaction: change traits especially generative of feedback loops; proactive workers → invite more change cues
- Motivation: change traits more motivating in dynamic jobs/cultures where cues for change are abundant

#### 2. Person–Situation Interactions
Core TAT domain. Key extension for dynamics:

- Tracking CSV requires tracking *changes* in situation trait relevance over time
- **Within-job bidirectionality**: agreeable leader helps a deserving member (+) then accommodates a loafer (−); same behavior, different demands, opposite value
- Team chemistry: member traits must align with teamwork, team task, team role, AND teammates' traits (mutual activation)
- **Cross-trait activation chains**: conscientiousness → high performance → recognition → demand for extraversion (social obligations); TAT chains extend between traits
- Need-based motivation: dynamic situational flux = competing needs (achievement vs. affiliation in promotion competition)

#### 3. Personality States
Brief thoughts/feelings giving rise to behavior; Fleeson's (2012) whole-trait theory: states are density distributions of underlying dispositions.

- States are proximate mediators of short-term trait effects; underlying traits set stochastic limits on state variability
- TAT taxonomy applies to momentary cues: momentary demands, distracters, constraints, releasers, etc.
- Bidirectionality applies to states: momentary performance judgments similarly structured
- States offer agility for moment-to-moment interpersonal variability that trait dispositions lack
- **Emotional contagion** = positive feedback loop in neuroticism/emotional stability activation
- States more affectively loaded than traits → motivational processes parallel to traits but shorter timescales

#### 4. Personality and Career Development
Timescale: years, decades, lifespans.

- CSC/CSV: social investment principle (Roberts et al. 2005) — aging norms drive population shifts in conscientiousness, dominance, emotional stability, agreeableness; habitual trait activation may cause these shifts
- Behavior/performance: trait effects shift across career/employment stages (Thoresen et al. 2004: openness/agreeableness → early transition, conscientiousness → maintenance stage)
- **Peter principle**: promotion turns strengths into liabilities — job A demands ≠ job B demands; demands in one = distracters in the other
- **"People make the place"** (Schneider 1987): trait expressions → organizational culture → further trait-relevant cues → dynamic interaction at org level
- Motivation: person–workplace fit as the long-term motivational target; RIASEC + TAT + ASA all frame career motivation via fit

#### 5. Self-Regulation (SR)
SR = attaining and maintaining goals; motivation from current–ideal-self discrepancy.

- TAT adds motivational rationale for *why* traits engage in SR: need satisfaction explains impulsivity, not just its interference with goal pursuit
- Both SR and TAT rely on behavior/performance distinction (differently: SR = goal attainment; TAT = demand-meeting)
- Interpersonal compatibility rarely studied in SR; TAT well-positioned to advance this
- Feedback loops: SR negative loop (goal–performance gap narrows → motivation decreases) ≠ TAT loops (situational cues change) — but compatible; dispositions can promote or inhibit further trait expression within SR pathways
- **Affect = common denominator**: SR motivation (approach/avoidance from gap reduction) and TAT motivation (need satisfaction pleasure) both reduce to affect; hybrid SR–TAT motivational system is feasible
