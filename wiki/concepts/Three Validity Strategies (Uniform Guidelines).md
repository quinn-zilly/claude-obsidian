# Three Validity Strategies (Uniform Guidelines)

The [[EEOC-1978-uniform-guidelines-employee-selection|Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection (1978)]] (§5, §14) accept three strategies to defend a selection procedure that has [[Adverse Impact]]. Every study must rest on a **review of job information** — a **job analysis** (§14A) — except criterion-related may skip full JA per §14B(3).

## 1. Criterion-Related Validity (§14B)
Empirical data showing the procedure **predicts / significantly correlates** with important elements of job performance. Requires: JA of the criterion, representative sample (incl. races/sexes/ethnic groups available), statistical significance p ≤ .05, fairness study where feasible, guards vs capitalization on chance (cross-validation).

## 2. Content Validity (§14C)
Procedure is a **representative sample of the job's content**. Requires JA of critical/important work behaviors. **Not appropriate** for measuring traits/constructs (intelligence, aptitude, personality, judgment, leadership) or for KSAs learned on the job in a brief orientation.

## 3. Construct Validity (§14D)
Procedure measures an identifiable **construct** shown important to performance. Most complex/arduous; requires JA that names/defines the construct + criterion-related evidence linking construct → performance.

## Guardrails
- **No assumption of validity** (§9): reputation, promo literature, usage frequency, testimonials never substitute for empirical evidence.
- **Transportability** (§7): may borrow another user's criterion-related study if validity + job similarity (same major work behaviors, shown by JA) + fairness are met.
- **Method of use** (§5G): ranking with greater adverse impact needs stronger evidence than pass/fail.

These map onto the psychometric [[Validity Typology]] but are the *legal-operational* version; note critiques of their scientific currency (validity generalization / unitary construct validity — cf. [[binning-barrett-1989-validity-personnel-decisions]], [[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection]]).
