# Pro-Management Bias

The tendency of I/O psychology to reflexively adopt the **managerial perspective** when management and worker interests diverge. Katzell & Austin (1992): "I-O psychology is still oriented overwhelmingly to the needs of management." Baritz (1960) named it in *The Servants of Power*.

[[Joel Lefkowitz|Lefkowitz]] (2008) gives two illustrations:

- **Wrongful discharge / employment-at-will (EAW)** — the field frames the *adverse* consequences of wrongful discharge as damage awards and lowered morale *from litigation*, rather than treating the aggrieved worker's legal recovery as a positive outcome or blaming the wrongful discharge itself.
- **Strategic HRM (SHRM)** — HR has shifted from representing employee interests to maximizing corporate financial objectives; even ethics gets "strategically co-opted" as a source of competitive advantage.

The remedy is explicit **employee advocacy** as a third pillar of professional identity — the humanist component of the [[Scientist-Practitioner-Humanist (S-P-H) Model]].
