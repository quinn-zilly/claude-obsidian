# Sensitization Perspective (Gender Role View)

A theory of **[[Work–Family Conflict]]** (Duxbury & Higgins 1991; Gutek et al. 1991) resting on **differential values** men and women place on work vs family roles due to socialization.

## Prediction
People are **more perceptible to** — more likely to notice, recall, and report — conflict intruding on their *more valued* domain (identity theory; Stryker 1968):
- Men value work more → higher **work salience** → report more **FIW** (intrusions into the valued work role).
- Women value family more → higher **family salience** → report more **WIF** (intrusions into the valued family role).

It is a theory about **subjective perception/labeling**, not objective events.

## Verdict in Shockley et al. (2017)
**Not supported** for either WIF or FIW. Work and family **salience did not mediate** the gender relationships. Directly loses in a head-to-head against the supported **[[Rational View (Work–Family)]]** (time, not values, does the work).
