# Idiosyncratic Deals (i-deals)

**Idiosyncratic deals (i-deals)** are individualized work arrangements negotiated between an employee and employer, usually at the employee's own request, that differentiate that employee from coworkers (Rousseau et al. 2006). They span financial i-deals (individualized pay) and non-financial ones (e.g. flexibility arrangements).

In the [[Total Rewards|total rewards]] context ([[fulmer-li-2022-compensation-benefits-total-rewards|Fulmer & Li 2022]]), i-deals are a form of **individualization** — a shift away from one-size-fits-all systems. They raise two concerns:

- **Peer reactions** — other employees respond to individual exceptions (Abdulsalam et al. 2021).
- **Equity/gap risk** — if i-deal access is systematically related to gender or race (some groups more likely to request or more effective in receiving them), i-deals can contribute to **total rewards gaps**.

Customized total rewards may nonetheless help organizations optimize incentive and sorting effects across a heterogeneous workforce.
