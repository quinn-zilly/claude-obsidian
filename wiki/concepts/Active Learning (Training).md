# Active Learning (Training)

## Definition

Training approach treating learner as active participant who self-regulates exploration, effort, and strategy — contrasted with behavioral/passive paradigm where trainee receives structured content.

## Origins and Rationale

Passive → active shift driven by:
1. Work complexity: behavioral expertise (routine) → adaptive expertise (complex, dynamic) requires flexible skills
2. Technology proliferation: e-learning gives learners unprecedented control
3. Cognitive science: metacognition, knowledge structures, self-regulation all matter for complex skill acquisition

## Core Active Learning Interventions (JAP evidence)

| Intervention | Mechanism | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Guided exploration** (Debowski et al. 2001) | Structured goal-directed search during learning | Better self-regulation, info acquisition |
| **Error management training** (Keith & Frese 2005, 2008) | Encourage errors, teach positive error framing + emotion control | Meta-analysis d = positive; interacts with ability and Openness |
| **Mastery goal framing** | Instructions + situational cues promoting learning orientation | Better transfer vs. performance framing |
| **Exploratory learning** | Self-directed discovery within constrained environment | Good for complex skills; harmful without scaffolding for low-ability |
| **Structured reflection** (DeRue et al. 2012) | Post-experience deliberate review | Enhances developmental value of experiences |

## Moderators

- **Cognitive ability**: high-ability trainees tolerate more unguided exploration; low-ability need more structure
- **Goal orientation**: learning orientation → adaptive for high ability; performance orientation effects depend on ability and outcome
- **Self-efficacy**: complex interplay — within-person, high self-efficacy can *reduce* cognitive resource allocation → worse performance
- **Openness to Experience**: benefits more from error training

## Design Implications

- Decompose active learning into instructional, motivational, emotional design elements (each with different timing)
- Emotion control strategies: timing (phased in vs. out) still debated
- Metacognition well-studied; mental models (coherence, breadth) understudied
- Support active learning without eliminating all structure (especially for low-ability)

## Open Questions (Bell et al. 2017)

- What are precise mechanisms? Metacognition well-mapped; mental model development less so
- Optimal sequencing of guidance → autonomy?
- How does active learning interact with informal and technology-based learning?
