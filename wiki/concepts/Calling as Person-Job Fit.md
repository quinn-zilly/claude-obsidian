# Calling as Person-Job Fit

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

[[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson's (2019)]] answer to the **question of differentiation** — how calling adds unique value beyond the many related OP/OB constructs (a field criticized for construct redundancy). Their move: once calling is conceptualized as a [[Transcendent Calling|transcendent calling]] (a perceived match between [[Inner and Outer Requiredness|inner and outer requiredness]] plus the resulting sense of destiny), it can be understood as a **strong form of person–job fit**.

But it is a distinctive kind of fit. Standard person–job fit (e.g., Cable & DeRue 2002) is the perception that job requirements match one's competencies and expectations. **Calling-as-fit** is instead a deep conviction that one's work matches **core passions (inner requiredness)** with **needs in the world (outer requiredness)**. Framing calling this way both locates it within an existing research stream and clarifies its incremental contribution.

The authors note this is only a start: the literature has largely sidestepped **discriminant validity** (few EFA/CFA studies separating calling from neighbors like work importance, job involvement, occupational identification, Protestant work ethic, duty orientation) and **incremental validity** (only a handful of studies control for related constructs — engagement, job involvement, ability, core self-evaluation — though in those, calling's effect survived). This connects the calling literature to the vault's broader construct-validity and [[Criterion Problem|criterion]] spine.
