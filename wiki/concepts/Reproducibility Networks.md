# Reproducibility Networks

National consortiums of researchers distributed across universities who coordinate **bottom-up** to drive rigorous, transparent research — working with policymakers in institutions, government, and funders.

## Significance

A leading example of the **research culture** front of open science (per [[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0|Thibault et al. 2023]]). They address the gap between ideals, formal policies, and actual researcher behavior. First launched in the UK ([[UK Reproducibility Network]]), they now exist in over a dozen countries.

The UKRN's coordinated voice secured a **£4.5M strategic investment from Research England** to roll out open science training across 18 institutions — a concrete instance of merging bottom-up enthusiasm with top-down funding, which Thibault et al. argue is what real change requires.

Related cultural infrastructure: the [[Center for Open Science]], DORA (Declaration on Research Assessment), NASA's Transform to Open Science (TOPS), and the Association for Interdisciplinary Meta-research and Open Science (AIMOS). See also [[Meta-Research]].
