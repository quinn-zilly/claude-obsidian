# Job Demands-Resources Model

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work Design (Concept)]] | [[Job Demands-Control Model]]

Model of well-being and motivation at work (Demerouti, Bakker, Nachreiner & Schaufeli, 2001). One of five historical work design clusters identified in [[parker-morgeson-johns-2017-100-years-work-design|Parker et al. (2017)]] (the pink cluster, ~65 terms).

## Core Idea

Every job has **demands** (physical/psychological costs — workload, time pressure, emotional demands, role overload) and **resources** (aspects that aid goal attainment, reduce demands, or foster growth — autonomy, support, feedback, rewards, security).

**Dual-path proposition:**

- Demands → strain/exhaustion → **burnout** (health-impairment path)
- Resources → engagement → **performance** (motivational path)

## Two Advances over Job Demands-Control

JD-R generalized [[Job Demands-Control Model|Karasek's (1979)]] model in two ways:

1. **Beyond control.** Resources are not limited to control/support — rewards, security, and many other features can buffer demands and stimulate growth.
2. **Both strain and motivation.** Explicitly incorporates a motivational path (engagement), not just a strain path — recognizing the need for multiple criteria when evaluating work design (Johns, 2010).

## Evidence & Open Questions

Many predictions supported (Schaufeli & Taris, 2014); extended to domains like employee safety (Nahrgang, Morgeson & Hofmann, 2011). But:

- Predicted **demands × resources interactions** remain elusive.
- Are demands and resources conceptually distinct, or do poor resources just constitute a demand?
- Are **engagement and burnout** distinct constructs or opposite ends of one continuum (Cole et al., 2012)?

## Related Distinctions

- **Challenge vs hindrance demands** (Cavanaugh et al., 2000): challenge demands (workload, responsibility) can aid development; hindrance demands (politics, role ambiguity/conflict) constrain it. Both relate to burnout, but hindrance effects are stronger (Crawford et al., 2010). See [[Role Theory (Work Design)]].
- Distinct from [[Job Demands-Control Model]] by its focus on engagement outcomes (absorption, dedication, vigor) alongside burnout.
