# Bias Intervention Taxonomy

A taxonomy of workplace bias-reduction interventions organized by the **[[Tripartite Model of Attitudes|attitude dimension]]** they primarily target ([[costa-2024-discrimination-interventions|Costa, 2024]]). Three broad classes emerged as significant for discriminatory (behavioral) outcomes:

## Classes

- **Disrupt stereotype processing** (cognitive) — e.g., structured evaluation (*g* = 0.51), majority signaling by targets / "whitening" résumés (*g* = 0.44).
- **Update affective states** (affective) — e.g., imagined contact (*g* = 0.47), multicultural experience (*g* = 0.46).
- **Inhibit bias manifestation** (behavioral) — directly blocks biased action; the **most effective class**. E.g., **making individuals accountable** (*g* = 0.80), **intervening on social norms** (*g* = 0.58).

## Headline Result

> [!key-insight] Passive out, behavioral in
> **Passive interventions** — short-term education, reminders that bias exists — are **largely ineffective** at changing behavior. Interventions that **inhibit the manifestation of bias** (accountability, social-norm change) are the strongest lever for reducing workplace discrimination. Overall workplace effect is modest: *g* ≈ 0.27.

Complements the framing debate in [[Multicultural vs. Colorblind Approaches]] and the ideology–outcome map in [[Diversity Ideologies]]: *how* you intervene (mechanism) matters as much as *what ideology* the organization holds.
