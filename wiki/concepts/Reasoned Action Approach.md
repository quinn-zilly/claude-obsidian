# Reasoned Action Approach

An integrative framework by Fishbein and Ajzen (2011) synthesizing the Theory of Reasoned Action (1980) and Theory of Planned Behaviour (1991). The most influential model of individual determinants of behaviour in social psychology.

## Core Structure

**Beliefs about outcomes** → **Behavioural attitudes** (evaluations of the behaviour) → **Intention** → **Behaviour**

**Normative beliefs** (what important others think) → **Subjective/injunctive norms** → **Intention**

**Control beliefs** (about ease/difficulty) → **Perceived behavioural control** ↔ **Behavioural skills** → **Intention/Behaviour**

## Key Constructs

- **Knowledge**: facts about an object, held with certainty; often confused with beliefs
- **Beliefs**: subjective probability that a behaviour leads to an outcome; weighted by evaluation of that outcome → indirect attitude measure
- **General attitudes**: evaluations of objects (attitude-towards-target)
- **Behavioural attitudes**: evaluations of the *behaviour itself* (attitude-towards-behaviour); stronger predictor than general attitudes
- **Injunctive norms**: perceived approval from important others
- **Perceived behavioural control / self-efficacy**: confidence in ability to perform the behaviour; related to [[Habits (Behaviour)]] and behavioural skills

## Critique from Albarracín et al. 2024

The RAA assumes individual determinants are primary. While it acknowledges social norms, they are filtered through individual perception. The 2024 review shows that *social-structural* interventions (access, social support) have larger effects than any individual-level target the RAA emphasizes. Access especially is structurally outside the RAA model.
