# Role Congruity Theory

Eagly & Karau's account (extending Eagly, Makhijani & Klonsky 1992; Heilman's "lack of fit"): prejudice against women arises from **incongruity between the female gender role and the requirements of male-typed roles** (especially leadership).

Two levels of bias in male-typed settings:
1. Women are viewed as **having less** of the male-typed ability (e.g., less agentic leadership ability).
2. Women who **do display** male-typed behavior are viewed as **less effective / less likeable** than men displaying the same behavior — and face **backlash** as threats to the status quo (Rudman et al. 2012).

## Role in Joshi et al. (2015)
Grounds Hypothesis 1: the **proportion of men** in an occupation signals "fit," driving stereotypic expectations that enhance sex differences in **both** performance evaluations and rewards. Even high-performing women in male-dominated occupations have their contributions **discounted and undervalued** (the Ann Hopkins example). Explains why women in high-status/complex jobs are treated as **atypical** and face reward penalties.
