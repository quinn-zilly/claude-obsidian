# Good Judgment Project

Large-scale calibration training and forecasting tournament sponsored by IARPA (Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Agency). Landmark applied decision science intervention.

## Design

- Thousands of participants, many with substantive expertise
- Hundreds of geopolitical forecasting events
- Scoring-rule feedback distinguishing **knowledge**, **resolution**, and **calibration** separately
- Variants compared (training types, team interaction, feedback formats)
- Built on Lichtenstein & Fischhoff 1980: earlier calibration training generalized beyond training tasks

## Key Results

1. **Brief, intense training + scoring-rule feedback → sustained improvement** in calibration
2. **Remote team interaction** helped somewhat
3. **Individual differences stable** → **superforecasters** identified: ~top 2%, stable, can be recruited deliberately
4. Self-selected participants already better calibrated than typical lab participants (selection effect)
5. Score decomposition useful: some participants have good knowledge but poor calibration; others reverse

## Superforecasters

Stable individual difference (not task artifact). Properties: actively open-minded thinking, calibration habits, willingness to update. Philip Tetlock's subsequent book popularized the concept.

## Connection to Intelligence Community

- IARPA sponsorship = CIA/intelligence community trying to improve analyst calibration
- Connects to Heuer (1999, veteran CIA analyst) who introduced decision science to CIA training
- National Academy of Sciences 2011 consensus report on applying decision science to intelligence analysis
- Lanir & Kahneman 2006: analysis of Israel's vulnerability to 1973 surprise attack → decision science entry into intelligence
