# Opt-Out Revolution

The popular narrative (Belkin 2003) that high-achieving women voluntarily **leave careers for family** — framing women's underrepresentation as a matter of individual **choice/preference**.

[[mainiero-sullivan-2005-kaleidoscope-careers|Mainiero & Sullivan (2005)]] offered the **[[Kaleidoscope Career Model]]** as an alternative explanation, arguing women's departures reflect shifting authenticity/balance/challenge parameters rather than simple opt-out.

**[[kossek-su-wu-2017-opting-out-pushed-out|Kossek, Su & Wu (2017)]]** reframe the debate: "opting out" (pulls) and being **"pushed out"** (organizational bias, overwork, stigma) are **coexisting, interconnected** forces — apparent "choices" are shaped by context. See **[[Opting Out vs Pushed Out]]**.
