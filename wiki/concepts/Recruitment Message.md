# Recruitment Message

The **content and framing of information** an employer conveys about an opening. Three dimensions shape its effect (Breaugh 2013): **amount**, **specificity**, and **realism** of the information conveyed.

## What works

- **Specific, detailed** messages raise perceived [[Perceived Fit (Recruitment)|fit]] versus general ones (Roberson et al. 2005).
- **Realism** — two-sided, credible messages (the [[Realistic Job Preview|RJP]] lineage) build trust and screen misfits.
- **Web-site features** — navigability, aesthetics, positive/credible content, **employee testimonials** (richer media > text), and **awards** improve attractiveness and culture perceptions (Cober et al. 2004; Walker et al. 2009; Braddy et al. 2006).
- **P-O fit feedback** embedded in the message deters unqualified applicants (Dineen et al. 2002, 2007).
- **Wording signals belonging** — **gendered wording** in ads (Gaucher et al. 2011) lowers women's interest in male-typed jobs, not via perceived skill but via a sense of not belonging.

## Theory hook

Message effects run through **[[Elaboration Likelihood Model|ELM]]** and **[[Signaling Theory (Recruitment)|signaling]]**: detailed job/org content invites central-route scrutiny (high EL) while surface features act as peripheral cues early on.
