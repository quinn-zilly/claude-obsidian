# Idiographic vs Nomothetic

A pair of terms defining the **focus of inquiry** (Hood & Johnson 1997).

- **Idiographic** (Greek *idios*, "applying to the individual") — focuses on understanding the individual as a unique, complex entity. Writing is descriptive and detailed (e.g., a biography or case study).
- **Nomothetic** (Greek *nomos*, "general patterns / law") — focuses on uncovering general, normative patterns of behavior. Goal is prediction and explanation, not in-depth individual understanding. Writing is objective and impersonal (e.g., a randomized experiment).

## Mapping to paradigms

[[Positivism]] and [[Postpositivism]] lean **nomothetic** (large samples, general laws). [[Constructivism–Interpretivism]] and the [[Critical–Ideological Paradigm]] lean **idiographic** (the individual's lived experience). Pairs with the [[Etic vs Emic]] distinction.
