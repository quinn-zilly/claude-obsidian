# ABCs of Teamwork Competencies

Nav: [[index]] | [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]]

The **ABCs of teamwork** are the **A**ttitudes, **B**ehaviors, and **C**ognitions (i.e., KSAOs) that constitute team competencies. Attitudes = affective attributes; Behaviors = skills/procedures; Cognitions = knowledge/experience. The taxonomy was first proposed by **Cannon-Bowers, Tannenbaum, Salas & Volpe (1995)** and **updated with empirical-support ratings** by [[salas-et-al-2009-wisdom-of-collectives|Salas, Rosen, Burke & Goodwin (2009)]] in their Table 3.1.

Understanding the ABCs is the basis for **designing tasks/equipment, training teams, evaluating performance, and defining selection criteria**. Crucially, teamwork competencies are distinct from *taskwork* competencies — feedback on individual KSAOs builds individual expertise, but feedback on teamwork is what turns "a team of experts into an expert team."

Support ratings: `+` substantial empirical support, `~` moderate, `x` none.

## Attitudes

| Competency | Support | Gist |
|---|---|---|
| Team/collective orientation | + | Preference to use & enhance others' inputs; value team goals |
| Team/collective efficacy | + | Shared belief in the team's ability to succeed |
| Team cohesion | + | Interpersonal attraction, pride, task commitment |
| Mutual trust | + | Belief members perform roles & protect teammates' interests |
| [[Psychological Safety]] | ~ | Shared belief the team is safe for interpersonal risk (Edmondson, 1999) |
| Team learning orientation | ~ | Shared goals around learning/competence development |
| Team empowerment | ~ | Collective belief in authority/responsibility over work |
| Team reward attitude | ~ | Positive evaluation of team- (vs individual-) based reward |
| Team goal commitment | ~ | Attachment to and determination to reach team goals |

## Behaviors

| Competency | Support | Gist |
|---|---|---|
| Mutual performance monitoring | + | Track teammates' work to catch errors/superior performance |
| Adaptability | + | Adjust strategies from environmental information + backup/reallocation |
| Backup / supportive behavior | + | Anticipate needs; shift workload to balance |
| Implicit coordination | + | Synchronize on unspoken assumptions; cut "communication overhead" |
| Closed-loop communication / info exchange | + | Initiate → acknowledge → confirm receipt |
| Conflict resolution/management | + | Preemptive + reactive management of task/interpersonal conflict |
| Task-related assertiveness | + | Communicate task info clearly and directly |
| Intrateam feedback | + | Prebrief/debrief; feed-forward and corrective feedback |
| Planning | + | Generate proposed action sequences; visualize failure points |
| Coordination | + | Orchestrate sequence & timing of interdependent actions |
| Team leadership | + | Direct/coordinate, assess, assign, motivate, set atmosphere |
| Problem solving | + | Identify present–desired gap; discover means to close it |
| Shared/distributed leadership | ~ | Transfer leadership function to leverage member strengths |
| Mission analysis | ~ | Interpret/evaluate mission, tasks, environment, resources |
| Problem detection | ~ | Sense that a problem exists or soon will |
| Motivation of others | ~ | Generate/maintain goal-directed effort in teammates |

## Cognitions

| Competency | Support | Gist |
|---|---|---|
| Cue–strategy associations (matching rules) | + | Repertoire of strategies matched to recurring situations |
| Accurate problem models | + | [[Shared Mental Models\|Shared]] understanding of situation, cause, cues, likely future, solution |
| Accurate/shared mental models — [[Transactive Memory Systems\|TMS]] & team SA | + | Who-knows-what + team situation awareness |
| Team mission, objectives, norms, resources | + | Shared knowledge of purpose, vision, means, expectations |
| Understanding of multiteam-system (MTS) couplings | ~ | How team performance ties into the larger org structure |

## Notes on the Update

Relative to 1995: team **attitudes/affect** gained the most new empirical grounding (psychological safety, learning orientation, empowerment, reward attitude, goal commitment are additions). **Behaviors** remain the most-studied because they are most measurable. **`task organization`** was dropped, subsumed under coordination. Cognitions stayed close to the original, anchored in shared-mental-model theory, with MTS knowledge added.
