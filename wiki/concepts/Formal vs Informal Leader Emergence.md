# Formal vs Informal Leader Emergence

A distinction [[badura-et-al-2022-leadership-emergence-integrative-review|Badura, Galvin & Lee (2022)]] use to correct a longstanding scope error: treating leader emergence as an *informal-only*, leaderless-group phenomenon.

**Informal leader emergence** — prospective followers *informally* grant the leader role, with **no change** in the focal individual's formal status or authority. This is the classic leaderless-group-discussion case.

**Formal leader emergence** — a more *visible and explicit* granting from followers or superiors that results in a legitimately **designated** leader with formal status and/or authority (e.g., being named team lead, promoted to CEO).

The two are **interrelated and reciprocal**: informal influence often precedes formal designation, and formal roles reshape informal standing. Emergence is thus a dynamic process spanning both, involving perceptions, behaviors, and social interaction between the prospective leader and relevant others (per [[Leadership Identity Construction|DeRue & Ashford 2010]]).

> [!contradiction] Corrects the leaderless-group assumption
> Earlier reviews (e.g., informal-only emergence) implicitly assumed emergence occurs only in leaderless settings. Badura et al. argue real organizations entail *webs of formal and informal leadership operating in tandem*, so a complete model must integrate both.
