# Performance Basis of Pay

The **performance basis of pay** is the *strength* of the empirical link between an individual's observable performance/rank and their pay — i.e., how much a marginal unit of measured productivity (a publication, a promotion, a sales target) actually moves compensation. It is the realized **pay-performance sensitivity**, distinct from whether a formal [[Pay-for-Performance]] policy exists on paper.

## Why it is a separate construct

An organization can have three partly independent pay attributes (Obloj & Zenger 2022):

- **Equity** — fairness/consistency with which pay is matched to performance and rank (low unexplained residual).
- **Equality** — compression; low variance in pay ([[Pay Dispersion]]).
- **Performance basis** — how *steeply* pay rises with measured performance.

These trade off. A firm can raise equity *and* equality simultaneously by making pay both **more precisely** and **more weakly** linked to individual performance — precision serves equity, weakness serves equality.

## The transparency effect

[[obloj-zenger-2022-pay-transparency-equity-equality|Obloj & Zenger (2022)]] show pay transparency causally **weakens** the performance basis of pay:

- Performance premium for a star (95th-pct) academic dropped **~42%** after transparency.
- Returns to rank advancement fell discretely (promotion-to-full premium 31.9%→25.2%).
- Effect held across disciplines and survived a non-mobile-workforce robustness check.

Interpretation: under social comparison, visible performance-based dispersion invites envy/injustice reactions ([[Distributive Justice]]), so allocators retreat from steep pay-performance gradients.

> [!key-insight] The under-discussed cost of transparency
> Advocates promote transparency for equity and equality. The overlooked consequence is a flatter pay-performance gradient — weaker individual incentives — which policymakers must weigh against the fairness gains.

## Connections

- Contrast with [[Pay Dispersion]]: dispersion is the *spread* of pay; performance basis is *what explains* the spread. [[Explained vs Unexplained Pay Dispersion|Legitimate dispersion]] is dispersion driven by a strong performance basis.
- Mirrors [[joshi-son-roh-2015-women-close-the-gap|Joshi et al. (2015)]]: when performance does **not** explain rewards (a weak/biased performance basis by gender), reward gaps persist despite equal performance.
