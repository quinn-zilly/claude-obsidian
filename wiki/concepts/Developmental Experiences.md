# Developmental Experiences

**Developmental experiences** — specific work assignments and situations that produce leadership growth through challenge, novelty, and consequential responsibility. Core building blocks of [[On-the-Job Development]].

## Key Experience Types (from Yost & Plunkett [[Leadership Map]])

- Start-up business
- Sustaining business
- Turnaround business
- Corporate staff role
- Sales/marketing role
- Global experience
- (Others org-specific)

## Why These Experiences Develop Leaders

McCall et al. (1988): effective executives report learning from experiences characterized by:
- Novel challenges requiring new skills
- High stakes / consequential outcomes
- Lack of prior roadmap
- Access to role models (positive or negative)

## Organizational Practice

Problem: managers receive detailed training course lists but rarely receive equivalent OJD experience lists. Fix: publish explicit OJD experience list on corporate intranet; link to talent management processes.

For each experience, specify:
1. What competencies leaders should develop from it
2. Specific actions to maximize learning (stretch goals, feedback-seeking, reflection)
