# Big Team Science

Research conducted by large, distributed, role-specialized teams rather than the traditional **PI-plus-trainees apprenticeship model**, in which each junior researcher leads almost every aspect of their own project.

## The argument

[[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0|Thibault et al. (2023)]] argue that poor distribution of labor and lack of specialization help explain why data/code sharing is rare, prior work gets overlooked, and sample sizes stay too small. Each phase of research benefits from distinct expertise — domain knowledge, statistics, data curation — so teams should include specialized roles (Data Manager, Systematic Reviewer, Statistician) recognized via [[CRediT]].

## Examples and the funding gap

- **UK Biobank** — 500,000+ participants, analyzed by 30,000+ researchers; 15 distinct internal teams.
- **BIDS** (Brain Imaging Data Structure) — 250+ contributors across 26 roles.
- **Distributed collaborations** — Psychological Science Accelerator, ManyBabies; open globally but struggle for sustained government funding.

> [!warning] Funding mismatch
> Academic schemes mainly fund small-to-medium teams. Large enduring teams are left to industry, government, and philanthropists, whose priorities may diverge from impartial science. CERN (high-energy physics) is the model of large-scale collaboration that other fields lack funding to emulate.
