# Team Effectiveness Criteria

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

How team effectiveness is defined and measured. Two complementary treatments live in the wiki.

## Hackman's Three-Dimensional Conception

From [[wageman-hackman-lehman-2005-team-diagnostic-survey|Wageman, Hackman & Lehman 2005]] — effectiveness is broader than convenient quantitative indicators:

1. **Productive output** meets/exceeds the standards of the team's **clients** (the people who receive/review/use it) — not members' own views.
2. **Social process** enhances members' capability to work together interdependently in the **future** (a team is effective only if it is *more* capable when the work finishes).
3. **Group experience** contributes positively to individual members' **learning and well-being** (not frustrating, alienating, or deskilling them).

### Process criteria (proxy for criterion 1)
A self-report tool can't judge client acceptability, so criterion 1 is assessed indirectly via three performance processes (Hackman & Morris 1975): sufficient **effort**, task-appropriate **performance strategy**, and adequate **knowledge/skill** brought to bear.

## Mathieu et al.'s Multiple-Constituencies View

From [[mathieu-et-al-2017-century-work-teams|Mathieu et al. 2017]] — a **multilevel, multiple-constituencies** framework. Two broad criterion classes: **tangible outputs** (productivity, efficiency, quality, retention, creativity) and **members' reactions** (attitudes, turnover intentions, well-being). Under-studied by-products: customer satisfaction, safety, conservation. Key stance: there is **no standard criterion set** — effectiveness is context-specific and shouldn't be forced into one measure.
