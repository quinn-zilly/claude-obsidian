# In-Principle Acceptance

In-principle acceptance (IPA) is the binding commitment a journal makes, in a [[Registered Reports|Registered Report]], to publish a study **after Stage-1 review of its design but before its results are known** — provided the registered protocol is followed and execution is competent.

## Why It Matters

IPA is what makes RRs the strongest form of [[Preregistration]]:

- A plain OSF preregistration documents the plan but does **not** guarantee publication.
- IPA removes outcome from the publication decision entirely, directly killing editorial [[Publication Bias]] — null and negative results get printed.

## The Two-Stage Flow

1. **Stage 1**: Reviewers assess question importance + method adequacy. Accept → IPA.
2. **Stage 2**: Authors submit completed study. Reviewers verify protocol adherence and that conclusions are evidence-bound. Published regardless of result direction.

## Evidence

[[soderberg-et-al-2021-registered-reports-quality|Soderberg et al. (2021)]] attribute much of the measured RR quality advantage to IPA's incentive shift: authors design harder to *earn* IPA, and reviewers evaluate methods undistracted by findings.
