# Open Science Framework

The Open Science Framework (OSF; osf.io) is a free, open-source research workflow platform developed and operated by the [[Center for Open Science]]. It supports the full research lifecycle with project management, version control, preregistration, and open archiving.

## Core Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Projects** | Hierarchical workspaces with components; wiki pages for documentation |
| **File storage** | Version-controlled; free up to 5 GB (private); unlimited (public) |
| **Preregistration** | Timestamped, immutable registration of study designs; multiple templates |
| **Registrations** | Public, permanent snapshots of any project state |
| **Collaboration** | Granular access control (admin, read/write, read-only) |
| **DOIs** | Permanent identifiers for projects, registrations, and datasets |

## Preregistration on OSF

OSF is the primary preregistration platform for psychology and social sciences:
- Free public preregistration with timestamp
- Multiple form templates (standard, qualitative, secondary data, systematic review)
- Linked to publication: preregistration DOI can be cited in the paper
- Searchable index of all public preregistrations

See [[Preregistration]] for full discussion.

## Integrations

OSF connects to: Zotero, GitHub, Dropbox, Google Drive, Figshare, Dataverse, ORCID, and 20+ other services. Researchers can store files in their preferred tool while OSF manages metadata and linking.

## The OSC 2015 Project

The [[open-science-collaboration-2015]] 100-replication project was entirely coordinated through OSF (https://osf.io/ezcuj):
- All replication protocols publicly registered
- All materials and data archived
- Controller R script archived for reproducing all analyses
- Independent audit trail maintained

This remains the flagship demonstration of OSF's infrastructure for large-scale collaborative open science.

## Significance

OSF is to open science what GitHub is to open source software: a platform that makes it easy to work transparently, share outputs, and allow others to build on your work. Its free-to-use model and institutional neutrality have made it the default infrastructure for the reform movement.

## Current Status

As of 2024, OSF hosts millions of projects across all research disciplines. COS has expanded its infrastructure to include: OSF Preprints (multiple discipline-specific preprint services), OSF Registries, and the OSF Meetings platform for conference poster archival.
