# Above-Average Effect

Nav: [[index]] | [[Dunning-Kruger Effect]]

The tendency of the average person to believe he or she is **above average** — a result that "defies the logic of descriptive statistics" (Alicke 1985; Kruger & Dunning 1999). Documented across leadership, getting along with others, written expression (high-schoolers), managerial ability, and "football sense," among many domains.

Kruger & Dunning proposed that the **metacognitive deficits of the unskilled** partly explain this overall tendency: because people usually choose what they think is the optimal option, failing to recognize poor performance leaves them assuming they did well. Thus the incompetent grossly overestimate their skills. See [[Dunning-Kruger Effect]] for the mechanism.

Source: [[kruger-dunning-1999-unskilled-and-unaware|Kruger & Dunning (1999)]].
