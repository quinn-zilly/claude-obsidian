# Time-, Strain-, and Behavior-Based Conflict

Greenhaus & Beutell's (1985) three **forms** of **[[Work–Family Conflict]]**, applied as a moderator in **[[shockley-et-al-2017-gender-work-family-conflict|Shockley et al. (2017)]]**.

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Time-based** | Demands in one role restrict the **time** available for the other |
| **Strain-based** | **Stress/strain** from one role carries over and impairs the other |
| **Behavior-based** | **Behavior** effective in one role is inappropriately applied to the other |

These function as indicators of a single underlying WFC construct (Matthews, Kath & Barnes-Farrell 2010) but may have some unique antecedents (Carlson 1999). Carlson, Kacmar & Williams' (2000) scale measures all three; Netemeyer et al.'s (1996) does not differentiate.

## Gender finding
Shockley et al. found results differed by type: **men reported more *time-based* WIF** than women — a counter-stereotypic result consistent with the **[[Rational View (Work–Family)]]** (men work more hours).
