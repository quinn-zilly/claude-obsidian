# Team Situation Awareness

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

A cognitive [[Emergent States (Teams)|emergent state]]: a **shared understanding of the current situation at a given point in time** (Salas, Prince, Baker & Shrestha 1995). Built on both the team's [[Shared Mental Models]] and each member's individual situation awareness.

## Three Ascending Levels (Endsley 1995)

1. **Perception** — of environmental elements in relation to time and space.
2. **Comprehension** — understanding which elements are noteworthy relative to team goals.
3. **Projection** — forecasting future events in light of the current situation. Level-3 TSA lets teams plan **proactively and flexibly** rather than react.

## Role in the Adaptive Cycle

An output of situation assessment (Phase 1) and a proximal input to both plan formulation (Phase 2, Prop 3) and plan execution (Phase 3, Prop 8) in the [[Team Adaptation (Burke Cross-Level Model)]]. Members need not hold *identical* information — their awareness must **overlap sufficiently** to build a shared model for diagnosing situations against team objectives. If members hold different vantage points they may attend to the wrong cues or misassign meaning, impeding plan development.

## Emergence Path

Composition (one member assesses and transmits; rest accept → aggregate by mean/sum) vs compilation (each member detects different cues, unique-but-compatible perspectives compile into a congruent whole → configural indices).
