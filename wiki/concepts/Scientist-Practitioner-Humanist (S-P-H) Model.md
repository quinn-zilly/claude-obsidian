# Scientist-Practitioner-Humanist (S-P-H) Model

[[Joel Lefkowitz|Lefkowitz's]] proposed expansion of the [[Scientist-Practitioner Model]] (first floated 1990, elaborated 2003, argued fully in [[lefkowitz-2008-values-organizational-psychology|2008]]). It adds a **third value commitment** — humanism — to science and practice.

The S-P-H model asks organizational psychology to:

1. **Recapture psychology's humanist tradition** — privilege employee welfare at least equally with shareholder value (cf. organizational health psychology, "making organizations great places to work").
2. **Adopt a normative stance** — be willing to say what organizations *ought* to be like and how they *ought* to behave morally, not just describe perceptions. Extends to [[Distributive Justice]], CSR, multiple-stakeholder concerns.
3. **Advocate employee rights** — differentiate the profession from management not just by method but by explicit advocacy of fairness and social justice.

Slogan: from "Good Science–Good Practice" to **"Good Science–Good Practice–Good Works."**

Grounded in the [[APA Ethics Code]] mandate to improve the condition of "individuals, organizations, **and** society."
