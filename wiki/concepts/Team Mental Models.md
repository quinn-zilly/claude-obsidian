# Team Mental Models

Team mental models are the team-level analogue of an individual mental model: collectively held, organized understandings of the task, roles, and situation that let members form expectations and coordinate. The term overlaps heavily with [[Shared Mental Models]], though "team mental models" foregrounds the team as the unit and can span both shared (overlapping) and distributed (complementary) knowledge.

In [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]], team mental models sit alongside [[Transactive Memory Systems]] within the broader [[Team Cognition]] literature. TMS is the *distributed* structure - knowledge divided across specialists and integrated through a shared expertise directory - whereas team/shared mental models emphasize commonality of representation.
