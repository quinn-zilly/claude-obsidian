# Leaders vs Leading

The first of the two crossed distinctions in [[carton-2022-science-of-leadership|Carton's (2022)]] meta-theoretical map — the **person versus the process**.

- **Leader** — an *individual* with a unique degree of status or authority to alter others' behavior toward agreed objectives. A leader sits at a **higher level of analysis** and is **temporally stable** (demography, formally designated role, others' standing attributions change slowly).
- **Leading** — a *social-influence process*: an individual (or individuals) attempting to influence others toward a common purpose. Leading is a **lower level of analysis** and is **variable** — a leader enacts different behaviors day to day. Carton notes behavior may vary as much *within* leaders as *between* them.

The distinction is grounded in [[Multilevel Theory in Organizations|multilevel theory]] ([[Multilevel Theory in Organizations|Kozlowski & Klein 2000]]): the leader (entity) is a higher-level construct than any single behavior (leading) they enact.

> [!key-insight] Why keep them separate
> Conflating a stable person with variable behavior invites level-of-analysis errors. Crossing "leaders vs leading" with [[Leadership as Cause vs Consequence]] produces the four cells that locate any leadership study.
