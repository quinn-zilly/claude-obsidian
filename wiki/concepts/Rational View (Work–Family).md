# Rational View (Work–Family)

A theory of **[[Work–Family Conflict]]** (Gutek, Searle & Klepa 1991): the conflict a person experiences is mostly a function of the **amount of time** spent in the work and family domains.

## Prediction
- More work hours → more **WIF** (work more likely to intrude).
- More family time demands → more **FIW**.
- Applied to gender: men (more paid hours) → more WIF; women (more household/care hours) → more FIW.

US time-use data: mothers spend ~2× fathers' hours on household/care; employed men work ~0.6–0.88 more hours/day.

## Verdict in Shockley et al. (2017)
**Supported for both WIF and FIW.** In the meta-analytic path model, work hours mediate gender→WIF and family hours mediate gender→FIW in the predicted directions. The **mothers-report-more-FIW** subgroup finding is the clearest real-world signature of the rational view. Contrast with the unsupported **[[Sensitization Perspective (Gender Role View)]]** (about *values*, not time) and the partial **[[Asymmetrical Domain Permeability Model]]** (about *boundaries*).
