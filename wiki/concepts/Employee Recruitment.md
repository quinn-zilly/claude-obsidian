# Employee Recruitment

**External recruitment** = an employer's actions intended to (1) bring an opening to potential candidates' attention, (2) influence whether they apply, (3) affect whether they maintain interest until an offer, and (4) influence whether an offer is accepted (Breaugh 2008; cf. Breaugh 1992: activities affecting the number/type of applicants and whether offers are accepted).

## Scope

Covers **targeting** (whom to recruit), **[[Recruitment Methods|methods]]** (how to reach them), the **[[Recruitment Message|message]]**, **recruiters**, the **site visit**, the **job offer**, and **timing** — plus recruiting **underrepresented groups**. Outcomes divide into **prehire** (applicant number/quality, offer-acceptance rate) and **posthire** (job performance, turnover/retention).

## State of the field

- **No general theory of recruitment** exists ([[breaugh-2013-employee-recruitment|Breaugh 2013]]) — only partial models of individual pieces.
- Heavy reliance on **college-student samples**.
- **Prehire** attention-getting and applicant-perspective variables are under-studied relative to posthire outcomes.
- Basic **persuasion/attitude** theory (Hovland, McGuire, [[Elaboration Likelihood Model|ELM]], mere exposure, selective exposure, confirmation bias) applies but is under-used.
