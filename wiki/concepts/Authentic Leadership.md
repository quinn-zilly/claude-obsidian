# Authentic Leadership

A form of positive leadership (Luthans & Avolio 2003; Gardner et al. 2005) whose leaders are "deeply aware of how they think and behave and are perceived by others as being aware of their own and others' values/moral perspectives, knowledge, and strengths." Being true to and acting consistently with one's own self and values, transparently.

## Dimensions (Avolio & Gardner 2005)

Positive moral perspective, self-awareness, balanced processing, relational transparency, positive psychological capital, and authentic behavior. Strong developmental focus — moral development of the leader and cultivation of authenticity in followers.

## Positioned as a "Root Concept"

Avolio & Gardner frame authenticity as a precursor/wellspring underlying all other positive leadership forms (transformational, ethical, servant): it answers "is this leadership genuine and beneficial, or abusive and unethical?" — the distinction behind authentic vs. *pseudo*-transformational leadership (Bass & Steidlmeier 1999).

## Empirical Profile ([[hoch-et-al-2018-ethical-authentic-servant-vs-transformational|Hoch et al. 2018]])

Smallest, most heterogeneous literature (41 studies, 45 samples; 44% dissertations) — interpret with caution. Links: engagement (ρ=.47), job satisfaction (ρ=.48), affective commitment (ρ=.53), trust (ρ=.69), LMX (ρ=.67).

> [!warning] Redundancy
> Correlates ρ=.75 with [[Transformational and Charismatic Leadership|transformational leadership]] — the **highest** overlap of the three moral forms, and thus the strongest candidate for **construct redundancy**. Adds little incremental variance. See [[Construct Redundancy (Leadership)]].

## Connections

- One of the three ethical/moral values–based leadership forms alongside [[Ethical Leadership]] and [[Servant Leadership]]
