# Sociotechnical Systems Theory

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work Design (Concept)]]

Arose from Tavistock Institute (Trist & Bamforth 1951). Organizations = people interacting with technical systems; social and technical systems are interdependent and must be *jointly optimized*.

## Core Principles (Cherns 1978)

1. Design process congruent with design outcomes (participative process → participative outcomes)
2. Minimal critical specification — don't over-specify; preserve flexibility
3. Control variance at its source → requires autonomy
4. Multi-functional workers with boundary task control + information access
5. Organizational systems (e.g., compensation) congruent with work design

## Conditions for STS Design (Cummings 1978)

1. Adequate task differentiation — autonomous, self-completing tasks
2. Adequate boundary control — influence over task environment
3. Control over immediate task environment for self-regulation

## Relation to Job Enrichment

Similar: autonomy, task feedback, completing whole work. Differs: **team level of analysis**, not individual.

## Critique

Key propositions (joint optimization, controlling variance at source) not fully empirically tested. "Remains exceedingly difficult to specify propositions that are empirically disconfirmable" (Hackman 1981). Still influential — formalizes group-level analysis in work design.
