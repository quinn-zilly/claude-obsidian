# Strategic HRM

Nav: [[index]] | [[Institutional & Strategy MOC]]

**Strategic Human Resource Management (SHRM)** theory holds that organizational performance improves when HR practices — including [[Work Design (Concept)|work design]] — are internally consistent and aligned with organizational strategy (Schuler & Jackson, 1987; Wood, Holman & Stride, 2006).

In the [[Work Design Influences Framework]], SHRM is the lens on **organizational strategy** as an antecedent of work design:

- A **cost-minimization / mass-market** strategy motivates "low-involvement" HR — Taylorist, simplified jobs, contingent contracts.
- A **differentiation / niche** strategy motivates "high-involvement" HR — enriched designs (high discretion, wide responsibility, challenging tasks), high pay, extensive training.

Empirically, strategy and work design co-occur as SHRM predicts, but the association is **relatively weak** — in many organizations strategy and work design are misaligned (Arthur, 1992; Batt, 2002), whether through managerial inability, misreading the strategic context, or deliberate counter-positioning.
