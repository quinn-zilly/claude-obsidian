# Attitude-Behavior Relations

Nav: [[index]] | [[Organizational Identification (Outcomes)]]

The classic psychology framework (Fishbein & Ajzen 1975; Ajzen & Fishbein 1977) relating **attitudes** to **behaviors**. A central principle is the **compatibility / correspondence** requirement: attitudes predict behavior best when measured at matching levels of generality — a **general attitude** (aggregated across targets/times) predicts **general behavior** (aggregated), whereas specific attitudes predict specific acts. (Compare the vault's [[Reasoned Action Approach]] and the compatibility principle noted in [[Tripartite Model of Attitudes]].)

## Use in the OI meta-analysis

[[lee-park-koo-2015-organizational-identification-meta-analysis|Lee, Park & Koo (2015)]] use this framework to test the *unique value* of **organizational identification**: they aggregate the attitudes (involvement, satisfaction, affective commitment) into a **general attitude** and the behaviors (in-role, extra-role) into **general behavior**, then position OI as an antecedent basis. Result: general attitude → general behavior β=.24, but OI predicts behavior **directly** beyond attitude (β from OI to attitude =.74) — evidence that identification is an upstream basis, not just a parallel attitude.

Source: [[lee-park-koo-2015-organizational-identification-meta-analysis|Lee, Park & Koo (2015)]].
