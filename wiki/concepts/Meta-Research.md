# Meta-Research

**Research-on-research**: studying the research ecosystem itself to identify problems, investigate them, develop solutions, and test those solutions. An iterative loop.

## Why it matters

[[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0|Thibault et al. (2023)]] make funding meta-research one of their 7 roadmap recommendations, because reforms we *assume* will work often turn out to be administrative burdens with negligible benefit (e.g., simply requesting ARRIVE compliance didn't improve reporting).

> [!example] The open-data-badge research cycle
> COS introduced open-data badges → a study found *Psychological Science* publications reporting open data rose sharply → other researchers tried to reproduce results from those datasets and largely failed → they proposed a reproducibility check *before* awarding the badge → which becomes the next interventional study. Without meta-research, we'd be promoting practices that don't achieve their aims.

Meta-research is also what makes datasets like [[CRediT]] statements valuable: they let researchers study the division of scientific labor and realign incentives. Institutional homes include METRICS (Stanford) and the BIH QUEST Center.
