# Knowledge Specialization

Knowledge specialization is the division of cognitive labor within a group: members develop deep, non-overlapping expertise in distinct domains rather than each trying to know everything. It is the structural foundation of a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]] and is operationalized in TMS research as [[Memory Differentiation]].

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] treat specialization as one half of what makes a TMS functional: a group must distribute knowledge across specialists (specialization / structure) *and* be able to integrate it through [[Task Coordination]] (process). Specialization expands the groups effective memory capacity and reduces redundant encoding, but only pays off when paired with an accurate directory of who holds which knowledge and [[Task Credibility|credible trust]] in those specialists.
