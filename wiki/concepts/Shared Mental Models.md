# Shared Mental Models

Shared mental models (SMM) are organized, overlapping knowledge structures held in common by team members about the task, equipment, team, and how members interact. The defining feature is *similarity*: effective teams converge on compatible representations, letting members anticipate one another and coordinate without explicit communication.

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] contrast SMM with [[Transactive Memory Systems]] as the two dominant forms of [[Team Cognition]]. SMM stresses shared, redundant knowledge; TMS stresses *differentiated*, complementary knowledge bound by a shared directory of expertise. The two are distinct but often co-develop: teams need enough overlap to coordinate and enough differentiation to cover the knowledge domain efficiently.

## Distinction from TMS
- **SMM**: convergence / overlap of knowledge.
- **TMS**: division of labor over knowledge plus metaknowledge of who knows what.
