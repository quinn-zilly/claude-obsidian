# Saying-Is-Believing

An **active exercise** technique used in [[Wise Interventions]]: recipients use their *own experience* to advocate the intervention's idea to other people (e.g. writing advice to future students). Described in [[Walton-2014-Wise-Psychological-Interventions|Walton (2014)]].

Why it works: it encourages recipients to **author** the intervention message themselves and to view their own experience through its lens — *without* feeling controlled or stigmatized. This makes it more powerful and persuasive than passive exposure to a message, and it sidesteps reactance. Used prominently in the [[Social Belonging Intervention]] (Walton & Cohen 2011).
