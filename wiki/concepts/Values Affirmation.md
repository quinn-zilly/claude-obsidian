# Values Affirmation

A brief [[Wise Interventions|wise intervention]] in which people write about **why their most important values** (e.g. relationships with friends and family) matter to them. Writing about core values bolsters a person's **sense of self-integrity**, which buffers against psychological threat and prevents poor performance from recurring in a negative [[Recursive Processes (Wise Interventions)|recursive]] cycle. Developed largely by [[Geoffrey Cohen]] and colleagues.

## Evidence (from Walton 2014)

- **Education** (Cohen, Garcia, Purdie-Vaughns, Apfel & Brzustoski 2009): several in-class value-affirmation exercises raised core academic grades for initially low-performing Black students over the **next 2 years**, by interrupting the threat→poor-performance loop.
- **Health** (Logel & Cohen 2012): affirmation reduced stress (a driver of weight gain) — treatment women weighed ~6 lb less, had smaller waists, and ate fewer cookies after exerting self-control, 2.5 months later.

## Timing matters

Because it works by breaking a recursive threat cycle, affirmation is most effective delivered **early**, before downward cycles establish; delivered afterward the same exercise is weaker (Cook et al. 2012). Closely tied to [[Stereotype Threat]] (the threat it buffers) and self-affirmation theory.
