# Personal Advisory Board

**Personal advisory board** — a curated group of 5–7 people who collectively serve the developmental functions traditionally assigned to a single mentor.

## Origins

Yost & Plunkett (2010) draw on Higgins & Kram (2001) developmental network theory. Core shift: single mentor → diverse network of developmental relationships.

Rationale: today's dynamic organizations require multiple types of guidance (industry, technical, political, personal, career) that no single mentor can provide.

## Construction Guidance

- 5–7 people
- Diverse functional/contextual perspectives
- Mix of internal and external stakeholders
- Align with [[Leadership Map]] relationship dimension (senior execs, customers, community, mentor/champion)
- Ibarra & Hunter (2007) on how leaders create and use networks

## Organizational Role

Catalyst #8 in [[yost-plunkett-2010-ten-catalysts]]: organizations should actively encourage and provide guidance for building personal advisory boards, not just assign mentors.
