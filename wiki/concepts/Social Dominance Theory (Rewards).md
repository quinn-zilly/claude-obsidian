# Social Dominance Theory (Rewards)

Sidanius and colleagues' framework applied by [[joshi-son-roh-2015-women-close-the-gap|Joshi et al. (2015)]] to reward allocation.

- **Hierarchy-enhancing agents / contexts** favor distributing positively valued goods (positive appraisals, rewards) to **dominant** groups (men) over **subordinate** groups (women).
- **Hierarchy-attenuating agents** facilitate egalitarian allocation.
- **Social dominance orientation (SDO)** — a dispositional tendency to favor dominant over subordinate groups — is likely high among key decision makers (senior executives, HR, supervisors) in prestigious settings.

> [!key-insight] Prestige as a hierarchy-enhancer
> Highly prestigious occupations operate as hierarchy-enhancing contexts. The prestige value of the context and the SDO of decision makers are **mutually reinforcing**, producing inequitable reward outcomes for women **even when their performance equals men's** — the paper's most striking finding (equal performance, unequal rewards in high-prestige occupations). See [[Occupational Prestige and Job Complexity]].
