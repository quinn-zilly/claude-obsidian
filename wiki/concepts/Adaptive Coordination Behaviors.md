# Adaptive Coordination Behaviors

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

The individual- and team-level behaviors that enact **plan execution** (Phase 3) in the [[Team Adaptation (Burke Cross-Level Model)]]. It is the *adaptive application* of these behaviors that lets teams capture process gains rather than suffer process losses.

## The Behaviors

- **Mutual performance monitoring** — members keep track of teammates' work to ensure things run as expected and procedures are followed (McIntyre & Salas 1995). Primarily cognitive. Enables recognition of when help is needed, awareness of timing/pacing, and higher [[Team Situation Awareness]].
- **Backup behavior** — discretionary provision of resources/task effort to a teammate failing to reach role goals (Porter et al. 2003). **Legitimacy matters**: when an underutilized member backs up an over-capacity member, teams achieve what individuals couldn't; illegitimate/unneeded backup produces *redundant* rather than complementary behavior and *detracts* from performance.
- **Communication** — accurate exchange of information in prescribed manner (Cannon-Bowers et al. 1995). Updates shared knowledge structures; makes monitoring functional (monitoring without speaking up is useless). See [[Communication]].
- **Leadership** — leaders structure member action, develop members, and maintain shared coherence (Kozlowski 1998). **[[Shared Leadership]]** — transference of the leadership function across members — improves adaptability; effective transfer needs shared knowledge structures + a climate accepting fluid roles (coordinated leadership transfer).
- **Coordination** — the *team-level* process: organizing the sequencing and timing of actions (Marks et al. 2001). Behavior weighting is contingent: in highly interdependent teams the behaviors are weighted equally; in less interdependent teams, monitoring/backup are less central.
