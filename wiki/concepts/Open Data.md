# Open Data

Open data in science refers to the practice of making research datasets publicly available — typically via repositories such as the Open Science Framework (OSF) — so that other researchers can verify analyses, reuse data, and conduct meta-analyses.

## Why It Matters

Open data is a cornerstone of [[Analytic Reproducibility]]: without access to the original data, it is impossible to independently verify whether reported analyses are correct. It also addresses the [[File-Drawer Problem]] by making null-result datasets accessible.

## Adoption

The [[TOP Guidelines]] include standards for data sharing at multiple levels, from disclosure of whether data exist to mandatory posting. Journals increasingly require or incentivize open data as a condition of publication.
