# Career Shocks

**Career shocks** are disruptive events — whether positive or negative — that trigger deliberation about a change in one's career behavior or career path (Seibert et al. 2013; Akkermans et al. 2018). The concept builds on the unfolding model of turnover (Lee & Mitchell 1994).

Shocks provide a **counterweight to the field's agentic, planned view** of careers: career success is shaped not only by what individuals proactively pursue but by external events that precipitate relatively sudden career decisions — school-to-work transitions, moves into entrepreneurship, and shifts in career trajectory that alter ultimate [[Objective Career Success|objective]] and [[Subjective Career Success|subjective]] success.

Open research needs (Akkermans et al. 2021b): conceptual clarity on event characteristics, mechanisms (how shocks impact success), and which shock types relate to which facets of OCS/SCS. Increasingly connected to the [[Sustainable Career]] perspective.
