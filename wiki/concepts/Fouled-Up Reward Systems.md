# Fouled-Up Reward Systems

Kerr's (1975/1995) colloquial term for formal reward structures in which the behaviors being reinforced are precisely those the rewarder is trying to discourage, while desired behaviors go unrewarded. Synonym for [[Reward System Misalignment]].

The term is deliberately irreverent — Kerr's original 1975 article was nearly rejected for tone. "Fouled up" signals that the problem is structural and common, not rare or exotic.

## Canonical Table (Kerr 1995)

| We hope for… | But we often reward… |
|---|---|
| Long-term growth; environmental responsibility | Quarterly earnings |
| Teamwork | Individual effort |
| Challenging stretch objectives | Achieving (easy) goals; making the numbers |
| Downsizing/rightsizing | Adding staff, budget, Hay points |
| Commitment to total quality | Shipping on schedule, even with defects |
| Candor; surfacing bad news early | Reporting good news (true or not); agreeing with the boss |
