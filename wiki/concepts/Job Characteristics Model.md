# Job Characteristics Model

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work Motivation Meta-Framework (Kanfer)]]

Hackman & Oldham (1975, 1976). Dominant context-based motivation theory for 25+ years. Specifies how job features produce internal work motivation through psychological states.

## Core Structure

**Five job characteristics** → **Three psychological states** → **Internal work motivation**

| Job Characteristic | Psychological State |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Skill variety | |
| Task identity | → Experienced meaningfulness of work |
| Task significance | |
| Autonomy | → Experienced responsibility for outcomes |
| Feedback | → Knowledge of actual results |

**Design goal:** "performance becomes its own reward, a virtuous self-perpetuating cycle of positive work motivation powered by self-generated rewards" (Hackman & Oldham 1976, p. 256)

**Moderator:** Growth need strength — individual differences in need for personal development moderate context–motivation relations.

## Empirical Support and Extensions

Humphrey, Nahrgang & Morgeson (2007) meta-analysis:
- Overall evidence largely supportive
- **Task meaningfulness = strongest mediator** (consistent with SDT prosocial motivation)
- Theory underspecified social and work-level variables → extended to include relational work design (interdependence, feedback from others, social support)

## Relation to SDT

JCM's autonomy characteristic maps directly to SDT's autonomy need. Task significance → relatedness + meaning. Competence need satisfied via skill variety + feedback loop. The psychological states (meaningfulness, responsibility) are the proximal mechanism SDT's need satisfaction operates through in work contexts.

## Limitations

- Measurement of job characteristics remains contested
- Does not address team-level dynamics, relational work features, or occupational/cultural context
- Leadership effects on motivation not incorporated (treated separately)

## Verdict After Hundreds of Studies (Oldham & Fried 2016)

Strong support for characteristic→attitude relations and for **experienced meaningfulness as mediator**; only **modest** characteristic→behavior relations; **weak** support for experienced responsibility and knowledge of results; **mixed** support for the GNS and context-satisfaction moderators (the knowledge/skill moderator was never directly tested). See [[Job Crafting]] for the employee-initiated successor stream.
