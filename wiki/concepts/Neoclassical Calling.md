# Neoclassical Calling

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

The **outside-in** camp of calling definitions ([[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson 2019]]). Anchored in the classical, religious origins of the concept and organized around **destiny, duty, and discovery**:

- **Destiny** — there exists some domain of work that is right for each individual; one is destined or meant for certain work.
- **Duty** — it is one's obligation to find and embrace that destined work (classically, to glorify God and serve the human family).
- **Discovery** — finding one's calling is a process of discovery beginning with an evaluation of one's distinctive talents, passions, and life opportunities.

The **neoclassical** (as opposed to purely classical) version is Weber's secularized reimagining: it retains destiny/duty/discovery but does **not** require explicit belief in a divine Caller. In the [[Inner and Outer Requiredness]] 2×2 it is **high outer, low inner** requiredness. Exemplar definition: Bunderson & Thompson's (2009) "neoclassical calling scale" — "that place in the occupational division of labor in society that one feels destined to fill by virtue of particular gifts, talents, and/or idiosyncratic life opportunities."
