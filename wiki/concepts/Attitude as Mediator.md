# Attitude as Mediator

**Attitude** = an individual's positive or negative feelings about performing a target behaviour (Fishbein & Ajzen 1975; Davis et al. 1989). It is the individual-characteristics construct that [[UTAUT]] omitted and [[Revised UTAUT Model|Dwivedi et al.]] reinstated.

## Theoretical grounding

Attitude is central to TRA, TPB, and DTPB. In TRA, attitude fully mediates the path from beliefs to intention; [[Technology Acceptance Model|TAM]] is effectively a special case of TRA with two beliefs (perceived usefulness, perceived ease of use) feeding attitude. UTAUT's drop of attitude was the outlier.

## Empirical role found by Dwivedi et al.

- **Partial mediation.** Attitude partially mediates the effects of performance expectancy, effort expectancy, facilitating conditions, and social influence on behavioural intention.
- **Direct effect on intention** (AT → BI = 0.37 in the proposed model).
- **Direct effect on usage** (AT → UB), independent of intention — people may use a system on the strength of attitude even without consciously intending to.
- The four exogenous constructs load **more strongly on attitude than on intention** (e.g., PE → AT = 0.47 vs PE → BI = 0.11).

## Why not just drop it?

Because intention's link to actual usage is often weak/unpredictable, the direct AT → UB path adds real explanatory power. Partial mediation is *not* a reason to relegate attitude: explicit modelling raises R² for both BI and UB.

> [!note]
> Practical upshot: attitude is a lever managers can pull directly (design, communication, training, social influence), not just a downstream byproduct.
