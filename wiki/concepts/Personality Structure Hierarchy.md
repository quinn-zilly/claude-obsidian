# Personality Structure Hierarchy

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

Personality-attribute dimensions emerge in a **radial hierarchy** (Saucier & Srivastava 2015, Fig. 13.1): as you allow more factors, broader constructs split into narrower ones. Robustness *decreases* as factor count rises.

## The levels

| Factors | Structure | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Evaluation** (good vs. bad) — the "Big One" | Recurs across cultures; partly heritable (Musek 2007) |
| **2** | **Social Self-Regulation** (communion / "getting along") vs. **Dynamism** (agency / "getting ahead") — Bakan's agency & communion | As replicable as 1-factor; **impervious to variable-selection** |
| **3** | Splits self-regulation → **sociomoral** (agreeableness+honesty) vs. **task-oriented** (conscientiousness), plus dynamism | 1–3 factor structures **most cross-culturally replicable** |
| **5** | The **[[Five-Factor Model|Big Five]]** | Clean recovery mainly in **Germanic/Slavic** languages; intellect–openness & neuroticism travel poorly |
| **6** | The **Big Six / [[HEXACO Model|HEXACO]]-like** (adds Honesty–Humility) | Less bound to N-European languages; may **exceed** Big Five generalizability |
| **7+** | No replicable independent structure | Big Seven (Tellegen & Waller) failed to replicate |

## Key implications

- The **Big Five is not privileged** by robustness — it is contingent on variable-selection (removing extremely evaluative terms) and on which languages were studied first (English, Dutch, German).
- Broader factors may be organized by **sociocultural schemas** (evaluating a person's worth for social purposes), while narrower factors better map to **biological** covariation.
- The **Big Two** (agency/communion) is arguably the most cross-culturally solid finding and connects to Osgood's evaluation/potency/activity affective-meaning dimensions.
