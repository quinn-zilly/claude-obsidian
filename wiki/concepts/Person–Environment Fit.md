# Person–Environment Fit

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

PE fit = the degree to which a person's characteristics match their work environment. Two classic subtypes (Kristof 1996):
- **Demands-abilities fit**: person's KSAs meet work demands (complementary)
- **Needs-supplies fit**: organization meets person's needs (supplementary)

## TAT Integration

[[Trait Activation Theory]] provides a uniquely comprehensive fit framework because **personality traits contribute to fit via both subtypes simultaneously** — traits are both needs (need satisfaction drive) and abilities (meeting demands):

| Construct | Demands-abilities fit | Needs-supplies fit |
|---|---|---|
| KSAs | ✓ (strong) | — |
| Values | — | ✓ (via work values) |
| Personality traits | ✓ (trait expression meets demands) | ✓ (trait-relevant cues satisfy needs) |

TAT's three-part motivation system formalizes this: intrinsic need satisfaction (needs-supplies) + extrinsic reinforcement for demand-meeting (demands-abilities) + differential reward preference.

## Levels of Fit (TAT)

| Level | Fit type | Primary outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Task | Complementary (demands-abilities) | Job performance |
| Social/Team | Both | Performance + work attitudes |
| Organizational | Supplementary (culture similarity) | Job satisfaction, commitment |

**Situational relevance** = the unifying principle across all fit variants. TAT-based personality-oriented work analysis identifies trait-specific demands, distracters, facilitators operating at each level.

## Related Frameworks

- **Theory of Work Adjustment** (Dawis & Lofquist 1984): needs-supplies fit via "occupational reinforcers"; TAT adds trait-expression mechanism and distinguishes intrinsic vs. extrinsic need satisfaction
- **Holland's RIASEC**: person drawn to environments matching personality; TAT = the activation mechanism underlying RIASEC fit dynamics
- **ASA Model** (Schneider 1987): attraction-selection-attrition based on personality similarity; TAT explains *why* — trait expression opportunities drive the fit preference
- **LMX theory**: mutual trait activation between leaders and followers as an exchange mechanism
