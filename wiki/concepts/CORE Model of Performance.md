# CORE Model of Performance

The **CORE model** ([[marshall-aguinis-beltran-2024-theories-of-performance|Marshall, Aguinis & Beltran 2024]]) is a meta-theory that integrates 239 performance theories into a single isomorphic equation applicable at both firm and individual levels:

$$P = C + O + RE$$

Performance (P) = **Capacity** (C) + **Opportunity** (O) + **Relevant Exchanges** (RE).

## The Three Components

| Component | Firm-level construct | Individual-level construct | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Capacity (C)** | Capabilities | KSAOs | Intra-agent potential — max performance given current resources under ideal conditions |
| **Opportunity (O)** | Structures | Roles | Structural situation enabling performance and knowledge sharing |
| **Relevant Exchanges (RE)** | Transactions | Relationships | Inter-agent exchanges conducted for a performance purpose |

## Core Claim: Isomorphism

The six meta-theoretical constructs (three firm, three individual) **mirror each other across levels**. Firm capabilities are the collective analogue of individual KSAOs; firm structures of individual roles; firm transactions of individual relationships. Raised to a higher level of abstraction, each pair merges into one CORE component. The micro–macro divide is therefore treated as largely **linguistic**, not foundational — the two literatures describe the same latent system in different words.

## Performance as a System

CORE reframes performance as a **multicomponent dynamic system** rather than a process *or* an outcome. Because a system's behavior lives in the interaction between parts, the intersections *between* C, O, and RE are where the model directs future research (e.g., roles×transactions = person-job fit → firm value capture).

## Why It Matters

- **Parsimony over theorrhea.** Three components + six constructs replace a proliferation of overlapping theories; the aim is to stop illusory novelty and channel effort into intersections.
- **Cross-level bridge.** By making firm and individual performance commensurable, it invites micro–macro collaboration (macro endogeneity methods meet micro experimental designs).
- **A "mind map."** Before a study, ask: does my question primarily concern capacity, opportunity, or relevant exchanges?

## Relation to Individual Performance Models

CORE abstracts *above* behavioral content. The [[Campbell Model of Job Performance|Campbell eight-factor model]] specifies the *content* of individual performance-as-behavior and sits inside the individual-level slice of CORE. The two are complementary: CORE gives the cross-level skeleton; Campbell gives the individual-level flesh.

## Lineage note

Carpini et al. (2017) earlier used "capacity" and "opportunity to perform" but defined them for the individual level only; CORE generalizes the labels across levels.
