# Experience-Driven Development

**Experience-driven development** — a leadership development framework that inverts the traditional training model by treating on-the-job experience as the primary arena for growth and formal training as a catalyst/accelerator for that experience-based learning.

## The Core Inversion

**Traditional model (training-centered):**
Training program → pre-training conditions → post-training transfer conditions → on-job application

**Experience-driven model (outside-in):**
On-the-job experience → formal training as *catalyst* → richer experience-based learning

The word "transfer" reveals the problem: it implies the classroom is the locus of learning and the job is where you "apply" it. EDD inverts this — the job *is* the learning, and the classroom is one of many events that can accelerate it.

## The 90% Principle

Starting assumption: **90% of a leader's development occurs outside the classroom.**

This is the explicit grounding of the [[70-20-10 Rule]]. If true, it follows that:
- Designing training to maximize in-class learning optimizes for 10% of development
- Designing training to maximize outside-class development optimizes for 90%

Source: [[yost-2013-experience-driven-development]] (Yost, 2013, ASTD Handbook); also foundational to [[yost-2014-leading-from-where-you-are]] and [[yost-plunkett-2010-ten-catalysts]].

## Three Roles of Formal Training in the EDD Model

1. **Highlight** — identify the short list of key developmental experiences worth pursuing
2. **Build meta-skills** — feedback-seeking, reflection, learning agility — the skills that extract lessons from experience
3. **Activate managers** — remind bosses they are the primary developers of their direct reports

## The Development Continuum

Experience-driven development spans a continuum from most formal to most experiential:

```
Formal Training → Transfer Design → Action Learning → Mentoring/CoPs → Job Rotation → Job Assignments
```

Each stage shifts the center of gravity further toward experience:
- **Formal Training**: in-class with experience reflection and future-planning exercises
- **Transfer Design**: explicit pre/post catalysts (boss conversations, peer coaching groups)
- **Action Learning**: real business problems as the vehicle; development happens en route
- **Mentoring / CoPs**: no classroom required; learning through relationships embedded in work
- **Job Rotation**: planned sequences of stretch roles; classroom = preparation/debrief only
- **Job Assignments**: trial-by-fire experiences where learning is required to survive

See [[yost-2013-experience-driven-development]] for the full continuum (Figures 57.1–57.2).
