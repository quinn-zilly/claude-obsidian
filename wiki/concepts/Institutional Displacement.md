# Institutional Displacement

One of four change pathways in the [[Institutional Change Pathways]] typology. Characterized by **revolutionary pace** and **transformational scope**: rapid, disruptive, field-level change that overturns shared understandings and replaces or significantly alters dominant institutional logics.

## Characteristics

- Triggered by potent macro-level dynamics: exogenous shocks (regulatory, political, financial, technological), or purposeful and effortful action by mobilized change agents
- Unfolds through "institutional wars" (Hoffman 1999) — conflict between challengers and incumbents with asymmetric power
- Outcomes: disappearance of organizational forms and practices; birth of new ones; field reconfiguration
- Historically the *most studied* pathway (47 of 119 studies in Micelotta et al. review)

## Two Research Streams

**Exogenous shock → displacement**: macro-environmental jolts alter the institutional environment; organizations respond (selection or adaptation). Population ecology variant predicts selection; strategic management variant predicts adaptive metamorphosis.

**Entrepreneurial → displacement**: disruptive institutional entrepreneurs (social movements, outsider challengers) mobilize to replace incumbent logics. Framing contests, adversarial resource mobilization, boycotts.

## Gaps (per Micelotta et al.)

Both streams assume a **monistic field** — one dominant logic prescribing behavior. Neither asks how the same displacement processes unfold in pluralistic fields with multiple coexisting logics. Key open questions:
- How does logic multiplicity at the field level moderate organizational responses to revolutionary change?
- How does institutional complexity at the organizational level shape interpretation of transformative change?
- How does field pluralism influence diffusion and institutionalization of the new arrangement?

Pluralism may either *facilitate* displacement (pockets of early adopters; "ambassadors" amplifying the message) or *hinder* it (slow the development of shared understanding; prevent widespread diffusion needed for institutionalization).

## Connections

- [[Institutional Isomorphism]]: displacement breaks the isomorphic equilibrium; the three isomorphic mechanisms then reassemble around the new logic
- [[Organizational Field]]: displacement redraws field boundaries, changes dominant actors, redistributes status and resources
