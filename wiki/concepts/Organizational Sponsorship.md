# Organizational Sponsorship

The extent to which organizations provide special assistance to employees to facilitate career success — the predictor family aligned with the **sponsored-mobility** lens ([[Contest-Mobility vs Sponsored-Mobility]]) in [[ng-eby-sorensen-feldman-2005-career-success-meta-analysis|Ng et al. (2005)]].

## Indicators used

Career sponsorship (support from senior employees; Dreher & Ash 1990), supervisor support, training and skill-development opportunities, and organizational resources (proxied by organization size).

## Empirical signature

Organizational sponsorship relates **more strongly to subjective career success** (career satisfaction) than to objective outcomes. Standout corrected correlations with **career satisfaction**: supervisor support ρ ≈ .46, career sponsorship ρ ≈ .44 — an order of magnitude above their correlations with salary/promotion. Relational support shapes how successful people *feel*, more than how much they are paid.
