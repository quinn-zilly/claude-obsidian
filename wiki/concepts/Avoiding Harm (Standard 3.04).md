# Avoiding Harm (Standard 3.04)

> [!note] Stub
> Seed page created to resolve inbound links from [[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct]]. Expand on next ethics ingest.

**Standard 3.04** of the [[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct|APA Ethics Code]] is the enforceable rule that psychologists **take reasonable steps to avoid harming** their clients, patients, students, research participants, and others they work with, and to minimize harm where it is foreseeable and unavoidable.

## The 2016 Amendment (3.04(b))

The Code's headline change to this standard: a **2016 amendment (effective January 1, 2017)** added subsection **3.04(b)**, explicitly stating that psychologists **do not participate in, facilitate, or assist any activity defined as torture or as cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment**. The amendment was a direct institutional response to controversy over psychologist involvement in national-security interrogations.

## Placement in the APA Code

- Sits in **Section 3, Human Relations**, alongside unfair discrimination, harassment, multiple relationships, conflict of interest, and [[Informed Consent]].
- Operationalizes the aspirational principles of **Beneficence and Nonmaleficence** (see [[Five General Principles (APA)]]).
