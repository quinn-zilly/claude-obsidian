# Work Design

Nav: [[index]] | [[Job Characteristics Model]] | [[Work Design Questionnaire]]

Study of the content, structure, and context of jobs and how they affect individual and organizational outcomes. Sits at the intersection of industrial and organizational psychology; integrates motivational theory + job analysis.

## Definition

Work design = "the content and structure of jobs individuals perform and the broader context within which work is performed" (Morgeson et al. 2012).

Distinct from *job analysis* (describing what exists) — work design addresses *how* work should be structured.

## Historical Arc

1. **Scientific management** (Taylor/Gilbreth 1911): specialization → efficiency
2. **Job enrichment** (Herzberg 1959; Hackman & Oldham 1976): motivational characteristics → satisfaction + motivation
3. **Sociotechnical systems** (Trist 1951): joint optimization of social + technical
4. **Social information processing** (Salancik & Pfeffer 1978): context constructs perceptions
5. **Interdisciplinary** (Campion 1988): four models with distinct outcome profiles
6. **JD-R model** (Bakker & Demerouti 2007): demands × resources → burnout/engagement
7. **Relational work design** (Grant 2007): social/prosocial mechanisms
8. **WDQ era** (Morgeson & Humphrey 2006+): 18-characteristic comprehensive taxonomy

## Four Characteristic Domains (WDQ)

- **Task:** autonomy, task variety, task significance, task identity, feedback from job
- **Knowledge:** job complexity, information processing, problem solving, skill variety, specialization
- **Social:** social support, interdependence, interaction outside org, feedback from others
- **Contextual:** ergonomics, physical demands, work conditions, equipment use

## Key Tensions

Motivational ↔ Mechanistic design: enriching work → satisfaction + training costs; simplifying → efficiency + dissatisfaction. Partially resolvable via specialization (Morgeson & Campion 2002; Edwards et al. 2000).

## Connections

- [[Job Characteristics Model]]: the dominant motivational framework; five task characteristics; largely subsumed into WDQ
- [[Self-Determination Theory]]: SDT's need satisfaction (autonomy, competence, relatedness) maps to JCM psychological states
- [[Work Motivation Meta-Framework (Kanfer)]]: work design = "context" cell; job characteristics as motivational antecedents operating through goal processes
- [[Job Demands–Control–Support Model]]: demands + control + support → strain/engagement; JD-R generalizes this
- [[Job Crafting]]: the employee-initiated, bottom-up counterpart to manager-imposed design (contemporary frontier)
