# Replication

Replication is the attempt to repeat a study's procedures and obtain the same results. It is the fundamental mechanism by which science tests the reliability and generalizability of empirical findings.

## Types of Replication

- **Direct (exact) replication**: repeating the original procedure as closely as possible to test reliability
- **Conceptual replication**: testing the same hypothesis with different methods or populations to test generalizability
- **Many Labs replication**: coordinated multi-site replications to estimate effect variability across contexts (see [[Many Labs]])

## Why Replication Matters

Without replication, individual findings may reflect sampling error, researcher degrees of freedom, or publication bias rather than true effects. The [[Replication Crisis]] revealed that a large proportion of published psychology findings fail to replicate.

## Replication as Reform

[[Replication as Theoretical Commitment]] frames replication not as mere checking but as theoretically motivated: choosing what to replicate and how signals what you believe matters about a finding.
