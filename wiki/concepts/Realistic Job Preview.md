# Realistic Job Preview

A **realistic job preview (RJP)** conveys **both positive and negative** attributes of a job/organization, rather than only favorable information. The logic: accurate expectations let applicants make an **informed self-selection** decision (withdraw if it's a poor fit), lowering later turnover and raising fit.

## Mechanisms & evidence

- **Realism hypothesis** (Breaugh 2013): certain **[[Recruitment Methods|methods]]** — especially **[[Employee Referrals|employee referrals]]** — yield more accurate expectations, so recruits self-select better and stay longer.
- Two-sided, credible messages persuade **better** than one-sided ones, particularly when the two-sided message **refutes** opposing points (O'Keefe 1999).
- RJPs and site visits promote higher **[[Perceived Fit (Recruitment)|perceived fit]]** than general strategies like newspaper ads (Kristof 1996).

## Caveats

RJP benefits depend on applicants having **self-insight** about their own abilities/needs — an assumption Breaugh (2013) flags as often unwarranted (people overrate their abilities; Dunning). The realism/prescreening mechanisms of referrals remain **largely untested** on current-employee data.
