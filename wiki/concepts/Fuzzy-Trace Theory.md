# Fuzzy-Trace Theory

Theory of decision making and memory (Reyna 2012) proposing that people represent information at multiple levels of precision, from **verbatim** (exact details) to **gist** (essential meaning). Decision quality depends on extracting the right level of representation.

## Core Claim

Better decisions often come from **gist processing** (categorical, qualitative) rather than verbatim processing (quantitative, exact). Experts and older adults tend to rely more on gist; adolescents and novices over-rely on verbatim.

## Application to Adolescent Health Decisions

**Sexual behavior program** (Reyna & Mills 2014): adding gist modules improved teen understanding.

Example gist translation: *"Even low risks add up to 100% if you keep doing it."*

This communicates the categorical meaning (repeated risky behavior = certain eventual harm) without requiring probability calculation.

## Decision Science Integration

Fuzzy-Trace Theory offers developmental psychology what decision science offers generally: a formal characterization of what a decision requires, mapped against what people actually extract.

Bridges decision science and developmental psychology — each offers what the other lacks (analytical methods vs. developmental/social context).
