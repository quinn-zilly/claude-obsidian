# Organizational Analysis (Training)

One of the three legs of [[Training Needs Analysis]]. Considers the **context** in which training will occur: whether training fits the company's business strategy, whether resources exist, and whether managers/peers support it. Usually conducted **first**, because it decides whether the company even wants to spend time and money on training.

## Three Questions (Noe)

1. **Strategic direction** — does training support the business strategy? Strategic role drives training frequency, type, and how the training function is organized (business-embedded / corporate-university models when strategic role is high).
2. **Support of managers, peers, employees** — peer/manager support is critical. Without supportive attitudes and opportunity to apply, employees won't transfer training. Links to [[Training Context and Transfer Climate]].
3. **Training resources** — budget, time, expertise. Drives the **buy-vs-build** decision (develop in-house vs. purchase from vendor/consultant).

> [!example] IBM
> Tracked learning-content usage and usefulness; eliminated 39% of content few employees used — 7,600 courses.

## Buy vs Build

Three strategies when a skill gap appears (e.g., new computer-based manufacturing): (1) internal consultants train everyone; (2) select/place computer-literate employees via tests, reassign those who fail (→ selection, not training); (3) buy training from a consultant when time/expertise lacking.

Sharing across similar units also surfaces resources — e.g., Kaiser Permanente's National Learning Leaders group standardizes learning across regions.
