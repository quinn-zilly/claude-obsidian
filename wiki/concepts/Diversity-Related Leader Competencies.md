# Diversity-Related Leader Competencies

The three trainable capacities that, per the [[Leading Diversity (LeaD) Model|LeaD model]] ([[homan-et-al-2020-leading-diversity-functional|Homan et al., 2020]]), let a leader **match** behavior to a diverse team's dominant process. Each competency maps to one step of the predict→diagnose→match chain.

## The three competencies

| Competency | Function | Cues used | Illustrative antecedents |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cognitive understanding** | *Predict* the future dominant process (proactive) | Faultlines vs cross-categorization, reward structure, team history, diversity climate | Multicultural experience, cultural intelligence (**P1**) |
| **Social perceptiveness** | *Diagnose* the current dominant process (reactive) | Bias: distant seating, closed posture, heated affect. Elaboration: task focus, clarifying questions, explicit task conflict | Openness to experience, emotional intelligence (**P2**) |
| **Behavioral flexibility** | *Match* — "respond in significantly different ways to correspondingly different situational requirements" (Zaccaro et al. 1991) | — | Interpersonal flexibility, openness to experience (**P3**) |

## Why this framing matters

- It **explains why leader traits moderate diversity effects**: traits (openness, cultural/emotional intelligence, multicultural experience) work *through* these competencies, not directly.
- It shifts diversity leadership from stable dispositional **styles** toward **trainable competencies** — recruit/select for the antecedent traits, then develop the competencies via targeted training, coaching, experience, and 360-feedback (rather than broad, often-ineffective [[Diversity Training|diversity training]]).
- The competencies are what make **[[Complementary vs Supplementary Matching|complementary/supplementary matching]]** possible; without them, a leader defaults to a preferred style regardless of team need.
