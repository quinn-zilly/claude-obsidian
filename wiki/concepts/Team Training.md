# Team Training

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

A set of theoretically based strategies designed to ensure team members obtain, understand, and practice the knowledge, skills, and attitudes (KSAs) required for effective team performance. Used both proactively (before performance) and reactively (correcting in-performance breakdowns). Definition: Salas & Cannon-Bowers (1997).

## Four Strategies

### Cross-Training
Each member learns teammates' duties and responsibilities. Builds shared knowledge of tasks, mutual performance monitoring, backup behaviors. → Directly develops [[Transactive Memory Systems]].

### Team Self-Correction Training
Develops team's ability to *internally* diagnose breakdowns and reach solutions continuously. Targets mutual performance monitoring, effective communication, leadership. (Smith-Jentsch et al. 2008)

### Team-Coordination Training
Improves shared mental model framework; builds common understanding of goal achievement issues. Targets backup behaviors, monitoring, teamwork skill understanding. (Entin & Serfaty 1999)

### Crew Resource Management (CRM)
Applies well-tested tools (simulators, role play) to specific content. Targets communication, briefing, backup behaviors, decision making, adaptability, shared situation awareness. (Salas et al. 2001)

Evidence: CRM training credited by NTSB for survival of US Airways Flight 1549 (Hudson River ditching, 2009).

## Meta-Analytic Effects

| Outcome | ρ | % Variance |
|---|---|---|
| Process | 0.44 | 19.36% |
| **Cognitive** | **0.42** | **17.64%** |
| **Performance** | **0.39** | **15.21%** |
| Affective | 0.35 | 12.25% |
| Combined | 0.34 | 11.56% |

**Strongest for cognitive and performance outcomes.** Stronger overall than [[Team Building]] (0.34 vs. 0.31). Surgical team training across 74 facilities → lower mortality rates (Neily et al. 2010).

## When to Use

- Teams need to build shared knowledge before performing together
- Task interdependence is **high** (coordination failures are costly)
- Performance errors are rooted in knowledge/skill gaps, not interpersonal conflict
- Teams need to improve decision making, coordination, self-correction capability
- High-stakes environments (aviation, medicine, emergency management)

## Caveats

- Resource intensive: requires needs analysis → KSA identification → learning objectives → design strategy
- Cannot shortcut the design process; poorly designed programs waste effort and miss targets
- Works best with realistic practice environments that enable transfer to actual performance context

## Psychological Safety Requirement

[[Psychological Safety]] is a prerequisite for effective team training. Lacerenza et al. (2018) recommend explicitly fostering it during training — members must feel safe to practice skills, make errors, and receive corrective feedback without fear of embarrassment. This is especially critical in simulation-based settings.

## Relationship to Learning Science

Team training instantiates the learning science principles from [[kraiger-ford-2021-workplace-instruction|Kraiger & Ford 2021]]: declarative → procedural → compiled knowledge via practice + feedback. Transfer requires similarity between training and performance environments (identical elements principle). Team self-correction training builds metacognitive regulation at team level.
