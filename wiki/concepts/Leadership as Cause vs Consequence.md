# Leadership as Cause vs Consequence

The second crossed distinction in [[carton-2022-science-of-leadership|Carton's (2022)]] framework: is leadership studied as an **independent variable** (a *cause* of outcomes) or a **dependent variable** (a *consequence* to be explained)?

Crossed with [[Leaders vs Leading]], it yields four categories:

| | Consequence (DV) | Cause (IV) |
|---|---|---|
| **Leader** | Leader as *attribute* — who becomes / is seen as a leader (categorization, **emergence**, evaluation) | Leader-level attributes → outcomes |
| **Leading** | Leading as DV — *directing attention* / attributions about the process | Leader *behaviors* → outcomes |

Most research crowds into **leading-as-cause** (behaviors predicting effectiveness); **leading-as-consequence** is comparatively neglected.

> [!info] Overlap with emergence
> The **leader-as-consequence** cell (who emerges as / is perceived a leader) is precisely the territory of [[Distal-Proximal Framework of Leader Emergence|leader emergence]] and [[Implicit Leadership Theories]] — a direct bridge between Carton's map and [[badura-et-al-2022-leadership-emergence-integrative-review|Badura et al. (2022)]].
