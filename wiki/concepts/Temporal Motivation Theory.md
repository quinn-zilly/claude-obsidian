# Temporal Motivation Theory (TMT)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multiple Goal Self-Regulation]]

Steel & König (2006). Integrates expectancy-value theory (Vroom 1964) with hyperbolic discounting (Ainslie 1992). Unifies procrastination, deadline effects, and goal prioritization under one formal model.

## Core Equation

**Utility = (Expectancy × Value) / (1 + delay)**

- Expectancy: probability of success
- Value: attractiveness of outcome
- Delay: temporal distance to outcome
- Denominator: hyperbolic discounting — value of future outcomes decreases as they move further away

## Key Predictions

1. **Near-term activities dominate** — immediate rewards crowd out long-term, higher-value tasks
2. **Difficult goals undertaken sooner** — higher value attached to attaining difficult goals
3. **Breaking distal deadlines into subgoals** — reduces discounting; increases likelihood of early start
4. **Procrastination** = rational within model; individual differences in impulsivity = steeper discounting

## Procrastination (Steel 2007 meta-analysis)
- + impulsiveness, boredom, distractibility
- − conscientiousness, need for achievement
- Mediates noncognitive traits → academic performance
- Reduced by: concrete thinking about behavior (vs. abstract), unpacking tasks into subtasks

## Deadline Effects
- Proximal deadlines → urgency → commitment
- But time pressure → narrowed attention → can impair creativity
- Zero time pressure also hurts (insufficient engagement)
- Optimal: meaningful work + sufficient but not excessive time pressure (Amabile et al. 2002)

## Planning Fallacy
- Underestimation of completion time; worse for open (distributed across occasions) vs. closed tasks
- Remedy: unpack tasks into subtasks (Kruger & Evans 2004)
- Start timing predicted by estimated duration; finish timing not (for open tasks)

## Integration with Control Theory

Vancouver, Weinhardt & Schmidt (2010) extend TMT into dynamic multi-goal model: expectancy computed dynamically as discrepancy / pace; value includes discrepancy magnitude. Explains deadline-shift from most-discrepant to most-achievable allocation strategy.
