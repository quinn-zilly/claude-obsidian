# Justice Measurement Taxonomy

The 2×2 framework for **how justice and fairness get operationalized** (Colquitt & Rodell 2015, Table 8.2). Crosses two distinctions.

## The Two Distinctions

1. **Emphasis** — descriptive **justice** (perceived rule adherence) vs. evaluative **fairness** (global appropriateness).
2. **Dimensionality** — keep the [[Four Justice Dimensions]] separate vs. collapse them.

|  | Assessing Justice | Assessing Fairness |
|---|---|---|
| **Dimensions kept** | **Faceted Justice** | **Faceted Fairness** |
| **Dimensions collapsed** | **Latent Justice** | **Overall Fairness** |

## The Four Approaches

### Faceted Justice
Measures each dimension's rule adherence separately. Moorman (1991) first; **Colquitt (2001)** is the modern standard four-factor scale. **Strength:** grounds justice in specific, *actionable* principles (intervention-ready — to be more procedurally just, provide voice, be consistent, etc.). **Drawback:** the dimensions correlate very highly (.37–.64, avg .49), creating **multicollinearity** and "bouncing betas."

### Latent Justice
Models the four dimensions as indicators of a **second-order latent factor** (Colquitt & Shaw 2005; loadings .64–.86). Appropriate when hypotheses don't reference specific dimensions, dimensions are highly correlated, and they're functionally substitutable — consistent with exchange-based logic. A "growth stock."

### Overall Fairness
A single global scale ("Overall, I'm treated fairly by my organization"). Already "realizing growth"; typically entity-structured (transcends specific events). Often included alongside faceted justice.

### Faceted Fairness
Dimensional distinctions kept but items reference global appropriateness, not rules (e.g., Price & Mueller 1986 distributive fairness). **Least growth potential** — the border between interpersonal fairness and overall fairness is "blurry" (the multiple meanings of *treat*).

## Three Conversions (Fundamental Tailoring)

Every measure is tailored on **focus** (organization vs. supervisor), **context** (selection, appraisal, compensation…), and **experience bracketing** (single event → multiple events → entity). See [[colquitt-rodell-2015-measuring-justice-fairness|Colquitt & Rodell (2015)]].

## When to Use Which

- Faceted/latent **justice** → best as **independent variables**.
- **Fairness** approaches → best as **mediators** (the sentiment encouraging reciprocation, per Blau) or **manipulation checks**.
- Exception: predicting **rule adherence itself** uses justice measures even as DVs.

## Emerging Issues

Anticipatory justice/expectations; within-person ([[Experience-Sampling Methodology|ESM]]) designs; multiple-source designs (employee–supervisor agreement is modest); and **operationalizing injustice** (current scales suffer "continuum truncation" — they sample adherence, not violation).
