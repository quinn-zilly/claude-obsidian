# Publish or Perish

"Publish or perish" names the incentive regime in which scientists, labs, institutions, and countries are evaluated by counts of papers and citations, so that career survival depends on frequent publication in high-ranking journals. It is the structural pressure that feeds [[Positive-Outcome Bias]] and [[Questionable Research Practices]].

## The Mechanism

Bibliometric evaluation (paper/citation counts) is increasingly adopted across fields and countries as a way to allocate funding and jobs. Because positive, novel, clean results are easier to publish, the pressure rewards producing such results — by chasing safe hypotheses, by selective reporting, or in the worst case by fabrication.

## Evidence It Distorts Science

- Per-capita output up while career duration falls (physical sciences).
- Rising journal rejection rates in top-tier journals.
- [[fanelli-2012-negative-results-disappearing|Fanelli (2012)]]: the US (a longer, more intense publish-or-perish system) shows stronger positive-outcome bias than the UK; Fanelli links national differences partly to publication pressure.
- Fanelli (2010): scientists in higher-output US states report more positive results — a proxy for bias.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

- Drives the [[Publication Bottleneck]] (more papers chasing limited journal slots).
- Upstream cause of [[Publication Bias]] and [[Positive-Outcome Bias]].
- Motivates incentive-realigning reforms: [[Registered Reports]] shift the reward from "positive findings" to "rigorous question + method" (see [[soderberg-et-al-2021-registered-reports-quality]]).
