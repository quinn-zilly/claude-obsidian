# Distal-Proximal Framework of Leader Emergence

The organizing scheme [[badura-et-al-2022-leadership-emergence-integrative-review|Badura, Galvin & Lee (2022)]] use to integrate the nomological network of [[Formal vs Informal Leader Emergence|leader emergence]].

**Distal antecedents** are stable, upstream individual differences — demographics (notably [[Gender and Leadership|gender]]) and traits (e.g., [[Five-Factor Model|Big Five]] extraversion, dominance, self-monitoring). **Proximal antecedents** are downstream and more malleable — behaviors and transient states — that sit closer to the emergence event.

The central claim: **distal antecedents predict emergence largely *through* proximal ones.** Traits do not directly make someone emerge; they shape behaviors and perceptions that followers then grant leadership to (linking to [[Leadership Identity Construction|claiming/granting]] and [[Implicit Leadership Theories]]).

> [!key-insight] Why the distinction matters
> It exposes where the literature is thin: the field has mapped many distal *antecedents* but under-studied the **proximal mediators** (the *why*) and **moderators** (the *when*) that convert stable attributes into actual emergence.
