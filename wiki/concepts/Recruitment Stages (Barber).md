# Recruitment Stages (Barber)

Barber's (1998) widely-used taxonomy dividing recruitment into **three stages**:

1. **Generating applicants** — getting an opening noticed and prompting application.
2. **Maintaining applicant status** — keeping applicants engaged through the process.
3. **Influencing job choices** — the final accept/reject decision.

Recruiters concentrate effort on **Stages 1–2**; Stage 3 is under-researched because non-persuaded candidates have already vanished from the funnel.

## Why stage matters (Uggerslev et al. 2012)

Stage **moderates** which predictors drive [[Applicant Attraction|attraction]], explained via the **[[Elaboration Likelihood Model|ELM]]**:

- **Early (low EL):** heuristics/simple cues → **[[Recruiter Behaviors|recruiter behaviors]]** carry the most incremental variance.
- **Later (rising EL):** effortful scrutiny → **organizational characteristics** (Stage 2) and **recruitment-process** characteristics grow more predictive; recruiter cues fade.
- **[[Perceived Fit (Recruitment)|Perceived fit]]** is the strongest predictor at *both* Stage 1 and Stage 2.

## Practical corollary

Spend the **first recruitment dollar** on fostering fit; spend **incremental dollars** on recruiter behaviors early, then org characteristics and process later.
