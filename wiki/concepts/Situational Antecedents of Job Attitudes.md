# Situational Antecedents of Job Attitudes

Situations influence job attitudes independently of dispositions. Interactionist perspective: dispositions and situations jointly shape attitudes — neither alone is sufficient.

## Job Characteristics

**[[Job Characteristics Model]]** (Hackman & Oldham 1976): five core features (skill variety, task identity, task significance, autonomy, feedback) → psychological states → satisfaction.

Subjective perceptions more predictive than objective analyst-based ratings (Judge et al. 2000). Cannot easily separate subjective perceptions from dispositional influence (personality shapes what people perceive). Job enlargement interventions historically effective at improving satisfaction (Neuman et al. 1989).

## Social Environment

Often as predictive as job content:

**Coworkers:** Meta-analysis (Chiaburu & Harrison 2008): coworker support → satisfaction, job involvement, organizational commitment. Coworker support effect stronger than coworker antagonism effect. Social network ties: job-related affect clusters among frequent interaction partners (Totterdell et al. 2004).

**Demographic similarity:** Ethnic dissimilarity → lower org. commitment but not job satisfaction (Liao et al. 2004). Extraversion/openness dissimilarity → lower coworker satisfaction. Age similarity → higher engagement (Avery et al. 2007). Note: personality *dissimilarity* can sometimes benefit (supervisor–subordinate control orientation; Glomb & Welsh 2005).

## Leadership

**Leader consideration** (showing concern, respect, appreciation): rc = .78 with subordinate satisfaction — "nearly synonymous" (Judge et al. 2004).

**Initiating structure:** rc = .33 with satisfaction — weaker but positive.

**Transformational leadership:** More positive employee emotions during workday; buffers emotion regulation–dissatisfaction link (Bono et al. 2007). LMX–employee attitude relationship strengthens when employees identify supervisor with organization (Eisenberger et al. 2010).

Declines in supervisor support during org. entry associated with declines in satisfaction (Jokisaari & Nurmi 2009). Abusive supervision → negative attitudes (Tepper 2000).

## Organizational Practices

**Organizational justice:**
- Distributive justice rc = .79 with pay satisfaction (Williams et al. 2006)
- Procedural justice rc = .42 with pay satisfaction
- Person–org. value congruence → positive attitudes (Edwards & Cable 2009)
- Interpersonal justice → org. commitment and supervisor satisfaction (Liao & Rupp 2005)
- Group-level justice climate moderates individual justice → attitudes (Mayer et al. 2007)

**HR practices:** Establishment-level high-performance HR practices → higher employee satisfaction and commitment (Takeuchi et al. 2009). Org. change favorableness, extent, and individual relevance → commitment (Fedor et al. 2006).

## Time

**Honeymoon-hangover pattern** (Boswell et al. 2005, 2009): high initial satisfaction after hire → rapid deterioration.

Org. commitment declines among newcomers (Bentein et al. 2005). Affective Events Theory: emotion-laden daily events explain day-to-day satisfaction variability.
