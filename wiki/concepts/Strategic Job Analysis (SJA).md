# Strategic Job Analysis (SJA)

## Definition

A purposeful, systematic process for collecting current and future work-related job information within an organization's **strategic context**. Extends traditional job analysis by aligning job requirements with org strategy and anticipated environmental change.

> "Strategic job analysis means aligning current and future jobs with the strategic orientation of the organization." — Singh (2008)

## Why Needed

Traditional JA assumes static jobs, stable KSAs, and 1-person-1-job structure. Modern workplaces feature:
- Team-based work
- Rapid tech change / short product cycles
- Jobs that don't yet exist
- "Boundaryless" and flat orgs
- Alternative work arrangements

## Core Process (Singh 2008 Framework)

1. **Environmental scan** — Delphi, scenarios, forecasting models, SME workshops
2. **Current job analysis** — standard JA + team-oriented KSA analysis
3. **Gap analysis** — compare present vs. future; three outcomes (no/moderate/significant change)
4. **Validity evaluation** — ongoing; link to selection and appraisal outcomes

## Key Distinction from Traditional JA

| Dimension | Traditional JA | SJA |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Time horizon | Current/past | Current + future |
| Job structure | Individual | Individual + team |
| KSA focus | Specific tasks | Broad/enduring abilities |
| Environmental stance | Reactive | Proactive |
| Strategic link | None | Explicit |

## Precursors / Related Approaches

- Schneider & Konz (1989) — first formal SJA framework (8-step)
- Snow & Snell (1993) — staffing models with strategy integration
- Sanchez (1994) — work analysis + scenario planning
- Siddique (2004) — competency-focused JA

## Org Strategy Fit

Best fit with **prospector** orgs (Miles & Snow 1978): decentralized, broad skills, rapid change.
Less applicable to **defender** orgs where traditional JA suffices.
