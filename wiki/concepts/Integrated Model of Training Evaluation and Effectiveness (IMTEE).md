# Integrated Model of Training Evaluation and Effectiveness (IMTEE)

The IMTEE ([[alvarez-et-al-2004-training-evaluation-effectiveness|Alvarez et al. (2004)]]) is a four-level model integrating all major prior training evaluation and effectiveness frameworks using a strict empirical inclusion criterion (≥3 studies finding significant relationships in the same direction across 73 studies, 1993–2002).

## Core Distinction

| | Training Evaluation | Training Effectiveness |
|-|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Focus | Outcomes (what happened) | Variables (why it happened) |
| View | Micro / measurement | Macro / system |
| Purpose | Describe results | Prescribe improvements |

Without effectiveness variables, weak inter-criterion correlations are uninterpretable (Holton, 1996).

## Model Structure

**Level 1 — Needs Analysis**: Feeds all three evaluation umbrella areas.

**Level 2–3 — Evaluation Measures** (organized into Kraiger's three umbrella areas):
- *Training Content & Design* → evaluated by **Reactions** (utility/relevance only; affective reactions r≈0 with learning)
- *Changes in Learners* → **Posttraining Attitudes** (self-efficacy) ↔ **Cognitive Learning** (reciprocal) → **Training Performance**
- *Organizational Payoffs* → **Transfer Performance** → **Results**

**Level 4 — Ten Effectiveness Variables** (consistently across studies):
Pretraining self-efficacy, Pretraining motivation, Experience, Cognitive ability, Age (negative for cognitive learning), Posttraining mastery orientation, Learning principles, Posttraining maintenance interventions, High difficulty, Positive transfer environment

## Key Empirical Anchors (Alliger et al. 1997 meta-analysis)
- Affective reactions → learning: r ≈ 0
- Utility reactions → learning: r = .26; → transfer: r = .18
- Cognitive learning → training performance: r = .18; → transfer: r = .11
- Training performance → transfer: r = .18

These small rs confirm: evaluation measures alone do not identify the constructs affecting training results.

## Unique Contributions
1. First model built from strict empirical criteria
2. First to propose relationships between posttraining attitudes and effectiveness variables
3. First to integrate all 6 evaluation measures with all 10 effectiveness variables
