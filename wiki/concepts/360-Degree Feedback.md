# 360-Degree Feedback

**360-degree (multisource) feedback** collects performance ratings from multiple role relationships — supervisors, peers, direct reports, customers, and self — rather than the supervisor alone. Rationale (Borman 1974): different sources observe **different aspects** of performance (customers see service; direct reports see a manager's coaching).

## Evidence and cautions
- Foundation traced to rating-source research (Springer 1953; Lawler 1967). Gained popularity in the 1980s; still widely used.
- **Sources differ systematically**; self-ratings are typically most lenient. No source shows high standalone reliability (Greguras & Robie 1998).
- Does multisource feedback actually **improve** performance? The jury is out (DeNisi & Kluger 2000; meta-analysis Smither, London & Reilly 2005 finds small, conditional effects).
- Primarily a **developmental** tool; quality drops when used for **decision-making** rather than development (Greguras et al. 2003). Coaching/facilitation can help (Seifert et al. 2003).
