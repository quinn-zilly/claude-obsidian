# Goal Displacement

When means become ends in themselves, displacing the original goals they were intended to serve. Identified by Blau & Scott (1962). Used by Kerr (1995) to explain university grading dynamics.

## Mechanism

1. Org has goal G (transfer knowledge)
2. Metric M proxies G (grades)
3. M gets rewarded (grad school, jobs, parental approval)
4. Rational actors optimize M, not G
5. M displaces G — grades matter; knowledge does not

## University Example (Kerr)

Grades were designed as feedback/motivation for knowledge transfer. Once grades became inputs to high-stakes decisions (grad admissions, employment), they became ends. Result: plagiarism, grade-seeking, grade inflation, lawsuits over C grades. Rational response to displaced goal structure.

## Broader Pattern

Goal displacement is a downstream effect of [[Reward System Misalignment]]. The reward attaches to the proxy metric; the metric then colonizes behavior; the underlying goal atrophies.

Related in measurement literature to **Goodhart's Law**: "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."

## Source

Blau, P. M., & Scott, W. R. (1962). *Formal organizations*. Chandler. Cited in Kerr (1975/1995).
