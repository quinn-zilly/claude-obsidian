# Equitable Data Collection

Data collection practices that ensure samples represent the full population of interest, including subgroups, and that analytic decisions do not introduce or perpetuate bias.

## Core Practices

**Representativeness**
- Verify sample demographics match target population
- Compare respondents vs. nonrespondents (survey response bias check)
- Explicitly document when subgroup data is missing — treat as limitation, not oversight

**Bias Detection**
- Document all analytic decisions (coding, cleaning, analysis)
- Peer quality-control checks across all participants
- Reference bias: in cultures with "always room for improvement" norms, respondents may underrate even effective programs
- Mischievous responders: flag implausible response patterns

**Disaggregated Analysis**
- Default: analyze by key characteristics (race/ethnicity, gender, location, experience level)
- Differential program impact is theoretically meaningful, not a nuisance
- Aggregated-only analysis can mask harm to subgroups

## Why This Matters for Validity

Missing data from marginalized groups → biased conclusions about program effectiveness. A reading intervention with 80% coverage but minimal ELL data cannot claim to show effectiveness for ELL students.
