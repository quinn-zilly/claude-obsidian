# Competency Modelling

## Definition

An approach that identifies broad behavioral competencies (rather than specific tasks/KSAs) required for effective performance. Often used as a bridge between traditional JA and strategic HRM.

## Key Competencies Emphasized

- Interpersonal skills
- Conflict resolution
- Innovative thinking
- Flexibility / adaptability
- Decision-making ability
- Self-motivation
- Teamwork orientation

## Relationship to Job Analysis

Cronshaw (1998): competencies "better suited to newer, more fluid organizational structures."

Siddique (2004): competency-focused JA → positive org performance. Recommends shifting from task/KSA focus to competency focus for strategic alignment.

Shippmann et al. (2000): SIOP task force called for closer attention to strategic/future-oriented needs via competency modelling.

## Vs. Traditional KSA Approach

| KSA Approach | Competency Modelling |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Task-derived | Strategy-derived |
| Specific | Broad / behavioral |
| Static | Dynamic |
| Legally defensible (established) | Legally defensible (evolving) |

## In Needs Assessment (Noe 2023)

[[noe-2023-needs-assessment|Noe]] positions competency modelling as a **worker-focused** ("how objectives are met") complement to task-focused ("what is accomplished") [[Task Analysis (Training)|task analysis]]. A competency = a set of skills, knowledge, abilities, and personal characteristics enabling successful job performance. A **competency model** lists competencies for a job/family/occupation, the behaviors showing proficiency, and mastery **levels** (e.g., 0–4 proficiency ratings for a systems engineer, Table 3.9).

**Competency modelling vs. job analysis** (Noe, citing Mirabile 1997; Shippmann et al. 2000):

| Job Analysis | Competency Model |
|--------------|------------------|
| Describes what *differs* across jobs | Competencies *common* to a group/org |
| Generates specific KSAs for selection | General, applies to selection, training, dev, perf. mgmt |
| Task/work-focused ("what") | Worker-focused ("how") |
| Less tied to strategy | More likely linked to business goals |

**Five-step development process** (Fig. 3.5): identify business strategy → identify jobs/positions → identify effective vs. ineffective performers → identify competencies driving each → **validate** the model. Raters should be trained to distinguish proficiency levels.

Uses for training/dev: pinpoint behaviors for 360 feedback, gauge current/future skill needs, map skills across career points, guide coaching, build succession road maps, integrate HR criteria.
