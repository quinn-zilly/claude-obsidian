# Causal Generalization

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

The problem of moving from the specific instances of an experiment to broader claims — the organizing theme of Shadish, Cook & Campbell's *Experimental and Quasi-Experimental Designs for **Generalized** Causal Inference* (2002).

## Two modes (Cronbach 1982)

- **Representation** — from observed operations *up* to the constructs they instantiate. Governed by [[Construct Validity (Causal Generalization)]].
- **Extrapolation** — from the studied instances *out* to different persons, settings, treatments, outcomes. Governed by [[External Validity]].

SCC's key move over Cook & Campbell (1979): both modes apply to **all** study elements — units, treatments, observations, and settings — not just cause and effect.

## Relation to the typology

The two generalization validity types (construct, external) sit alongside the two "local molar causal inference" types (statistical conclusion, internal). See [[Validity Typology]]. Establishing that a cause worked *here* (internal validity) is logically prior to asking what it represents and where else it holds.
