# Organizational Memory

Organizational memory is the stored knowledge — in people, routines, documents, systems, and culture — that an organization carries across time and uses to inform present action. A [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]] is one mechanism of organizational memory: knowledge held in individual members plus a shared directory of who knows what.

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] devote a section to scaling TMS from the work group to the organizational level, where it intersects organizational memory most directly. Generalization is hard because organizations are far larger than work groups, contain multiple subgroups with limited cross-boundary communication, are geographically distributed, and hold much knowledge tacitly (and so harder to access across distance). The review surveys technological approaches (expertise directories/repositories, Web 2.0 tools, expertise mining, small-world networks) and interpersonal approaches (locating the knowledgeable person, member rotation, building accessibility and trust) for sustaining organizational-level transactive memory. Relating group-level TMS to organizational-level TMS is named as a key open research direction.
