# Vertical vs Shared Leadership

Nav: [[index]] | [[Leadership MOC]]

Two sources of team leadership ([[zhu-et-al-2018-shared-leadership-review|Zhu et al. 2018]]):

- **Vertical leadership** — top-down influence stemming from a single formal team leader.
- **[[Shared Leadership]]** — horizontal, lateral influence stemming from team members.

## Key Point: Complements, Not Substitutes

Vertical and shared leadership are **not alternatives**; both are important, can operate in tandem, and should be studied together. Ensley et al. (2006) found both independently predicted new-venture performance. Formal leaders are often *facilitators* of shared leadership (via empowering, servant, and transformational behaviors), though LMX **differentiation** can undermine it.

## Interplay and Dynamics

- In teams *with* a formal leader, shared leadership emerges when leadership roles are "distributed" or "passed" downward — "the encouragement of leadership from below."
- In teams *without* one (self-managed teams), members take on the full range of leader roles.
- Teams may **shift** between vertical and shared leadership across phases and situations — e.g. under time pressure or crisis, a shift toward vertical leadership may allow faster resource allocation. Identifying triggers of this shift is an open research question.

In [[Adaptive Leadership Theory|DeRue's]] framework, vertical and shared leadership are simply different points in a magnitude/dispersion space (centralized vs. shared patterns), and a group can move between them as needs change.
