# Personality–Performance Process Models

Models specifying **the mechanisms through which personality influences job performance** — the mediators and moderators of the trait → performance link. The past decade produced a surge of these, shifting focus from *whether* personality predicts to *how* it does ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson 2013]]).

## Direct vs indirect determinants

Performance is determined **directly** by knowledge, skill, motivation (Campbell 1990; Motowidlo, Borman & Schmit 1997). Personality is an **indirect** determinant (like ability, education, experience) that operates *through* the direct determinants.

## Major models

- **[[Jeff W. Johnson|Johnson]] (2003)**: adds **work habits** as a fourth direct determinant (distinct from motivation). Splits motivation (per Mitchell & Daniels 2003) into **proactive cognitive processes** (expectancies, self-efficacy, goal setting), **online cognitive processes** (self-regulation), and **psychological motives** (values, interests, attitudes) as a mediating third component. Personality has differential relationships with different motivation components (Johnson, Duehr, Hezlett, Muros & Ferstl 2008).
- **Barrick, Mitchell & Stewart (2003)**: three motivational mediators — **accomplishment striving, status striving, communion striving** — with **[[Situation Strength|situational demands]]** (cooperative vs competitive) moderating which mediates. Trait–behavior link is stronger when the situation is relevant to the trait and allows behavioral variance.
- **[[Trait Activation Theory|Tett & Burnett (2003) trait activation theory]]**: traits are activated by trait-relevant situational **cues**; trait expression counts as performance only when the organization values it → positive trait–performance correlations when expression meets work demands, negative when it interferes.
- **Job satisfaction as mediator**: satisfaction partially mediates personality → OCB and personality → [[Counterproductive Work Behavior|CWB]] (Ilies et al. 2009; Mount, Ilies & Johnson 2006).
- **Van Iddekinge, Ferris & Heffner (2009)**: multistage leader-performance model — leadership experience and motivation are proximal, partially mediating distal personality and cognitive ability.

## Integration

**Johnson & Hezlett (2008)** folded these into a single high-level general model: personality is a determinant of **every** direct determinant of performance *and* a potential moderator of many paths. The models are broadly **consistent**, each emphasizing a different aspect of the process. Johnson & Schneider propose advancing the area by **pitting competing theories against each other** — shifting from confirmation to **disconfirmation**.
