# Team Development Interventions

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

> **Core claim:** Not all team development interventions are equal. Two types — [[Team Building]] and [[Team Training]] — target different needs via different mechanisms and produce distinct outcome profiles. Science-based design is required for effectiveness.

## Definition

Structured efforts to improve team performance by developing the attitudes, behaviors, and cognitions needed for effective teamwork. Targets are typically team-level KSAs: knowledge of team roles/member expertise, observable teamwork skills (adaptability, communication), and attitudes (trust, cohesion, collective efficacy).

## Design Framework (Salas & Cannon-Bowers 1997)

Effective interventions integrate four interdependent elements:
1. **Tools** — team task analysis, simulations, feedback mechanisms, principles
2. **Methods** — information-based, demonstration-based (video), practice-based (guided practice, role play)
3. **Strategies** — cross-training, coordination training, team leader training, etc.
4. **Content** — competencies, knowledge, skills, attitudes to be developed

Each element constrains and informs the others. Cannot be selected independently.

## Four TDI Types (Lacerenza et al. 2018)

Extends the Shuffler et al. (2011) two-type framework. Two overarching categories:
- **Training interventions** — for leaders, ad hoc, or intact teams: [[Leadership Training (Teams)]], [[Team Training]]
- **Process interventions** — for intact teams only: [[Team Building]], [[Team Debriefing]]

See individual concept pages for full treatment.

## Two Major Types (Shuffler et al. 2011 framework)

See [[Team Building]] and [[Team Training]] for full treatment.

| | Team Building | Team Training |
|---|---|---|
| **Timing** | During/after performance problems | Before and during performance |
| **Target** | Interpersonal/process problems in intact teams | Knowledge, skills, attitudes for teamwork |
| **Best for** | Affect + process outcomes | Cognitive + performance outcomes |
| **Task interdependence** | Low | High |
| **Overall ρ** | 0.31 | 0.34 |

## Teamwork vs. Taskwork

**Taskwork** = performing the actual team task. **Teamwork** = team-level attitudes, behaviors, cognitions that affect *how* tasks are performed. Interventions primarily target teamwork. Teamwork breakdown ≠ taskwork failure — teams can be technically skilled but fail due to teamwork deficits (e.g., 1978 United Airlines crash: crew missed fuel warnings while focused on landing gear).

## Science Infrastructure

Key scientific contributions supporting TDI design:
- **Team KSA frameworks** (Cannon-Bowers et al. 1995) — define required competencies
- **Team task analysis** (Cannon-Bowers & Bowers 2010) — identify tasks, behaviors, KSAs
- **Team selection methods** — personality tests, simulated performance
- **Performance measurement systems** — theoretically grounded, multi-method assessment
- **Diagnostic feedback** — immediate post-performance; diagnosis + improvement

## Relationship to Adjacent Theories

- **[[Trait Activation Theory]]**: TAT provides the mechanism for *which* traits should be activated in which team context; TAT's team-building application (mutual trait activation, chemistry) sits above TDI science as a selection/composition theory; once team is composed, TDIs develop the behavioral repertoire
- **[[Transactive Memory Systems]]**: cross-training directly develops TMS (shared knowledge of teammates' expertise); team cognition is a primary training outcome
- **[[Organizational Learning]]**: team self-correction training = organizational learning process at team level; teams that diagnose their own breakdowns = learning units
- **[[Teamwork Processes (Marks et al. Taxonomy)]]**: process diagnosis enables targeted TDI selection — transition deficits → team building; action deficits → team training; LePine et al. 2008 establishes the ρ values linking each process type to performance
- **[[Goal-Setting Theory]]**: goal-setting component of team building leverages GST (specific + challenging goals → motivation); TAT discretionary cues + GST goals both contribute to team motivation
