# Miles and Snow Strategic Typology (1978)

## Overview

Framework classifying orgs by how they relate to their market environment. Each type has distinct strategy, structure, technology, and process configurations.

## Four Types

### Defender
- Stable, narrow domain
- Limited product lines
- Capital-intensive, functional structure
- Emphasis: production efficiency, cost control, skill specialization
- Long-term perspective, top-down control
- **JA implication**: Traditional JA appropriate — jobs are stable

### Prospector
- Seeks new product/market opportunities
- Decentralized, low formalization
- Emphasis: innovation, flexibility, broad skills
- Rapid change tolerance
- **JA implication**: SJA best fit — broad KSAs, multi-skilling, empowerment, teams

### Analyzer
- Hybrid of Defender + Prospector
- Stable core + selective innovation

### Reactor
- No consistent strategy
- Considered a strategic failure (not studied in depth)

## HRM Implications (Singh 2008 via Ragburam & Arvey 1994)

| Strategy | Staffing | Training | JA Type |
|----------|----------|----------|---------|
| Defender | Narrow skills, internal | Specialist | Traditional |
| Prospector | Broad skills, flexible | Generalist | Strategic |

- Miles, R., & Snow, C. (1978). *Organizational Strategy, Structure and Process.*
