# Complementary vs Supplementary Matching

The matching rule at the heart of the [[Leading Diversity (LeaD) Model|LeaD model]]: leader behavior should **counteract ineffective team processes and maximize effective ones**. Because diversity instigates one of two [[Categorization-Elaboration Model|processes]], and leadership splits into two broad behaviors, there are four cells — two matches and two mismatches.

## The 2×2

| Dominant process → | Person-focused behavior | Task-focused behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **Intergroup bias** | **Complementary match** ✓ | Mismatch ✗ |
| **Information elaboration** | Mismatch ✗ | **Supplementary match** ✓ |

- **Complementary matching** (bias × person-focused): supply what the team *lacks* — relational bonding. Mechanisms: **recategorization** (common ingroup identity), **decategorization/individuation** (see members as unique persons), **reducing identity threat** (psychological safety, trust). Proposition **P4**.
- **Supplementary matching** (elaboration × task-focused): add to what is *already working* — structured information exchange. Mechanisms: **task structure/goals** (shared reality), **feedback**, **accountability → epistemic motivation**. Proposition **P5**.

## Why the mismatches fail

- **Task-focus during bias**: people reject information from disliked outgroup members, so pushing information exchange can escalate conflict rather than help.
- **Person-focus during elaboration**: redundant (the team already has trust) and can be counterproductive — too much cohesion breeds consensus-seeking, groupthink, and the common-knowledge/hidden-profile bias, suppressing the very information diversity was supposed to add.

## Payoff

This rule **dissolves the field's contradictions**. When the same behavior helped in one study and hurt in another (e.g., person-focused leadership in Klein et al. 2011 vs Homan & Greer 2013), the teams differed in their dominant process, not in the behavior's "true" effect. Matching requires the leader to first predict/diagnose the process — see [[Diversity-Related Leader Competencies]]. The person/task split itself is the same structure as [[Leader Behavior and Ohio State Studies|consideration vs initiating structure]].
