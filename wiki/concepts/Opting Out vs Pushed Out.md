# Opting Out vs Pushed Out

The framing device at the heart of **[[kossek-su-wu-2017-opting-out-pushed-out|Kossek, Su & Wu (2017)]]**.

## The two explanations
- **"Opting out" (pulls)** — women leave or avoid leadership tracks because of internal **preferences**, values, and perceived misfit (the **[[Career Preference Perspective (Gender)]]**). Popularized as the **[[Opt-Out Revolution]]** (Belkin 2003).
- **"Pushed out" (pushes)** — women are excluded by organizational **bias**, stereotyping, hostile climates, overwork, and the **[[Motherhood Wage Penalty|penalties]]** attached to using flexibility (the gender-bias and work–family perspectives).

## Kossek et al.'s thesis
The two are **not rival hypotheses but coexisting, interconnected forces.** What look like women's individual "choices" (pulls) are shaped by the social contexts (pushes) they are embedded in — e.g., a woman "opts out" of a role that has been made unwelcoming. Sandberg's epigraph captures it: "Women rarely make one big decision to leave the workforce. Instead, they make a lot of small decisions along the way."

Prescription: unpack the **cross-level dynamics** of both pulls (selection into supportive contexts) and pushes (chilling effects of multiple constraints) rather than pitting perspectives against each other.
