# Construct Validity

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

## Two Competing Definitions

### Interpretation Camp (Messick 1989; Kane 1992)
Validity is a property of **interpretations** of test scores, not of tests themselves. Endorsed by Clark & Watson (1995, 2019) and the *Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing* (AERA/APA/NCME). Because measurement context affects scores, validity claims are always conditional on the sample and context.

### Test Validity View (Borsboom et al. 2004)
Revives Kelley (1927): validity is simply whether "test X measures attribute Y." Requires (a) Y exists and (b) variations in Y *cause* variations in X. Validity is a property of the test, not inferences. Clark & Watson (2019) explicitly reject this view citing the Standards.

> [!note] No resolution
> As of 2021 (Jebb et al.), this debate shows no signs of resolution. See Lissitz & Samuelson (2007), Hood (2009), Cizek (2012) for integration attempts.

## Validity Is Unitary

Both camps agree: **there are not different "types" of validity.** Language like "content validity," "criterion-related validity," and "discriminant validity" is misleading — it implies these produce categorically different kinds of evidence pointing to independent inferences. In reality, all evidence contributes to the single central inference about score meaning.

As of 2013, **122 distinct subtypes** had been named in the literature (Newton & Shaw 2013), many coined after the 1985 Standards explicitly stated that type-language was inappropriate.

**Practical implication:** Do not think "I need to show discriminant validity." Think "I need to provide evidence bearing on discriminability — and I can use any analytic method to do that, including those traditionally labeled 'criterion validity' analyses."

## Construct Validity Since Cronbach & Meehl (1955)

Smith (2005) reviews 50 years of developments: begins with philosophy of science, centers on a five-step validation model (specify constructs → revise theory). Also critically reviews MTMM matrices and generalizability theory.

## Key Papers

- Cronbach & Meehl (1955) — original construct validity framework
- Borsboom et al. (2004) — test validity revival
