# Performance Management

**Performance management (PM)** is the broad, continuous set of activities, policies, and interventions aimed at identifying, measuring, and developing individual and team performance and **aligning it with the organization's strategic goals** (Aguinis 2013). It *begins* with [[Performance Appraisal]] but extends well beyond it to feedback, goal setting, coaching, incentives, and development.

> [!info] PM vs PA
> [[Performance Appraisal]] is a discrete, formal, infrequent **evaluation event** (once or twice a year). PM is the **ongoing process** in which appraisal is only one component. PA is a subset of PM.

## The central problem
Across three major reviews in the wiki, one theme dominates: **PM as practiced does not demonstrably improve performance, and its link to firm outcomes is unproven.**
- [[denisi-murphy-2017-100-years-appraisal|DeNisi & Murphy (2017)]]: 100 years of appraisal research never established that improving individual performance improves firm performance — "an elusive goal."
- [[pulakos-2019-evolution-of-pm|Pulakos et al. (2019)]]: PM is costly (~250 hrs/manager+employee per year), universally disliked, and shows "virtually no positive impact" on business outcomes (CEB 2012).
- [[schleicher-2019-pm-effectiveness-review|Schleicher et al. (2019)]]: we can't even *say* whether PM works, because the field never mapped what "effective" means — see [[PM Effectiveness Value Chains]].

## Where the value actually is
The convergent answer: **not in better measurement, but in behavior and cognition.**
- Pulakos et al.: clear expectations, real-time informal feedback, coaching, agile goals — the behaviors of good managers (Google Project Oxygen; CEB 2004).
- Schleicher et al.: possibly in **what managers learn by doing PM** and in the **quality of human-capital decisions** it enables.

## Components (typical formal system)
[[Cascading Goals & SMART]] · behavioral + results ratings · [[Competency Modeling]] · [[360-Degree Feedback]] · calibration · pay/bonus decisions. Reform movement is stripping these down — see [[Rating-less Reviews]] and [[Forced Distribution]] (in decline).
