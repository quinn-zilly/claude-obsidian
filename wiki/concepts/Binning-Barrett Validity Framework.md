# Binning-Barrett Validity Framework

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

A framework ([[binning-barrett-1989-validity-personnel-decisions|Binning & Barrett 1989]]) that recasts personnel-selection validation as a **system of logical inferences** and treats construct-, content-, and criterion-related validity as **evidential bases** for one overriding inference — not as distinct "types" of validity.

## The key inferences (selection version)

| # | Links | Testable? |
|---|-------|-----------|
| 5 | Predictor measure ↔ criterion measure | Empirically (the validity coefficient) |
| 6 | Predictor measure → predictor construct domain | Judgmentally / convergent-discriminant |
| 7 | Predictor construct domain → performance domain | **Theoretical only** (two hypothetical domains) |
| 8 | Criterion measure → performance domain | Partly empirical (criterion construct validation) |
| **9** | **Predictor measure → performance domain** | **The overriding inference; justified indirectly** |

Inference 9 is what validity evidence ultimately serves. It is supported *directly* (empirical Inference 5) or *indirectly* by confirming Inferences 6 + 7 + 8.

## Three evidential bases, one validity

Validity is **unitary** (converging with [[Construct Validity]]). The trinity are strategies for justifying Inference 9:

- **Criterion-related** — empirical predictor↔criterion link (Inference 5); a special case of **convergent construct evidence**, *not* inherently superior to the others.
- **Content-related** — rational demonstration the predictor samples the domain.
- **Construct-related** — convergent/discriminant support for Inferences 6 + 7.

## The criterion side and its corollary

The **[[Criterion Problem|criterion problem]]** is a **truncated nomological net** — neglect of Inferences 7, 8, 10 (job demands → performance domain), 11 (behavior-outcome links). Criteria must be validated with the same rigor as predictors.

> [!key-insight] Corollary
> When Inference 8 (criterion → performance domain) is weak, empirical Inference 5 becomes a poor surrogate for Inference 9 — so **construct-related evidence can beat criterion-related evidence** for defending a selection decision. Undercuts the *Uniform Guidelines* (1978) preference for criterion-related evidence.
