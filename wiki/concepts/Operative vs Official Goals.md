# Operative vs Official Goals

Distinction from Perrow (1961), used by Kerr (1995) to explain reward misalignment in politics.

**Official goals** — publicly stated, intentionally vague, inoffensive, high acceptance. Example: "All Americans are entitled to health care." Offend no one; commit to nothing.

**Operative goals** — specific, funded, trade-offs explicit, lower acceptance. Example: "We will fund X by taxing Y and cutting Z." Reveal costs, priorities, losers.

## The Political Trap

Citizens claim to want operative goals (candor, specifics). But voters punish candidates who give them (low acceptance). Candidates are therefore rationally rewarded for official goals and punished for operative ones. Classic [[Reward System Misalignment]]: electorate hopes for B (candor), rewards A (vagueness).

## Source

Perrow, C. (1961). The analysis of goals in complex organizations. *American Sociological Review, 26*, 854–866. Cited in Kerr (1975/1995).
