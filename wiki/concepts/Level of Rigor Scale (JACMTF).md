# Level of Rigor Scale (JACMTF)

The **Level of Rigor Scale** is the comparison framework produced by the SIOP **Job Analysis and Competency Modeling Task Force (JACMTF)** in [[Shippmann-et-al-2000-practice-of-competency-modeling|Shippmann et al. (2000)]] to distinguish [[Competency Modelling|competency modeling]] from [[Traditional Job Analysis|job analysis]] not by *what* they study but by **how rigorously** each is typically practiced.

## Structure

17 variables in two blocks (as tabulated by [[Sackett-Walmsley-Laczo-2013-job-and-work-analysis|Sackett et al. (2013)]], Table 4.1):

**Evaluative criteria** (front-end activities shaping inference quality) — 10 variables:
method of investigation/data collection; type of descriptor content; procedures for developing content; level of detail; **linking results to business goals**; extent of content review; ranking/prioritizing content; **assessment of reliability**; retention criteria for items; documentation of the research process.

**Non-evaluative criteria** (uses and characteristics) — 7 variables:
focus on core competencies; focus on technical skills; organizational fit vs job match; focus on values/personality orientation; face validity; training & development applications; selection & decision applications.

## The verdict

| Rated more rigorous for **Job Analysis** | Rated more rigorous for **Competency Modeling** |
|---|---|
| Every evaluative criterion **except** business-goal linkage (method, content, procedures, detail, review, prioritization, **reliability**, retention, documentation) | Linking results to business goals/strategy |
| Focus on technical skills | Focus on core competencies |
| Selection & decision applications | Organizational fit vs job match; values/personality focus; face validity; training/development applications |

> [!key-insight] Leverage, don't pick a winner
> The task force's constructive framing: use each camp's strength to shore up the other's weakness — competency modeling's **strategic alignment** and face validity enrich job analysis; job analysis's **methodological rigor and documentation** discipline competency modeling. This anticipates the "complementary, not competing" stance in [[Sanchez-Levine-2009-competency-modeling-vs-job-analysis|Sanchez & Levine (2009)]].

> [!warning] Caveat on the evidence base
> Some conclusions rest on a small number of expert opinions (37 SME interviews) — a limitation the authors flagged and [[Sackett-Walmsley-Laczo-2013-job-and-work-analysis|Sackett et al.]] reiterate.
