# Generalized Work Activities (GWAs)

**GWAs** are the **42 broad, cross-occupational work-activity descriptors** in [[Occupational Information Network (O-NET)|O*NET]], derived from a literature review of work-activity taxonomies (Cunningham & Ballentine; McCormick et al.) to form a **common metric applicable to all occupations**. They sit at the molar (general) end of the work-activity specificity continuum, above tasks/functions/duties.

> [!note] Reliability & redundancy
> Per [[Sanchez-Levine-2012-rise-fall-job-analysis|Sanchez & Levine (2012)]]: GWA interrater reliabilities are generally lower than task-level ratings (e.g., 0.65 for GWAs vs 0.80 for tasks in Dierdorff & Morgeson 2009), and O*NET importance vs level scales for GWAs are largely redundant (r ≈ 0.92).
