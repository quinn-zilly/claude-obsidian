# Traditional Job Analysis

Task- and KSA(O)-based analysis of **existing** jobs. Rooted in Taylorist scientific management: static jobs, clear boundaries, one-person-one-job. Produces long lists of tasks + psychologically-worded KSAOs.

Per Sanchez & Levine (2009), TJA is **descriptive, past-oriented, job-focused**, aimed at **typical** performance of an "average" incumbent, and treats the job as an external object measured via latent traits — the counterpoint to **Competency Modeling** on all six dimensions. See [[TJA vs CM (Six Dimensions)]]. TJA and CM **supplement** each other rather than compete; TJA supplies the KSAO "enablers" and the job-relatedness evidence CM lacks ([[Job-Relatedness]]).
