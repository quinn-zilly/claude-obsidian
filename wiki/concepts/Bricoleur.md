# Bricoleur

Denzin & Lincoln's (2000) figure for the qualitative researcher: a "**Jack of all trades or a kind of do-it-yourself person** [who deploys] whatever strategies, methods, or empirical materials are at hand. … If new tools or techniques have to be invented, or pieced together, then the researcher will do this."

Ponterotto (2005) uses **bricolage** to describe how counseling researchers draw across [[Research Paradigm|paradigms]] and methods. [[Consensual Qualitative Research|CQR]]'s creators acted as bricoleurs, integrating grounded theory, phenomenology, and comprehensive process analysis to balance postpositivist and constructivist lenses. The notion can unsettle researchers socialized into [[Positivism|positivist]]/[[Postpositivism|postpositivist]] paradigms but fits the methodological flexibility of [[Constructivism–Interpretivism|constructivist]] and [[Critical–Ideological Paradigm|critical]] traditions. Ponterotto predicts more counseling researchers will become bricoleurs as the field moves toward methodological pluralism.
