# IPO–IMOI Framework

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

The organizing meta-framework of team research, and its evolution.

## IPO (Input–Process–Output)

McGrath (1964); Hackman & Morris (1975). Teams described as `inputs → processes → outputs`. Grew from McGrath's task-contingency heritage and guided four decades of research. Increasingly criticized as a **limiting factor** — its linearity stifles dynamic, multilevel, reciprocal theorizing (Ilgen, Hollenbeck, Johnson & Jundt 2005; Mathieu, Maynard, Rapp & Gilson 2008).

## IMOI (Input–Mediator–Output–Input)

Ilgen et al. (2005): `inputs → mediators → outputs → (feedback as new inputs)`.
- **"Mediator"** replaces "process" because mediators include both dynamic **processes** *and* **[[Emergent States (Teams)|emergent states]]** (a process is an act; an emergent state is a property at a moment).
- The **added "I"** captures feedback: outputs (and emergent states) loop back as inputs to subsequent cycles, making the framework dynamic and episodic.

## In the Centennial Review

[[mathieu-et-al-2017-century-work-teams|Mathieu et al. (2017)]] use this evolution to frame a century of JAP team research and to argue their three substantive domains (tasks/structure, composition, processes/emergent states) are **reciprocal and dynamic**, not a pipeline — motivating research on teams in context and in complex multiteam systems.
