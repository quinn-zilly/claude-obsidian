# Job-Relatedness

The requirement that a selection procedure be demonstrably tied to the job — documented **linkage between KSAOs and critical job behaviors**. Under EEO law and the [[EEOC-1978-uniform-guidelines-employee-selection|Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection (1978)]], job-relatedness (with business necessity) is the justification that rescues a procedure with [[Adverse Impact]]. It is established through a **job analysis** and one of the [[Three Validity Strategies (Uniform Guidelines)]].

## Why It Anchors Selection
- **Legal**: inaccurate work analysis / job spec can exclude qualified people (incl. protected groups); rigorous WA "provides crucial data for ensuring EEO objectives are met" (Biddle & Kuthy 2012).
- **CM tension**: Sanchez & Levine (2009) note the (often misguided) practice of rating whether strategic competencies "apply" to each job was driven by desire to satisfy the Guidelines' job-relatedness provisions — orgs may need to **supplement CM with TJA** to comply. See [[TJA vs CM (Six Dimensions)]].
