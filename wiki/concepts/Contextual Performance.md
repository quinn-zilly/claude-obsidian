# Contextual Performance

**Contextual performance** (Borman & Motowidlo 1993, 1997) consists of activities that "service and maintain the technical core" — replenishing supplies, distributing products, planning, supervising, or staffing — that enable the organization to function effectively, as opposed to **core task performance** (transforming raw materials into the organization's products/services).

Borman & Motowidlo (1997) specified **five behavioral subfactors** of contextual performance. There is near-complete overlap between these subfactors and the six factors of [[Organizational Citizenship Behavior]] — "some mild argument over whose language is the most interesting" ([[campbell-wiernik-2015-modeling-assessment-work-performance|Campbell & Wiernik 2015]]).

## In the Campbell model

Contextual/citizenship behaviors map onto Campbell's initiative-persistence-effort factor and the peer/team leadership and management factors — a higher-order "citizenship performance" grouping. Distinct from (and not the inverse of) [[Counterproductive Work Behavior]].
