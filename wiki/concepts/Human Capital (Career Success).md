# Human Capital (Career Success)

Individuals' educational, personal, and professional experiences that enhance career attainment (Becker 1964). In [[ng-eby-sorensen-feldman-2005-career-success-meta-analysis|Ng et al. (2005)]] it is the predictor family aligned with the **contest-mobility** lens ([[Contest-Mobility vs Sponsored-Mobility]]) — you get ahead on merit accumulated as human capital.

## Indicators used

Hours worked, work centrality, job tenure, organization tenure, work experience, willingness to transfer, international experience, education level, career planning, political knowledge/skills, and social capital.

## Empirical signature

Human capital relates **more strongly to objective career success** (salary, promotions) than to subjective success. Standout corrected correlations with **salary**: education level ρ ≈ .29, hours worked ρ ≈ .24, organization tenure ρ ≈ .20. The same predictors are near-zero with career satisfaction — human capital buys pay and rank, not necessarily felt success.
