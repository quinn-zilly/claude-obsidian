# Competency Modeling

A **competency model** is a set of behaviorally-defined performance factors used as the basis for behavioral ratings in [[Performance Management]]. Widely used to articulate an organization's strategy, values, culture, and priorities.

## Definitions in tension
- **Boyatzis (1996)**: a competency is any individual characteristic — motive, trait, skill, attribute, or body of knowledge — related to successful job performance. (Broad; includes internal constructs.)
- **Klein (1996)**: competencies are **related, observable behaviors** that differentiate effective from ineffective performance. (Narrower; behavior-only.)

Modern models mostly follow Klein's behavioral concept, ideally with a **job-analytic backbone** (Schippmann 1999). David McClelland is credited with introducing the competency idea (Dubois 1993).

> [!info] The catch
> Just like the [[Performance Appraisal|rating-format]] research it echoes, **competency-based rating formats are no more accurate or unbiased than any other format** (Pulakos et al. 2019). The value is in articulating expectations, not in measurement precision.
