# Superforecasters

Individuals identified in the [[Good Judgment Project]] who consistently produce **substantially better-calibrated probability forecasts** than average participants (~2 SD above mean). Their advantage is stable across events and years.

## Properties

- Actively open-minded thinking (willing to revise beliefs)
- Treat beliefs as testable hypotheses, not identities
- Track record: update frequently and appropriately (not anchored)
- Better calibration, resolution, and knowledge components all

## Significance

Superforecasters demonstrate that calibration is a **stable individual difference**, not a task artifact. They can be identified in advance and preferentially recruited for high-stakes forecasting tasks (intelligence, policy).

## Distinction from DMC

- [[Decision-Making Competence]] (DMC): general skill across judgment, preference, and choice domains; validated against real-world behavioral outcomes
- Superforecasters: domain of probabilistic forecasting of geopolitical events specifically; validated against ground truth event outcomes

Both are stable individual differences in decision/judgment skill. Likely correlated.
