# Human Agency

Nav: [[index]] | [[Social Cognitive Theory]]

**To be an agent is to intentionally make things happen by one's actions** (Bandura 2001). Agency is not a discrete entity in a particular place but is distributed across endowments, belief systems, and self-regulatory capabilities. People are **producers as well as products** of social systems. SCT models **emergent interactive agency** — thought as an emergent brain activity exerting *downward causation*, not an epiphenomenon.

## Four core features

| Feature | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Intentionality** | A representation of a future course of action + a *proactive commitment* to bring it about (not mere prediction). Joint action requires shared intentions and coordinated plans. |
| **Forethought** | Temporal extension of agency; foreseeable future events, represented cognitively, become current motivators. Grounds [[Outcome Expectancies]] and hierarchical (distal + proximal) goals. See [[Forethought and Anticipatory Self-Guidance]]. |
| **Self-reactiveness** | Self-regulation linking thought to action: self-monitoring, performance guidance via personal standards, corrective self-reactions. Includes moral agency (inhibitive + proactive) and [[Moral Disengagement]]. |
| **Self-reflectiveness** | The metacognitive capacity to reflect on one's own functioning and verify one's thinking. **Efficacy beliefs are the foundation of agency.** |

## Why efficacy is central

> [!key-insight]
> Among the mechanisms of agency, none is more central or pervasive than **[[Self-Efficacy (Goal Setting)|perceived self-efficacy]]**. Efficacy beliefs determine what challenges people undertake, how much effort they expend, how long they persevere, and whether failure is motivating or demoralizing. This is the hinge connecting agency to motivation ([[schunk-usher-2012-social-cognitive-theory-motivation|Schunk & Usher 2012]]) and to metacognitive miscalibration ([[kruger-dunning-1999-unskilled-and-unaware|Kruger & Dunning]]).

## Rejected dualisms

Bandura rejects treating personal agency vs. social structure, self-efficacy vs. communality, and individualism vs. collectivism as genuine oppositions. Personal agency operates *within* a network of sociostructural influences via [[Triadic Reciprocal Causation]].
