# Evaluation Is Not a Field

A thesis in [[Sechrest-Figueredo-1993-Program-Evaluation|Sechrest & Figueredo (1993)]] (following Shadish et al. 1991): evaluation is an **enterprise for deciding the worth** of activities, not a *field of inquiry*.

## Implications

- **No cumulative substantive knowledge** attaches specifically to evaluation. "We can evaluate anything," so reviewing evaluation findings across fields would satisfy only idle curiosity — those reviews belong to the substantive fields themselves.
- **Progress is measured differently.** Not by positive/negative findings (any more than progress in statistics is measured by the count of significant results), but by advances in the **conceptualization** of the enterprise and improvements in its **methods**.
- **Weak cumulation observed.** Evaluation texts cite little recent work relative to comparison disciplines; core journals (*Evaluation Review*, *New Directions*) increasingly prefer methodological/conceptual pieces, relegating actual evaluation reports to specialized journals and the "fugitive literature" (technical reports, government documents). Consequence: many practitioners rarely see an *exemplary* full evaluation report to model.

This is why the authors' own review deliberately targets **methods and concepts**, not a catalogue of "what worked."
