# Presence vs Search for Calling

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

A measurement and developmental distinction introduced by [[Dik-Duffy-2009-Calling-and-Vocation|Dik & Duffy (2009)]] and treated by them as **critical**. At any point an individual may either **currently have** a sense of calling/vocation ("presence of") or be **actively looking for one** they do not yet have ("search for"). The two are not opposite ends of one scale but separately measurable states, tracking different points in the career-development trajectory.

The distinction was subsequently built into the standard instruments: the **Brief Calling Scale** (Steger & Dik 2006) and the **Calling and Vocation Questionnaire** (Dik et al. 2012) each contain separate presence-of and search-for subscales (in the CVQ, 4 items per calling dimension for presence + 4 for search, 24 items total). Empirically, higher presence associates with career decidedness, comfort, and vocational self-clarity, while higher search associates with the opposite (undecidedness, discomfort, lower clarity) — consistent with search reflecting an unresolved developmental state.
