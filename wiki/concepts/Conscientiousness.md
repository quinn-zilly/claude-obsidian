# Conscientiousness

The [[Five-Factor Model|Big Five]] dimension capturing being organized, dependable, achievement-striving, and self-disciplined. In work settings it is **the single best, generalizable Big Five predictor of job performance** — the only trait that predicts overall JP with consistent validity across essentially all jobs ([[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al. 2007]]; [[zell-lesick-2022-big-five-performance-synthesis|Zell & Lesick 2022]] ρ ≈ .19, ≥46% larger than the other four traits).

## Facets

Conscientiousness decomposes into facets that predict *differentially* (Dudley et al. 2006), so facet-level analysis often out-predicts the broad factor — see [[Facet-Level Personality Measurement]]:

| Facet | Notable operational validities (Ones et al. 2007) |
|---|---|
| **Achievement** | task performance .22; job dedication .34; overall JP .18 |
| **Dependability** | job dedication .40; avoiding CWB .30; overall JP .22 |
| **Order** | generally weak (overall JP .09) |
| **Cautiousness** | near-zero for JP (−.01) |

## Selected broad-factor validities (operational ρ, Ones et al. 2007)

Overall JP .23 · objective performance .19 · task performance .15 · avoiding organizational deviance .38 · leadership emergence .30 · training performance .23 · procrastination −.68.

## Why it matters

Conscientiousness anchors the case for personality in selection: even under conservative corrections it reaches useful validity everywhere, and its facets let practitioners tune predictor–criterion matching. Different *other* Big Five traits become useful for different occupations, but C is the constant.
