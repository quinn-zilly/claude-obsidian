# Sources of Team Leadership (2×2)

[[morgeson-et-al-2010-leadership-in-teams-functional|Morgeson, DeRue & Karam (2010)]] locate the *source* of team leadership on two crossed dimensions:

- **Locus** — is the source **internal** (a team member) or **external** (someone outside the team)?
- **Formality** — is the role **formal** (appointed, designated) or **informal** (emergent)?

| | Formal | Informal |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Formally appointed team leader | **Shared / distributed** leadership among members |
| **External** | Sponsor, coach, manager, team leader outside the team | Mentors and informal external influencers |

Two implications:

1. **Multiple sources coexist.** In any real team, several of these operate simultaneously — the leadership capacity of a team is routinely *underestimated* when only the formal internal leader is counted.
2. **Sources are differentially suited to functions.** Each source is best positioned for a different subset of the [[15 Team Leadership Functions]] (e.g., external sources for boundary management; internal members for self-management and social climate).

> [!info] Bridge to shared leadership
> The **informal-internal** cell is exactly [[Shared Leadership]]; the framework thus embeds shared/[[Vertical vs Shared Leadership|vertical]] leadership inside a single functional map rather than treating them as rival theories.
