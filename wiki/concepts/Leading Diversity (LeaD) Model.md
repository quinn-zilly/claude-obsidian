# Leading Diversity (LeaD) Model

A dynamic, functional theory of leading diverse teams from [[homan-et-al-2020-leading-diversity-functional|Homan, Gündemir, Buengeler & van Kleef (2020)]]. Its purpose is to explain **why the same leadership behavior helps diverse teams in some studies and hurts in others**: because diversity creates *different team needs*, and effectiveness depends on **matching** behavior to need.

## The three components

1. **Knowledge of diversity processes** — leaders must understand that diversity instigates either [[Categorization-Elaboration Model|intergroup bias or information elaboration]].
2. **Mastery of two leadership behaviors** — [[Complementary vs Supplementary Matching|person-focused and task-focused]] leadership (the parsimonious two-factor behavioral split).
3. **[[Diversity-Related Leader Competencies|Diversity-related competencies]]** — cognitive understanding (predict), social perceptiveness (diagnose), behavioral flexibility (match).

## The core prediction

At any time a diverse team primarily exhibits one dominant process, creating a specific need:

- **Intergroup bias** → needs **person-focused** leadership (**complementary match**): recategorization, individuation, reduced identity threat.
- **Information elaboration** → needs **task-focused** leadership (**supplementary match**): task structure, feedback, accountability.

Off-diagonal cells are **mismatches** that degrade performance.

## Proactive vs reactive

- **Proactive** (first-stage moderation): leaders shape *which* process arises.
- **Reactive** (second-stage moderation): leaders manage a process already active. LeaD's addition of the reactive/second-stage path extends the [[Categorization-Elaboration Model|CEM]], which had emphasized shaping.
- **Behavioral dynamism**: needs change over time, so effective leaders shift behaviors (even successful matching creates the next need).

## Why it matters

LeaD reframes diversity leadership away from stable "styles" (and away from the [[Transformational and Charismatic Leadership|transformational-leadership]] catch-all whose diversity effects are inconsistent) toward **trainable competencies + situational matching**. It is a domain-specific application of [[Functional Team Leadership Theory]] (leadership exists to satisfy team needs) to the particular need created by team diversity.
