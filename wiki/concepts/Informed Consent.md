# Informed Consent

> [!note] Stub
> Seed page created to resolve inbound links from [[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct]]. Expand on next ethics ingest.

**Informed consent** is the ethical requirement that people agree to participate in research, assessment, or professional services only after being given adequate, comprehensible information about what participation involves — and that their agreement is voluntary. In the [[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct|APA Ethics Code]] it appears both as a general standard (3.10, Informed Consent) and in domain-specific standards for therapy, assessment, and research (Section 8, Research and Publication).

Core elements typically include the purpose and expected duration, procedures, the right to decline or withdraw without penalty, foreseeable risks and benefits, limits of confidentiality, and whom to contact with questions. Special provisions govern deception research (permitted only when justified and followed by debriefing) and participants with limited capacity to consent.

## Placement in the APA Code

- Appears under **Human Relations** and again under **Research and Publication** ([[apa-2017-ethical-principles-code-of-conduct]] structure).
- Expresses the aspirational principles of **Respect for People's Rights and Dignity** (autonomy) and **Integrity**.
