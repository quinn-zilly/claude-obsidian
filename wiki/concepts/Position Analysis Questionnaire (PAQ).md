# Position Analysis Questionnaire (PAQ)

The **PAQ** (McCormick & Jeanneret) is a standardized, **quantitative, taxonomy-based** job-analysis questionnaire: a rater judges the degree to which each of **187 statements** describes the job, yielding a profile (or a smaller set of derived dimensions). Because it works at the level of relatively general work activities applicable across many jobs, it enables **cross-job comparison** — but at a fixed level of generality.

> [!note] Example use & caveat
> Per [[Sackett-Walmsley-Laczo-2013-job-and-work-analysis|Sackett et al. (2013)]]: Arvey & Begalla's PAQ analysis of "homemaker" found the most-similar jobs were patrolman, home economist, and kitchen helper — a **trouble-shooting/emergency-handling** theme. PAQ scales typically reflect **time spent**; the authors note "level required" might yield different similarity patterns, illustrating that the operationalization of *importance* (time / variance-contribution / level) shapes conclusions.
