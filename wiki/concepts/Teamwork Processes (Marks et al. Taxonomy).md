# Teamwork Processes (Marks et al. Taxonomy)

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

> **Definition**: "members' interdependent acts that convert inputs to outcomes through cognitive, verbal, and behavioral activities directed toward organizing taskwork to achieve collective goals" (Marks, Mathieu & Zaccaro 2001, p. 357).

Distinct from **taskwork** (performing the actual task content). Teamwork is *how* members interact to accomplish tasks, not *what* tasks they perform.

## Hierarchical Structure

Three-level hierarchy confirmed meta-analytically (LePine et al. 2008, 138 studies, 1,507 correlations):

**Level 3 (general)**: Overall teamwork process factor
**Level 2 (intermediate)**: Three first-order dimensions (avg true correlation among them = .83)
**Level 1 (narrow)**: 10 specific processes

Structure organized around **performance episodes** — distinguishable periods of work and evaluation.

---

## Three Intermediate Dimensions

### 1. Transition Processes
*When*: Between performance episodes. Focus: review the past, prepare for future.

| Narrow Process | Description |
|---|---|
| Mission analysis | Identify/evaluate tasks, challenges, resources, environmental conditions |
| Goal specification | Identify and prioritize team goals |
| Strategy formulation & planning | Develop action courses, contingency plans; adjust to environmental change |

**Connection to [[Goal-Setting Theory]]**: these processes *are* goal-setting and planning at team level. Specific, challenging team goals enhance motivation (Buller & Bell 1986) — exactly what team building goal-setting interventions target.

---

### 2. Action Processes
*When*: During episodes. Focus: accomplish goals in real time.

| Narrow Process | Description |
|---|---|
| Monitoring progress toward goals | Attend to, interpret, communicate progress information |
| Systems monitoring | Track team resources (money, inventory) and environmental factors |
| Team monitoring & backup behavior | Assist teammates: feedback/coaching, direct help, task compensation; overlaps group-level OCB and workload sharing |
| Coordination | Synchronize activities by sequence and timing |

**Connection to [[Transactive Memory Systems]]**: systems monitoring and team monitoring require knowing *who knows what* — TMS enables efficient backup behavior (knowing when and how to help). Cross-training directly develops these processes.

---

### 3. Interpersonal Processes
*When*: Ongoing — both during and between episodes. Focus: manage relationship dynamics.

| Narrow Process | Description |
|---|---|
| Conflict management | Proactively/reactively manage conflict; mutual respect, compromise, harmony norms |
| Motivating & confidence building | Develop/maintain motivation and collective confidence in goal achievement |
| Affect management | Foster emotional balance, togetherness, effective stress/frustration coping |

**Connection to [[Trait Activation Theory]]**: interpersonal compatibility via mutual trait activation (TAT "chemistry") is the compositional substrate underlying interpersonal process quality. Teams where members activate each other's valued traits experience less conflict and better affect management.

---

## Meta-Analytic Effects on Outcomes

All processes positively and similarly related to both team performance and member satisfaction (LePine et al. 2008):

| Level | ρ performance | ρ satisfaction |
|---|---|---|
| Transition | .29 | .45 |
| Action | .29 | .46 |
| Interpersonal | .29 | .37 |
| Overall | .31 | .43 |

Narrow processes: ρ range .17–.35 (performance), .29–.47 (satisfaction). No significant differences across process types — hierarchy is confirmed but discriminant validity at criterion level is weak.

**Emergent states** (cohesion, potency): processes strongly related — ρ cohesion .53–.61, ρ potency .63–.70.

## Moderators

**Task interdependence** (B=.02, p<.05): process–performance stronger when interdependence higher. Rationale: when members must integrate efforts, processes become the critical bottleneck.

**Team size** (B=.03, p<.05): larger teams face more coordination challenges, motivation losses — processes matter more.

Neither moderates process–satisfaction links.

## Measurement Implications

**Compatibility principle** (Fishbein & Ajzen 1974): match specificity of process measure to specificity of theory and criterion.
- General theory → broad overall process measure
- Phase-specific theory → appropriate intermediate-level measure  
- Fine-grained process distinctions → narrow measure

**Temporal design imperative**: simultaneous survey assessment of all processes produces inflated estimates (halo effects, attributional bias). Time-based designs — measuring transition processes between episodes, action processes during episodes — are needed to show discriminant validity.

## Intervention Targeting

Process diagnosis → targeted intervention (rather than generic team building):

| Failure locus | Targeted intervention |
|---|---|
| Transition (planning/goal breakdown) | [[Team Building]]: goal-setting, role clarification |
| Action (coordination/monitoring failure) | [[Team Training]]: coordination training, cross-training, CRM |
| Interpersonal (conflict, low cohesion) | [[Team Building]]: interpersonal-relationship management |
| All processes (pre-performance) | [[Team Training]]: team KSA development |
