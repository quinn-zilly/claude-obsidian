# Modes of Human Agency

Nav: [[index]] | [[Human Agency]]

SCT distinguishes **three modes** through which people exercise influence over their lives (Bandura 2001):

1. **Direct personal agency** — acting oneself to produce effects through cognitive, motivational, affective, and choice processes.
2. **Proxy agency** — a *socially mediated* mode: getting others who have resources, expertise, power, or influence to act on one's behest. Relies on **perceived social efficacy** to enlist others. Used when one lacks the means, believes others do it better, or wants to shed the burdens/responsibilities of direct control. Trade-off: overuse breeds a "vulnerable security" resting on others' competence and favor, and can impede development of personal competencies.
3. **Collective agency** — exercised through socially coordinated, interdependent effort. Rests on **[[Collective Efficacy|perceived collective efficacy]]**, an emergent group-level property.

> [!note] Blend, not either/or
> Successful functioning involves a *blend*: relying on proxy agency in some domains frees time and effort to manage others directly. "No one has the time, energy, and resources to master every realm of everyday life."

Source: [[bandura-2001-social-cognitive-theory-agentic|Bandura (2001)]].
