# Team Cognition

Team cognition refers to the mental models, knowledge structures, and information-processing activities that emerge at the level of a group rather than residing solely in any single member. It is the umbrella construct under which [[Transactive Memory Systems]], [[Shared Mental Models]], and [[Team Mental Models]] all sit: each describes a different way that knowing becomes a collective, rather than purely individual, property.

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] position TMS as one of the most heavily studied forms of team cognition. Where shared mental models emphasize *overlap* — members holding similar representations of the task and team — transactive memory emphasizes *distribution* — members holding *complementary* knowledge plus a directory of who knows what. Both are emergent states that develop through interaction and predict downstream [[Group Performance]].

## Role in TMS

TMS is the "distributed" branch of team cognition. Its three behavioral indicators — [[Memory Differentiation]] (specialization), [[Task Credibility]] (trust in expertise), and [[Task Coordination]] — describe how a group encodes, stores, and retrieves knowledge as a single cognitive system.
