# Organizational Citizenship Behavior (OCB)

Discretionary actions that support the **psychological and social environment** in which work occurs — less enforceable as job requirements, less likely to be explicitly rewarded (Organ 1997). Examples: helping, courtesy, civic virtue. The **exemplar reciprocative behavior** in social-exchange-based justice research.

## Why OCB Is the SET Exemplar

Its discretionary nature gives OCB **particularism** — the identity of the provider matters, and particularistic resources are exchanged in the open-ended fashion characteristic of social exchange ([[Foa & Foa Resource Theory]]). OCB resembles Blau's "advice and assistance" — exemplars of reciprocation. So when supervisors/organizations treat employees fairly, employees reciprocate with citizenship (see [[Norm of Reciprocity]]).

## Targets / Foci

OCB is partitioned by **who benefits**:

- **OCB-O** — directed at the organization
- **OCB-S** — directed at the supervisor
- **OCB-I** — directed at individual coworkers (Williams & Anderson 1991)

Multifoci research (Masterson et al. 2000): [[Perceived Organizational Support|POS]]→OCB-O; [[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)|LMX]]→OCB-S. But [[colquitt-et-al-2013-justice-millennium-decade-later|Colquitt et al. (2013)]] found focus-matching is **weaker than assumed** — and OCB-I relates to justice despite coworkers not setting justice levels, suggesting employees may reciprocate in **coarse terms** ("be a good employee") rather than target-precisely.

## OCB vs. CWB — Not Opposite Ends

Counterproductive work behavior (CWB) — intentional behaviors that hinder organizational goals (theft, absenteeism, sabotage, harassment). Blau's exchange formulation did **not** treat avoiding negative behavior as reciprocation, though Gouldner did describe "refraining from injuring" as reciprocity.

> [!key-insight]
> [[colquitt-et-al-2013-justice-millennium-decade-later|Colquitt et al. (2013)]] found social-exchange-quality indicators mediate justice→OCB and justice→task performance, but **NOT** justice→CWB. CWB relates weakly to exchange quality — it likely arises from a **different mediator** (e.g., affect). This empirically supports Dalal (2005): OCB and CWB are **not** opposite ends of one discretionary continuum.

## Two Mediating Pathways

- **Exchange pathway** — justice/POS/LMX → reciprocated OCB.
- **Affect pathway** — [[Affective Events Theory]]: positive affect → affect-driven OCB; this pathway may be especially relevant where the exchange pathway fails (CWB).
