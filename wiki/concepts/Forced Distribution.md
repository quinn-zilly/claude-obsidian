# Forced Distribution

**Forced distribution** requires raters to slot employees into fixed proportions (e.g., top 10–15%, middle ~80%, bottom 10%). Popularized by GE CEO **Jack Welch** as **"rank and yank"** — top group promoted, bottom group separated.

## Problems
- The top 10% of a low-performing group may be no better than the bottom 10% of a high-performing group → **fairness and accuracy concerns** if groups are blindly combined. Mitigated (imperfectly) by **calibration sessions**, which become less informed as they roll up organizational levels.
- Time-consuming; corrosive to collaboration.

## Decline
Use fell from **49% (2009) to 14% (2011)** (i4cp 2011) — GE itself abandoned it. Part of the broader shift toward [[Rating-less Reviews]] and simpler PM. Some format research on forced distribution continued (Scullen, Bergey & Aiman-Smith 2005).
