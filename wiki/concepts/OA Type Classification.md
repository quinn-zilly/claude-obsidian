# OA Type Classification

The standard scheme for sorting open-access publications by *route* to openness, defined by [[Unpaywall]] and popularized by Piwowar et al. (2018). Five mutually exclusive categories:

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Gold** | Published in an OA journal indexed by DOAJ. Free at publisher, clear license. |
| **Green-only** | Toll-access at publisher, but a free version sits in an OA repository (self-archived). |
| **Hybrid** | Free under an OA license inside an otherwise toll-access journal (usually APC-funded). |
| **Bronze** | Free to read on the publisher page but **no clearly identifiable license**. |
| **Closed** | Everything else (incl. shared only on academic social networks or Sci-Hub). |

Refines the older binary [[Open Access#Two Main Models|gold vs green]] split by separating license-clear OA journals (gold) from license-clear journal articles in toll journals (hybrid) and license-unclear free-to-read (bronze).

## Assignment rule (in practice)

A paper can be both self-archived *and* journal-OA. Studies typically assign **one** category, prioritizing journal-based status over self-archiving — as [[basson-et-al-2022-oa-dimensions-wos]] does.

## Why the categories carry signal

The mix of types is diagnostic, not just descriptive:
- **Green-only** dominance signals a **self-archiving culture** — repositories + mandates, concentrated in the West.
- **Bronze** dominance signals **license-unclear free-to-read** — common in smaller/national journals with non-standard metadata that can't get DOAJ-listed (so they miss gold). Inflated in broad-coverage databases like [[Dimensions]].

So the same overall "% OA" can decompose very differently across databases and regions — see [[Geographic Bias in Indexing]].
