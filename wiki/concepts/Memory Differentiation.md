# Memory Differentiation

Memory differentiation is one of the three behavioral indicators of a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]]. It captures the degree to which team members specialize in *different* knowledge domains rather than all holding the same information. High differentiation means the group spreads its knowledge across members — each becomes the "go-to" person for a domain — which expands the total knowledge the team can hold and reduces redundant storage.

In [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]], memory differentiation is the *structural* component of TMS: it describes how knowledge is organized across individual memories plus the links connecting them into a collective network. It is closely tied to [[Knowledge Specialization]]. Differentiation alone is not sufficient — a functional TMS also requires [[Task Coordination]] (the transactive process that integrates the specialized knowledge) and [[Task Credibility]] (trust that specialists actually know their domains).

> [!tip] Ren & Argote emphasize that you need *both* specialization (structure) and coordination (process) for a functional TMS.
