# Intrinsic Motivation

Inherent tendency to seek out novelty and challenges, to extend and exercise one's capacities, to explore, and to learn. Doing an activity for its inherent satisfaction rather than for some separable outcome.

## Nature

Evolved propensity; present from birth (children are active, inquisitive, curious, playful even without specific rewards). Prototypic manifestation of human growth tendency. Source of enjoyment and vitality throughout life (Csikszentmihalyi & Rathunde 1993).

SDT does **not** ask what *causes* intrinsic motivation (viewed as innate) — asks what conditions *elicit and sustain vs. subdue and diminish* it.

## Conditions That Support vs. Undermine

Per [[Cognitive Evaluation Theory]]:

**Support:**
- Positive performance feedback (→ perceived competence)
- Optimal challenges
- Effectance-promoting feedback
- Freedom from demeaning evaluation
- Choice, feeling acknowledgment, opportunities for self-direction
- Autonomy-supportive social agents (teachers, parents, coaches)
- Secure relational base (distal condition)

**Undermine:**
- All expected tangible rewards contingent on task performance (Deci, Koestner & Ryan 1999 meta-analysis — definitive)
- Threats, deadlines, directives, pressured evaluations, imposed goals
- Cold/uncaring social agents
- Controlling teaching/parenting styles

## Relation to Extrinsic Motivation

Intrinsic motivation exists for activities holding intrinsic interest (novelty, challenge, aesthetic value). For activities lacking such appeal, CET principles don't apply — must understand [[Extrinsic Motivation]] and [[Internalization (SDT)]].

Authentic motivation (intrinsic or well-internalized extrinsic) → more interest, excitement, confidence → enhanced performance, persistence, creativity, vitality, self-esteem, well-being (even controlling for perceived competence/self-efficacy).
