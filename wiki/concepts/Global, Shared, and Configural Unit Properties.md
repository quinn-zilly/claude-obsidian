# Global, Shared, and Configural Unit Properties

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

Three basic types of **unit-level construct**, distinguished by their **level of origin** and their **form of emergence**. Typing a construct correctly *drives how it must be measured and analyzed* — the central move for avoiding [[Levels of Analysis Fallacies|misalignment]].

## The three types

**Global unit properties** — objective, descriptive, observable characteristics that **originate at the unit level**. Single-level phenomena; do *not* emerge from individuals. Examples: group size, unit function, team location (proximal vs virtual). No within-unit variation is meaningful (if members disagree on group size, someone miscounted).

**Shared unit properties** — originate in individual members' experiences/perceptions/attitudes and **converge** (via ASA, socialization, leadership, social interaction) to characterize the unit. Based on **[[Composition vs Compilation|composition]]** (isomorphism). Examples: organizational climate, collective efficacy, group norms, team cohesion.

**Configural unit properties** — also originate at the individual level, but do **not** coalesce; they capture the **array, pattern, distribution, or variability** of members' contributions. Based on **compilation** (discontinuity). Examples: diversity, personality/ability configuration, network density, pay dispersion, configural team performance.

## Measurement follows type

| Type | Data source & level | What to establish |
|---|---|---|
| **Global** | Single expert informant; measured at **unit** level | (nothing to aggregate) |
| **Shared** | **Individual** members; aggregate (mean) only after justifying | Restricted within-unit variance ([[Aggregation Justification Indices]]) — evaluates the composition/isomorphism assumption |
| **Configural** | Individual members (or expert for manifest features) | Summary that captures the *pattern*: min/max, variance/dispersion, profile similarity, network indices — **not** the mean alone |

> [!key-insight]
> There is **no single best way** to measure a unit-level construct. Global → measure at unit level. Shared and configural → measure at the **level of origin** (individuals) and let the **form of emergence** dictate how data are combined. A "simple" unit-level model of a shared construct effectively contains a compositional multilevel model inside its very operationalization.

Expert informants are appropriate for **global** and **manifest configural** properties; least appropriate for **shared** and **latent configural** properties (a CEO cannot know members' inner perceptions of climate).
