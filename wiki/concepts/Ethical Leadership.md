# Ethical Leadership

"The demonstration of normatively appropriate conduct through personal actions and interpersonal relationships, and the promotion of such conduct to followers through two-way communication, reinforcement, and decision-making" (Brown, Treviño & Harrison 2005).

## Two Components

- **Moral person** — trait side: integrity, fairness, social responsibility, thinking through consequences.
- **Moral manager** — behavior side: actively promoting workplace ethicality through role modeling, rewards, communication, and punishment.

Grounded in social learning theory (Bandura 1986): ethical leaders shape follower behavior by modeling and reinforcing ethical conduct. Unlike other positive forms, ethics is the *focal* dimension rather than an ancillary one.

## Empirical Profile ([[hoch-et-al-2018-ethical-authentic-servant-vs-transformational|Hoch et al. 2018]])

Largest and most confident of the three moral forms' literatures (96 studies, 124 samples). Strong meta-analytic links: deviance (ρ=−.45), OCB (ρ=.29), job satisfaction (ρ=.50), affective commitment (ρ=.48), LMX (ρ=.71), trust (ρ=.66).

> [!warning] Redundancy
> Correlates ρ=.70 with [[Transformational and Charismatic Leadership|transformational leadership]] and shows the same outcome pattern → largely **redundant** with TL, adding little incremental variance. See [[Construct Redundancy (Leadership)]].

## Connections

- One of the three ethical/moral values–based leadership forms alongside [[Authentic Leadership]] and [[Servant Leadership]]
- Rose to prominence after the Enron-era corporate scandals
