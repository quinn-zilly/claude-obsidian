# Content Validity (Training Criteria)

## Definition in Training Evaluation Context

The degree to which a training criterion measure closely reflects the content of the training itself. Alliger et al. (1997) operationalized this as a binary moderator: **close** (highly content valid) vs. **divergent** (less content valid).

## Moderator Analysis Findings

From [[alliger-et-al-1997-training-criteria-meta-analysis]]:

| Content Pairing | Mean weighted r | n correlations |
|---|---|---|
| Close–Close | .16 | 45 |
| Divergent–Close | .04 | 42 |

Difference significant at p < .001. However, **confound**: all "divergent" coded measures were reactions, so level of criteria and content validity are confounded.

## Practical Implication

Low inter-criterion correlations may reflect **methodological mismatch** (criteria measuring different content) rather than true discriminant validity across training levels. Researchers and evaluators should pursue a conscious **criterion-development strategy** with explicit attention to content validity before interpreting low correlations as evidence that levels are unrelated.

In some cases, low overlap between criteria is *desirable* — using multiple divergent criteria gives a more complete picture of training effects.
