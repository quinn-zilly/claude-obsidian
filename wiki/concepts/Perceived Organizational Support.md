# Perceived Organizational Support (POS)

The degree to which employees believe the **organization values their contributions and cares about their well-being** (Eisenberger, Huntington, Hutchison & Sowa 1986). The signature indicator of the **employeeâ†”organization** social exchange relationship.

## Social Exchange Logic

POS is understood in **reciprocal** terms (see [[Norm of Reciprocity]]): an employee who perceives the employer as supportive is likely to **return the gesture**. POS is roughly the inverse of an organization's commitment to the employee. When POS is high, employees engage in more [[Organizational Citizenship Behavior|OCB]], higher job performance, and reduced absenteeism.

## POS vs. LMX (Multifoci Model)

POS and [[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)|LMX]] index **different exchange relationships**:

| | POS | LMX |
|---|---|---|
| Exchange partner | Organization | Supervisor |
| Best predicts | OCB-O, commitment, turnover intentions | OCB-S, job satisfaction, performance ratings, favors for supervisor |
