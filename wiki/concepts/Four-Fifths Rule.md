# Four-Fifths Rule

The **80% rule** for flagging [[Adverse Impact]] under the [[EEOC-1978-uniform-guidelines-employee-selection|Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection (1978)]] (§4D). A selection rate for any race/sex/ethnic group that is **less than four-fifths (80%) of the rate for the highest-scoring group** is generally treated by federal enforcement agencies as **evidence of adverse impact**. A rate above 4/5 generally is not.

## Caveats
- Smaller differences may **still** be adverse impact if significant in **both statistical and practical** terms, or where user actions discouraged applicants.
- Larger differences may **not** be adverse impact if based on small numbers that are not statistically significant, or where special recruiting made the pool atypical.
- Small unreliable samples → longer-timeframe or same-procedure-elsewhere data may be considered.
- Failure to keep required impact data → enforcement may infer adverse impact if a group is underutilized vs the relevant labor market.

## Example
If the highest group's selection rate is 50%, the 4/5 threshold is 40%. A group selected at 35% falls below → evidence of adverse impact.
