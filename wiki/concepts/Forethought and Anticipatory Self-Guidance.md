# Forethought and Anticipatory Self-Guidance

Nav: [[index]] | [[Human Agency]]

One of the four **[[Human Agency|core features of agency]]**. Future events have no actual existence and cannot cause current action — **but represented cognitively in the present, foreseeable future events are converted into current motivators and regulators of behavior**. This is *anticipatory self-guidance*: behavior is driven by projected goals and anticipated outcomes, not pulled by an unrealized future state.

## Mechanisms

- **[[Outcome Expectancies|Outcome expectations]]** — constructed from observed conditional relations between events and the outcomes actions produce. People adopt courses likely to produce positive outcomes.
- **Not just external rewards** — if people acted only for anticipated external rewards/punishments they would behave "like weather vanes." Self-evaluative outcomes (against personal standards) can augment or override external ones.
- **Hierarchical goal structure** — distal aspirations set the general course; **proximal subgoals** mobilize self-influence in the here and now. Goal effectiveness depends on specificity, challenge, and temporal proximity (converges with [[Goal-Setting Theory]]).

Source: [[bandura-2001-social-cognitive-theory-agentic|Bandura (2001)]].
