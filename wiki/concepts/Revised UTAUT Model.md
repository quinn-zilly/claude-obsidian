# Revised UTAUT Model

The alternative to [[UTAUT]] proposed by [[dwivedi-2019-utaut-revision|Dwivedi et al. (2019)]]. Built in three moves from the original.

## Three modifications

1. **Drop the four moderators** (gender, age, experience, voluntariness) → leaves the "basic UTAUT" of direct effects only.
2. **Add FC → BI** — facilitating conditions predict behavioural intention directly, a path the original UTAUT omitted.
3. **Add [[Attitude as Mediator|attitude]]** as a mediating construct between the exogenous constructs and behavioural intention.

## The emergent model

Path analysis surfaced three further data-driven paths (high modification indices, theoretically defensible):

- **AT → UB** — attitude directly drives usage, beyond the route through intention.
- **FC → AT** — infrastructure (training, help desks) shapes attitude.
- **SI → AT** — others' shared experiences shape attitude.

Final structure: PE, EE, FC, SI → AT → BI → UB, with attitude partially mediating all four exogenous constructs and direct AT → UB and FC → BI links.

## Why it matters

Explicitly modelling attitude lifts explained variance: BI 38% → 45%, UB 21% → 27%, and the final model fits far better (RMSEA 0.111 → 0.041). The exogenous constructs hit attitude harder than intention — attitude is the convergence point for technology attributes and contextual factors.

> [!key-insight]
> This is a parsimony-vs-explanatory-power trade. Reinstating the construct UTAUT removed for elegance buys both better fit and more variance explained.
