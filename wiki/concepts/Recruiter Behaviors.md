# Recruiter Behaviors

Behaviors recruiters engage in during recruitment — commonly **competence, personableness, trustworthiness, informativeness, and time spent** (Barber 1998). Recruiter *demographics* (race, gender) have little impact on applicants (Chapman et al. 2005) and are excluded from the category.

## Effect on attraction

Long debated: several studies found recruiters had little effect over and above job/org attributes (Rynes & Miller 1983; Powell 1984; Taylor & Bergmann 1987). [[uggerslev-et-al-2012-recruiting-through-stages|Uggerslev et al. (2012)]] settle it: recruiter behaviors carry the **second-largest incremental variance** in [[Applicant Attraction|attraction]] (ΔR² ≈ .096) — real, unique, and **concentrated at early [[Recruitment Stages (Barber)|stages]]**. **Personableness** is the single strongest recruiter behavior ("being friendly matters most").

## Mechanism

Early, low-elaboration applicants use recruiter cues as **heuristics** and **[[Signaling Theory (Recruitment)|signals]]** of working conditions ([[Elaboration Likelihood Model|ELM]]); as applicants gain information and motivation, these simple associations fade in persuasive force.

> [!key-insight] Trainable lever
> Unlike org size/location/image, recruiter behavior is **controllable** — firms can select and train for personableness knowing it makes a unique contribution to early attraction. Investment here "would not be wasted."
