# Leadership Training (Teams)

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

A **training intervention** targeting individual leader KSAs. Leaders are essential to team functioning; developing their capabilities improves team-level outcomes indirectly via better team processing.

## Meta-Analytic Effect

Leadership training improves learning, transfer, and organizational outcomes by up to **29%** (Lacerenza et al. 2017, *Journal of Applied Psychology*).

- Heredity ≈ 30% of leadership variance; training + experience ≈ 70% (Arvey et al. 2006)
- Effects cascade: leader capabilities → team performance, subordinate job satisfaction, turnover
- **Stronger for cognitive and skill-based outcomes** than affective outcomes (Avolio et al. 2009; Lacerenza et al. 2017)

## Team-Relevant Content

For team-level outcomes (cohesion, potency, performance):
- **Transformational leadership**: charisma, intellectual stimulation, inspirational motivation → team cohesion, potency, performance (Judge & Piccolo 2004)
- **Empowering leadership**: distributes authority → team performance (Lim & Ployhart 2004)
- **Initiating structure**: managing workload, clarifying expectations → team-level effects

## Design Recommendations

- Identify desired outcome first — design should match (cognitive/skill-based content → cognitive/skill-based outcomes)
- Needs analysis: gaps between current and required KSAs; stakeholder alignment
- Multiple delivery methods: information (lecture) + demonstration (video) + practice (role play) + feedback
- Feedback framed as **diagnostic not evaluative** (Kluger & DeNisi 1996) — reduces reactance

## Distinction from Team Training

| | Leadership Training | Team Training |
|---|---|---|
| Target | Individual leader | Team members together |
| For | Ad hoc or intact, any team | Ad hoc or intact teams |
| Primary focus | Leader KSAs | Team KSAs |
| Team impact | Indirect (via leader behavior) | Direct (team-level goals, practice) |
