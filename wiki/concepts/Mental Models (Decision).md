# Mental Models (Decision)

Beliefs about **how systems behave**, paralleling formal models of those systems. A person's mental model of HIV/AIDS includes beliefs about transmission routes, progression, treatability — which jointly determine risk-relevant decisions.

## Decision Science Approach

Standard literacy tests assess whether people know specific facts. A decision science approach begins by **analyzing what facts people need** to make specific decisions. Mental model studies then assess whether people have those facts.

**Methodology**:
1. Formal task analysis: what determines outcomes of decisions in this domain?
2. Open-ended interviews structured around the analysis — capture lay formulations and modes of expression
3. Closed surveys and structured tasks to measure gaps

## Domain Examples

Mental model studies conducted for: HIV/AIDS, climate change, contraceptives, energy consumption, pandemics, radon (Bruine de Bruin & Bostrom 2013 review).

**Domain-specific findings**:
- People tend to ignore **herd immunity** when thinking about vaccines (Downs et al. 2008)
- People overestimate ability to detect a partner's STI (Downs et al. 2004)
- Difficulty predicting **nonlinear processes** (climate change, risk accumulation over time) — general pattern

## Connection to Intervention

Mental model gaps identify specific intervention targets (unlike literacy scores, which show *that* people know less but not *what* is missing or *why* it matters for decisions).

Example: von Winterfeldt (2013) — decision analysis for breech baby delivery. Fischhoff et al. (2006) — pandemic process analysis.
