# FAIR Principles

**FAIR** = **F**indable, **A**ccessible, **I**nteroperable, **R**eusable — a standard for how shared research data (and other digital objects) should be structured so both humans and machines can integrate and reuse them.

## Why it matters

Open sharing alone is not enough: data dumped as custom spreadsheets is technically "open" but practically useless for synthesis. FAIR is what makes the **standardization and interoperability** requirement of [[Open Science 2.0]] real — the precondition for AI-powered evidence synthesis and large-scale reanalysis (GenBank, UniProt, AlphaFold are the exemplars).

> [!contradiction] Openness ≠ FAIR
> [[thibault-2023-open-science-2-0|Thibault et al. (2023)]] note that even when data *is* shared, it is often **not** FAIR — evidence in psychology suggests shared data frequently fails reusability. Counting "data available" statements overstates real progress (a meta-analysis found 5–11% declared open data but only 1–3% actually had it).
