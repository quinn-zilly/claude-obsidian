# Work as a Calling

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

**Calling** is a way individuals relate to their work in which the work is experienced as more than a job or a career — as purposeful, meaningful, and often connected to a cause beyond the self. It is among the oldest constructs in the study of work, traced to the Protestant Reformation (Luther and Calvin held that secular work, not just the ministry, is a divinely appointed calling) and secularized over the intervening centuries (Weber 1930: "the idea of duty in one's calling prowls about in our lives like the ghost of dead religious beliefs").

The modern OP/OB and vocational-psychology literatures revived the construct after **Wrzesniewski et al. (1997)** (the job/career/calling work-orientation distinction) and **[[Dik-Duffy-2009-Calling-and-Vocation|Dik & Duffy (2009)]]**. Its central puzzle is definitional: [[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson (2019)]] found **14 distinct formal definitions** and 23 measures across 84 studies, with **no consensus**.

## The two ideal types

Definitions cluster into two camps that Thompson & Bunderson call outside-in vs inside-out:

- **[[Neoclassical Calling]]** (outside-in) — destiny, duty, discovery; an external summons or societal obligation.
- **[[Modern Calling]]** (inside-out) — internal passion, self-expression, self-fulfillment.

The integrative resolution treats these not as endpoints of one continuum but as two orthogonal dimensions ([[Inner and Outer Requiredness]]), whose joint high-high cell is the **[[Transcendent Calling]]**.

## Established correlates

Robustly (though mostly via self-report and cross-sectional data) associated with work/life/career satisfaction (the best-established finding), meaningfulness, organizational and occupational attachment, lower burnout and turnover intentions, efficacy, and occupational clarity. Also carries a **dark side**: heightened moral duty, willingness to sacrifice, career inflexibility, discounting of negative feedback, and regret when a calling is felt but unlivable.
