# Naturalistic Decision Making (NDM)

Research program studying expert decision makers in real settings rather than laboratory choice tasks. Associated with Klein (Recognition-Primed Decision model), Orasanu & Connolly (1993), Cannon-Bowers & Salas (1998).

## Core Claim

In complex, time-pressured, high-stakes, uncertain, dynamic environments, expert choice rarely involves deliberative evaluation of alternatives. Instead:

1. Expert **recognizes** situation as belonging to a familiar class
2. **Matches** recognized class to an action that "fits" situational demands
3. Implements without extensive comparative evaluation

→ **Recognition-Primed Decision (RPD) model**

## Contrast with Conventional JDM

| Conventional JDM | NDM / RPD |
|---|---|
| Deliberate weighing of options | Rapid situation recognition |
| Evaluate → discount → add | Recognize → match → act |
| Error from cognitive limitations | Expert performance from pattern library |
| Lab/scenario-based | Field studies, think-aloud protocols |

## Implications

- Expert performance may rely on fundamentally different processes than novice/deliberative decision making
- "Deciding" vs. "doing what you know how to do" may be distinct phenomena
- Conventional JDM models may not apply to expert performance
- Deliberative decision aids (decision trees, MAUT) may be inappropriate for expert settings

## Limitations Noted by Connolly et al.

- Addresses phenomena "rather different from what has conventionally been called decision making"
- Expert success under RPD does not mean deliberative analysis is wrong in other contexts
- Less studied in ambiguous, novel, one-shot decisions
