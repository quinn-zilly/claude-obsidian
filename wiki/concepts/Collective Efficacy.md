# Collective Efficacy

Nav: [[index]] | [[Modes of Human Agency]]

**A group's shared belief in its collective power to produce desired results.** The efficacy analogue of [[Self-Efficacy (Goal Setting)|personal self-efficacy]], at the group level, underpinning **collective agency** (see [[Modes of Human Agency]]).

## Emergent, not additive

> [!key-insight]
> Perceived collective efficacy is an **emergent group-level property, not simply the sum** of members' individual efficacy beliefs, because collective performance involves transactional, coordinated, synergistic dynamics. Yet there is **no disembodied group mind** — it is people acting conjointly on a shared belief who do the cognizing, aspiring, and regulating.

## Consequences

The stronger the perceived collective efficacy, the higher the group's aspirations and motivational investment, the greater its staying power against setbacks, the higher its morale and resilience, and the greater its performance. Evidenced across educational systems, businesses, athletic and combat teams, neighborhoods, and political groups.

Connects to the vault's team literature — a plausible antecedent alongside [[Enabling Conditions for Team Effectiveness]] and [[Team Effectiveness Criteria]].
