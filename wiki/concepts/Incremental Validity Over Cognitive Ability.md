# Incremental Validity Over Cognitive Ability

The **added predictive power a non-cognitive measure contributes to a selection system that already includes a general mental ability (GMA / *g*) test**. Because GMA is the single strongest predictor of overall job performance and personality measures correlate only weakly with GMA, personality can supply **largely non-redundant** predictive variance. Operationalized as the increase in the multiple R (or ΔR²) when the personality predictor is added to GMA.

## Evidence (Ones et al. 2007)

- **[[Integrity Tests|Integrity tests]]** add **~.14** validity points over GMA for overall JP. Combined GMA + integrity multiple Rs: **.64 / .67 / .71** for low / medium / high complexity jobs (Ones et al. 1993; Schmidt & Hunter 1998).
- **Other [[Compound Traits (Personality)|compound scales]]** add **.07–.16** over GMA for overall JP.
- These increments involve **no** corrections for predictor unreliability or imperfect construct measurement — i.e., they are conservative.

## Why it matters

Incremental validity is the pragmatic case for personality in selection even to a skeptic who prizes GMA: the two predictor families are near-orthogonal, so adding a personality/compound measure to a cognitive battery reliably raises validity and utility. This point was acknowledged even by critics on the [[Morgeson et al. (2007) Panel Critique|Morgeson panel]] (Murphy, Campion).
