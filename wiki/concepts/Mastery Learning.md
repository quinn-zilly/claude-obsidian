# Mastery Learning

## Definition (CMU Eberly Principle 4)

Mastery requires three distinct elements, not just content knowledge:

1. **Component skills** — discrete sub-skills and knowledge pieces
2. **Integration** — practice combining components into fluent, automatic wholes
3. **Transfer** — knowing *when* and *how* to apply what has been learned

## Why This Matters

Most instruction covers component skills but underinvests in integration and transfer. Students may know sub-skills in isolation yet fail on complex tasks because they cannot combine them fluidly (fluency/automaticity) or recognize the right context for application (transfer).

> [!key-insight] Instructors often lack conscious awareness of their own mastery
> Experts have automated their component skills to the point of invisibility. This makes it easy to skip teaching integration steps that novices still need.

## Three-Phase Model

| Phase | What happens | Instructional support needed |
|-------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Acquisition | Learn discrete components | Direct instruction, worked examples |
| Integration | Combine components fluently | Varied practice, reduced scaffolding |
| Transfer | Apply appropriately in new contexts | Case variety, explicit generalization |
