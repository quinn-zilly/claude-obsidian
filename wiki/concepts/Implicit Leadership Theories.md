# Implicit Leadership Theories (ILTs)

Cognitive schemas that perceivers use to evaluate and categorize leaders. Introduced as a term by **Eden & Leviatan (1975)** to describe how rater belief structures contaminate behavioral ratings of leadership.

## Core Insight
The factor structure of leader behavior rating scales can be replicated even when raters have *no information* about a leader's actual behavior. This means questionnaire ratings reflect rater schemas as much as (or more than) actual leader behavior — a fundamental challenge to the Ohio State behavioral measurement tradition.

## Key Findings
- **Eden & Leviatan (1975)** [JAP, Table 1]: ILTs introduced; behavioral ratings reflect follower cognitive schemas
- **Weiss & Adler (1981)**: ILTs do not depend on cognitive complexity of raters — they are widely shared
- **Epitropaki & Martin (2004)**: ILTs are stable over time in applied settings
- **Epitropaki & Martin (2005)**: Better match between actual leader profile and employee's ILT → higher quality LMX
- **Lord, Foti & De Vader (1984)**: Leader categorization theory — leadership perceptions as prototype matching; prototypes are dynamic and context-sensitive (vary by race, gender, ethnicity of leader)
- **Foti & Hauenstein (2007)**: Prototypical leadership characteristics are processed as a pattern, not individual traits

## Leader Categorization Theory
Perceivers automatically categorize leaders in terms of implicit theories and use underlying category structure to generate behavioral ratings. Leadership perception = match to a prototype (set of attributes typically associated with leaders). This explains:
- Why behavioral ratings correlate with performance outcomes (rater inferences, not just real effects)
- Why emotions, race, and gender affect leadership ratings
- How single-source common method bias inflates correlations

## Methodological Implication
All-same-source designs are especially vulnerable to ILT contamination. Effect of rated leadership on outcomes may be partly artifactual.
