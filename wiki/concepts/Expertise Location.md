# Expertise Location

Expertise location is the process - and supporting capability - of identifying *who* in a group or organization holds the knowledge needed for a task. It is the operational heart of the "directory" in a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]]: the metaknowledge that lets a member retrieve knowledge they do not personally hold by routing to the right person.

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] highlight expertise location as central to scaling TMS beyond the small group to the [[Organizational Memory|organizational level]], where members may not know each other directly. They review *technological* approaches (expertise directories and repositories, Web 2.0 tools, mining email/messaging to infer expertise, small-world networks) and *interpersonal* approaches (member rotation, building accessibility and trust in experts). Faraj & Sproull (2000) is cited as one of the established TMS measurement approaches.
