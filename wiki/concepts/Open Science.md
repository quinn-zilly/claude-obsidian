# Open Science

Open Science refers to a movement and set of practices aimed at making scientific research more transparent, accessible, reproducible, and collaborative. It encompasses open access publishing, open data, open materials, preregistration, and institutional reforms to align research incentives with accuracy over novelty.

## Core Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Open Access** | Published findings freely available without subscription barriers |
| **Open Data** | Raw data deposited in public repositories; reusable by others |
| **Open Materials** | Stimuli, instruments, code, and protocols publicly available |
| **[[Preregistration]]** | Study designs and analysis plans documented before data collection |
| **[[Registered Reports]]** | Publication decisions based on design quality, before results known |
| **Open Peer Review** | Transparent, potentially post-publication review processes |

## Why "Open" Is Not Redundant

[[munafo-2017-manifesto]] notes that transparency is theoretically a scientific ideal — science *should* be inherently open. In practice, most data, code, and materials underlying published results are not accessible. The "open science" label flags the gap between ideal and practice.

## The Institutional Frame

Open science is fundamentally about **incentive alignment**: current academic incentives reward novelty, positive results, and publication volume, not accuracy, openness, or replication. Open science reforms try to make practices that are good for science also good for individual scientists' careers:
- Badges (COS) for open data
- Journal credit for preregistration
- Funder mandates for data sharing (NIH, NSF)
- Hiring criteria incorporating open science practices

## Key Infrastructure

- **[[Open Science Framework]] (OSF)** — project management, preregistration, data archiving
- **[[metaBUS]]** — open database of coded research findings
- **Zenodo, OSF, figshare** — data repositories
- **PsyArXiv, bioRxiv** — preprint servers
- **GitHub/GitLab** — code and analysis versioning

## Relationship to Knowledge Management

Open science is a **collective knowledge management strategy** for science as an organization. From the perspective of [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm|KBV]]:
- **Knowledge codification**: open data and materials make tacit methodological knowledge explicit
- **Knowledge retention**: permanent archives prevent knowledge loss
- **Knowledge transfer**: open access and open data enable cross-institution knowledge transfer
- **Knowledge quality**: preregistration and multiverse analysis improve the reliability of stored knowledge

The [[LLM Wiki Pattern]] this vault is built on is itself an instance of open science principles applied to individual knowledge management.
