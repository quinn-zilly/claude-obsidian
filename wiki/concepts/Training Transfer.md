# Training Transfer

## Definition

Application of trained knowledge and skills to the **work environment** after training. In the [[Augmented Training Criteria Framework]] this is Level 3.

Critical distinction from Level 2c (behavior/skill demonstration): transfer requires the behavior to occur *on the job*, not just in the training context. Maps onto "does do" rather than "can do."

## Determinants

Transfer is a function of two components (Baldwin & Ford, 1988):
1. **Knowledge acquisition** — what was learned in training
2. **Work environment favorability** — degree to which the environment supports application (supervisor support, opportunity to use, climate)

This two-component structure explains why learning measures alone predict transfer imperfectly, and why utility reactions may outperform them (trainees implicitly factor in environment when rating utility).

## Empirical Predictors (from [[alliger-et-al-1997-training-criteria-meta-analysis]])

| Predictor | r with Transfer |
|---|---|
| Utility reactions | Moderate |
| Combined reactions | Moderate |
| Immediate learning | Lower than utility reactions |
| Retained learning | Lower than utility reactions |
| Behavior/skill demo (2c) | Similar to utility reactions |
| Affective reactions | Weak |

> [!key-insight]
> The finding that utility reactions outpredict learning for transfer is counterintuitive but replicable. Learning measures capture capability but not motivation or environmental readiness; utility reactions may capture both.

## Measurement

On-the-job performance indicators: supervisor ratings, work samples, work output, behavioral observation. Must be measured *after* training in the actual job context.

## Three-Factor Model (Burke & Hutchins 2007)

Integrative review of ~170 empirical studies identifies three factor categories:

1. **Learner characteristics** — cognitive ability (rc=.43), self-efficacy (strong, malleable), pretraining motivation (r=.45), organizational commitment (rc=.45–.61), perceived utility
2. **Intervention design** — learning goals (r=.37), content relevance (r=.61), practice & feedback, behavioral modeling, error-based examples; active learning *advocated but not empirically proven*
3. **Work environment** — transfer climate (rc=.37), peer support (B=.65), supervisory support, opportunity to perform

**Transfer problem:** Only 40–50% of training investments yield sustained performance change.

**Transfer paradox:** Repeated exact-task practice + frequent feedback → mastery but NOT transfer. Whole-part sequencing + scaffolding better for complex skill transfer.

## After-Event Reviews as Transfer Mechanism

AERs (Ellis & Davidi 2005) serve as structured posttraining feedback mechanisms. Key finding: reviewing both successes and failures produces richer mental models and greater performance improvement than failure-only review. Mental model richness mediates learning-to-performance pathway.

## Smith-Jentsch (2020) — Practitioner Perspective on Transfer Failure

Three hidden causes of declining transfer over time within organizations:
1. **Training needs changed** — tasks evolved, technology changed, virtual work replaced co-location, new-hire KSA profiles shifted
2. **Implementation changed** — instructor turnover erodes program fidelity; mode shifts to e-learning disrupt active practice and intellectual flow
3. **Transfer environment changed** — performance appraisal criteria no longer reward trained behaviors; supervisor support weakened; timing of training relative to experience suboptimal

Key recommendation: continually monitor transfer metrics with *consistent measurement* across cohorts so decline trends can be identified early and attributed to specific root causes.

## IMTEE Perspective on Transfer Predictors (Alvarez et al. 2004)

The [[Integrated Model of Training Evaluation and Effectiveness (IMTEE)]] identifies 10 effectiveness variables. Transfer-specific predictors (≥3 studies in consistent direction):
- Pretraining self-efficacy
- Learning principles (practice, feedback, behavioral modeling, identical elements)
- Posttraining maintenance interventions (goal-setting, visualization, mastery manipulation)
- High difficulty training
- Positive transfer environment
