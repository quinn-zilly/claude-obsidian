# Training Transfer

## Definition

Application of trained knowledge and skills to the **work environment** after training. In the [[Augmented Training Criteria Framework]] this is Level 3.

Critical distinction from Level 2c (behavior/skill demonstration): transfer requires the behavior to occur *on the job*, not just in the training context. Maps onto "does do" rather than "can do."

## Determinants

Transfer is a function of two components (Baldwin & Ford, 1988):
1. **Knowledge acquisition** — what was learned in training
2. **Work environment favorability** — degree to which the environment supports application (supervisor support, opportunity to use, climate)

This two-component structure explains why learning measures alone predict transfer imperfectly, and why utility reactions may outperform them (trainees implicitly factor in environment when rating utility).

## Empirical Predictors (from [[alliger-et-al-1997-training-criteria-meta-analysis]])

| Predictor | r with Transfer |
|---|---|
| Utility reactions | Moderate |
| Combined reactions | Moderate |
| Immediate learning | Lower than utility reactions |
| Retained learning | Lower than utility reactions |
| Behavior/skill demo (2c) | Similar to utility reactions |
| Affective reactions | Weak |

> [!key-insight]
> The finding that utility reactions outpredict learning for transfer is counterintuitive but replicable. Learning measures capture capability but not motivation or environmental readiness; utility reactions may capture both.

## Measurement

On-the-job performance indicators: supervisor ratings, work samples, work output, behavioral observation. Must be measured *after* training in the actual job context.
