# Spacing Effect

The spacing effect is the finding that learning is more durable and transferable when study or practice sessions are distributed over time (spaced practice) rather than massed into a single session (massed practice or "cramming").

## Core Finding

Distributed practice produces better long-term retention than massed practice, even when total study time is held constant. One of the most replicated findings in cognitive psychology.

## Mechanism

Spaced practice is a [[Desirable Difficulties|desirable difficulty]] — it introduces short-term difficulty (forgetting between sessions) that forces effortful retrieval, strengthening memory traces.
