# Trainee Characteristics

## Definition

Individual-level variables that influence training motivation, learning processes, and outcomes. Includes capabilities, personality, motivation, values/interests, attitudes/emotions, and perceptions.

## Historical Progression

**Pre-1960s**: GMA and aptitude dominant. IQ → trade learning success (Cowdery 1922). Military applications.

**1960s–70s**: Personality and attitudes enter; trainability testing emerges (Robertson & Downs 1979, 1989). Trainability = structured instruction + learning assessment — distinct from job success.

**1980s**: Self-efficacy enters (Gist et al. 1989); Kanfer & Ackerman (1989) integrative information-processing model (ability × motivation × task demands across practice).

**1990s**: Goal orientation (Brett & VandeWalle 1999); motivation to participate; socialization research. Colquitt & Simmering (1998): conscientiousness + goal orientation → motivation to learn longitudinally.

**2000s**: Colquitt, LePine & Noe (2000) meta-analysis — integrative theory of training motivation; GMA + personality + climate + age → motivation → learning. Within-person dynamics (Vancouver & Kendall 2006).

## Key Constructs

### General Mental Ability (GMA)
- Consistent positive predictor of training success across all eras
- More important early in skill acquisition; practice reduces ability-based performance gaps (Eyring et al. 1993)
- Interacts with error training: high ability benefits more

### Self-Efficacy
- Between-persons: positive → learning (Gist et al. 1989; Bell & Kozlowski 2002)
- **Within-person: NEGATIVE** → learning (Vancouver & Kendall 2006) — high self-efficacy reduces planning resource allocation
- Mediates behavioral modeling training → job search behavior (Eden & Aviram 1993)
- Malleable through training

### Goal Orientation
- Learning (mastery) orientation → skill improvement goals → better training performance (Brett & VandeWalle 1999)
- Payne et al. (2007) meta-analysis: learning GO positive, avoid GO negative, prove GO ≈ zero with learning
- Interacts with ability: Bell & Kozlowski (2002) — learning GO adaptive only for high-ability trainees
- Interacts with instructor style: Towler & Dipboye (2001) — high mastery + organized/inexpressive instructor → worst problem-solving

### Conscientiousness
- Positively related to motivation to learn and learning (Colquitt & Simmering 1998)

### Proactive Personality
- Emerging as important for informal and technology-based learning contexts
- Likely supports planning in self-directed e-learning

### Age
- Negatively affects dispositional factors predisposing participation in development (Maurer et al. 2003)

## Trainability

Distinct from job success. Trainability testing = structured instruction period + assessment of learning = predicts short-term success better than long-term (Robertson & Downs 1979, 1989). Minicourses (Reilly & Israelski 1988) have similar validities to work samples.

## Colquitt et al. (2000) Meta-Analytic Model

Training motivation explains incremental variance beyond GMA. Effects of personality, climate, and age on training outcomes partially mediated by self-efficacy, valence, and job involvement.

## Open Questions (Bell et al. 2017)

- Trainee characteristics for informal learning: self-efficacy, goal orientation, personality — evidence emerging but thin
- Proactivity in tech-based training: planning matters; proactive personality may buffer against obstacles/interruptions
- Knowledge-sharing characteristics: what makes trainees receive AND share knowledge effectively?
- Within-person dynamics over training: how do ability, self-efficacy, goal orientation interact *dynamically* across practice trials?
