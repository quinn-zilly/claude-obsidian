# Institutional Change Pathways

A 2×2 typology developed by [[Evelyn Micelotta]], [[Michael Lounsbury]], and [[Royston Greenwood]] in [[micelotta-2017-pathways-institutional-change]] to organize the fragmented institutional change literature (1990–2015). The framework uses two dimensions drawn from the institutional logics perspective:

## The Two Dimensions

**Scope (transformational vs. developmental)**
- *Transformational*: shared understandings defining what is accepted and valued in the field are overturned or significantly altered — through replacement, blending, or segregation of institutional logics
- *Developmental*: alterations are relatively narrow; existing arrangements are *stretched* rather than discarded — through contraction, assimilation, or elaboration of logics

**Pace (revolutionary vs. evolutionary)**
- *Revolutionary*: potent macro-level dynamics interrupt institutional reproduction; fast; disruptive conflict between challengers and incumbents with asymmetric power
- *Evolutionary*: slower processes; modest innovations; persuasive embedding; "unobtrusive diffusion" (Smets et al. 2012); pragmatic rather than projective agency

## The Four Pathways

| | **Transformational** | **Developmental** |
|---|---|---|
| **Revolutionary** | [[Institutional Displacement]] | [[Institutional Accommodation]] |
| **Evolutionary** | [[Institutional Accretion]] | [[Institutional Alignment]] |

### Distribution in the Literature (1990–2015)
- Displacement: 47 studies (most studied)
- Alignment: 46 studies (second most)
- Accommodation: 15 studies (understudied)
- Accretion: 11 studies (least studied)

The skew toward displacement and alignment reflects an implicit linear causality assumption: revolutionary forces → transformation; evolutionary forces → modest change. The understudied pathways reveal nonlinear dynamics.

## Why the Gap Matters

**Accommodation** (revolutionary pace, developmental scope): revolutionary forces get *dampened* — negative feedback loops, cooptation, settlement, increasing negative returns. Change agents engage but the result is incremental revision, not field-level overhaul. Requires relational rather than actor-centric framing.

**Accretion** (evolutionary pace, transformational scope): small micro-level improvisations *accumulate and escalate* — positive feedback loops, amplification, escalation. No single shock or heroic entrepreneur, but transformation results. The most novel theoretical territory per Micelotta et al.

## The Role of Institutional Pluralism

A key integrating theme: prior theory assumed monistic environments (one dominant logic). Real fields are pluralistic. Pluralism:
- Moderates how exogenous shocks propagate through displacement
- Shapes whether alignment strategies succeed or fail with heterogeneous audiences
- Determines what "settlements" look like in accommodation
- Creates the institutional complexity that generates micro-level pragmatic responses underlying accretion
