# Operational Validity (Selection)

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

**Operational validity** = the estimated correlation between a selection procedure and job performance in the applicant population, corrected for **criterion unreliability** and **[[Range Restriction Correction|range restriction]]** — but *not* for predictor unreliability (because the operational predictor is used as-is, with its imperfect reliability).

## Why it matters

It is the number practitioners cite when ranking selection methods (structured interviews vs. cognitive ability vs. personality). The canonical table was [[Frank L. Schmidt|Schmidt]] & Hunter (1998); [[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|Sackett et al. (2022)]] revised it downward by .10–.20 for most procedures after fixing range-restriction overcorrection.

## Correction stack

1. Observed validity *r*.
2. Correct for **criterion measurement error** → reliability-corrected validity.
3. Correct for **range restriction** (or, per Sackett et al., often *don't* for concurrent studies).

The result is operational validity; correcting *additionally* for predictor unreliability would give the theoretical **construct-level** true score correlation, which overstates what an operational test achieves.

## Selected revised estimates (Sackett et al. 2022)

Structured interviews ρ = .42 (top-ranked); job knowledge .40; cognitive ability .31 (down from .51); conscientiousness .19. See [[Criterion-Related Validity of Personality]] and the [[Sackett-et-al-2022-validity-overcorrection|source page]] for the full table.
