# Social Support (Behaviour)

The provision of informational, instrumental, or emotional help from others to facilitate a particular behavioural goal. A social-structural determinant with medium intervention efficacy — second only to [[Behavioural Access Interventions]].

## Definition

> "Informational, instrumental or financial help to facilitate a particular behaviour." — Albarracín et al. 2024 (Table 3)

Distinct from [[Descriptive Norms]]: norms concern whether others *approve* of or *perform* a behaviour; social support concerns whether others *actively help* with a specific behavioural goal.

## Correlational Evidence

Variable but substantial associations:
- Medical adherence: 1.74× higher with cohesive families; 1.53× lower with high-conflict families
- Exercise: family support d=0.36–0.44; class leaders and classmates d=0.31–0.32
- Childcare behaviours: emotional, material, informational support each r≈0.27–0.31

## Intervention Efficacy

**Medium** (mean OR≈2.53).

### Forms of social support interventions

- **Support groups**: facilitate dietary/physical activity goals for weight loss, medication adherence, behavioural change
- **Antiretroviral adherence support**: OR=1.66
- **Suicide prevention**: OR=0.48
- **Public commitment + social support**: d=0.26–0.58 for conservation behaviour
- **Promotoras model** (community health workers): peer-led support from demographically similar facilitators, especially effective in Latino communities

## Distinction from Norms

| | Norms | Social support |
|---|---|---|
| Content | What others do/approve | Active help for *your* goal |
| Mechanism | Social comparison, obligation | Tangible/informational assistance |
| Effect size | Small (both injunctive and descriptive) | Medium |
