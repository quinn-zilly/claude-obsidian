# Salary Structure

A **salary structure** (pay scale, pay structure) is a tool that creates pay levels and pay opportunities that are **internally fair, externally competitive, and cost-effective**, supporting an organisation's pay strategy. It is the Stage-2 output of [[Job Evaluation]]: grades (e.g. Paterson A–F) with attached **pay ranges** (minimum → lower guide → midpoint → upper guide → maximum), anchored on market data at the midpoint.

The [[Job Evaluation|job-evaluation]] grade is the reference point and anchor: wrong grades → wrong salary reference point.

## Influences on structure

Supply and demand; market rate; cost of living; the firm's/industry's financial position; management policy; trade unions and existing agreements; the current wage structure; the number of grades top-to-bottom; and market stance/relativity.

## Strategic pre-checks

Whether the structure supports business strategy; compatibility with total-remuneration design (fixed vs variable, the guaranteed-pay-to-variable ratio); external equity (competitive practice); internal equity (does it map to the org/job-grade structure?); administrative policy; and available funds.

Sits within an organisation's broader [[Total Rewards]] strategy; the residual snapshot of actual pay across employees is [[Pay Dispersion]].
