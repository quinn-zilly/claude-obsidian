# Causal Description vs Causal Explanation

A distinction (Shadish, Cook & Campbell 2002; used centrally in [[Theory-Driven Evaluation]]):

- **Causal description** — establishing *that* a causal relationship exists between A and B (a molar cause produces an effect). This is what randomized experiments are especially good at.
- **Causal explanation** — explaining *how and why* A causes B: the mediating mechanisms, moderating conditions, and the components of cause and effect responsible for the relationship.

TDE's distinctive value claim is that it targets **causal explanation** — knowing which elements are essential for replication, and, when a program fails, whether the failure is implementation, context, or theory. Critics (Scriven) counter that explanation is beyond most evaluators' remit and not required to judge merit and worth (the aspirin analogy: you can know aspirin works without a theory of how).
