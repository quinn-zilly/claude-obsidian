# Inner and Outer Requiredness

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

The two-dimensional framework [[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson (2019)]] use to organize the fragmented calling literature, adapted from **Maslow (1967)**:

- **Inner requiredness** — the "I want to": intrinsic interest, consuming passion, fascination, personal meaning, enjoyment. Felt as self-indulgence, not duty. The core of [[Modern Calling|modern]] conceptions.
- **Outer requiredness** — the "I must": duty, obligation, responsibility, a need in the world that "calls for" a response (Maslow's analogy: a fire "calls for" putting out). The core of [[Neoclassical Calling|neoclassical]] conceptions.

## The key theoretical move

Instead of arraying calling definitions on a single neoclassical-to-modern continuum, the authors treat inner and outer requiredness as **two orthogonal dimensions**, producing a 2×2:

| | Low outer | High outer |
|---|---|---|
| **High inner** | [[Modern Calling]] | **[[Transcendent Calling]]** |
| **Low inner** | Job or career | [[Neoclassical Calling]] |

The high-high cell — where consuming passion matches a genuine external need — is the **[[Transcendent Calling]]**, felt (following Maslow) as harmony, fit, or destiny when "I want to" coincides with "I must." Of the 14 definitions Thompson & Bunderson tabulated, 6 emphasize primarily inner requiredness, 7 primarily outer, and only [[Dik-Duffy-2009-Calling-and-Vocation|Dik & Duffy (2009)]] and Hunter et al. (2010) explicitly straddle both; just 5 include destiny.
