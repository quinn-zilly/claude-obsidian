# KSA Framework

## Definition

The core output of job analysis: specification of **Knowledge**, **Skills**, and **Abilities** required for effective job performance.

## Traditional Use

Derived from current task analysis. Used to drive:
- Selection/staffing criteria
- Training program design
- Performance appraisal standards
- Compensation benchmarking

## Critique (Singh 2008)

Traditional KSA derivation assumes static jobs. In dynamic environments:
- Specific KSAs become obsolete quickly
- Team-based work requires inter-personal and coordination KSAs not captured by task inventories
- Jobs that don't yet exist can't be task-analyzed

## SJA Shift: Toward Enduring Abilities

Singh (2008) argues future-oriented JA should emphasize **broad, enduring abilities**:
- Adaptability / flexibility
- Ambiguity tolerance
- Learning agility (learn + relearn)
- Creativity and problem-solving
- Cooperative / team-oriented work

## Team KSAs (Campion 1994, Sanchez 1994)

For team-based work, KSA focus shifts to:
- Task coordination
- Participation
- Conflict resolution
- Problem-solving
- Communication
- Organizational citizenship
- Customer orientation
