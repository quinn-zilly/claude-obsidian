# Team Adaptation (Burke Cross-Level Model)

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

The classic multilevel model of team adaptation from [[burke-et-al-2006-team-adaptation|Burke, Stagl, Salas, Pierce & Kendall (2006)]]. Not to be confused with the newer [[Team Adaptation (Stimulus-Based Model)|Pearsall et al. (2025) stimulus-based model]].

## Definition

**Team adaptation** = a change in team performance, in response to a salient cue or cue stream, that yields a *functional outcome for the entire team*. The functionality requirement distinguishes it from innovation and problem management.

## The Four-Phase Adaptive Cycle

A recursive input–throughput–output cycle producing [[Adaptive Team Performance]]:

1. **Situation assessment** → emergent cognitive states. Cue scanning, recognition, meaning ascription; habitual routines *impede* cue detection. Produces [[Shared Mental Models]] + [[Team Situation Awareness]]. (Props 1–2)
2. **Plan formulation** → deciding action, roles, priorities. Proximal inputs: [[Team Situation Awareness]] and [[Psychological Safety]] (which lets members speak up / play devil's advocate). Revises shared cognition. (Props 3–5)
3. **Plan execution** → enacted via [[Adaptive Coordination Behaviors]] (mutual performance monitoring, backup behavior, communication, leadership, coordination). (Props 6–8)
4. **Team learning** → feedback; competencies acquired *from* adapting feed back as inputs.

## Emergent States (cross-phase)

[[Shared Mental Models]], [[Team Situation Awareness]], and [[Psychological Safety]] recur as both proximal inputs and outputs of each phase. They emerge via **composition** (isomorphic, identical knowledge → aggregate by mean/sum) or **compilation** (unique-but-compatible → configural indices), depending on definition (Kozlowski & Klein 2000).

## Distal Inputs

Two categories feed the cycle: **individual characteristics** (knowledge, task/team expertise, attitudes like team orientation, traits like openness/cognitive ability) and **job design characteristics** (leadership, self-management). Only secondary variables with supporting evidence were included, for parsimony.

## Scope Conditions

Most illustrative for **moderate-to-high task/outcome interdependence**; component weighting shifts as interdependence falls. Models **bottom-up emergence only** — top-down and MTS/organizational dynamics deliberately excluded.

## Lineage

Seminal roots for later adaptation theory. See the contradiction callout in [[burke-et-al-2006-team-adaptation]] on how [[Team Adaptation (Stimulus-Based Model)]] recasts these inputs as phase-specific moderators and centers the *stimulus* rather than the response.
