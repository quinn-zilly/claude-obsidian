# Internal Validity

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

The validity of inferences about whether observed **covariation between A and B reflects a causal relationship** from A to B, in the form the variables were manipulated or measured. Second component of the [[Validity Typology]].

## Three requirements for a causal inference

1. **A precedes B** in time.
2. **A covaries with B** (this is the [[Statistical Conclusion Validity]] question).
3. **No plausible alternative explanation** for the covariation.

## Nine threats

1. **Ambiguous Temporal Precedence** — unclear which variable came first.
2. **Selection** — pre-existing systematic differences between conditions.
3. **History** — concurrent external events cause the outcome.
4. **Maturation** — natural change over time mistaken for effect.
5. **Regression** to the mean — extreme-score selection.
6. **Attrition** — differential dropout by condition.
7. **Testing** — prior test exposure affects later scores.
8. **Instrumentation** — the measure itself changes over time/conditions.
9. **Additive and Interactive Effects of Threats** — threats compound.

See [[Threats to Validity]] for the ruling-out method.

## Randomization

Random assignment is the salient design control for internal validity: it makes selection, and most other threats, implausible by design. But it does **not** touch attrition, and it can hamper [[External Validity]]. The rest of the SCC book covers design controls for when randomization is impossible or breaks down.
