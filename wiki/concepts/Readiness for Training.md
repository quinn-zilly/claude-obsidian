# Readiness for Training

Whether the conditions are in place for training to succeed, assessed during [[Person Analysis (Noe Model)]]. Two components (Noe):

1. **Personal characteristics** — employees have the ability, attitudes, beliefs, and [[Motivation to Learn|motivation]] needed to learn the content and apply it on the job.
2. **Work environment** — facilitates learning and does not interfere with performance (adequate input: tools, time, social support, opportunity to perform).

## Person Characteristics Assessed

- **Basic skills** — cognitive ability, reading/writing (see [[Literacy Audit]]).
- **Cognitive ability** — verbal comprehension, quantitative ability, reasoning; predicts learning and performance, more so as jobs grow complex (cashier vs. ER physician; FAA air-traffic-controller cognitive testing).
- **Self-efficacy** — belief one can succeed; boostable (see [[Motivation to Learn]]).
- **Awareness** of skill gaps, career interests, goals — employees must see the link between training and their weaknesses.
- **Age / generation** — short-term memory and processing speed decline with age, but experience compensates; some methods suit older trainees better.

Assessing readiness prevents sending unprepared trainees into programs — a failure mode needs assessment exists to catch.
