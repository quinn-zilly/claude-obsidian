# Scientist-Practitioner Model

The dominant self-definition of organizational psychology: understand **and** improve the structure and functioning of organizations and the people in them, by means of rigorous scientific methods. Originated as a graduate-training model for clinical psychology (Raimy 1950).

[[Joel Lefkowitz|Lefkowitz]] (2008) argues the S-P model is the **entire** explicitly avowed values system of the field — and that it is, in criterion-development terms, both:

- **Deficient** — it fails to represent the interests of individuals and the common good (one of [psychology's "two cultures," Kimble 1984]).
- **Contaminated** — it has been consumed by free-enterprise, shareholder-value-above-all-else assumptions.

His remedy is to expand it into the [[Scientist-Practitioner-Humanist (S-P-H) Model]].
