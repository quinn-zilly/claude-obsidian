# Vertical and Horizontal Gender Segregation

The two structural indicators of (in)equality in **[[Women's Career Equality|women's career equality]]** (Kossek, Su & Wu 2017).

## The two axes
- **Vertical integration** — equal representation of women and men in **leadership across hierarchical levels**. Underrepresentation up the hierarchy = **vertical segregation** (the **[[Glass Ceiling]]**).
- **Horizontal integration** — equal representation across **occupations that are pathways to the top** (STEM, finance, line vs staff roles). Underrepresentation across functions = **horizontal segregation**.

## Why it reproduces inequality
Women are overrepresented in HR and administrative services (Catalyst 2015), which advance less often. This structure **culturally reproduces** inequality by giving women less access to power, decision-making, opportunities, and networks (Kanter 1977). Career-equality metrics should therefore measure **both** axes simultaneously alongside intrinsic/extrinsic outcomes.
