# Integrated Trait-Behavioral Model of Leadership

The conceptual framework from [[derue-et-al-2011-trait-behavioral-leadership-integration|DeRue et al. (2011)]] (Figure 1) that unifies the two historically separate paradigms — the trait paradigm and the behavior paradigm — into one causal model of leadership effectiveness.

## Structure

**Leader traits** organized into three categories:

- **Demographics** — gender, age, ethnicity, height, education
- **Task competence** — intelligence, Conscientiousness, Openness, Emotional Stability
- **Interpersonal attributes** — Extraversion, Agreeableness, communication skills, emotional intelligence

**Leader behaviors** organized into four categories:

- **Task-oriented** — initiating structure, contingent reward, management-by-exception-active
- **Relational-oriented** — consideration, empowering, participative, servant
- **Change-oriented** — transformational, charismatic/inspirational
- **Passive** — MBE-passive, laissez-faire

**Effectiveness** conceptualized on three dimensions: *content* (task / affective-relational / overall), *level of analysis* (individual / dyadic / group / organizational), and *target of evaluation* (leader vs. other outcome).

## Central Claim

Traits influence effectiveness through **two mechanisms**: (1) behaviors act as a *mediator* — traits produce behaviors that produce effectiveness; (2) traits shape *attributions and identification* — followers judge leaders on salient traits independent of actual behavior. Behaviors are positioned as more proximal to the act of leadership, so they predict more variance than distal traits (backed by [[Trait Activation Theory]]: traits only manifest when the situation makes them salient).

## Empirical Payoff

Traits + behaviors explain 31–92% of effectiveness variance; behaviors carry 62–94% of that; behaviors partially mediate trait effects (intelligence/Conscientiousness → structure + transformational; Extraversion/Agreeableness → consideration). See the source page for full numbers.

## Why It Mattered

Ended the practice of testing single traits or single behaviors in isolation, and gave later work (e.g., [[Construct Redundancy (Leadership)|construct-redundancy]] tests) a template for direct relative-validity comparison rather than construct proliferation.
