# Reflexive Learning (Team Adaptation)

**Phase 5** of the [[Team Adaptation (Stimulus-Based Model)|Pearsall et al. (2025) stimulus-based model]].

## Definition

Within a specific performance episode, reflexive learning is "an ongoing process of reflection and action, characterized by asking questions, seeking feedback, experimenting, reflecting on results, and discussing errors or unexpected outcomes of actions" (Edmondson, 1999, p. 354).

Researchers refer to this "within episode" process as **reflexive learning** (Harvey et al., 2023): cycles of learning and performance during a task allow for more effective adaptation of strategies and procedures.

## Mechanism

As the team implements its adaptive response (Phase 4), it receives feedback — internal (reduced stress, enhanced capacity) or external (performance metrics, goal achievement). This feedback triggers two reflective sub-processes:

1. **Updating earlier phase assessments** — revisiting urgency and duration estimates in light of new information ("revising provisional sensemaking to incorporate new cues"; Christianson, 2019, p. 45)
2. **Microadjusting current adaptive behaviors** — modifying how the response is being executed

## What Gets Updated?

- **Phase 2 (Urgency)**: A stimulus initially viewed as low-urgency may escalate (e.g., a storm changing course). Magnitude assessments may persist even as threats grow.
- **Phase 3 (Duration)**: A "short absence" becomes a permanent departure; the short-term workaround must be abandoned for a long-term restructuring.
- **Phase 4 (Response)**: Even well-matched responses may prove suboptimal. Bricolage (applying novel resource combinations) may be required.

## Key Examples

- Ellis et al. (2003): Teams facing unknown threats experimented, learned, collectively shared → adapted tactics mid-episode
- Geiger et al. (2021): Temporal fit between internal routines and external environment requires multiple iterations — not simultaneous
- Konradt et al. (2021): Reflection + feedback updated team/task mental models → improved adaptive performance over time

## Enabling Factors

- **Psychological safety** — critical for teams to readjust after misclassification errors (Edmondson, 1999)
- Empowering leadership (enhances sensemaking) vs. directive leadership (may hinder reflection in learning phases)
- Team learning orientation

## Distinction from Phase 6

Reflexive learning is *within-episode*, focused on improving the current adaptive response. [[Knowledge and Skill Acquisition (Team Adaptation)]] is *post-episode*, focused on developing the team's future adaptability.
