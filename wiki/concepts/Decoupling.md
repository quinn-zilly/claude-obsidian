# Decoupling

**Decoupling** is the gap organizations deliberately maintain between their *formal structure* (the legitimacy-conferring blueprint of policies, programs, and offices) and their *actual work activities*. Introduced by [[John W. Meyer]] and [[Brian Rowan]] in [[meyer-rowan-1977-institutionalized-organizations]] (Proposition 4).

## Why Organizations Decouple

Tight coordination and control — inspection, output measurement, evaluation — would expose the inconsistency and inefficiency that follows from conforming to [[Rationalized Myth|rationalized myths]] that don't fit the technical core. A faithful public record of that mismatch destroys legitimacy. So organizations **buffer** the formal structure from technical work instead.

## Observable Signatures

> [!example] How decoupling shows up
> - **Delegation to professionals** — activity happens beyond managers' purview; professionalism is actively encouraged.
> - **Vacuous, categorical goals** — hospitals "treat" rather than "cure"; schools "produce students," not "learning."
> - **Invisible output data** — cure rates ignored, effectiveness measures avoided, achievement de-emphasized.
> - **Ceremonialized inspection** — integration avoided, implementation neglected, evaluation made ritual.
> - **Informal coordination** — formal rules, if applied literally, would generate contradictions, so people "get along" off the books; that skill is highly valued.

## The Payoff

Decoupling lets organizations keep **standardized, legitimating formal structures** while their actual practices vary freely with practical conditions. This is why firms in an industry look alike in structure (shared institutional origin) yet differ widely in practice. It also minimizes visible conflict, widening the range of external constituents who lend support.

## Pairing

Decoupling does not produce anarchy because it is sustained by the [[Logic of Confidence]] and good faith. The two devices together resolve the tension between ceremonial conformity and efficiency.

## Related

- [[Loose Coupling]] (Weick 1976) — the broader, more descriptive notion Meyer & Rowan draw on; decoupling is its strategic, legitimacy-driven form.
- [[Institutional Isomorphism]] — decoupling explains how isomorphic *structures* can coexist with diverse *practices*.
