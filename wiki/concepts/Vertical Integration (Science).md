# Vertical Integration (Science)

## Definition

The standard model of scientific investigation in which a single individual or small research team controls the full pipeline: conceiving the question, designing studies, implementing them, analyzing data, and writing up the report. The team operates as a closed, vertically integrated unit — analogous to a vertically integrated business that controls its full supply chain.

Term and critique from Uhlmann et al. (2019), [[uhlmann-2019-crowdsourcing-science|Scientific Utopia III]].

## Characteristics

- **Localized:** funding distributed to specific labs and institutions
- **Resource-intensive:** project divided among few individuals with limited capacity
- **Small scope:** limited sample size, stimuli, operationalizations, and analytic alternatives
- **Philosopher-scholar model:** independent thinker generates stand-alone scholarship; credit directly linked to origination
- **Reward structure:** publication in prestigious outlet; novelty and discovery valued

## Strengths

- Efficient for initial evidence generation
- Allows deep dives into methodology and phenomenon
- Decentralized nature fosters diverse ideas
- Credit allocation is unambiguous

## Structural Limitations

- Can only collect so much data, so many analyses, so many design alternatives
- Susceptible to researcher degrees of freedom (P-hacking, HARKing) because one team's career depends on result
- Typically single population, limiting generalizability
- Produces "small science" — many underpowered, locally constrained studies
- Matthew effect: early advantages in institution, connections, grants accumulate; limits access for researchers from less-resourced settings

## Relationship to Replication Crisis

Vertical integration is a structural contributor to the replication crisis: when one small team controls the full pipeline, analytic flexibility is unchecked, the team's incentives are aligned with positive results, and the sample is constrained. See [[Conflict of Interest in Science]] and [[Motivated Reasoning]].
