# Prediction Markets (Science)

Mechanism for aggregating expert beliefs about whether a study will replicate. Participants buy/sell contracts: worth $1 if study replicates, $0 if not. Contract price = probability estimate.

## Performance

Prediction markets predict replication outcomes with r = **0.52** (Nosek et al. 2022 meta-summary across 5 projects). Dichotomous accuracy: **72%** of 123 findings predicted correctly.

Comparable methods:
- Surveys: r = 0.48, 64% accuracy
- Structured elicitations (IDEA protocol): r = 0.75 (best)
- Machine learning: ≈ prediction markets

## Key Projects Using Prediction Markets

- Dreber et al. (2015) — OSC 2015 findings
- Camerer et al. (2016, 2018) — economics and Nature/Science social science
- Forsell et al. (2019) — Many Labs 2 (less successful for effect size prediction)
- Ebersole et al. (2020) — Many Labs 5

## Implication

Replicability is **predictable before costly replication**. Features of original studies (p-value, sample size, effect size, whether main vs. interaction effect) carry credibility information. Machine learning models suggest scalable screening is feasible.

Lead researcher: [[Anna Dreber]] (Stockholm School of Economics).
