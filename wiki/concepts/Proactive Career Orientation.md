# Proactive Career Orientation

**Proactive career orientation** is the single general factor that [[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal (2019)]] found underlying three previously separate career-orientation components: protean **self-directed**, protean **values-driven**, and boundaryless **psychological mobility**. It reflects "a broad tendency to take a confident, self-initiated, goal-directed approach toward one's career."

The finding collapses the traditional [[Protean Career Orientation|protean]]-vs-[[Boundaryless Career|boundaryless]] distinction: these are not empirically separable orientations but facets of one proactive disposition toward one's career.

## What stays distinct

Boundaryless **physical (organizational) mobility preference** is *not* part of this factor — it is weakly related and has a divergent nomological net. Lumping physical and psychological mobility under "boundaryless" obscures two different constructs.

## Nomological net & validity (meta-analysis, 135 samples / 45,288 people)

- Strongly tied to **proactive personality, self-efficacy, Openness, Extraversion, Conscientiousness**.
- **Strong** predictor of [[Career Self-Management Behavior]] and career satisfaction ([[Subjective Career Success|SCS]]).
- **Weak** predictor of [[Objective Career Success]] (\|ρ\| < .20) and of physical mobility behavior.
- Much predictive power is *shared* with personality traits, but shows **incremental validity for career satisfaction and turnover intentions** (ΔR² ≈ .10) — strongest where conceptually aligned.

## Position in the causal chain

A **characteristic adaptation** sitting between broad traits and career behavior: proactivity + self-efficacy → proactive career orientation → career self-management + positive career evaluation. Being an adaptation (not a trait), it is more **malleable via career counseling** — the practical payoff.
