# Motivation and Learning

## Definition (CMU Eberly Principle 3)

Motivation **determines, directs, and sustains** what students do to learn. As students gain autonomy over study habits in college, motivation becomes the primary driver of learning behavior quality.

## Three-Factor Model

Students are strongly motivated to learn when they:

1. **Find positive value** in the learning goal or activity (interest, relevance, importance)
2. **Expect to succeed** — perceived self-efficacy and outcome expectancy
3. **Perceive environmental support** — feel belonging and that the environment is conducive to success

> [!key-insight] Motivation is multiplicative
> Low value, low expectancy, or hostile environment can each independently undermine motivation — all three must be present.

## Connections to Other Wiki Pages

- **Value** links to [[Intrinsic Motivation]] and [[Extrinsic Motivation]] — intrinsic value is more durable

## Instructional Implications

- Make relevance explicit — connect content to students' goals and interests
- Calibrate challenge to support expectancy (not too easy, not overwhelming)
- Foster belonging through inclusive practices, reducing stereotype threat
- Use [[Learning Goals vs Performance Goals]] framing — learning goals sustain motivation longer
