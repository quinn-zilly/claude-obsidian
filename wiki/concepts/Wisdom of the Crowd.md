# Wisdom of the Crowd

Pooled judgment of a crowd is more accurate than any individual member when members' judgments are **correlated with the criterion but uncorrelated with each other** (ideally negatively correlated).

## Formal Conditions (Davis-Stober et al. 2014)

Accuracy of crowd > accuracy of best individual requires:
1. Each member knows *something* different (diverse information)
2. Member errors are independent or negatively correlated

Crowd accuracy can be evaluated analytically **without already knowing the correct answers** for some predictions.

## Limitation

Crowd accuracy typically comes **without explanation**. Recipients can only guess:
- What evidence supports a prediction
- How general it is
- What it implies for mental models of underlying processes

This is an open research gap: explaining crowd/ML black-box predictions.
