# Postpositivism

Arose from dissatisfaction with [[Positivism]]. Accepts an objective reality but holds it can only be apprehended **imperfectly** — human intellectual mechanisms are flawed, phenomena are intractable, so a "true" reality is never fully captured. This stance is **critical realism** (vs positivism's naïve realism).

## Key distinction from positivism

Positivism stresses **theory verification**; postpositivism stresses **theory falsification** (Popper). Guba & Lincoln's illustration: "a million white swans can never establish that all swans are white, [but] one black swan can completely falsify it."

Despite this, the two share much: both aim at prediction and control, emphasize generalizable cause–effect links, posit a detached/objective researcher, and operate from [[Idiographic vs Nomothetic|nomothetic]] + [[Etic vs Emic|etic]] perspectives. Both anchor **quantitative research**.

## Stance on the five [[Philosophy of Science Parameters|parameters]]

- **Ontology**: critical realism — one real reality, imperfectly knowable.
- **Epistemology**: modified dualism/objectivism — researcher may have some influence, but objectivity/independence remain guiding ideals.
- **Axiology**: values contained as much as possible; biases acknowledged and bracketed before/after the study.
- **Rhetoric**: objective, "scientific."
- **Methodology**: experiments, analog, quasi-experimental when no alternative.

## "Postpositivizing" — the failure mode

Ponterotto's (2005) central caution: quantitatively-socialized researchers often adopt qualitative methods while keeping a postpositivist lens, **"postpositivizing"** constructivist work. Symptoms: literature-driven standardized semistructured interviews, full sample chosen up front (vs theoretical sampling), theme categories set before the study, counting how many participants fall in each theme. "Round peg into a square hole." See [[Constructivism–Interpretivism]] for the contrast and [[Consensual Qualitative Research]] for a method that deliberately straddles the two.
