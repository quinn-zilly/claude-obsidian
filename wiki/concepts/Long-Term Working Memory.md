# Long-Term Working Memory (LT-WM)

Ericsson & Kintsch (1995): experts extend their **functional** working memory *in-domain* by acquiring LT-WM skills — using LTM, via pre-trained retrieval structures, as fast-access working storage to support planning, reasoning, and evaluation.

## What it explains

- Chess masters recalling **up to nine positions** from fast presentation; playing near-normal-strength **blindfold** (10 simultaneous games in memory).
- Medical experts summarizing clinical findings into higher-level inferences held for flexible diagnostic reasoning.
- Mental calculators retaining distinctive encodings of intermediate products to avoid proactive interference.

## Relation to prior accounts

LT-WM generalizes [[Skilled Memory Theory]] and **subsumes** the pure-STM [[Chunking]] model of Chase & Simon (1973) — a single mechanism replacing what Chase & Simon needed separate constructs (e.g. the "Mind's Eye") to explain. It also demonstrates that expert memory is **selective** (relevant info only), not a general capacity boost.
