# Postheroic Leadership

**Postheroic leadership** names the family of "new," less individualistic models that recast leadership as a collaborative, relational, distributed *process* rather than the heroic action of a few individuals at the top. The term and its critical analysis come from [[fletcher-2004-paradox-postheroic-leadership|Fletcher (2004)]], building on Pearce & Conger and the relational-leadership tradition (Mary Parker Follett as earliest advocate).

## Three defining characteristics (Fletcher)

1. **Leadership as practice** — shared and distributed across all levels; positional leaders are "tips of icebergs" sustained by invisible enabling work; blurs the leader/follower line.
2. **Leadership as social process** — dynamic, multidirectional, emergent, occurring in and through relationships; requires a shift in the sense of self from *independent entity* to **self-in-relation**.
3. **Leadership as learning** — legitimated by its outcomes (mutual learning, collective understanding, positive action); reenvisions power from **"power over" to "power with."**

## The critical claim

Postheroic models are usually presented as **gender- and power-neutral**, but they are not. Their relational skills are culturally coded **feminine** and their "power with" stance is misread as **powerlessness**. Because work interactions are occasions to [[Doing Gender, Doing Power, Doing Leadership|"do gender" and "do power"]], enacting postheroic leadership can register as "doing femininity/powerlessness" — displays not associated with leaders. This produces three paradoxes: the resilience of heroic individualism, the non-materializing **female advantage**, and the risk that the model's transformational potential is **co-opted** (skills named, belief-system challenge silenced → "postheroic heroes" with old power intact).

## Place in the wiki

Postheroic leadership is the *critical/feminist* framing of the same third-wave phenomena tracked elsewhere as [[Shared Leadership]], [[Co-Production of Leadership]], and relational/[[Transformational and Charismatic Leadership|transformational]] leadership (see [[Three Waves of Leadership Research]]). It is the sharpest counterpoint to accounts — including [[homan-et-al-2020-leading-diversity-functional|Homan et al.'s]] competency-and-matching model — that prescribe relational/person-focused behavior without theorizing its gender and power freight.
