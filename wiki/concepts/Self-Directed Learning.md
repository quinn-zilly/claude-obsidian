# Self-Directed Learning

Self-directed learning (SDL) is a process in which individuals take initiative in diagnosing their learning needs, formulating goals, identifying resources, implementing strategies, and evaluating outcomes — with or without the help of an instructor.

## Core Characteristics

- Learner controls the pace, sequence, and content of learning
- Requires metacognitive awareness and motivation
- Shifts responsibility from instructor to learner
- Common in workplace contexts, e-learning, and professional development

## Relevance to Workplace Instruction

In the context of the [[Science of Workplace Instruction]], SDL represents a shift from instructor-led to learner-driven development. It is particularly relevant for:
- On-the-job development and informal learning
- Technology-enabled learning environments
- Developing [[Learning Agility]]
