# Need Frustration (SDT)

Active thwarting of basic psychological needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness). Distinct from mere absence of need satisfaction — frustration is a separate construct with distinct predictors and outcomes.

## Distinction from Need Satisfaction

| | Need Satisfaction | Need Frustration |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | Needs are met and supported | Needs are actively blocked or thwarted |
| Primary prediction | Well-being, vitality | Ill-being, psychopathology |
| Relationship | Not simply opposites | Empirically distinct constructs |

Low need satisfaction ≠ high need frustration. A context can be neutral (neither supporting nor thwarting). The Balanced Measure of Psychological Needs (BMPN; Sheldon & Hilpert 2012) and Basic Psychological Need Satisfaction and Frustration Scale (BPNSFS; Chen et al. 2015) capture both.

## Predictors of Ill-Being

Need frustration predicts:
- Psychopathology (anxiety, depression)
- Defensive functioning (rationalization, projection)
- Controlled/introjected motivation
- Maladaptive behaviors (aggression, substance use)
- Burnout (sport: autonomy frustration r = −.50, competence frustration r = −.52; Li et al. 2013)

## Empirical Gap

As of Ryan et al. (2022): **need frustration has not yet been subject to a dedicated meta-analysis**. This is identified as a priority gap. Individual studies confirm the distinction but consolidated effect sizes are not yet available.

## Applied Implications

Contexts/relationships/environments should be evaluated not only for whether they satisfy needs but whether they actively frustrate them. Removing frustrating conditions may be as important as adding need-supportive ones.
