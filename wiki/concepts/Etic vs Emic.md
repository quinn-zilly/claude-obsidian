# Etic vs Emic

Terms from communication/linguistics, used frequently in multicultural counseling (Pedersen 1999).

- **Etic** (from *phonetic*, "language general") — universal laws and behaviors that transcend nations and cultures and apply to all humans. Example: humans are biological organisms that need to eat, drink, and sleep.
- **Emic** (from *phonemic*, "language specific") — constructs or behaviors unique to an individual sociocultural context, not generalizable. Example: the Jewish High Holy Days or Christian Easter — religion-specific, not universally acknowledged.

## Mapping to paradigms

[[Positivism]] / [[Postpositivism]] prefer **etic** (universal, generalizable findings). [[Constructivism–Interpretivism]] and the [[Critical–Ideological Paradigm]] prefer **emic** (context-specific meaning). Pairs with [[Idiographic vs Nomothetic]].
