# Chan's Typology of Composition Models

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

A **composition model** specifies the **functional relationship** between a construct at a lower level and "the same" construct at a higher level — constructs that reference essentially the same content but are qualitatively different across levels (Rousseau 1985). [[chan-1998-composition-models-typology|Chan (1998)]] proposes **five ideal-type forms**. A single focal construct's theory may combine more than one. The typology's payoff: it organizes existing constructs (so labels like "collective efficacy" can be disambiguated), reconciles apparently contradictory findings, and guides development of new constructs.

## The five forms

| Form | Functional relationship | Typical combination | Empirical support |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Additive** | Higher = **summation** of lower units, *regardless of variance* among them | Sum / average lower scores | Validity of the additive index (e.g., the mean) — Glick's (1985) organizational climate |
| **Direct consensus** | Meaning of the higher construct is in the **consensus** among lower units | Within-group agreement (e.g., $r_{wg}$) justifies aggregation | Agreement index value + validity of aggregated scores |
| **[[Referent-Shift Consensus]]** | Referent is **shifted** to a new (conceptually distinct) form *before* consensus is assessed | Agreement on the **new referent**, then aggregate | Same as direct consensus, applied to shifted referent |
| **Dispersion** | Meaning of the higher construct **is** the within-group **variance/dispersion** itself | Within-group variance as the operationalization | **Absence of multimodality** in within-group distributions + validity of the dispersion index |
| **Process** | Higher-level process **parameters are analogues** of lower-level process parameters | No simple algorithm; ensure every critical parameter & interrelation has a higher-level counterpart | Nomological validity for source & target constructs at each level |

## Key conceptual points

> [!key-insight]
> **Dispersion promotes within-group variance from nuisance to construct.** In consensus models, low agreement is a *problem* that blocks aggregation. In a dispersion model, that same variance **is** the higher-level construct (e.g., **climate strength**). Crucial distinction: low agreement (unimodal, platykurtic) ≠ high disagreement (**multimodal** → subgroups exist). Multimodality signals the wrong level of aggregation. Also test for **beta/gamma change** (measurement recalibration / conceptual shift) before comparing variances across groups.

> [!note] Process vs the rest
> The first four forms concern **static** attributes; **process composition** concerns dynamics (e.g., team self-regulation, team proceduralization composed from individual analogues). Functional relations of parameters are **analogous**; of parameter *interrelations*, **homologous**. Process composition is the least developed form and often must be preceded by other composition forms to build its component constructs.

Chan's models feed directly into [[kozlowski-klein-2000-multilevel-approach|Kozlowski & Klein (2000)]]'s treatment of emergence and item referents.
