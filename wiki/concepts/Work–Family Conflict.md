# Work–Family Conflict

The most-researched work–family construct; the focal variable of **[[shockley-et-al-2017-gender-work-family-conflict|Shockley et al. (2017)]]**.

## Definition (Greenhaus & Beutell 1985)
A form of **inter-role conflict** in which the role pressures from the work and family domains are mutually incompatible in some respect — participation in one role is made more difficult by participation in the other.

## Bidirectionality
- **WIF** — **work-interference-with-family**: work makes family participation harder.
- **FIW** — **family-interference-with-work**: family makes work participation harder.

The two directions have distinct (though correlated) antecedents and should be measured separately; WIF is generally reported at higher levels than FIW (Byron 2005). Studies collapsing the directions, or measuring undirected "incompatibility," were excluded from the Shockley meta-analysis.

## Types
Crosscut by the **[[Time-, Strain-, and Behavior-Based Conflict|time-, strain-, and behavior-based]]** typology (Greenhaus & Beutell 1985). These behave as indicators of an underlying WFC construct (Matthews et al. 2010) but can carry unique antecedents.

See **[[Gender Similarity in Work–Family Conflict]]** for the finding that men and women barely differ in reported WFC.
