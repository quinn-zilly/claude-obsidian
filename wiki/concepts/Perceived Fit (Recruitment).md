# Perceived Fit (Recruitment)

Applicants' perceptions of how well their goals, values, and needs suit the **job** (person–job fit) and the **organization** (person–organization fit) — the recruitment-side operationalization of **[[Person–Environment Fit]]**. Most primary studies measure **supplementary fit** (similarity to others); **complementary fit** (being a missing puzzle piece) is rarely measured.

## Why it matters

In [[uggerslev-et-al-2012-recruiting-through-stages|Uggerslev et al. (2012)]], perceived fit is the **single strongest predictor of [[Applicant Attraction|applicant attraction]]** — a large effect, strongest both zero-order and incrementally (ΔR² ≈ .40), and strongest at both early stages. **But** it is a **nonsignificant** predictor of actual **job choice**.

## Fostering fit (the "first recruitment dollar")

Because fit dominates attraction, the practical recommendation is to spend recruiting resources first on **cultivating fit perceptions**:

- **Fit feedback** — telling applicants their degree of fit raises subjective fit and attraction (Dineen et al. 2002).
- **Tailoring** message content to each applicant's information preferences (Kraichy et al. 2009).
- **[[Realistic Job Preview|RJPs]] and site visits** — surface true values/culture, letting misfits self-select out (Kristof 1996).
- **Ethics caveat** — emphasize *true* fit; don't misrepresent the organization (Buckley et al. 1997).
