# Modern Calling

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

The **inside-out** camp of calling definitions ([[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson 2019]]). Presumes the construct has diverged from its classical roots and now aligns with modern **expressive individualism**: callings are expressions of internal passions and interests, pursued for the enjoyment and fulfillment they bring rather than out of societal duty. Work is meaningful if and when it is significant **to the individual**, not necessarily because it benefits society; callings are **chosen and enacted** as personal expression rather than destined and awaiting discovery.

In the [[Inner and Outer Requiredness]] 2×2 it is **high inner, low outer** requiredness. Exemplar definitions: Wrzesniewski et al. (1997) — a person with a calling "works not for financial gain or career advancement, but instead for the fulfillment that doing the work brings to the individual"; Dobrow & Tosti-Kharas (2011) — "a consuming, meaningful passion people experience toward a domain." Where prosocial impact appears in these definitions, it is peripheral rather than central.
