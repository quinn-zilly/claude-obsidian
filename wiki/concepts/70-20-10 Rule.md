# 70-20-10 Rule

**70-20-10 rule** — empirical heuristic for where leadership development comes from: approximately 70% from on-the-job experience, 20% from relationships with other people (coaching, mentoring, feedback), 10% from formal training.

## Origin

Lombardo & Eichinger (1988) and McCall et al. (1988) — Center for Creative Leadership research on how effective executives develop.

## Organizational Implication

Most organizations invert the investment relative to the return:
- Over-invest in training (10% developmental return)
- Under-invest in structured OJD (70% return)

Yost & Plunkett (2010): hardwire the ratio into development plan forms — force employees to label each activity as OJD / other people / training and monitor the distribution.

## Practical Operationalization (Catalyst #5)

Development plan form design:
- Checkbox per activity: OJD (70%) / Other people (20%) / Training (10%)
- Self-assessment of plan quality built into form
- Link to OJD experience list from form
- Monitor completion + quality org-wide

## Cautions

Ratio is heuristic, not precise formula. Varies by role, career stage, development goal. Value is directional: challenge orgs to shift investment toward experience-based learning.
