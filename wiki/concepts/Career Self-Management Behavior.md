# Career Self-Management Behavior

**Career self-management behaviors** are the proactive actions individuals take to manage their own career development: career exploration and planning, networking, seeking feedback, and participating in training and self-development (Z. King 2004; De Vos & Soens 2008).

## Role in career theory

They are the most **proximal criterion** for [[Proactive Career Orientation]] / [[Protean Career Orientation]] — the behavioral channel through which a self-directed career attitude gets enacted. In [[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal's (2019)]] meta-analysis, proactive career orientation is a **strong** predictor of career self-management behavior (one of its two strongest criteria, alongside career satisfaction).

Distinct from **mobility behavior** (changing employers): switching organizations is only *one* way to self-manage a career; many alternative methods exist, so career orientations relate to self-management broadly but only weakly to physical mobility behavior specifically.
