# Independent Vicarious Learning (IVL)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Learning & Development MOC]]

**Independent vicarious learning (IVL)** is [[myers-2018-coactive-vicarious-learning|Myers's (2018)]] label for the **prevailing, intrapersonal, one-way** view of vicarious learning: a learner attentively **observes** a model (directly, or symbolically via a case, video, or KM database), then independently **retains, reproduces, and imitates** the observed behavior. Rooted in Bandura's social learning theory ([[Enactive and Vicarious Learning]]) — attention → retention → reproduction → motivation.

## Defining assumptions

- The **model is a non-participant** in the learner's processing (in symbolic cases, the "model" is just an artifact of a prior actor's performance).
- The learner can **observe what's relevant** and **infer the model's underlying goals** — i.e., what is to be learned is **overtly observable or symbolically codifiable**, definable *ex ante*.
- Learning occurs **"essentially at arm's length"** (Bresman, 2013).

## Where it holds and where it breaks

IVL was well-suited to high-volume manufacturing (replicable "best practices") and childhood education (Bandura's original context). It **breaks down** in distributed/virtual, **tacit**, ambiguous knowledge work, where the "lesson" can't be deduced without discussing the model's reasoning — motivating [[Coactive Vicarious Learning (CVL)]]. Myers frames CVL as **challenging and complementing** IVL: independent modes "certainly can be effective for learning in particular circumstances."

## Related IVL-family perspectives

Myers groups under the one-way assumption: [[Knowledge Transfer]] (reservoirs/conduits; content defined ex ante) and, with caveats, [[Communities of Practice]] (situated content but low intentionality, unintentional teaching).
