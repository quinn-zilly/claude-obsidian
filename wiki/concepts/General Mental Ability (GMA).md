# General Mental Ability (GMA)

**GMA** (general cognitive ability, *g*) is the broad capacity underlying reasoning, learning, and problem-solving. In personnel selection it is among the most studied predictors of job performance, generalizing across most jobs and settings.

> [!note] Why it appears here
> [[Sanchez-Levine-2012-rise-fall-job-analysis|Sanchez & Levine (2012)]] argue that validity generalization for GMA tests helped **depress interest in detailed job analysis** — feeding the (over)generalized conclusion that a detailed analysis is an unnecessary expense when the goal is to establish GMA-test generalizability. They reject the premise, noting it wrongly extends to other predictors (personality, psychomotor, interviews) and assumes current practice already provides the best moderator information.

See also [[Incremental Validity Over Cognitive Ability]] for what predicts performance *beyond* GMA.
