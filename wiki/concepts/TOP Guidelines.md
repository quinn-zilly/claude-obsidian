# TOP Guidelines (Transparency and Openness Promotion)

10 policy standards for scientific transparency and reproducibility, each with 3 levels of stringency (0 = no policy, 1 = disclose, 2 = share, 3 = require verification). Published by Nosek et al. (2015) in *Science*. URL: https://cos.io/top/

## The 10 Standards

1. Citation standards for data/materials
2. Data transparency
3. Analytic methods (code) transparency
4. Research materials transparency
5. Design and analysis reporting standards
6. Preregistration of studies
7. Preregistration of analysis plans
8. Replication
9. Badges
10. Publication bias policy

## Adoption (2021 audit; Nosek et al. 2022)

Audited N=40 random + N=50 high-impact psychology journals.

**83% at level 0** (median across standards). Most adopted:
- Data citation: 36%
- Reporting guidelines: 36%

Least adopted:
- Preregistration of studies: 12%
- Analysis plans: 13%

High-impact journals slightly more likely to have any policy, but overall frequency comparable (17% vs. 15%).

APA committed to move all core journals to ≥level 1 across 8 standards by end of 2021.

## Significance

TOP represents the **policy layer** of culture change — "make it required." In COS's Rogers diffusion model, policy is the final and necessary intervention after infrastructure, UX, community, and incentives. But policy without normative support = bureaucratic burden → failure.
