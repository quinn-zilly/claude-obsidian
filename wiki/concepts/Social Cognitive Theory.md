# Social Cognitive Theory

Social Cognitive Theory (SCT), developed primarily by Albert Bandura, explains human behaviour as arising from the dynamic interaction of personal factors, behaviour, and environmental influences — a model Bandura called triadic reciprocal determinism.

## Core Constructs

- **Self-efficacy**: belief in one's capacity to execute the behaviour required to produce specific outcomes; the most influential SCT construct for behaviour change
- **Outcome expectations**: beliefs about the consequences of performing a behaviour
- **Observational learning (modelling)**: learning by observing others, without direct reinforcement
- **Self-regulation**: setting goals, self-monitoring, and adjusting behaviour accordingly
- **Reciprocal determinism**: person, behaviour, and environment continuously influence each other

## Relevance to Behaviour Change

SCT is a foundational theory in behaviour change interventions across health, education, and organizational contexts. It is frequently cited alongside the [[Reasoned Action Approach]] and [[Information-Motivation-Behavioural Skills Model]] as a competing or complementary framework.
