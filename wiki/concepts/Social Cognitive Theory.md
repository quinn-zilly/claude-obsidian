# Social Cognitive Theory

Social Cognitive Theory (SCT), developed primarily by **[[Albert Bandura]]**, explains human behaviour as arising from the dynamic interaction of personal factors, behaviour, and environmental influences — **[[Triadic Reciprocal Causation|triadic reciprocal causation]]** (Bandura's term; also "reciprocal determinism"). Its most recent synthesis reframes SCT around **[[Human Agency|human agency]]**.

## Core Constructs

- **[[Self-Efficacy (Goal Setting)|Self-efficacy]]**: belief in one's capacity to execute the behaviour required to produce specific outcomes; the most influential SCT construct and the **foundation of human agency**. Four sources: mastery experience, vicarious experience, verbal persuasion, physiological/affective states.
- **[[Outcome Expectancies|Outcome expectations]]**: beliefs about the consequences of performing a behaviour.
- **Observational learning (modelling)**: learning vicariously by observing others, without direct reinforcement (enactive vs. vicarious learning).
- **[[Self-Regulatory Skills|Self-regulation]]**: self-monitoring, personal standards, corrective self-reactions.
- **[[Triadic Reciprocal Causation|Reciprocal determinism]]**: person, behaviour, and environment continuously and bidirectionally influence each other.

## The Agentic Perspective (Bandura 2001)

Bandura's [[bandura-2001-social-cognitive-theory-agentic|2001 Annual Review synthesis]] frames SCT around **[[Human Agency]]** — "to be an agent is to intentionally make things happen by one's actions." Four core features: **intentionality**, **[[Forethought and Anticipatory Self-Guidance|forethought]]**, **self-reactiveness** (including [[Moral Disengagement]]), and **self-reflectiveness**. Three **[[Modes of Human Agency|modes of agency]]** — personal, proxy, collective (see [[Collective Efficacy]]). Adds **[[Agentic Management of Fortuity]]**.

## Applications: motivation and learning

[[schunk-usher-2012-social-cognitive-theory-motivation|Schunk & Usher (2012)]] operationalize SCT for motivation: goals + self-evaluations of progress, outcome expectations, values, social comparisons, and self-efficacy govern task choice, effort, and persistence.

## Relevance to Behaviour Change

SCT is a foundational theory in behaviour change interventions across health, education, and organizational contexts. It is frequently cited alongside the [[Reasoned Action Approach]] and [[Information-Motivation-Behavioural Skills Model]] as a competing or complementary framework.
