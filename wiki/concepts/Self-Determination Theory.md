# Self-Determination Theory (SDT)

Approach to human motivation and personality using organismic metatheory. Developed by [[Richard Ryan]] and [[Edward Deci]] at University of Rochester. Core question: what social-contextual conditions foster vs. thwart human self-motivation, growth, and well-being?

## Core Premise

Humans have evolved inner resources — growth tendencies + innate psychological needs — that are the basis for self-motivation and personality integration. The natural propensity is *active* (curious, vital, engaged). But it requires supportive conditions to flourish; it can be readily undermined.

## Three Basic Psychological Needs

Identified inductively from empirical work; posited as innate, universal, and developmentally persistent:

1. **[[Competence (SDT)]]** — feeling effective; mastering challenges
2. **[[Autonomy (SDT)]]** — acting from volition; internal locus of causality (≠ independence)
3. **[[Relatedness (SDT)]]** — feeling connected and cared for by others

Satisfaction of all three → enhanced self-motivation + mental health. Thwarting any → diminished motivation + ill-being. Cannot thrive without all three (like water + food).

## Subtheories

### [[Cognitive Evaluation Theory]] (CET)
Explains variability in **intrinsic motivation**. Social-contextual events that support competence + autonomy → enhance intrinsic motivation. Controlling events (rewards contingent on performance, threats, deadlines) → undermine intrinsic motivation via external perceived locus of causality.

### [[Organismic Integration Theory]] (OIT)
Details forms of **extrinsic motivation** and conditions promoting [[Internalization (SDT)]]. Self-determination continuum from amotivation → external → introjected → identified → integrated → intrinsic regulation.

### [[Causality Orientations Theory]] (COT)
Stable individual differences in tendency to orient toward autonomy, control, or impersonal regulation. Predicts regulatory style above personality traits.

### [[Basic Psychological Needs Theory]] (BPNT)
Formally articulates the three universal needs. Key distinction: need *satisfaction* → well-being; need *frustration* → ill-being. Not merely opposites.

### [[Goal Contents Theory]] (GCT)
Intrinsic vs. extrinsic goal contents. Intrinsic aspirations → well-being (r = .24); extrinsic relative centrality → lower well-being (r = −.22).

### [[Relationships Motivation Theory]] (RMT)
High-quality relationships require autonomy support and need satisfaction within the relationship, not just closeness. Newest mini-theory; no meta-analyses yet (2022).

## Meta-Analytic Support (Ryan et al. 2022)

60+ meta-analyses confirm core SDT propositions across education, health, work, sport, parenting. Effects typically r = .15–.54; small-to-medium but consistent and theoretically coherent. See [[ryan-et-al-2022-sdt-meta-review]].

## Key Applications

Education, health care, work, sport, religion, psychotherapy. Social agents who support needs → engagement and well-being. Agents who thwart needs → alienation and ill-being.
