# Voice Effect

Having the **opportunity to have a say** ("voice" / process control) in a process that affects you produces more favorable reactions to that process — even when you have no control over the actual decision (Thibaut & Walker 1975; Folger 1977; Lind & Tyler 1988). One of the most robust findings in [[Procedural Justice|procedural justice]] research.

## Two explanations

- **Instrumental** — voice is valued because process control can shift the outcome your way.
- **Relational / group-value** — voice is valued because being heard by an authority signals valued group-member status, regardless of outcome (Lind & Tyler 1988; Tyler & Lind 1992).

## Boundary conditions

The voice effect is **attenuated** when: outcomes are already favorable (Lind & Tyler 1988), relationship quality with the authority is low (Tyler & Lind 1992), and possibly by organizational tenure (Avery et al. 2011 — "does voice go flat?").

## In appraisal

Appraisal participation operationalizes voice. In [[pichler-2012-social-context-appraisal|Pichler (2012)]], participation correlated Mr ≈ .48 with [[Appraisal Reactions|reactions]] — meaningful, but weaker than [[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)|relationship quality]] (.66). Participation did **not** moderate the relationship-quality → reactions link.
