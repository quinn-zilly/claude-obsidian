# Total Rewards

**Total rewards** is the holistic combination of compensation and benefits (and, in the broadest models, well-being, recognition, and development/career opportunities) that an organization offers, viewed as a single package rather than as separate pay and benefit streams.

Practitioners and employees tend to view pay and benefits holistically (using terms like *total rewards*, *compensation and benefits package*, *reward mix*), whereas academic research has historically studied compensation *or* benefits, but rarely the two together — a disconnect [[fulmer-li-2022-compensation-benefits-total-rewards|Fulmer & Li (2022)]] highlight. WorldatWork organizes its model around compensation, benefits, well-being (incl. workplace flexibility), recognition, and development/career.

## Core total rewards (Fulmer & Li's scope)

= compensation + employer-provided **retirement**, **health**, and **work-life** benefits.

## Why it matters

- **Attraction**: meta-analytically, benefits (r=.31) predict applicant attraction *more strongly* than pay/salary (r=.23); combined "total compensation" r=.29 (Uggerslev et al. 2012).
- **Total rewards gaps**: combining pay dispersion and benefits dispersion (plus differential i-deal access) can theoretically produce gender/race **total rewards gaps** distinct from the documented wage gap — under-researched because benefit value is hard to quantify financially.
- **Individualization**: customized/individualized total rewards may address heterogeneous employee preferences, optimizing [[Incentive Effect (Compensation)|incentive]] and [[Sorting Effect (Compensation)|sorting]] effects across a diverse workforce.

## Gig/contingent work

Gig and contingent workers typically self-assemble their own "total rewards" without employer-provided health/retirement safety nets — a growing gap in both practice and research.
