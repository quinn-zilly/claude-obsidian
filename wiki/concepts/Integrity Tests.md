# Integrity Tests

A **[[Compound Traits (Personality)|compound personality measure]]** — one that taps more than one Big Five dimension (chiefly low **[[Conscientiousness]]**, low Agreeableness, low Emotional Stability) — designed to predict **[[Counterproductive Work Behavior|counterproductive work behavior]]** and overall job performance. The most-studied compound trait in I-O psychology.

## Two families

- **Overt integrity tests** — ask directly about attitudes toward theft/dishonesty and about past counterproductive behavior.
- **Personality-based integrity tests** — infer integrity indirectly from personality-style items, without transparent honesty content.

## Validity (applicant-based, predictive; Ones et al. 1993)

| Criterion | Operational validity |
|---|---|
| Supervisory ratings of overall job performance | .41 |
| Externally-detected CWB — overt tests | .39 |
| Externally-detected CWB — personality-based tests | .29 |
| Production records (productivity) | .28 |

These are among the **highest criterion-related validities of any individual-differences predictor of overall job performance — only general mental ability is superior** ([[ones-et-al-2007-personality-assessment-support|Ones et al. 2007]], quoting Ones, Viswesvaran & Dilchert 2005b). Crucially, the headline figures come from **predictive studies on actual job applicants**, so they already carry whatever naturally-occurring [[Directed Faking vs Applicant Response Distortion|response distortion]] applicants produce.

## Incremental validity

Integrity tests add **~.14** validity points over GMA for overall JP; combined multiple Rs of **.64–.71** across low/medium/high job complexity. See [[Incremental Validity Over Cognitive Ability]].

## Why it matters

Integrity tests are the strongest counter to the "personality has trivial validity" argument: a single compound scale reaches ~.40 for supervisory JP ratings, rivalling structured interviews and beating assessment centers and biodata.
