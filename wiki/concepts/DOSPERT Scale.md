# DOSPERT Scale (Domain-Specific Risk-Taking)

A self-report measure of **risk-taking propensity that varies by domain** (Weber et al. 2002). Key insight: risk tolerance is not a general trait but is domain-specific.

## Key Insight

People who take risks in one domain (health, sports, research) need not take risks in others (investment, child care, social situations). Early decision research found almost no individual differences in risk-taking because it sought a domain-general trait that doesn't robustly exist.

DOSPERT resolved this by separating domains and allowing domain-adapted versions.

## Domains (standard)

Financial, health/safety, recreational, ethical, social

## Significance

- Broke the "no individual differences in risk" impasse (Huber 1983 had recommended abandoning the search)
- Enabled comparisons across domains and contexts
- Can be adapted to specific domains (e.g., Medical Maximizer-Minimizer Scale for health decisions)
