# Motherhood Wage Penalty

The persistent earnings and advancement gap incurred by women who reduce hours or take career breaks for family — a gap they **rarely catch up on**, affecting high-skilled women across nations (Mandel 2012). Cited in the work–family perspective of **[[kossek-su-wu-2017-opting-out-pushed-out|Kossek, Su & Wu (2017)]]**.

## Mechanisms
- Care-driven schedule changes (reduced hours, breaks) violate the **[[Ideal Worker Norm]]** and trigger the **[[Flexibility Stigma]]**.
- Marriage/parenthood attributions differ by gender: married men attributed higher performance and lower layoff risk; the opposite for married women (Jordan & Ziteck 2012).
- Classic executive studies (Stroh, Brett & Reilly 1992; Brett & Stroh 1997, 2003): executive men far more likely to be married with children and to have a non-working "facilitating" spouse; the pay payoff of extreme hours was **~13× larger for men** (+$55k vs +$4k).

Compounds the broader **[[Gender Gap in Rewards vs Performance]]** — reward gaps decoupled from performance.
