# Basic Psychological Needs (SDT)

Three innate psychological needs posited by [[Self-Determination Theory]] as essential for healthy motivation and well-being. Identified inductively from empirical work by [[Richard Ryan]] and [[Edward Deci]].

**Definition**: A basic need (psychological or physiological) = energizing state where satisfaction → health/well-being; deprivation → pathology/ill-being.

## The Three Needs

| Need | Core meaning |
|------|-------------|
| [[Competence (SDT)]] | Feeling effective; experiencing mastery |
| [[Autonomy (SDT)]] | Acting from volition; internal locus of causality |
| [[Relatedness (SDT)]] | Feeling connected, cared for, belonging |

## Key Properties

- **Innate** — not learned; evolved psychological requirements
- **Universal** — cross-cultural; mode of expression varies but underlying need is invariant
- **All three required** — cannot thrive with any one missing (water-vs-food analogy)
- **Synergistic** — simultaneous satisfaction → full integration; conflicts between needs (e.g., forced autonomy sacrifice for love) → alienation + psychopathology

## Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Aspirations

Goals that directly satisfy the three needs (affiliation, personal growth, community) → well-being. Goals that indirectly satisfy or undermine (wealth, fame, image) → ill-being. This holds cross-culturally (Russian sample replication: Ryan, Chirkov et al. 1999).

Need deprivation → substitutive extrinsic goal pursuit (cold/controlling maternal care → materialistic teens; Kasser et al. 1995).

## Daily Fluctuations

Within-person daily variability in all three need satisfactions independently predicts same-day well-being fluctuations (Reis, Sheldon et al., in press at time of paper).
