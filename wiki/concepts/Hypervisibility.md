# Hypervisibility

## Definition

Hypervisibility is one of the three states of [[Complex Visibility]]. A minority employee is hypervisible when they are subject to excess scrutiny and tokenism — they stand in as a representative of their entire demographic group rather than being seen as an individual.

## Mechanisms

**Hyperscrutiny:** Performance evaluated more critically; errors noticed and remembered more prominently; behavior subject to additional interpretation through a group-membership lens. "All eyes on you" is a real career tax.

**Tokenism:** One or few minority members are present, making their presence symbolic. Token status generates pressure to be "the voice" of the group, undermines individual attributions of success (success attributed to quota rather than merit), and creates performance burden.

**Stereotype threat:** Awareness of negative group stereotype impairs performance (Steele & Aronson, 1995); hypervisibility activates this mechanism continuously in workplace settings.

## Career Consequences

- Mistakes amplified → disproportionate career penalties
- Successes discounted → slower advancement despite equivalent performance
- Extra labor of representing group → depletes psychological capital (self-efficacy, resilience)
- Heightened scrutiny → risk-aversion in developmental assignments (can't afford to fail) → reduced OJD uptake

## Relationship to Other Visibility States

Hypervisibility and [[Invisibility (Career)]] can co-exist. The minority employee is hyper-visible as a token (group representative) while invisible as a contributor (individual voice not heard). [[Managed Visibility]] is the adaptive response: code-switching, downplaying identity, strategic self-presentation.
