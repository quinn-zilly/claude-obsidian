# Aspirational vs Enforceable Standards

A structural distinction inside the [[APA Ethics Code]]:

- **Aspirational** — the Preamble and [[Five General Principles (APA)|five General Principles (A–E)]]. They guide and inspire toward "the highest ideals." They are **not** obligations and **cannot** be the basis for sanctions. Relying on them to sanction "distorts both their meaning and purpose."
- **Enforceable** — the **Ethical Standards** (ten sections). These set rules that can be applied by the APA and other adopting bodies. Violations can trigger sanctions up to loss of membership.

Caveats the Code makes explicit: the Standards are **not exhaustive** (conduct not addressed is not automatically ethical or unethical), most are written **broadly** to span varied roles, and modifiers like *reasonable* deliberately preserve professional judgment.

This two-tier design is why a profession can hold lofty **values** in its aspirational layer yet behave differently in practice — the gap [[Joel Lefkowitz]] (2008) exploits when he says the field's *ethics* are fine but its *values* fall short.
