# Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

Davis (1989); Davis, Bagozzi & Warshaw (1989). A parsimonious model of IS/IT adoption derived from the Theory of Reasoned Action, built on two beliefs:

- **Perceived Usefulness (PU)** — degree to which a technology improves performance.
- **Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU)** — degree to which it is effort-free.

These beliefs shape attitude and, through it, intention and usage. TAM can be read as a special case of TRA with only two beliefs comprising attitude.

## Relevance here

TAM is one of the eight models [[UTAUT]] synthesized. Notably, TAM retained **attitude** in its original formulation — and the role of attitude in TAM is part of [[dwivedi-2019-utaut-revision|Dwivedi et al.'s]] argument for reinstating [[Attitude as Mediator|attitude]] in UTAUT. Lineage: TAM2 (Venkatesh & Davis 2000) added subjective norm and image; TAM3 (Venkatesh & Bala 2008) added further determinants of PEOU.
