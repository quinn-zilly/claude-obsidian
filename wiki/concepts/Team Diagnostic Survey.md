# Team Diagnostic Survey

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

A survey instrument ([[wageman-hackman-lehman-2005-team-diagnostic-survey|Wageman, Hackman & Lehman 2005]]) built to serve **both** scholarly research and practical team diagnosis — bridging the "seemingly contradictory imperatives" of psychometric rigor and practitioner usefulness. Grounded explicitly in [[Enabling Conditions for Team Effectiveness|Hackman's model]].

## What It Measures

- The five [[Enabling Conditions for Team Effectiveness]] (real team, compelling direction, enabling structure, supportive context, expert coaching), each with subdimensions.
- The three process criteria (effort, strategy, knowledge/skill) and criteria 2–3 of [[Team Effectiveness Criteria]].
- Team task processes, interpersonal relations, and individual motivation/satisfaction.
- Team **authority** classification: self-governing → professional → self-designing → self-managing → manager-led → hybrid.

## Design & Scoring

~15–20 minutes, 10 sections, mostly 5-point *highly inaccurate → highly accurate* with reverse-scored items. Team scores average across **items and members**. Group-level reliability via **Spearman-Brown** (individual α = .50 → team α = .80 at n=4). Size and diversity use **algebraic difference** scores with 0 = optimum (an inverted-U: too small/large, too homogeneous/heterogeneous are both bad).

## Validation

Development across a diversity of organizations; headline validity sample **2,474 members / 321 teams**; revised-instrument psychometrics from 140 teams (N = 1,202). All team-level reliabilities satisfactory. Interpret *size*, *diversity*, *whole task*, *autonomy/judgment* subcomponents with caution (low reliability; weak discriminant validity for diversity and whole task). Higher-order constructs are intentionally *not* internal-consistency-tested — their components vary independently by design.
