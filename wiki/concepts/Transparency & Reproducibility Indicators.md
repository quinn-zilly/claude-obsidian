# Transparency & Reproducibility Indicators

A set of **observable, codable signals** in a published article that proxy for whether the work can be evaluated, verified, and built on. The unit of [[Meta-Research]] that lets reformers measure adoption across a literature instead of arguing about it anecdotally.

## The Standard Indicator Battery

Developed across cross-disciplinary prevalence sweeps (biomedicine: Iqbal 2016, Wallach 2018; social science + psychology: Hardwicke et al. 2020, 2022):

- **Open access** — is a public version retrievable, or paywall-only?
- **Materials availability** — survey instruments, stimuli, software shared?
- **Protocol availability** — prespecified methods document shared?
- **Raw data availability** — underlying data shared and accessible?
- **Analysis-script availability** — code reproducing the analysis shared?
- **Preregistration** — timestamped read-only hypotheses/methods/analysis plan?
- **Funding disclosure** — sources (or absence) declared?
- **Conflict-of-interest disclosure** — COIs (or absence) declared?
- **Replication** — does the article claim to replicate, or get cited as replicated?
- **Evidence synthesis** — included in a systematic review / meta-analysis?

## Why Indicators, Not Outcomes

Indicators measure **reported practice**, the leading edge reform can actually move. They are deliberately not measures of whether science is *true* — they are necessary conditions for independent verification.

> [!warning] Adoption ≠ Sufficiency
> An indicator firing does not mean the goal is met. Shared-but-undocumented data fails analytic reproducibility; a vague preregistration fails to constrain [[Researcher Degrees of Freedom]]. The indicator is a floor, not a ceiling.

## Key Failure Modes

- **Reporting ≠ availability (link-rot).** A "materials available" statement is worthless if the link is dead. Hardwicke et al. (2022) found 7/26 such links broken.
- **Mandate effects.** Indicators journals *require* (funding, COI) show much higher prevalence than indicators left to author initiative (data, scripts). Disclosure rates track journal policy, not researcher virtue.
- **Norm mismatch across fields.** The protocol indicator reads 0% in psychology partly because psychology lacks a separate-protocol norm, not purely because of non-transparency.

## Use as a Baseline

The headline use is **temporal**: fix a battery, measure prevalence now, re-measure later, attribute the delta to reform. [[hardwicke-et-al-2022-transparency-prevalence-psychology|Hardwicke et al. (2022)]] is the psychology baseline (2014–2017). See [[Meta-Research]] for the broader genre and [[Open Science 2.0]] for the argument that hitting these indicators is only stage one.
