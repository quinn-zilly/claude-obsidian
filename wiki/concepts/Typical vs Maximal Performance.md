# Typical vs Maximal Performance

A distinction within the *static* dimensionality of criteria (DuBois, Sackett, Zedeck & Fogli 1993; Sackett, Zedeck & Fogli 1988):

- **Typical performance** — the average level of what an employee **will** do day-to-day.
- **Maximal performance** — the peak level of what an employee **can** do.

Employees perform at maximum when they (a) know they are being evaluated, (b) accept instructions to maximize, and (c) face a short-duration task.

> [!key-insight] The two barely correlate
> Typical and maximal measures correlate only **~.20** (objective checkout-clerk speed/accuracy; Sackett et al. 1988) to **~.40** (subjective military-recruit ratings; Ployhart, Lim & Chan 2001). They are largely different things.

- **General mental ability** predicted maximal but not typical performance (Witt & Spitzmuller 2007).
- **Motivation** (direction, level, persistence of effort) relates more strongly to maximal than typical performance (Klehe & Anderson 2007).

Implication: motivation/personality drive *typical* performance; ability shows up in *maximal* performance — so which you measure changes what you're validating against.
