# Attitude Dimension Consistency

The **attitude dimension consistency perspective** ([[costa-2024-discrimination-interventions|Costa, 2024]]) applies the **principle of compatibility** to bias reduction: an intervention most effectively shifts an outcome when the **attitude dimension it primarily targets matches the dimension of the outcome measured**.

Using the **[[Tripartite Model of Attitudes|ABC model]]**:

- Cognitively-focused interventions best shift **cognitive** outcomes (stereotyping).
- Behaviorally-focused interventions best shift **behavioral** outcomes (discrimination).
- Affectively-focused interventions do **not** show the same matching advantage — affect is deeply ingrained, hard to measure reliably, and subject to social-desirability bias.

## Conceptual Cousin

Mirrors the **consistency premise** in [[Diversity Ideologies|Leslie et al. (2020)]]: each diversity ideology has its strongest effect on the intergroup outcome most consistent with its content (e.g., colorblindness — a cognitive strategy — most affects stereotyping). Both papers, in the same journal, converge on *content–outcome alignment* as the key to reconciling mixed findings.

> [!key-insight]
> "Does the intervention work?" is the wrong question. Ask: **does the dimension it targets match the outcome you care about?** A mismatch predicts a null result even for a well-designed intervention.
