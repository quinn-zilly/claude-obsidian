# Lumped vs Split Conceptualizations of Leading

[[carton-2022-science-of-leadership|Carton's (2022)]] central prescriptive argument about how scholars conceptualize [[Leaders vs Leading|leading]].

- **Lumped** — bundle *many* leader behaviors into a single meta-construct. The paradigm case is [[Transformational and Charismatic Leadership|transformational leadership]] (idealized influence, inspirational motivation, intellectual stimulation, individualized consideration collapsed into one score). Lumping is the *most popular* subcategory of leadership research.
- **Split** — isolate *single*, observable leader behaviors and study their distinct causes and effects.

Carton grants that lumped constructs are convenient — especially for leader development and for demonstrating cross-situational or temporal stability — but argues they **obscure causal mechanisms** and impede cumulative science, echoing [[Construct Redundancy (Leadership)|construct-redundancy]] critiques.

> [!key-insight] The agenda
> Carton recommends shifting toward **split** conceptualizations so leadership science can accumulate precise, additive causal evidence about which specific behaviors do what — rather than attributing outcomes to broad, overlapping meta-constructs.
