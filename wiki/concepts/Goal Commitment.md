# Goal Commitment

Nav: [[index]] | [[Goal-Setting Theory]]

Degree of attachment to and determination to reach a goal. Most critical moderator of goal → performance when goals are **difficult** (Klein et al. 1999). Easy goals require little commitment; hard goals require high commitment because effort is high and success probability is lower.

## Key Antecedents

**Importance factors** (make attainment matter):
- Public commitment (integrity mechanism — Hollenbeck, Williams & Klein 1989)
- Leader vision + supportive behavior
- Participation in goal setting (when it raises goal level, not just ownership)
- Anticipated outcomes (outcomes of attaining the goal)

**Self-efficacy** — belief one can reach the goal; directly facilitates commitment; leaders can raise via mastery, modeling, persuasion

**Monetary incentives** — more money → more commitment; BUT:
- Task-and-bonus on very hard goals can *backfire*: if reward looks unattainable, personal goal + SE drop → performance drops
- Piece-rate safer for very hard goals than all-or-nothing bonus
- Moderately difficult goals: both incentive types work

## Participation Note

Participation in *goal setting* ≠ higher commitment via ownership. Participation's primary benefit is **cognitive** (information exchange), not motivational. Assigned goals = participatively set goals in performance IF rationale given. Meta-analytic ES for participation on performance = .11 (Wagner & Gooding 1987).

## Measurement

Seijts & Latham (2000) — goal commitment questionnaires with high reliability and validity.
