# Self-Regulatory Skills

Nav: [[index]] | [[Self-Management Training]] | [[Work Motivation Meta-Framework (Kanfer)]]

Kanfer's (1970–1982) taxonomy of skills enabling people to govern their own behavior without continuous external control.

## Core Skills

1. **Self-observation** — systematically monitor own behavior (frequency, context, antecedents)
2. **Self-evaluation** — compare observed behavior to a reference standard (goal)
3. **Self-reinforcement** — administer consequences (positive or negative) contingent on goal progress

## Why Skills, Not Just Motivation

Self-regulation failures often reflect **skill deficits** rather than motivational deficits. People may want to change behavior but lack the techniques to manage environmental obstacles, set effective goals, or identify workable self-reinforcers.

→ Training in self-regulatory skills treats behavior problems as **skill acquisition problems**. See [[Self-Management Training]].

## Relation to F.H. Kanfer vs. Ruth Kanfer

**F.H. Kanfer** (Frederick Kanfer) — clinical/behavior therapy tradition; developed the three-stage self-regulation loop; behavioral contracts, self-monitoring diaries, reinforcement contingencies. Source for [[frayne-latham-1987-self-management-attendance|Frayne & Latham 1987]].

**Ruth Kanfer** — IO-psychology; developed resource allocation theory of motivation and the [[Work Motivation Meta-Framework (Kanfer)|tripartite meta-framework]]. Different lineage, shared surname.
