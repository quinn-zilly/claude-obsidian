# Employee Referrals

A **[[Recruitment Methods|recruitment method]]** in which a current employee brings an opening to a prospective candidate's attention. Employers generally view current employees as the **best method** for reaching desirable candidates (Breaugh 2009).

## Why referrals work (two mechanisms)

1. **Realism** — referrers give accurate job information, so referrals form realistic expectations and self-select well (the [[Realistic Job Preview|RJP]] logic).
2. **Prescreening** — referrers only pass along candidates they judge qualified.

## Evidence

- Referrals show **better application credentials, are more likely to be hired, and perform better** than nonreferrals (Fernandez & Weinberg 1997; Castilla 2005 — referrals had higher initial job performance, attributed to coaching/pressure from the referrer).
- **Referrer performance matters:** candidates referred by **high performers** score higher on selection measures than those referred by low performers, who still beat internet recruits (Yakubovich & Lup 2006).
- Kirnan et al. (1989): referrals superior on prehire outcomes; small retention edge for the informal (referral + direct-applicant) group.

## Caveat

Earlier findings that minorities were **less likely** to be reached via current-employee methods (1980s–90s research) qualify the diversity implications — see [[breaugh-2013-employee-recruitment|Breaugh (2013)]] on recruiting underrepresented groups.
