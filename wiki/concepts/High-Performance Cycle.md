# High-Performance Cycle

Nav: [[index]] | [[Goal-Setting Theory]]

Model from Locke & Latham (1990) explaining how goal setting produces sustained motivation. Resolves the job satisfaction–performance puzzle.

## The Cycle

```
High Goals
    ↓
High Performance (productivity, quality, cost reduction)
    ↓
Rewards (recognition, promotion, pay)
    ↓
High Satisfaction + High Self-Efficacy
    ↓
Willingness to commit to even higher goals
    ↓ (back to top)
```

## Key Insight: Satisfaction Causality

**Satisfaction is the result, not the cause, of high performance** (when rewards are commensurate with performance). This explains decades of puzzlement over the weak/inconsistent job satisfaction → productivity correlation.

Satisfaction → subsequent performance only if:
1. It fosters organizational commitment
2. That commitment is to specific, challenging goals
3. Moderators (feedback, commitment, complexity) are appropriately managed

## Why People Set High Goals Despite Lower Trial-by-Trial Satisfaction

People anticipate distal outcomes of goal attainment — pride, academic/career advancement, salary, life benefits — not just immediate task satisfaction. High-goal setters trade short-term satisfaction for long-term valued outcomes.
