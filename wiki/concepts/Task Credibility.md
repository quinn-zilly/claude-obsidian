# Task Credibility

Task credibility is one of the three behavioral indicators of a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]]: members' mutual trust in the reliability and competence of one another's expertise. A directory of "who knows what" only works if members actually believe the designated specialist's knowledge - otherwise they duplicate effort, second-guess, or route around the expert.

[[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] describe task credibility as knowledge-based trust and note it is the form of trust most closely related to TMS. The review flags the broader role of trust as theoretically unsettled - treated variously as antecedent, dimension, and moderator - but task credibility specifically is consistently treated as a core TMS dimension alongside [[Memory Differentiation]] and [[Task Coordination]].
