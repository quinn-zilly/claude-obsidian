# Informal Field-Based Learning (IFBL)

A subset of informal learning defined as "engaging in **intentional, self-directed** behavior aimed at learning new, work-oriented, and organizationally valued content **outside of a formal learning program**" (Wolfson et al. 2018, p. 16). Reviewed and framed in [[tannenbaum-wolfson-2022-informal-field-based-learning]].

## Position in the Learning Construct Space

IFBL sits at the informal, self-directed, and intentional ends of three continua. Intentionality distinguishes it from **incidental learning** (unplanned by-product of other activity). It is narrower than umbrella "informal learning" and neighbors continuous learning, action learning, experiential learning, and coactive vicarious learning.

## Three IFBL Behaviors

1. **Feedback / reflection-based** — seeking feedback, coaching, debriefing.
2. **Vicarious learning** — observing others, asking experts, demonstrations.
3. **Experimentation / new experiences** — novel task approaches, trial-and-error, stretch assignments.

## CAM-OS Framework

Engagement and payoff depend on **personal readiness** and **situational readiness**:

| | Factor | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Personal | **C**apability | Teachable informal-learning skills (observe, ask, seek feedback, self-reflect) |
| Personal | **A**wareness | "Learning attunement" — what/when/how to learn, and landmines to avoid |
| Personal | **M**otivation | Discretionary willingness; linked to learning goal orientation, promotion focus |
| Situational | **O**pportunity | Richness · slack · malleability · appropriateness (Figure 4) |
| Situational | **S**upport | Work climate ([[Psychological Safety]]), manager, peer, org practices |

Personal factors likely operate **compensatorily** — a deficit in one may be offset by the others.

## Four Dimensions of Opportunity

- **Richness** — barren → rich in naturally occurring learning chances
- **Slack** — spare time/resources; small positive but possibly **curvilinear** effect
- **Malleability** — fixed → highly malleable (job crafting can create opportunities)
- **Appropriateness** — constructive → neutral → **detrimental**

## The Dark Side

IFBL is usually but not always positive. Detrimental IFBL can cause safety hazards, distract from core work, damage customer relationships, be perceived as overreach, or increase stress/burnout. Tools like the **Red-Yellow-Green chart** and **Field Learning Needs Analysis** help steer toward constructive IFBL.

## Contrast with Formal Training

Formal T&D prespecifies objectives and embeds learning experiences; IFBL relies **completely** on on-the-job opportunities the learner must find or create — so awareness and capability matter far more. The formal transfer literature emphasizes *applying what was taught*, not fostering new emergent learning.
