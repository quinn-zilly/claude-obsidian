# Adaptive Team Performance

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

The **proximal antecedent** of team adaptation in the [[Team Adaptation (Burke Cross-Level Model)|Burke et al. (2006) model]]. Defined as an emergent phenomenon that **compiles over time** from a recursive cycle whereby one or more members use their resources to functionally change current cognitive or behavioral goal-directed actions/structures to meet expected or unexpected demands.

## Performance as Action, Not Result

Following Campbell (1990) — "performance is not the consequence of action; it is the action itself" — Kozlowski and colleagues argue team performance is the *longitudinal enactment of processes*, not the outcome. Team **outcomes** (member outcomes, team outcomes, individual/team changes; Tannenbaum et al. 1992) are downstream consequences.

## Emergence: Composition vs Compilation

- **Composition** (e.g., a management brainstorm): pooled interdependence, each member contributes ~equally; performance ≈ sum of individual effort; operationalized as the mean.
- **Compilation** (patterned emergence): members do *different* tasks in *different* amounts (e.g., one member gives vision, another provides backup, a third gathers info). Team-level performance is the *pattern* of unique individual/dyadic/team contributions.

Adaptive team performance sits toward the **compilation** end of the discontinuity continuum — best captured by a patterned emergence model, not a simple aggregate.
