# Agentic Management of Fortuity

Nav: [[index]] | [[Human Agency]]

Much of the course lives take is shaped by **fortuity** — chance encounters whose intersection occurs randomly (though each chain of events has its own determinants). A fortuitous event is "not uncaused, but rather there is a lot of randomness to the determining conditions of its intersection."

> [!key-insight] Fortuity ≠ uncontrollability
> People can **make chance work for them**. On the *personal side*, attributes, interests, and competencies determine whether a chance encounter becomes a lasting influence; on the *social side*, the holding/molding power of the milieu matters. People can also *make chance happen* by leading an active, inquisitive, venturesome life that raises the level and type of fortuitous encounters. "Chance favors only the prepared mind" (Pasteur).

Self-development is therefore doubly agentic: it cultivates the resources to **exploit** promising fortuities and the self-regulative capabilities to **resist** fortuitous social traps.

Source: [[bandura-2001-social-cognitive-theory-agentic|Bandura (2001)]].
