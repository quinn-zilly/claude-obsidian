# Recruitment Methods

The **channels** an employer uses to bring an opening to candidates' attention — e.g., **[[Employee Referrals|current-employee referrals]]**, college placement offices, the employer's **web site**, and **job boards** (third-party sites), plus job ads and job fairs.

## Why method matters — two hypotheses

1. **Realism hypothesis** — some methods (especially referrals) give recruits **more accurate expectations**, enabling informed self-selection (the [[Realistic Job Preview|RJP]] logic).
2. **Individual-difference hypothesis** — different methods reach systematically **different people** on attributes linked to recruitment outcomes (e.g., direct applicants show more motivation than newspaper-ad respondents).

## Evidence highlights

- **Referrals** consistently superior on prehire and posthire outcomes (see [[Employee Referrals]]).
- **Employer web sites** — content, navigability, testimonials, and awards drive impressions; adding **P-O fit feedback** curbs unqualified applications.
- **Reputation/publicity** and **early recruitment actions** (sponsorship, advertising, WOM) raise applicant quantity, quality, and attention (Collins and colleagues; Turban & Cable 2003).
- **Goal-directed attention:** to reach **passive job seekers**, go beyond ads/website — otherwise openings are processed peripherally or missed entirely.
