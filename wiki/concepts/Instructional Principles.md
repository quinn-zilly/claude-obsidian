# Instructional Principles

Core component of the [[Science of Workplace Instruction]] ([[kraiger-ford-2021-workplace-instruction|Kraiger & Ford 2021]]). Empirically supported propositions that guide the design and delivery of effective training; act on both *instructional events* (how training is structured) and *learning events* (how learners process material).

Contrast with **methods** (behavioral modeling, error management) and **media** (VR, online, classroom). Principles are independent of method and medium — they improve any delivery when applied.

## Five Core Principles with 15 Specifics

### 1. Organize Content
Goal: reduce extraneous cognitive load; build coherent schema before detail.

| Principle | Description | Effect Size |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Coherence** | Remove nonessential material; make main points prominent | d = 0.86 (K=23) |
| **Contiguity** | Present related knowledge close in space and time | d = 0.80 (K=29) |
| **Advanced organizer** | Pretraining scaffold (video, outline, concept map) showing structure | d = 0.46–1.10 |

### 2. Optimize Sequencing
Goal: match challenge level to learner; build from simple to complex.

| Principle | Description | Effect Size |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Scaffolding** | Expert support that fades as competence grows | g = 0.46–0.63 |
| **Adaptive difficulty** | Adjust task difficulty based on mastery (not fixed schedule) | g = 0.75 (K=7) |
| **Interleaving** | Mix different problem types/content rather than blocked practice | g = 0.42 (K=59) |

### 3. Engage Learner in Own Learning
Goal: active processing > passive reception.

| Principle | Description | Effect Size |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Generative effects** | Prompt learner to generate own examples, explain to others | d ≥ 0.40 across 8 strategies |
| **Metacognitive prompts** | Queries that trigger self-monitoring during learning | Consistent positive |
| **Retrieval practice (testing effect)** | Recall from long-term memory; more effective than rereading | Large (Dunlosky 2013) |

### 4. Conduct Effective Practice
Goal: conditions of practice should match (or exceed) conditions of performance.

| Principle | Description | Effect Size |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Variability of practice** | Vary tasks, people, situations encountered | Large (Kirschner & Van Merriënboer 2008) |
| **Spaced practice** | Distribute sessions over time vs. massed practice | d = 0.46 (K=112) |
| **Identical elements** | Stimuli/responses during training mirror operational context | |

### 5. Develop Past Initial Mastery
Goal: durable retention, not just criterion performance.

| Principle | Description | Effect Size |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Knowledge of results** | Correct/incorrect feedback (KR); what the correct answer is (KCR) | KCR: d = 0.33 |
| **Feedback/feedforward** | Information to modify thinking for improved learning | d = 0.49 (elaborative) |
| **Overlearning** | Continued practice after criterion mastery | d = 0.63 (K=88) |

## What Doesn't Work

**Learning styles (meshing hypothesis):** No empirical support. 50+ distinct learning style theories; no study meeting methodological standards supports matching modality to preference (Pashler et al. 2008; Aslaksen & Lorås 2018 meta-analysis: "still no replicable statistical evidence").

**Age-specific ATI instruction:** Age differences in processing speed are real, but designing distinct training for older workers is not supported. Age-inclusive design with standard principles benefits all, possibly more for older learners.

## Key Implication for Practice

Heterogeneity in training method meta-analyses (behavioral modeling, cross-cultural training, emotional intelligence training) is largely explained by variance in *whether principles were incorporated* — not by which method was used. Principles are the mechanism, methods are the vehicle.
