# Dynamic Decision Making

Decision problems where the decision maker acts repeatedly on an environment that:
1. Responds to their actions (endogenous change)
2. Changes independently over time (exogenous change)

Example: senior manager addressing low morale with multiple interventions over months, while external factors also affect morale.

## Core Challenges

- **Feedback delays**: interventions take time to show effect → misattribution of causality
- **System dynamics**: coupled feedback processes make response over time nonintuitive; subjects generate large oscillations in capital-budgeting tasks (Sterman 1987)
- **Complexity**: hard to derive optimal strategies analytically
- **Lack of control**: problem at time *t* partly determined by earlier choices, not just experimental conditions

## Research Paradigms

**Simulated medical diagnosis** (Kleinmuntz & Kleinmuntz 1981): 60-time-period task with tests, treatments, fluctuating patient health. Bayesian strategy yielded only modest advantage over simpler heuristics.

**Artificial worlds** (European tradition): firefighting, third-world economic development, smallpox epidemic simulations. Typical: poor initial performance → learning with repeated play; feedback delays impede learning substantially; decentralization opportunities mainly ignored.

**Systems dynamics** (MIT group — Diehl & Sterman 1993; Sterman 1987): coupled feedback → oscillations; capital budgeting with equipment ordering lags → large costly oscillations despite knowing system structure.

## Key Findings

- Subjects initially perform poorly but can learn with repeated experience
- Feedback delays = primary impediment to learning
- Experts use [[Naturalistic Decision Making]] / RPD rather than deliberative analysis
- Escalation of commitment ([[Sunk Cost Effect]]) common across linked decision sequences

## Why It Matters for Organizations

Real management is mostly dynamic: inventory management, hiring cycles, product development, performance management. Laboratory static-choice models poorly represent this reality.
