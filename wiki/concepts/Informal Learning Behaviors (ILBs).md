# Informal Learning Behaviors (ILBs)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Learning & Development MOC]]

**Informal learning behaviors (ILBs)** are **non-curricular behaviors and activities pursued in service of knowledge and skill acquisition that take place outside formally-designated learning contexts.** They are **predominantly self-directed, intentional, and field-based**; not syllabus-based, discrete, or linear (Cerasoli et al., 2018, building on Tannenbaum et al., 2010).

The construct's contribution is to **separate the behavior from the learning outcome**: "informal learning" is K/S *gained through* ILBs (observing a coworker, discussing a problem with a supervisor, asking questions on the job), whereas "formal learning" is K/S gained through **formal learning behaviors (FLBs)** (attending class, taking notes, assigned readings).

## Defining features

- **Non-curricular** — no assigned set of K/S to master; goals are set by the learner, not a trainer/instructor/org.
- **Self-directed / self-initiated / individually controlled.**
- **Field-based** — occur in the workplace, in the flow of work, across varied job contexts.
- **Intentional & conscious** — require agency. This is the boundary against incidental / implicit / accidental learning, which lacks conscious goal-direction.
- **Non-linear** — no clear beginning/end, variable intensity over time.

**Context matters**: the *same* behavior (asking a question) is an ILB only outside a formal training context. ILBs are "particular kinds of behavior within a particular context."

## Distinguished from neighboring constructs

| Construct | How it differs from ILBs |
|---|---|
| **FLBs** | curricular, externally directed, discrete, linear |
| Incidental/implicit learning | unconscious, not goal-directed |
| Continuous learning | often refers to leisure-pursued *formal* courses |
| Mentoring/coaching | usually agreed-upon criteria + linear progression |
| Social/vicarious/network learning | require interaction with/observation of others (see [[Coactive Vicarious Learning (CVL)]]) |

## Nomological network (Cerasoli et al., 2018 meta-analysis)

Antecedents at three levels of specificity feed engagement in ILBs; ILBs in turn drive three outcome families. Meta-analytic corrected correlations (k=49, N=55,514):

**Antecedents.** Situational effects were largest on average. **Support** ρ=.32 (strongest; formal/tangible .38 ≥ informal .30 ≈ people .31); **job/task** ρ=.21 (control/autonomy .31, resources .30, demands .13); **individual predispositions** ρ=.27 (learning motives .33, personality/propensity .27); **opportunities for learning** ρ=.04 (equivocal). **Demographics** near-zero overall (education .10, rank/tenure .18, married .17 the only notable non-nulls).

**Outcomes.** Attitudes ρ=.29 · knowledge/skill acquisition ρ=.41 · performance ρ=.42.

Full study details: [[cerasoli-et-al-2018-informal-learning-behaviors-meta-analysis]].

## Relationship to IFBL

[[Informal Field-Based Learning (IFBL)]] (Tannenbaum & Wolfson, 2022) is the later conceptual sibling from the same [[Scott I. Tannenbaum]] lineage — both foreground *field-based, self-directed, intentional* learning outside formal programs. ILBs is the behavior-level meta-analytic construct (2018); IFBL is the broader 2022 conceptual treatment.
