# Stimulus Detection (Team Adaptation)

**Phase 1** of the [[Team Adaptation (Stimulus-Based Model)|Pearsall et al. (2025) stimulus-based model]].

## Core Process

Teams operate in uncertain environments and must discriminate which demands require collective adaptation from those that do not. Detection typically requires that a member *notice* a potential demand AND *voice* it to the rest of the team — individual noticing is insufficient.

How quickly detection occurs determines how many options remain for the subsequent urgency and duration assessment phases. Delayed detection limits response options and may force the team to scramble reactively.

## Factors Enabling Early Detection

- **External focus** (Ancona & Bresman, 2007) — externally oriented teams detect threats and opportunities earlier
- **Boundary-spanning activities** (Cross et al., 2000; Denison et al., 1996; Edmondson, 2003) — teams that sense the external environment
- **Coordination of information and peripheral members** (Mayo, 2022) — interacting with peripheral members surfaces cues; live discussion > virtual
- **Nonroutine event verbalization** (Waller, 1999) — explicitly naming anomalies counts as detection; task prioritization and distribution follow quickly
- **Cognitive pauses / interruptions** (Zellmer-Bruhn, 2003) — counterintuitively, interruptions create receptivity to environmental changes

## Barriers to Detection

- Uncertainty and information overload drown out important cues (Rerup, 2009; Weick & Sutcliffe, 2006)
- Too much competing information (Waller, 1999 — bad weather + noisy descent masked hydraulic failure for flight crews)
- Gradual accumulation of minor issues (Bhopal, BP Deepwater Horizon — small problems discounted until disaster)
- Attentional resources are limited

## Future Directions (from Pearsall et al.)

- Correctly identifying *whether* a detected stimulus requires a response (avoiding false alarms vs. missed demands)
- Role of team tenure, training, "reflective pauses," and flowcharts in detection
- Status differences, culture of limited hierarchy, motivational factors
- Missed *opportunities* (not just threats) remain nearly unstudied
- "Critical mass" threshold: how many members must detect before team moves to Phase 2?
