# Goal-Directed Practice and Feedback

## Definition (CMU Eberly Principle 5)

Learning and performance are best fostered when practice is:
- **Goal-directed** — focused on a specific criterion or target
- **Appropriately challenging** — not too easy (no growth) nor too hard (overwhelm)
- **Sufficient in quantity and frequency** to meet performance criteria

And coupled with feedback that:
- Explicitly references the performance criterion
- Provides actionable information for improvement
- Is timely enough to be useful

## Practice Quality vs. Quantity

Naive view: more practice = more learning. CMU Eberly view: practice quality matters more. Unfocused repetition builds automaticity on whatever behavior is practiced — including errors.

> [!key-insight] Aimless repetition ≠ deliberate practice
> Practice must target a criterion to improve performance on that criterion. This mirrors Wiggins (1998) on educative assessment and Ericsson's deliberate practice framework.

## Feedback Dimensions

| Dimension | What it means |
|-----------|--------------|
| Criterion-referenced | Feedback says *how performance compares to the goal*, not just "good" or "bad" |
| Informative | Points toward what to change, not just that something is wrong |
| Timely | Given while students can still act on it |
| Frequent enough | Sparse feedback → slow course correction |
