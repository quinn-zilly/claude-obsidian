# Institutional Accretion

One of four change pathways in the [[Institutional Change Pathways]] typology. Characterized by **evolutionary pace** and **transformational scope**: slow, bottom-up accumulation of micro-level changes that eventually produces field-level institutional transformation — without exogenous shock or heroic entrepreneur.

## The Core Dynamic

Seemingly inconsequential micro-level improvisations accumulate, concatenate, and escalate through "revamping" mechanisms until they spill over into transformational field-level change. Smets, Morris & Greenwood (2012) is the canonical empirical example: new practices introduced unobtrusively within a firm "moved up" to the field level and shifted institutional logics.

This reveals that evolutionary pace and developmental scope are not causally linked. Small changes can aggregate into transformation.

## Characteristics

- Driven by accumulation of uncoordinated actions (Dorado 2005; Leblebici et al. 1991)
- Embedding of seemingly inconsequential micro-level practice changes (Ansari & Phillips 2011; Smets et al. 2012)
- Amplification of micro-level interactions (Gray et al. 2015)
- No deliberate institutional project; improvisations may be purely pragmatic responses to daily work
- Flies "under the radar" — low profile reduces resistance
- Most novel and theoretically underdeveloped: only 11 of 119 studies

## Revamping Mechanisms

- Positive feedback loops
- Increasing positive returns on practice spread
- Positive amplification
- Escalation
- Network diffusion of micro-level innovations

## Key Gaps (per Micelotta et al.)

- Under what conditions do micro-level acts of improvisation stimulate broader field-level transformations, and under what conditions do they not (f1)?
- Which organizational responses to institutional complexity feed back to the field and are conducive to transformational change, vs. remaining "local" (f2)?

## Theoretical Integrations Recommended

Micelotta et al. suggest combining:
- Practice theory + ambidexterity (Jarzabkowski et al. 2013)
- Institutional logics + institutional work (Zilber 2013)
- Identity work + institutional change (Creed et al. 2010)
- Emotions + institutional work (Voronov & Vince 2012)

## Connections

- [[Microfoundations]] (Barney & Felin 2013): both concern how individual-level variation aggregates to collective outcomes; accretion is the *emergent* (non-additive) form of this aggregation in institutional theory
- [[Organizational Learning]]: accretion resembles learning curves — accumulated experience with new practices eventually transforms organizational and field-level knowledge
- [[Knowledge Transfer]]: unobtrusive diffusion of practices is a form of knowledge transfer that bypasses the gatekeeping mechanisms that filter visible/contested knowledge transfers
- [[Publication Bottleneck]]: accretion in science — gradual unnoticed shifts in norms — is how methodological norms drift without triggering conscious resistance; connects to [[HARKing]] and [[P-hacking]] as accreted field-level practices
