# HEXACO Model

A **six-factor** lexical model of personality (Ashton, Lee & colleagues): **H**onesty-Humility, **E**motionality, e**X**traversion, **A**greeableness, **C**onscientiousness, **O**penness to Experience. Emerged from reanalysis of lexical studies across seven languages (Dutch, French, German, Hungarian, Italian, Korean, Polish), later replicated in at least a dozen languages plus expanded English adjective sets.

> [!note] E ≠ Neuroticism
> In the **[[Five-Factor Model|FFM]]**, E = Extraversion. In HEXACO, **E = Emotionality** and **X = Extraversion**. HEXACO Emotionality (sentimentality, vulnerability, fearfulness, anxiety, dependence) blends FFM Neuroticism *and* Agreeableness components but **lacks the anger** component; HEXACO Agreeableness folds in that (reversed-anger) component.

Not merely "Big Five + honesty":

- **Honesty-Humility** correlates only weakly/moderately with FFM factor-level variables; it captures traits poorly represented in the Big Five — Machiavellianism, narcissism, psychopathy, social adroitness, self-monitoring, altruism, modesty, fairness, greed avoidance.
- Across four cross-cultural samples the six factors accounted for **10–15% more variance in predicting workplace delinquency** than the FFM (Lee, Ashton & de Vries 2005).
- **Honesty-Humility logically predicts [[Counterproductive Work Behavior|counterproductive work behavior]]** (consistent with integrity-test validity).

Limitations per [[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough, Oswald & Ock (2015)]]:

- Still **hierarchical** and still **missing** many personality variables.
- The claim that HEXACO beats the Big Five on validity/R² rests on **weak criteria** (self-reported performance, behavioral-likelihood ratings) subject to common-method variance; tests with *good* performance criteria are lacking.
- Verdict: an **improvement** over the FFM (it *encompasses* more, not just reorganizes) but **superseded** by the authors' broader call to work at the [[Facet-Level Personality Measurement|facet level]] beyond any hierarchical model.
