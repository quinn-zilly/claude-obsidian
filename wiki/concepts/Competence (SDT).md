# Competence (SDT)

One of three [[Basic Psychological Needs (SDT)]] in [[Self-Determination Theory]].

**Definition**: Need to feel effective; to experience mastery and control over outcomes. Roots in White (1963) effectance motivation; Harter (1978) developmental model.

## Role in Intrinsic Motivation ([[Cognitive Evaluation Theory]])

Social-contextual events that conduce toward feelings of competence → enhance intrinsic motivation. Optimal challenges, effectance-promoting feedback, freedom from demeaning evaluation all facilitate it.

Positive performance feedback → ↑ intrinsic motivation (effects mediated by perceived competence; Vallerand & Reid 1984). Negative feedback → ↓ intrinsic motivation.

**Constraint**: Competence alone not sufficient. Must co-occur with sense of [[Autonomy (SDT)]] for intrinsic motivation.

## Role in Internalization

Supports adoption of activities valued by relevant social groups. People more likely to internalize behaviors when they feel efficacious with respect to those activities. Directing children to perform behaviors before developmentally ready → partial internalization at best (external or introjected, not integrated).

## Need Satisfaction Context

A context can afford competence while failing to support relatedness or autonomy — resulting in some well-being impoverishment. All three needs required for flourishing.
