# Multifoci Justice & Target Similarity

An approach to [[Organizational Justice]] that organizes justice around its **accountable source/foci** (supervisor, organization, coworkers, customers) rather than only its **type** ([[Four Justice Dimensions|distributive/procedural/interactional]]).

## Core claims

- Justice **"types" were never perceptual targets** — they are sets of *normative rules* used to judge whether a specific **accountable party** is fair (Lind & Tyler 1988). Justice without a "face" is attributionally ambiguous.
- **Target similarity** (Lavelle, Rupp & Brockner 2007): reactions to (in)justice are most strongly directed at the party *held accountable*. Aligning justice **source** with outcome **target** improves prediction.
- **Two engines**:
  - *Methodological* — [[Bandwidth-Fidelity Dilemma|bandwidth-fidelity]]: prediction is best when predictor and criterion specificity match.
  - *Theoretical* — [[Social Exchange Theory|social exchange]]: fair treatment by a party engenders a source-specific exchange relationship ([[Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)|LMX]] with supervisor, [[Perceived Organizational Support|POS]] with organization) that is reciprocated back to *that* party.

## Evidence

[[rupp-et-al-2014-multifoci-justice-meta-analysis|Rupp, Shao, Jones & Liao (2014)]] (k = 647, N = 235,682): source-based justice out-predicts type-based for matched outcomes (H1); target-similar > target-dissimilar effects (H2); source-specific social exchange mediates (H3). E.g., supervisory justice → LMX r = .65 vs. organizational justice → LMX r = .42.

## The dispute

> [!contradiction] Multifoci vs. type-based (Rupp 2014 vs. Colquitt 2013)
> [[colquitt-et-al-2013-justice-millennium-decade-later|Colquitt et al. (2013)]], using **bivariate correlations**, found focus-matching did **not** reliably improve prediction — supervisor-focused justice is just stronger in general. [[rupp-et-al-2014-multifoci-justice-meta-analysis|Rupp et al. (2014)]], using **regression + path analysis** on overlapping data, found matching **does** help via source-specific exchange. [[adamovic-2023-organizational-justice-review|Adamovic (2023)]]: the divergence is method-driven (source- vs. type-based justice, overall-per-source vs. faceted, coding liberality, study count). Unresolved.

## Extensions

Justice foci extend beyond supervisor/organization to **coworkers/peers** ([[Justice Climate|peer justice]]) and **customers**. Grounded in the **multiple needs model** (Cropanzano, Byrne, Bobocel & Rupp 2001): control, belonging, self-esteem, meaningful existence.
