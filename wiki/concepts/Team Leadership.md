# Team Leadership

Research on how leader behavior influences team processes, emergent states, and performance. A major third-wave expansion that integrates behavioral, cognitive, and motivational traditions.

## Functional Leadership Perspective
Leaders should provide what teams *need, when needed* (Hackman & Wageman 2005). Key functions:
- Clear direction and mission
- Enabling structure and context
- Coaching and resource provision
- Facilitating collective leadership capacity

Influences team performance through cognitive (mental models), motivational (potency, cohesion), and affective processes.

## Key Findings

### Marks et al. (2000) [Table 1, JAP]
Leader briefing quality → team member communication processes → similarity and accuracy of shared mental models → team performance. Effect stronger on **novel vs. routine tasks** — leader functional behavior is more critical when teams face non-routine demands.

### Functional Leadership in Context
- **DeChurch & Marks (2006)**: Functional leadership in multiteam systems — direction setting and enabling coordination → improved performance
- **Yun, Faraj & Sims (2005)**: Directive vs. empowering leadership in trauma resuscitation teams — when severity high and experience low, directive is more effective; matching leader behavior to task demands
- **Morgeson (2005)**: External leaders of self-managing teams; preparation and coaching especially valuable when teams face novel/disruptive events

### Transformational Leadership in Teams
- **Bass et al. (2003)**: Transformational → team potency, cohesion → team performance
- **Schaubroeck et al. (2007)**: Team values moderate transformational leadership–team performance link
- **Kearney & Gebert (2009)**: Transformational leadership → information elaboration → diversity benefits
- **Zhang & Peterson (2011)**: Transformational → advice exchange networks in teams

### Multilevel Leadership Research
- **Chen & Bliese (2002)**: Different levels of leadership predict self- vs. collective efficacy
- **Chen et al. (2007)**: Leadership climate → team empowerment; LMX → individual empowerment — level-specific effects

## Leader Role Changes Over Team Development
**Kozlowski et al. (1999, 2009)**: Leader roles change as team matures from novice to expert state. Early: more directive and structuring. Later: more empowering and adaptive.
