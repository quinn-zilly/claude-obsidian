# Organismic Integration Theory (OIT)

Second subtheory of [[Self-Determination Theory]] (Deci & Ryan 1985). Details different forms of [[Extrinsic Motivation]] and contextual factors promoting or hindering [[Internalization (SDT)]] and integration.

## The Taxonomy (Self-Determination Continuum)

Left → right = increasing self-determination:

**Amotivation** → **External regulation** → **Introjected regulation** → **Identified regulation** → **Integrated regulation** → **Intrinsic motivation**

- **Amotivation**: no intention to act; not valuing, not feeling competent, not expecting desired outcome
- **External**: comply with demand/contingency; alienated; external locus
- **Introjected**: taken in but not accepted; ego-involvement; avoid guilt/attain pride; contingent self-esteem; somewhat external locus
- **Identified**: consciously valued; personally important; accepted as own; somewhat internal locus
- **Integrated**: fully assimilated; congruent with other values/needs; internal locus; shares qualities with intrinsic but done for separable outcomes
- **Intrinsic**: inherent satisfaction; highly autonomous; prototypic self-determination

Ryan & Connell (1989): quasi-simplex intercorrelation confirms underlying continuum.

## Conditions Promoting Internalization / Integration

1. **Relatedness** — initial internalization: behaviors performed because valued by significant others one wants to feel connected to
2. **Competence** — efficacy supports adopting valued activities
3. **Autonomy** — critical for integration; holistic processing requires freedom from excessive external pressure

Without autonomy support: can get external or introjected regulation but not integrated. Deci, Eghrari, Patrick & Leone (1994) lab demonstration: meaningful rationale + autonomy support + relatedness → internalization + integration.
