# External Validity

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]]

The validity of inferences about whether a **causal relationship holds over variation** in persons, settings, treatments, and outcomes — both those included in the study and those not. Fourth component of the [[Validity Typology]]; a form of [[Causal Generalization]].

## Scope of generalization targets

- Narrow → broad (sample → population)
- Broad → narrow (sample → single case, e.g. one patient)
- Similar level (state → comparable state)
- Similar *or* different kind (male applicants → female applicants elsewhere)
- Random sample → population members (rare)

SCC side with **Cronbach**: inferences to as-yet-unstudied applications are legitimate and necessary to science and policy, not out of bounds. Most external-validity questions arise *after* a study, about instances too late to include.

## Five threats (interaction effects)

1. Interaction with **Units**
2. Interaction with **Treatment Variations**
3. Interaction with **Outcomes**
4. Interaction with **Settings**
5. **Context-Dependent Mediation** — mechanism differs across contexts.

Each is a way the causal relationship changes across some dimension. See [[Threats to Validity]].

## Tension with internal validity

Randomization and treatment standardization sharpen [[Internal Validity]] and construct clarity but can reduce external validity to real-world settings. The tradeoff has no universal resolution.
