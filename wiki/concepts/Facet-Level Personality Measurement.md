# Facet-Level Personality Measurement

**Facets** are narrower latent traits subsumed by (or lying beneath) the broad [[Five-Factor Model|Big Five]] factors. The central argument of Hough and colleagues: **measuring personality at the facet level often provides more useful information than measuring at the broad-factor level.**

## Why facets matter

- **Facets of the same factor correlate very differently with the same criterion** (Hough 1992) — relationships masked when broad measures are used. E.g., Conscientiousness's Achievement vs Dependability facets are differentially important by job; Extraversion's Dominance predicts many criteria while Sociability does not.
- A "**mountain of evidence**" ([[hough-oswald-ock-2015-beyond-the-big-five|Hough et al. 2015]]) shows **facets frequently out-predict their parent factor**, depending on the criterion (Ashton 1998; Dudley et al. 2006; Paunonen & Ashton; LePine et al. 2000; etc.).
- Facet placement is **model-dependent** (Costa & McCrae's 30 facets vs Saucier & Ostendorf's 18 vs Soto & John's 15), disagreeing on warmth, anxiety, adventurousness — evidence that structure is **lattice-like, not strictly hierarchical**.

## The Dominance "win–win" (adverse impact)

> [!key-insight] Facet measurement can raise validity AND lower adverse impact
> Blacks score ≈ Whites on the **Dominance** facet but ~0.30–0.40 SD lower on **Sociability** (both Extraversion facets). Because Dominance is a strong, broad predictor and Sociability is not, using a **facet-level Dominance measure** (when job analysis implicates it) yields **higher criterion validity and lower adverse impact against Blacks** than a broad Extraversion measure — which averages the two. This **contradicts** common advice to measure at the factor level to control adverse impact. ([[hough-johnson-2013-personality-in-work-settings|Hough & Johnson 2013]])

## Relation to structure and prediction

- Facets can be aggregated up into factors or into higher-order **[[Compound Traits (Personality)|compound traits]]**.
- The facet-vs-factor question is the empirical face of the **[[Bandwidth-Fidelity Dilemma]]** — a theory/practice tension, not a dichotomy.
- Facet-level linking is the basis for **[[Nomological-Web Clustering]]** and for tailored **[[Synthetic Validity|synthetic-validity]]** batteries.
- Methodological caution: broad factors are more reliable; small samples + poor criteria + factor-level-only reliability may have historically **conspired to obscure** true facet findings.
