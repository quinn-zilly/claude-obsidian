# Electronic Brainstorming (EBS)

Computer-mediated group idea generation. Participants type ideas simultaneously on networked computers; ideas shared on common screen. Developed 1980s; associated with Nunamaker, Dennis, Valacich, Connolly groups.

## Performance Hierarchy

> Nominal groups > F2F brainstorming (standard finding since McGrath 1984)
> Large EBS groups > nominal groups (Valacich, Dennis & Connolly 1994)

Confirmed by multiple meta-analyses: Dennis et al. 2001; Dennis & Williams 2005; DeRosa, Smith & Hantula 2007.

## Why EBS Outperforms F2F

**F2F failure mechanism**: *production blocking* — only one person talks at a time → others forget ideas, construct counterarguments, lose thread (Diehl & Stroebe 1987).

**EBS solution**: simultaneous input removes production blocking; all members productive at once while drawing stimulation from others' ideas.

**Additional EBS effects**:
- Anonymity → more ideas, more controversial ideas (Connolly, Jessup & Valacich 1990; Cooper et al. 1998)
- Anonymity may → less task satisfaction

## Size Threshold

EBS advantage appears only with large groups (~8+). Mechanism unclear (Connolly 1997).

## Why Organizations Still Use F2F

Despite evidence:
- Strong intuitive appeal — people *predict* F2F will be superior (Paulus et al. 1993)
- F2F superior on group well-being and member support (Dennis & Reinicke 2004)
- Limited EBS equipment access
- Kavadias & Sommer (2009): relative effectiveness depends on problem structure + team skill diversity

## Relevance to Information Sharing in Groups

EBS = technology solution to common information problem. Related to hidden profile issue ([[connolly-2012-jdm]] §Multiple Decision Makers): when unique information must surface, structured simultaneous input helps.
