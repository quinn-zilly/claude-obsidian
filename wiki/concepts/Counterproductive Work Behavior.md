# Counterproductive Work Behavior

**Counterproductive work behavior (CWB)** — "scalable actions and behaviors that employees engage in that detract from organizational goals or well-being" (Ones & Dilchert 2013). The civilian counterpart of Project A's "maintaining personal discipline" factor and Factor 4 of the [[Campbell Model of Job Performance]].

## Structure

Two primary subfactors:
- **CWB-O** — deviance directed at the organization (theft, absence, sabotage).
- **CWB-I** — deviance directed at other individuals (bullying, gossiping).

Substance abuse clusters with CWB-O factor-analytically. A further **approach vs. avoidance** distinction applies within each: actively acting against (property deviance, personal aggression) vs. staying away / undermining (production deviance, political deviance). A strong general CWB factor is present.

## Key point

CWB is **not** simply the negative pole of positively-stated dimensions like OCB / [[Contextual Performance]] (Berry et al. 2007; Ones & Dilchert 2013) — it is a distinguishable dimension with different determinants. Measures should avoid mixing OCB and CWB items on the same scale.
