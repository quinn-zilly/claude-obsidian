# Leading-Following Double Interact

Nav: [[index]] | [[Leadership MOC]]

The **basic unit of interaction** in DeRue's ([[derue-2011-adaptive-leadership-complex-adaptive-process|2011]]) [[Adaptive Leadership Theory]]. Borrowed from Hollander & Willis (1967) and Weick (1979): a **double interact** occurs when actor A engages in a leading act, actor B follows, and A subsequently leads again. The behaviors of each actor are contingent on *and* influence the behaviors of the other, forming a contingent response pattern with feedback loops.

## Why the Double Interact Matters

- **Anyone can lead or follow.** Because the unit is an *act* (not a formal position), all group members can engage in both leading and following.
- **Effect is contingent on response.** A leadership act's influence depends on whether others reciprocate with following — connecting to Barnard/Simon's **zone of acceptance**: the same behavior is "leadership" in one group and illegitimate coercion in another.
- **Causality is not leader-first.** Acts of leading may *respond* to prior acts of following, so leadership need not begin with an act of leading.

## From Double Interacts to Structures

Aggregating double interacts across a group, characterized by **magnitude** (network density of reciprocated interacts) and **dispersion** (Freeman centralization + centrality variance over time), yields the four patterns — centralized, distributed, [[Shared Leadership|shared]], and leadership void.
