# Justice Climate

The team-level analogue of [[Organizational Justice|justice]]: "a distinct **unit-level cognition** regarding **shared** justice perceptions of treatment by organizational authorities" (Whitman et al. 2012). Shifts the referent from *"How just am I treated?"* to *"How just is my **team** treated?"* — a [[Referent-Shift Consensus|referent-shift]] emergence. First studied by Mossholder, Bennett & Martin (1998).

## Two properties

- **Justice climate level** — the *average* perception of how justly the team is treated by an authority figure.
- **Justice climate strength** — the *degree of agreement* among members (inverse of within-team variance). High strength **amplifies** the effect of climate level on outcomes (Whitman et al. 2012; Li, Cropanzano & Molina 2015).

Both matter (Colquitt, Zapata-Phelan & Roberson 2005). Climate organized around the same four dimensions + social accounts (Spell & Arnold 2007).

## Why it matters

- Collective justice perceptions predict **incremental variance** in extra-role behavior, task performance, and organizational commitment **beyond** individual justice perceptions (Whitman et al. 2012). "Team contexts can magnify the importance of justice" (Colquitt, Greenberg & Scott 2005).

## Peer justice

An extension of the [[Multifoci Justice & Target Similarity|multifoci]] idea: **coworkers/teammates** as a *source* of justice, historically neglected (Cropanzano, Li & Benson 2011; Rupp & Paddock 2010). Especially salient in **self-managed teams**, where members collectively allocate tasks, workload, praise, and rewards — implicating distributive, procedural, and interpersonal justice *among peers*.
