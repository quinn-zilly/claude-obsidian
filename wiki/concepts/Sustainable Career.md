# Sustainable Career

A **sustainable career** is one that dynamically balances **happiness × health × productivity** over the life course (De Vos et al. 2020). A career is sustainable when there is a fit between the career and the person's current mental and physical capacities such that these three stay in dynamic balance.

- **Happiness** ≈ facets of [[Subjective Career Success]].
- **Health** ≈ physical and mental health (the dimension career research has usually neglected).
- **Productivity** ≈ performance, citizenship behavior, and [[Objective Career Success]].

Example of *un*sustainability: someone who is highly productive (many promotions) but overwhelmed loses happiness and health; or someone happy and healthy but consistently underperforming loses the job.

## Why Seibert et al. (2024) highlight it

The sustainable-career lens **reconciles** OP's happiness focus with OB's productivity focus, adds the missing **health** perspective, foregrounds the **individual × context interplay**, and forces a **temporal** view (most career-success studies are still cross-sectional and miss dynamic change). It pairs naturally with the [[Career Shocks]] perspective.
