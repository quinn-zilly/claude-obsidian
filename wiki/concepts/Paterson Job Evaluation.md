# Paterson Job Evaluation

The **Paterson method** grades jobs by the **kind and complexity of decisions** an employee must make. Developed by [[Thomas Thomson Paterson]] (Scotland, 1963); widely used in Africa (South African government reporting uses Paterson) and ILO-recognised. A close cousin to Elliott Jaques' [[Stratified Systems Theory]].

## Six decision bands (A–F)

| Band | Level / kind of decision | Indicative roles |
|------|--------------------------|------------------|
| **F** | Policy-making (top management) | CEO, executive directors |
| **E** | Programming / long-term strategy (senior/general mgmt) | General managers |
| **D** | Interpretive / probabilistic (middle mgmt, advanced specialist) | Department/section managers |
| **C** | Deterministic / process-system (skilled, advanced operational) | Supervisors, artisans, specialists |
| **B** | Operational / subsystem (semi-skilled) | Operators, clerks, drivers |
| **A** | Defined (basic skills) | Trainees, basic tasks |

All bands except A split into **upper/lower** sub-bands — the upper reflecting supervision/coordination of others in the same band, or **sapiential authority** (authority from accumulated wisdom).

## Three grading steps

1. **Band** each job A–F by decision type.
2. **Sub-band** (upper/lower) by whether the role coordinates same-band work / sapiential authority.
3. **Sub-grade** within a band using: **variety, complexity, precision, physical/mental effort**, qualified by education, training time, and competence required.

> [!note] Volume ≠ grade
> More tasks or higher volume do not raise a grade; only complexity, accountability, decision-making, impact, and skill/knowledge do. Grade the highest-graded task in the job.

## Paterson Points (JEasy)

A points-factor variant (21st Century) built on the five factors in most equal-pay legislation: Knowledge & Skills, Problem Solving, Judgement, Accountability, Impact (Skill / Effort / Responsibility). Web-based, WFA-friendly, with an audit trail; available in five languages.

Feeds directly into a Paterson-graded [[Salary Structure]].
