## Organizational Knowledge & Learning

- [[Knowledge Integration]] — organizational capability for creating novel combinations of knowledge; the raison d'etre of the firm (mature)
- [[Organizational Learning]] — change in organizational knowledge as a function of experience; four processes: search, creation, retention, transfer (mature)
- [[Knowledge Management]] — deliberate management of knowledge creation, storage, transfer, and application; grounded in KBV (mature)
- [[Learning Curves]] — performance improves at decreasing rate with cumulative experience; knowledge depreciates when not practiced (mature)
- [[Transactive Memory Systems]] — distributed group-level memory; who knows what and how to access it (developing)
- [[Knowledge Transfer]] — moving knowledge across people, units, or organizations; barriers include causal ambiguity and tacitness (mature)
- [[Tacit vs Explicit Knowledge]] — experiential/articulable distinction central to KM design and transfer strategy (mature)
- [[Knowledge-Based View of the Firm]] — firm exists to integrate specialist knowledge; knowledge is the primary source of sustainable competitive advantage (mature)
- [[Knowledge Retention]] — embedding knowledge in distributed repositories (members, tools, routines, TMS) so it persists (developing)
- [[Knowledge-Based Theory of the Firm]] — Grant (1996); firm as knowledge integrator; four mechanisms (rules, sequencing, routines, group problem-solving); common knowledge (developing)
- [[Microfoundations]] — individual-level mechanisms underlying collective outcomes; additive vs. emergent aggregation (developing)

## Open Science & Meta-Science

- [[Replication Crisis]] — systematic failures of replication in psychology and other sciences; root causes include publication bias, p-hacking, HARKing (mature)
- [[Open Science]] — transparency, open data, open materials, preregistration; reform movement for credible knowledge accumulation (mature)
- [[Preregistration]] — committing to research design and analysis plan before data collection; separates confirmatory from exploratory research (mature)
- [[Registered Reports]] — publishing format with in-principle acceptance before results known; eliminates publication bias at editorial decision point (developing)
- [[Publication Bias]] — file drawer problem; positive/significant results disproportionately published; inflates effect sizes in literature (developing)
- [[P-hacking]] — analytic flexibility; data-contingent analysis decisions that inflate false-positive rates (developing)
- [[HARKing]] — Hypothesizing After Results are Known; presenting post-hoc hypotheses as a priori predictions (developing)
- [[Analytic Reproducibility]] — reproducing published results from same data; one of four credibility dimensions in LeBel's framework (developing)
- [[Open Science Framework]] — osf.io; free platform for preregistration, data archiving, and research collaboration (developing)
- [[metaBUS]] — 1M+ curated bivariate findings from applied psychology; enables rapid, reproducible meta-analysis (developing)
