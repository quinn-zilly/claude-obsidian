# Behavioral Learning Objectives

Statements describing what learners will be **able to do** (observable, measurable behavior) as a result of instruction. Operationalize Bloom's taxonomy at the practitioner level. Core to instructional design, training evaluation, and transfer.

## Criteria (APA Education Directorate)

Well-written objectives are:
- Observable and measurable (action verb + behavior specification)
- Learner-focused (not content- or instructor-focused)
- Appropriate in breadth (3–4 per 4-hour program)
- Sufficient depth for target audience
- Linked to program narrative, supporting references, and promotional materials

## Verb Taxonomy

**Use:** list, describe, recite, write, identify, compute, discuss, explain, predict, apply, demonstrate, prepare, use, analyze, design, select, utilize, compile, create, plan, revise, assess, compare, rate, critique

**Avoid:** know, understand, learn, appreciate, become aware of, become familiar with

Avoidance rationale: mental-state verbs are unobservable → cannot be measured → cannot confirm learning occurred.

## Format

"At the end of this [program], the learner will be able to [action verb] [object] [conditions/criteria]."

## Links to Transfer

Explicitly stated learning objectives:
- Function as **advance organizers** (Kraiger et al. 1995) → trainees understand what's expected post-training → increases transfer (Burke & Hutchins 2007, r = .37)
- Provide criteria for **AER** — after-event reviews can check performance against stated objectives for both successes and failures (Ellis & Davidi 2005)
- Define Kirkpatrick Level 2 (Learning) and Level 3 (Behavior) measurement criteria
- Align training content with organizational strategy — strategic link → transfer (Montesino 2002)
