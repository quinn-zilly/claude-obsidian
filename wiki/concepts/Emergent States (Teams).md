# Emergent States (Teams)

Nav: [[index]] | [[concepts/_index]]

Team-level concepts that reflect shared affect, cognitions, or motivational states. Unlike processes (which are activities), emergent states are *properties* of the team at a point in time. They can function as **inputs, mediators, or outcomes** depending on when they are measured in a team's lifecycle (Marks et al. 2001).

## Key Emergent States

### Cohesion
Members' attraction and commitment to their team, teammates, and the team's task (Evans & Jarvis 1980). Affective + task dimensions.

Meta-analytic relationships with broad teamwork processes (LePine et al. 2008):
- Transition processes: ρ = .60
- Action processes: ρ = .61
- Interpersonal processes: ρ = .53

### Potency
Shared belief that the team can be effective (Shea & Guzzo 1987). Motivational; more akin to collective efficacy.

Meta-analytic relationships:
- Transition processes: ρ = .63
- Action processes: ρ = .65
- Interpersonal processes: ρ = .70

Both cohesion and potency positively associated with team performance (Gully et al. 1995, 2002).

### Transactive Memory Systems
A cognitive emergent state — distributed knowing of *who knows what* (Wegner 1987). Unlike cohesion/potency, TMS is specifically a knowledge structure. Developed via cross-training and shared experience. See [[Transactive Memory Systems]].

### Shared Mental Models
Team members' common understanding of the task, team, equipment, and situation. Developed via team coordination training. Targets of [[Team Training]].

## Relationship to Processes

Emergent states and teamwork processes are **mutually reinforcing**:
- High-quality processes → develop positive emergent states (cohesion, potency, TMS)
- Positive emergent states → enable higher-quality processes (trust → better conflict management; potency → more confident backup behavior; TMS → efficient monitoring and coordination)

This reciprocal relationship is why the IMOI (Input–Mediator–Output–Input) model replaces the linear IPO — emergent states feed back as new inputs.

## IPO → IMOI Framework

Classic **Input–Process–Output** (McGrath 1964):
`inputs → processes → outputs`

Updated **IMOI** (Ilgen et al. 2005):
`inputs → mediators (processes + emergent states) → outputs → (feedback as new inputs)`

Emergent states are distinct mediators alongside processes. The distinction matters for measurement: process = dynamic act; emergent state = property at a moment.
