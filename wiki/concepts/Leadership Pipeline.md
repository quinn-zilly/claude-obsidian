# Leadership Pipeline

A model of leadership development developed by Charan, Drotter & Noel (2001) identifying six sequential **passages** leaders must navigate from individual contributor to CEO. Each passage requires changes in **skills**, **time applications**, and **work values**.

The model is diagnostic, developmental, and succession-planning tool simultaneously.

## The Six Passages at a Glance

| Passage | From → To | Core value shift |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Managing Self → Managing Others | Valuing others' productivity over own output |
| 2 | Managing Others → Managing Managers | Valuing managerial work in others; pure management |
| 3 | Managing Managers → Functional Manager | Valuing unfamiliar functions; long-term functional strategy |
| 4 | Functional Manager → Business Manager | Profit perspective; integrating functions; short+long balance |
| 5 | Business Manager → Group Manager | Valuing *others'* business success over own |
| 6 | Group Manager → Enterprise Manager | Visionary thinking; external focus; letting go of parts |

See [[charan-leadership-pipeline-ch1]] for detailed passage descriptions.

## The Three Dimensions of Each Passage

Every passage requires change in all three:
- **Skills** — new technical and interpersonal capabilities
- **Time applications** — longer horizons, more delegation, different activities
- **Work values** — what the leader believes matters most

> [!key-insight] Values are the hardest to change
> A leader can appear to have completed a passage (new behaviors) while still holding old values. Under stress, they revert. Pipeline clogs happen when values don't shift.

## Pipeline Clogs and Cascade Effects

When a leader skips or incompletely completes a passage:
- Brings wrong skills/time/values to new level
- Holds subordinates accountable for wrong things
- Passes incorrect values down the chain
- A clogged passage at any level degrades performance across all levels below it

This is structural derailment — see [[Derailment]] for individual-level causes.

## Connection to Stretch Experiences

[[Stretch Experiences]] are the mechanism through which leaders develop within and across passages. The Pipeline model specifies *what* needs to be learned at each level; stretch experiences are *how* that learning happens. Skipping stretch = risk of skipping or inadequately completing a passage.

## Connection to Derailment

[[Derailment]] is often the outcome of an incomplete passage — particularly failure to adapt values (Passage 1 skippers who remain individual contributors in manager bodies) or failure to transition to valuing others' success (Passage 5 failure). Many derailment causes map directly to incomplete passage work.

## Organizational Applications

1. **Succession planning**: identify which passage each candidate is currently working; select for next level when working fully at current level AND demonstrating some skills of next level
2. **Development**: targeted programs addressing specific passage gaps (not generic "leadership training")
3. **Diagnosis**: when a leader struggles, diagnose which passage they skipped or are stuck in
4. **Selection**: objectifies promotion decisions; reduces reliance on past performance, personal connection
5. **Technical pipeline**: high-performing technical contributors need their own parallel advancement track (prevents promoting unwilling/unready people into management)

## Small Business Adaptation

Passages compress — Group Manager often absent; Enterprise Manager = Business Manager role. The principles (skill/time/value shifts at each transition) still apply.
