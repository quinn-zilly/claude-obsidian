# Vocation (Dik & Duffy)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

In [[Dik-Duffy-2009-Calling-and-Vocation|Dik & Duffy's (2009)]] framework, **vocation** is the sibling construct to [[Work as a Calling|calling]], differing on exactly one dimension.

> "A vocation is an approach to a particular life role that is oriented toward demonstrating or deriving a sense of purpose or meaningfulness and that holds other-oriented values and goals as primary sources of motivation."

The definition is identical to their definition of **calling** except that it **omits the transcendent summons**. Both connect work to purpose/meaning toward other-oriented ends; **only calling** adds the perception that the impetus originates from a source **external to the self** (God, society's needs, fate). So vocation is the broader, more inclusive construct — it captures internally-sourced motivation to approach a life role in a purposeful, prosocial way, whereas calling is the externally-summoned subset.

The authors argue every person **potentially** has a vocation, which is what makes the construct usable in universal career-development practice. Comparing individuals with a calling to those with a vocation-but-not-a-calling is offered as a way to study externally-occurring vs self-generated sources of work motivation.
