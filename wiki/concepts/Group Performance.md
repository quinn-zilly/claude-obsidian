# Group Performance

Group performance is the central dependent variable in the transactive memory literature — the set of behaviors and outcomes a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]] is theorized to improve. [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] organize TMS consequences into three families.

**Performance behaviors:** [[Team Learning]], team creativity, and team reflectivity.

**Performance outcomes:** effectiveness (work quality, meeting goals), efficiency (time to completion, cost), recall accuracy, and decision quality.

**Member affective outcomes:** job satisfaction, team identification, and commitment.

The TMS→performance link is *moderated*, not uniform. The review identifies group size (smaller groups show stronger effects), task complexity (complex tasks benefit more), task type and task change, environmental turbulence (mixed effects), relationship conflict (negative), trust (unclear), geographic distribution in virtual teams, and group membership change as moderators.
