# Adverse Impact

A selection procedure has **adverse impact** when it disproportionately screens out members of a protected race, sex, or ethnic group. Under the [[EEOC-1978-uniform-guidelines-employee-selection|Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection (1978)]] (§3A), a procedure with adverse impact **is considered discriminatory unless validated** per the Guidelines (or §6 satisfied).

Distinct from **disparate treatment** (intentional unequal treatment): even a validated procedure can't be applied to one group but not others (§11).

## Trigger Logic
- No adverse impact → no validity study required.
- Adverse impact → must validate, drop, or substitute a suitable **less-adverse alternative** of substantially equal validity (§3B).
- "**Bottom line**" (§4C): if the *total* process has no adverse impact, components usually aren't scrutinized.

## Measurement
Screened primarily via the [[Four-Fifths Rule]], but smaller differences can still count if statistically **and** practically significant; larger differences may not if based on small non-significant numbers.
