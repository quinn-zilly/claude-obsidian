# Online Training Transfer Taxonomy

Proposed by [[shukla-et-al-2024-transfer-online-training]] (Shukla, Dash & Kumar, 2024) — first systematic taxonomy of factors affecting training transfer specifically in the online/digital medium.

## The Four Categories

### 1. Computer (Digital) Literacy & Attitudes
**Online-unique** — absent from all offline transfer frameworks (Baldwin & Ford, 1988; Burke & Hutchins, 2008).

Key factors: computer anxiety, computer confidence, computer liking, computer self-efficacy, computer literacy, technology readiness.

> [!key-insight]
> Digital literacy/attitudes is the novel contribution of this taxonomy. Organizations moving training online need to assess and address computer anxiety and self-efficacy *before* expecting transfer gains.

### 2. Learner Characteristics
Most-studied category (~14 factors, 22 studies). Includes:
- Self-regulation, self-efficacy, motivation to transfer, motivation to learn
- Internal values, participation, reactions, learner control
- Enjoyment, satisfaction, cognitive skills, perceived usefulness, previous experience

### 3. Online Learning Design & Planning
Instructional design adapted for digital context:
- Training usefulness, learning strategy and design, course learnability
- Ease of learning, needs analysis, goal setting, learning flow
- Teaching methodology, teaching models, built-in evaluations

### 4. Learning Environment & Organizational Support
Mirrors offline transfer climate literature:
- Technology, infrastructure, organizational culture
- Organizational support, supervisor support, peer support
- Interaction opportunities, opportunity to use
- Organizational climate, policies, readiness, learning environment

## What's Shared with Offline Transfer

Self-efficacy, peer support, learning strategy, training usefulness, organizational support — all documented in offline frameworks too. Online training research can leverage the mature offline literature for these factors.

## What's Unique to Online

Computer/digital literacy and attitudes. This category is underresearched even within the online training literature — a priority gap for future work.

## Research Gaps

- No study has tested contextual parameters (training purpose, industry type) as *moderators* of these factors
- Majority studies cross-sectional — causality unclear
- Digital medium sophistication (mobile vs. VR vs. browser) not differentiated
- Measurement of "transfer of online training" lacks consensus definition/validated scale
