# Team Learning

Team learning is the process by which a group acquires, shares, and applies knowledge to improve its collective performance over time. In [[ren-argote-2011-tms|Ren & Argote (2011)]] it is classified as one of the team *performance behaviors* through which a [[Transactive Memory Systems|transactive memory system]] yields better outcomes - alongside team creativity and team reflectivity.

The relationship runs both ways: a well-developed TMS enables more effective team learning (members know where to direct questions and whom to trust), and learning together helps build and refine the TMS. The review connects this to TMS as the mechanism explaining why prior shared team experience benefits performance, including in new ventures, because the Task-Expertise-Person links built through learning carry forward to similar tasks.
