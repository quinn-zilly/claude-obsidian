# Philosophy of Science Parameters

**Philosophy of science** = the conceptual roots undergirding the quest for knowledge (science defined broadly as the systematic quest for knowledge). Ponterotto (2005), following Guba & Lincoln, decomposes a [[Research Paradigm]] into five parameters. Knowing where a paradigm stands on each is what lets you "locate" a study.

## The five parameters

- **Ontology** — the nature of reality and being. *What is the form of reality and what can be known about it?* Positivism: one true reality (naïve realism). Postpositivism: one reality, only imperfectly apprehended (critical realism). Constructivism: multiple constructed realities (relativism). Critical: reality shaped by power relations.
- **Epistemology** — the relationship between the **knower** (participant) and the **would-be knower** (researcher). Positivism: dualism + objectivism (researcher independent of, and uninfluenced by, the participant). Postpositivism: modified dualism/objectivism. Constructivism: transactional + subjectivist (interaction is how meaning is co-constructed). Critical: transactional, subjectivist, and **dialectic**.
- **Axiology** — the role and place of researcher **values** in the process. Positivism/postpositivism: values excluded or contained. Constructivism: values bracketed but not eliminated. Critical: values **central** — the researcher hopes their values transform participants (emancipation).
- **Rhetorical structure** — the language used to present the research. Positivist/postpositivist: precise, objective, "scientific." Constructivist/critical: first-person, personalized, biases and emotional impact disclosed.
- **Methodology** — the process and procedures. Positivist/postpositivist: experiments, analog, quasi-experimental (control/manipulate variables). Constructivist/critical: **naturalistic inquiry** — in-depth interviews, participant observation, researcher immersed in participants' world.

Methodology flows downstream from ontology → epistemology → axiology. The parameters are not independent: a position on reality constrains how you can know it, which constrains what role values play, which shapes rhetoric and method.

## Cross-cutting distinctions

Two pairs of terms recur across the parameters:

- [[Idiographic vs Nomothetic]] — focus on the unique individual vs general/universal patterns.
- [[Etic vs Emic]] — universal, culture-general constructs vs context-specific, culture-specific ones.

Positivism/postpositivism lean **nomothetic + etic**; constructivism and the critical paradigm lean **idiographic + emic**.
