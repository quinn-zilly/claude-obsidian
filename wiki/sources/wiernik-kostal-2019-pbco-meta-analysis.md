# Wiernik & Kostal (2019) — Protean and Boundaryless Career Orientations: A Critical Review and Meta-Analysis

> [!abstract] One-line
> First systematic review + psychometric meta-analysis of protean/boundaryless career orientations (PBCO): 135 samples, 45,288 individuals, 35 countries. The headline finding collapses the traditional protean-vs-boundaryless distinction into a **single general factor — [[Proactive Career Orientation]]**.

## Design

Psychometric (Schmidt-Hunter random-effects) meta-analysis correcting for sampling and measurement error. **135 samples / 45,288 individuals / 35 countries / all 10 GLOBE clusters.** Mostly employed adults, 73% university-educated, mean age 33; 90% cross-sectional. Most common measures: Briscoe et al. (2006) PBCO scales and Baruch et al. (2005).

## Three research questions and answers

**1. Structure.** The three components — protean **self-directed**, protean **values-driven**, and boundaryless **psychological mobility** — are substantially intercorrelated, share a general factor, and show similar criterion and personality-trait patterns. The authors label this shared factor **proactive career orientation**. Boundaryless **physical (organizational) mobility preference** is weakly related to the others and has a divergent nomological net — it is a *distinct* construct. Grouping psychological and physical mobility under one "boundaryless" label obscures their difference (echoing Feldman & Ng 2007). → **No support for the traditional protean/boundaryless taxonomy.**

**2. Criterion-related validity.**

| Criterion | Relation to proactive career orientation |
|---|---|
| Career self-management behaviors | **Strong** |
| Career satisfaction (SCS) | **Strong** |
| Non-career-focused attitudes (job satisfaction, turnover intent) | Weaker than for career satisfaction |
| Objective career success (salary, promotions/level) | **Weak** (\|ρ\| < .20; consistent with Ng et al. 2005) |
| Physical mobility *behavior* | Weak (partly a rare-behavior / low-variance artifact) |

Supports Hypothesis 6 (PBCO align better with career-focused than non-career attitudes) and the |ρ|<.20 objective-success prediction (H5).

**3. Nomological net / incremental validity.** Proactive career orientation is strongly tied to **proactive personality, self-efficacy, Openness, Extraversion, Conscientiousness**. Much of its predictive power is *shared* with these traits (negligible incremental validity for many criteria) — BUT it shows **substantial incremental validity for career satisfaction and turnover intentions** (ΔR² ≈ .10), strongest for the criteria to which it is most conceptually aligned. This confirms the bandwidth-fidelity/construct-correspondence logic: contextualized career measures out-predict broad traits when matched to career criteria.

## Integrative model

PBCO sit **intermediate between broad traits and career behaviors/outcomes**. Individuals high on proactivity and self-efficacy are predisposed to adopt a proactive career orientation (especially when social/economic context supports it), which in turn drives career self-management and positive career evaluation. Framed via the traits-vs-**characteristic-adaptations** distinction (DeYoung 2015; McAdams & Pals 2006): proactive career orientation is a characteristic adaptation — more **malleable via counseling** than broad traits, the paper's key practical implication.

## Contradiction / tension with vault

> [!contradiction] Single-factor vs two-factor view of the protean construct
> Wiernik & Kostal find protean self-directed + values-driven + psychological mobility load on **one** general factor. [[hall-yip-doiron-2018-protean-careers-at-work|Hall, Yip & Doiron (2018)]] treat **self-direction and values orientation as two distinct, jointly necessary dimensions** of protean career orientation, and treat protean vs boundaryless as related-but-independent. The disagreement is partly empirical (factor structure in pooled data) and partly conceptual (values-driven as a separable meaning engine vs absorbed into a general proactivity factor). Flagged on [[Protean Career Orientation]] and [[Proactive Career Orientation]].
