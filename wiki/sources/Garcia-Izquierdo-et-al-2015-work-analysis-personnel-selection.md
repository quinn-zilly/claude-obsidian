# García-Izquierdo, Díaz Vilela & Moscoso (2015) — Work Analysis for Personnel Selection

## Summary

Chapter-length review of **Work Analysis (WA)** for selection. Uses Sánchez & Levine's broad "work analysis" term (not "job analysis") to cover tasks, abilities, roles, and evolving nature of work. Positions WA as cornerstone of HRM: crucial hiring/promotion/training decisions rest on WA output. Changing nature of jobs (IT-driven, cognitively loaded, blurred boundaries) demands more active, updated WA.

## Core Definitions

WA = systematic process gathering info about **activities** (tasks, duties, responsibilities) + **personal attributes** needed to meet org objectives, via analysis-synthesis-inference. Two outputs:
- **Job description** — activities, workflow, conditions
- **Job specification** — inferred worker traits (KSAOs) for successful performance

Info classified into three: job identification, job description, job specification. WA feeds both **predictor** and **criterion** development, and predictor-criterion alignment. Historical roots: scientific management (Taylor; Lillian & Frank Gilbreth); European selection pioneers (Lahy, Lipmann, Stern, Mira).

Cost-benefit: high-cognitive, full-time, long-term, upper-org jobs justify WA more than part-time/temporary low-demand jobs.

## Two Orientations

See [[Task-Oriented vs Worker-Oriented Analysis]].

- **Task-oriented** — duties/functions/tasks as basis; jobs independent of people; org structure stable. Methods: Time & Motion, **Functional Job Analysis** (Fine 1988; feeds O*NET), TI/CODAP, Work Performance Survey System, **Critical Incident Technique** (Flanagan 1954, for high-stakes decisions).
- **Worker-oriented** — individual characteristics required. Traits (stable: aptitude, personality) vs competencies (trainable behaviors leading to success — Boyatzis 1982, McClelland 1973). Growing consensus around competency model → research on worker traits dropped sharply. **Competence Modelling (CM)** ties to org goals/mission, applied across org, not linked to specific tasks (contrast [[Sanchez-Levine-2009-competency-modeling-vs-job-analysis|Sanchez & Levine (2009) — TJA vs CM]]).

## Sources of Information

Incumbents (most common), supervisors, peers, analysts. Blurred boundaries + teamwork → broaden informants (internal/external customers), add cross-functional coordination (Guder 2012), cross-supervision. Panels of experts add reliability vs single informant (Wernimont 1988).

## Methods (Pearlman & Sánchez 2010: 4 WA applications)

Predictor development (most used), criterion development, validity evidence development, validity evidence extension. WA procedures should be **iterative** (observe → describe → confront reality → reformulate).

**Qualitative**: observation (first-hand, in-depth, face validity — Martinko 1988), interview (initial unstructured to gather; verification structured on discrepancies — Gael 1988).

**Quantitative instruments**:
- **Job-task inventories** — task lists + Likert scales (importance, frequency, criticality, complexity)
- **F-JAS** (Fleishman Job Analysis Survey) — 73 items, 3 KSAO domains (cognitive, psychomotor, physical); ~20 SMEs for interrater reliability
- **PIC** (Hogan & Rybicki 1998) — personality-based, FFM/HPI, 48 items, 7 dims
- **PPRF** (Raymark, Schmit & Guion 1997) — 107 items, 12 personality dims mapped to FFM; interrater .66–.92
- **PAQ** (Position Analysis Questionnaire) — 195 items, worker-oriented, 6 domains, 32+13 factor dims; job component validity vs GATB
- **Databases**: O*NET (replaced DOT; 800+ occupations, 6 descriptor sets — 3 worker-oriented, 3 job-oriented); ISCO-08 (ILO)

## Reliability & Validity

Threats: rater KSAOs, informant position, demographics (Arvey et al. 1977). Morgeson & Campion (1997): **16 sources of inaccuracy** in two classes — social (social influence, group polarization, motivation loss, impression management, social desirability, demand) and cognitive (info overload, heuristics, categorization, carelessness, extraneous/inadequate info, order/contrast, halo, leniency/severity, method).

Key point: **consistency ≠ agreement**. Interrater r ≈ .90 can come from raters with very different means → cut-off score based on mean represents none of them. Reliability in WA should capture agreement, not just consistency. Uses omega (Revelle & Zinbarg 2009): cluster analysis of analysts should yield one cluster if agreement. Wernimont's (1988) **6 guarantees of content validity** in selection-oriented JA: job familiarity, expert panel, trained observers, test/attribute expertise, task/attribute taxonomy knowledge, content-valid task-attribute relationships.

## Person-Work Fit

Job fit = match of employee characteristics to job + environment. Kristof-Brown et al. (2005): "compatibility between individual and work environment when characteristics well matched." Complementary vs supplementary fit (Muchinsky & Monahan 1987). Interest shifted job fit → group/org fit → job-market fit (employability). Org fit tied to org culture, values.

## WA & Equal Employment Opportunity

WA is **essential to avoiding discrimination**. EEO demands documented linkage between KSAOs and job tasks (job-relatedness). Inaccurate WA/spec may exclude qualified people, incl. protected groups (Biddle & Kuthy 2012). EU parallel: directives on equal pay/treatment (75/117, 76/207, 2000/78, 2002/73, 2006/54); direct vs indirect discrimination. Selection decisions must rest on tools with criterion-oriented validity. Direct link to US [[EEOC-1978-uniform-guidelines-employee-selection|Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection (1978)]].

## Recruitment

Job description → basis of recruitment ad → affects applicant attraction. Hurtz & Wright (2012): 4-layer work-description framework (traditional desc → electronic DB → graphics/interactivity → multimedia) serving 3 recruitment goals (attract, maintain interest, persuade to accept). Link [[breaugh-2013-employee-recruitment]].

## Key Takeaways

1. WA is cornerstone of HRM; both task- and worker-orientation needed, choice depends on purpose (selection → worker orientation unavoidable).
2. IT + boundaryless work shift emphasis from job fit → org/market fit; continuous job crafting.
3. Reliability must mean **agreement**, not mere consistency — practical warning about cut-off scores.
4. Rigorous WA is the legal foundation for defensible, non-discriminatory selection.
