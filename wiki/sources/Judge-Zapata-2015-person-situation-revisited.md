# Judge & Zapata (2015) — The Person–Situation Debate Revisited

Nav: [[index]] | [[sources/_index]] | [[Personality at Work MOC]]

> [!abstract] One-line
> A meta-analytic **interactionist** test: Big Five validity for job performance is systematically **higher in weak situations** ([[Situation Strength]]) and **when the job activates the specific trait** ([[Trait Activation Theory]]) — reconciling "personality has low validity" critiques with the interactionist reality.

## The model

Integrates two previously-unjoined theories to explain *when* personality predicts performance, coding **125 studies** of occupationally homogeneous jobs with **O*NET** context ratings:

- **General interactionism — situation strength** (Mischel; Meyer, Dalal & Hermida 2010): strong situations (clarity, consistency, constraints, consequences) suppress *all* trait validities; weak situations amplify them. Operationalized as **outcomes** (impact/consequences/responsibility) and **process** (unstructured work, freedom, variety) composites.
- **Specific interactionism — trait activation** (Tett & Burnett 2003): each trait predicts better only in jobs whose cues **activate** that trait (e.g., extraversion in social/competitive jobs; openness in innovative jobs).

## Key findings

Baseline meta-analytic validities (corrected ρ): **Conscientiousness .21**, Emotional Stability .12, Extraversion .11, Agreeableness .06, Openness .04 — consistent with Barrick et al. (2001), confirming the sample is representative.

- **H1 supported (situation strength).** For **all five traits**, the **process** composite predicted validity — weak (unstructured, discretionary) situations produce significantly higher personality validities. The **outcomes** composite predicted validity for agreeableness and openness.
- **Trait activation (H2–H6) largely supported:**
  - Conscientiousness & openness → stronger validity in jobs affording **independence**.
  - Emotional stability → stronger in jobs requiring **social skills** and **dealing with unpleasant/angry people** (H3 supported).
  - Extraversion → stronger in **social-skill**, **competitive**, and **unpleasant-people** jobs (H4a/b/c all supported).
  - Agreeableness → **weaker** in **competitive** jobs (H5b supported).
  - Openness → stronger in **innovation** jobs (H6).
  - Non-support: conscientiousness × attention-to-detail (H2b) did not hold in the aggregate — but a facet-level follow-up showed **dutifulness/order** validity *is* predicted by attention-to-detail, while **achievement** runs the other way (a facet-masking effect).

## The horse race

Both frameworks explained unique variance, but a direct comparison suggested **trait activation may be relatively more important** than situation strength — though situation strength's contribution was "far from trivial." Overall the interactionist model received **general support**: the situation exerts both **general** and **trait-specific** effects on personality validity.
