# Approaches for Diverse, Equitable, and Inclusive Evaluation

Practice brief on DEI-informed program evaluation for school and nonprofit leaders.

## Core Argument

Inclusive evaluation produces better evidence AND improves org outcomes (morale, PR, funder relations). Diverse teams perform better; this applies to evaluation teams too.

## Three Goals + Strategies

### Goal 1: Engage Representative Stakeholders

**Strategies:**
- Schedule periodic reflection on systemic inequity drivers and personal biases (background reading, site visits, colleague consultation)
- Ask for and incorporate stakeholder feedback on design, enactment, and dissemination
- Encourage participation via accessible locations/times (coffee shops, community centers, outside business hours)

*Example: For anti-racist school environment program — read on racism/oppression/white privilege to understand systemic drivers before designing evaluation.*

### Goal 2: Ensure Quality and Validity of Data

**Strategies:**
- Collect data from individuals reflecting the full group of interest; sufficient subgroup N
- Examine data and analysis process for errors or biases; document decisions; quality control by team members; watch for mischievous responders and reference bias
- Assess differential impact across groups (disaggregated analysis)

*Example: If 80% of reading intervention data collected but ELL students largely missing → explicit limitation.*

### Goal 3: Communicate Across Differences

**Strategies:**
- Multiple modalities, formats, and languages (online + paper surveys, email + text outreach)
- Report findings disaggregated by key characteristics (location, race/ethnicity, age)
- Honor cultural norms for communication (e.g., vision wall using sticky notes for Native communities)

## Key Concepts

- **Reference bias**: survey responses influenced by social context (culture of "always room for improvement" → reluctance to rate highly)
- **Mischievous responders**: individuals who provide intentionally inaccurate data
- **Accessible evaluation design**: flexibility in timing, location, format to reach diverse participants
