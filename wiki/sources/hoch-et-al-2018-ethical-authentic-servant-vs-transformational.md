# Hoch et al. 2018 — Ethical, Authentic, Servant vs. Transformational Leadership

A construct-redundancy audit of the three "ethical/moral values–based" leadership forms that surged after the Enron-era corporate scandals. Directly answering [[derue-et-al-2011-trait-behavioral-leadership-integration|DeRue et al.'s (2011)]] call to test whether new leadership constructs actually add anything, Hoch and colleagues ask: do ethical, authentic, and servant leadership explain **incremental variance beyond transformational leadership**, or are they empirically redundant with it?

## Core Question

Since the 1980s, transformational leadership (TL) has dominated positive-leadership research. Critics argued TL lacks an explicit moral dimension (a "transformational" leader can be a Ken Lay), motivating three newer moral forms. But new leadership theories routinely get promoted without discriminant-validity checks — the [[Construct Redundancy (Leadership)|construct proliferation]] problem. Hoch et al. run four meta-analyses (authentic, ethical, servant, and the largest TL meta-analysis to date) and pit the newer forms against TL two ways: relative-weights analysis and hierarchical incremental-validity regression.

## Method

Four meta-analyses using Hunter–Schmidt random-effects procedures (corrected for measurement and sampling error, not range restriction). Study base: authentic 41 studies/45 samples; ethical 96/124; servant 41/49; transformational 155/179. Nine criteria shared across ≥2 emerging forms: job performance, OCB, deviance, engagement, job satisfaction, organizational commitment, affective commitment, trust, LMX.

## Key Findings

**Correlations with transformational leadership (the redundancy test):**

| Form | ρ with TL | Extant literature |
|---|---|---|
| Authentic | **.75** | small, heterogeneous (interpret with caution) |
| Ethical | **.70** | largest, most confident |
| Servant | **.52** | sparse but promising |

> [!warning] Redundancy verdict
> Authentic and ethical leadership correlate so strongly with TL (ρ = .75 and .70) that they show the **same pattern of relationships** with most outcomes and add **little incremental variance** — evidence of construct redundancy. Their utility is low "unless being used to explore very specific outcomes."

**Incremental variance beyond TL (ΔR², from Table 8):** authentic and ethical leadership add ~.01–.03 for most criteria; the notable exceptions are **affective/organizational commitment** and, for servant leadership, **LMX** and behavioral outcomes. Servant leadership adds the most incremental variance and across more outcomes.

**Relative-weights analysis (Table 7):** puts each form on equal footing rather than making the new form clear the incremental-validity bar. Average relative weights: servant **58%**, ethical **~55%**, authentic **>54%** — all edging out TL. TL dominated only job performance. The authors treat incremental validity as the stricter, more meaningful test.

## Verdict

> [!quote] Bottom line
> Authentic and ethical leadership are largely **redundant** with transformational leadership. **Servant leadership** is the most defensible stand-alone form — it targets outcomes (following, serving, LMX, extra-role behavior) that TL under-explains — though its evidence base is still thin.
