# Integrating Motivational, Social, and Contextual Work Design Features — Humphrey, Nahrgang & Morgeson (2007)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work Design (Concept)]] | [[Job Characteristics Model]] | [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]]

The definitive meta-analytic summary of the work design literature. Summarizes **259 studies / 219,625 participants / 6,333 corrected correlations**, tests the Job Characteristics Model, and extends it by integrating **social** and **work context** characteristics alongside the classic **motivational** ones. Functions as the empirical backbone behind the [[Work Design Questionnaire]].

## Motivation for the Study

Two "curious effects" of the motivational approach's success since the 1970s:
1. It narrowed attention to a small set of motivational features (skill variety, autonomy), neglecting the social environment and work context.
2. The sense that work design was "case closed" contributed to a **decline in work-design research** in top I/O and management journals after the late 1980s (their Figure 1).

The authors use meta-analysis to re-invigorate the field, replicate/extend Fried & Ferris (1987), and run the first formal meta-analytic test of Hackman & Oldham's mediation model.

## The Expanded Model (3 categories, 14 meta-analyzed characteristics)

> [!abstract] Work characteristics → mediators → outcomes
> Motivational (individual job components) + Social (interactional) + Work context (contextual) → critical psychological states → behavioral / attitudinal / role-perception / well-being outcomes.

**Motivational (task-focused):** autonomy (3 facets — work scheduling, work methods, decision-making), skill variety, task variety, task significance, task identity, feedback from the job.
**Motivational (knowledge-focused):** information processing, job complexity, specialization, problem solving.
**Social:** interdependence, feedback from others, social support, interaction outside the organization.
**Work context:** physical demands, work conditions, ergonomics.

(Specialization, problem solving, and ergonomics had <5 studies for job satisfaction, so were coded but not meta-analyzed — leaving 14 of 18 characteristics in the quantitative review.)

## Headline Results

- The 14 work characteristics explained, on average, **43% of variance** in the 19 outcomes.
- Motivational characteristics alone explained, e.g., **34% of variance in job satisfaction**, 25% in subjective performance, 24% in organizational commitment, 26% in role perceptions, 2% in turnover perceptions.

### Hypothesis 10 — the incremental-variance payoff (the paper's signature contribution)

Beyond motivational characteristics:

| Added category | Incremental variance explained |
|---|---|
| **Social** → subjective performance | +9% |
| **Social** → turnover intentions | +24% |
| **Social** → job satisfaction | +17% |
| **Social** → organizational commitment | +40% |
| **Social** → role perceptions | +18% |
| **Work context** → job satisfaction | +4% |
| **Work context** → stress | +16% |

This is the empirical case that social and contextual features are **non-redundant** with motivational ones — the justification for the broad WDQ taxonomy.

### Mediation test (Hypothesis 3 — first formal meta-analytic test)

- **Experienced meaningfulness = strongest, most encompassing mediator** of all five core characteristics (consistent with Johns, Xie & Fang 1992).
- **Experienced responsibility:** weaker support.
- **Knowledge of results:** little support.
- → The clean "each state mediates its own characteristic" version of JCM is not supported; meaningfulness carries most of the mediational load.

### Selected effect sizes (corrected ρ)

- Motivational chars ↔ job satisfaction mean ρ ≈ .41; growth satisfaction ≈ .55; internal work motivation ≈ .39.
- Autonomy ↔ objective performance ρ = .17 (only motivational char with non-zero CI); subjective performance mean ρ ≈ .18.
- Autonomy ↔ absenteeism ρ = −.15; feedback ↔ role ambiguity ρ = −.43; feedback ↔ anxiety ρ = −.32.
- Motivational characteristics intercorrelate (mean ρ = .46) more than with social (.27) or context (−.05) → three distinct sets.
- Feedback-from-job and feedback-from-others correlate ρ = .57 but share only ~⅓ variance → worth studying separately.

## Why This Source Matters

- Empirically validates broadening work design beyond the [[Job Characteristics Model]]'s five characteristics.
- Supplies the meta-analytic evidence repeatedly cited in [[Work Design (Concept)]], [[Work Design Questionnaire]], and [[Job Demands–Control–Support Model]].
- Establishes meaningfulness as the proximal mechanism — the bridge to [[Self-Determination Theory]] need-satisfaction accounts.

## Method Notes

Schmidt–Hunter psychometric meta-analysis; corrected for unreliability in predictor and criterion; composite correlations to avoid double-counting; OLS meta-analytic regression with harmonic-mean sample sizes; interrater agreement 93% (construct labels and correlations).
