# Bezrukova et al. (2016) — Diversity Training Meta-Analysis

*Psychological Bulletin, 142*(11), 1227.

**Note:** This is a seed page created from citations in [[cheng-et-al-2019-diversity-training-myths|Cheng et al. (2019)]]. Primary source not directly ingested.

## Key Findings (as reported by Cheng et al., 2019)

- Integrated 40+ years of diversity training research
- **Overall effect size**: *g* = .38
- **Largest effect on reactions**: *g* = .61
- Longer, temporally-spaced programs outperform shorter/one-shot programs; studies typically included ~24 hrs total
- Voluntary programs yield more positive reactions than mandatory
- Mandatory management attendance positively associated with perceived training effectiveness
- Groups with higher % women show stronger positive reactions
- Awareness + skills training over prolonged periods is most effective

## Significance

Primary meta-analytic anchor for Cheng et al.'s (2019) myth-debunking review. Provides the empirical backbone for claims about overall diversity training effectiveness and moderating conditions.
