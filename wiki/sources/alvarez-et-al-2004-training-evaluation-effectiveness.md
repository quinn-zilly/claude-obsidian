# Alvarez, Salas & Garofano (2004) — Integrated Model of Training Evaluation and Effectiveness

## Summary

This article reviews a decade (1993–2002) of empirical research across 73 studies to construct the **Integrated Model of Training Evaluation and Effectiveness (IMTEE)**. The authors synthesize four prior evaluation models and ten years of effectiveness research, proposing the first model explicitly linking effectiveness variables to all six evaluation measures, including posttraining attitudes.

> [!key-insight] Core Contribution
> The IMTEE is the first model (a) built from a strict inclusion criterion (≥3 studies finding significant relationships in the same direction), and (b) explicitly integrating posttraining attitudes with both training effectiveness variables and other evaluation measures.

---

## Conceptual Distinction: Evaluation vs. Effectiveness

- **Training evaluation** = measurement of outcomes (micro-level); *what happened*
- **Training effectiveness** = study of variables influencing outcomes (macro-level); *why it happened*
- Evaluation without effectiveness context yields weak, uninterpretable correlations

---

## Four Prior Evaluation Models (synthesized in IMTEE)

| Model | Key Claims |
|-------|-----------|
| Kirkpatrick (1976) | 4 levels: Reactions → Learning → Behavior → Results; hierarchical causal chain |
| Tannenbaum et al. (1993) | Added posttraining attitudes; split behavior into training performance vs. transfer performance; reactions not related to other outcomes |
| Holton (1996) | Reactions = mediator, not primary outcome; 3 targets: Learning, Transfer, Results; integrates effectiveness |
| Kraiger (2002) | 3 umbrella areas: Training Content & Design, Changes in Learners, Organizational Payoffs; reactions assess design only |

---

## The IMTEE — Four Levels

**Level 1: Needs Analysis** — feeds all three evaluation umbrella areas (not subjected to inclusion criteria but universally recognized)

**Level 2: Evaluation Umbrella Areas** (Kraiger 2002)
- Training Content & Design → assessed by Reactions
- Changes in Learners → assessed by Posttraining Attitudes, Cognitive Learning, Training Performance
- Organizational Payoffs → assessed by Transfer Performance, Results

**Level 3: Six Evaluation Measures** and their inter-relationships:
- Posttraining self-efficacy (the only posttraining attitude meeting criteria) ↔ Cognitive Learning (reciprocal), → Training Performance, → Transfer Performance
- Cognitive Learning → Training Performance, → Transfer Performance
- Training Performance → Transfer Performance
- Transfer Performance → Results
- Reactions (utility/relevance only) → Training Content & Design; not related to learning

**Level 4: Training Effectiveness Variables** — 10 variables that consistently influence outcomes:

| Variable | Category | Outcomes Influenced |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| Pretraining self-efficacy | Individual | Reactions, Posttraining SE, Cognitive Learning, Transfer |
| Pretraining motivation | Individual | Reactions, Cognitive Learning |
| Experience | Individual | Posttraining SE, Cognitive Learning, Training Performance |
| Cognitive ability | Individual | Cognitive Learning, Training Performance (mixed) |
| Age | Individual | Cognitive Learning (negative); not related to performance |
| Posttraining mastery orientation | Individual | Posttraining SE, Cognitive Learning, Transfer (near-criteria) |
| Learning principles (practice, feedback, behavioral modeling, identical elements) | Training | All changes-in-learners measures, Transfer |
| Posttraining maintenance interventions (goal-setting, visualization, mastery manipulation) | Training | Posttraining SE, Transfer |
| High difficulty | Training | Transfer |
| Positive transfer environment | Organizational | Transfer |

## Key Empirical Findings from Alliger et al. (1997) Meta-Analysis

- Affective reactions → learning: **r ≈ 0** (no relationship)
- Utility reactions → learning: **r = .26**; → transfer: **r = .18**
- Cognitive learning → training performance: **r = .18**
- Cognitive learning → transfer performance: **r = .11**
- Training performance → transfer: **r = .18**
- Hierarchical causal chain (Kirkpatrick) NOT supported; each level is not a sufficient predictor of the next

---

## Research Gaps Identified

1. **Reaction measures**: Need new methods tied to training content/design; utility and relevance dimensions are best starting points
2. **Motivation**: No unified construct; need a measure combining motivation-to-learn + motivation-to-transfer (Naquin & Holton, 2002 scale proposed)
3. **Organizational characteristics**: Only 4 studies; inconsistent measurement of climate; most important understudied category

---

## Limitations

- Criteria for inclusion (≥3 studies, consistent direction) excluded potentially important variables
- Cannot use SEM to verify direct relationships due to small N per variable
- Organizational characteristics pooled into one "positive transfer environment" variable
