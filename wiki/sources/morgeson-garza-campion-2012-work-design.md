# Work Design — Morgeson, Garza & Campion (2012)

Nav: [[index]] | [[Work Design (Concept)]] | [[Work Design Questionnaire]]

Comprehensive handbook chapter updating Morgeson & Campion (2003). Covers six major perspectives, the full WDQ taxonomy (18 characteristics), mediating mechanisms, outcomes meta-analysis, individual differences, redesign interventions, and future tensions.

## Integrated Framework

Contextual influences → Characteristics of Work → Mediating Mechanisms → Outcomes

Four characteristic domains: **(a) Task, (b) Knowledge, (c) Social, (d) Contextual**

## Major Perspectives

| Perspective | Core Focus |
|---|---|
| Scientific Management (Taylor 1911) | Specialization + simplification → efficiency |
| Motivator-Hygiene (Herzberg 1959) | Intrinsic motivators vs. hygiene factors |
| Job Characteristics Theory (Hackman & Oldham 1976) | 5 characteristics → 3 psych states → outcomes |
| Sociotechnical Systems (Tavistock/Trist 1951) | Joint optimization of social + technical systems |
| Social Information Processing (Salancik & Pfeffer 1978) | Social context constructs job perceptions |
| JD-CS / JD-R (Karasek 1979; Bakker & Demerouti 2007) | Demands × resources → strain/engagement |
| Interdisciplinary (Campion 1988) | Mechanistic / motivational / perceptual / biological |

## WDQ: 18 Work Characteristics

### Task Characteristics
- **Autonomy** (work scheduling, decision-making, work methods)
- **Task variety** — range of activities
- **Task significance** — impact on others; Grant (2008) renewed attention; related to overload (ρ=0.38)
- **Task identity** — completing whole piece; burnout ρ=−0.28
- **Feedback from job** — direct performance information; motivation ρ=0.42

### Knowledge Characteristics
- **Job complexity** — high-level challenging work; satisfaction ρ=0.37
- **Information processing** — monitoring/attending to data; overload ρ=0.58
- **Problem solving** — generating novel solutions; engagement ρ=0.28
- **Skill variety** — multiple skills vs. multiple tasks; motivation ρ=0.42
- **Specialization** — depth of knowledge/skill; related to efficiency

### Social Characteristics
- **Social support** — supervisor + coworker + friendship; org commitment ρ=0.82 (!)
- **Interdependence** — initiated (outflow) + received (inflow); performance ρ=0.18
- **Interaction outside org** — suppliers, customers; satisfaction ρ=0.X
- **Feedback from others** — supervisors/coworkers; role ambiguity ρ=−0.32

### Contextual Characteristics
- **Ergonomics** — posture/movement correctness
- **Physical demands** — job satisfaction ρ=−0.17
- **Work conditions** — environment; stress ρ=−0.42 (explains 16% incremental variance!)
- **Equipment use** — technology variety/complexity

## Measurement: Objective vs. Subjective

- Job-level convergence (self vs. supervisor): mean r=0.59 (autonomy/variety highest ~0.70)
- Individual-level convergence: mean r=0.22 (much lower)
- Common method variance: Glick et al. found job characteristics → 2% of job satisfaction variance after CMV removal (down from 67%!)
- **Conservative estimate:** 10–20% of variance attributable to objective job features

## Mediating Mechanisms

1. **Critical psychological states** (Hackman & Oldham): experienced meaningfulness (strongest), responsibility, knowledge of results
2. **Perceived social impact / social worth** (Grant): task significance → contact with beneficiaries → persistence (fundraiser studies)
3. **Psychological empowerment** (Spreitzer 1995): meaning, competence, self-determination, impact
4. **Role-breadth self-efficacy** (Parker): autonomy → RBSE → proactive behaviors
5. **Quick response / knowledge** (Wall & Jackson 1995): autonomy → system knowledge → faster problem response
6. **Regulatory focus** (Parker & Ohly 2008): enriched work → promotion focus → creativity; narrow work → prevention focus → safety compliance

## Outcomes (Humphrey et al. 2007 meta-analysis)

**Attitudinal:** autonomy → job satisfaction ρ=0.48; org commitment ρ=0.37; work motivation ρ=0.38
**Behavioral:** autonomy → objective performance ρ=0.17; absenteeism ρ=−0.15
**Cognitive:** feedback → role ambiguity ρ=−0.43; social chars → turnover intentions ρ=−0.17 to −0.34
**Well-being:** autonomy → stress ρ=−0.23; burnout ρ=−0.26 (avg for autonomy/SV/TS/TI)

## Individual Differences

- **GNS** (Growth Need Strength): most studied moderator; Tiegs et al. (1992, N=6,405 jobs) found virtually no support
- **Cognitive ability:** knowledge characteristics (complexity, info processing, problem solving) → cognitive ability cluster
- **Big Five:** conscientiousness moderates task significance → performance (Grant 2008a)
- **Psychological flexibility** (Bond et al. 2008): moderates control-enhancing redesign → mental health + absenteeism
- **Temporal focus** (Shipp et al. 2009): future focus → anticipated job characteristics → current satisfaction

## Redesign Tensions

Four tradeoff resolution approaches (Campion et al. 2005):
1. **Compromise** — choose target outcomes, design accordingly
2. **Level-separation** — different models at different org levels
3. **Sequential** — mechanistic first, then motivational
4. **Synthesis** — gains from one model without its costs (e.g., specialization ↑ efficiency while ↑ skill utilization)

Key finding: task simplicity and specialization are *negatively* related → specialization can be efficient AND motivating

## Future Directions (per chapter)

- Error criticality context (high stakes constrain positive reactions to autonomy)
- Cultural moderators of WDQ relationships
- Broader personality moderators beyond GNS
- Psychological flexibility + temporal focus
- Defining "good work" beyond narrow efficiency/satisfaction tradeoff
