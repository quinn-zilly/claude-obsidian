# Kozlowski & Klein (2000) — A Multilevel Approach to Theory and Research in Organizations: Contextual, Temporal, and Emergent Processes

> [!abstract] One-line
> The foundational synthesis of the **multilevel paradigm**: a book-length chapter that clarifies and extends principles for building, measuring, sampling, and analyzing multilevel models — and elaborates the neglected **compilation** (configural) forms of bottom-up emergence.

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

## Argument in brief

"Organizations are multilevel systems… but organizational science is not." The chapter aims to end the fragmentation between micro and macro by giving multilevel theorists a shared conceptual and methodological toolkit. Three sections: (1) roots in **general systems theory** and the climate literature; (2) principles for **theory building** and **model specification**; (3) an **extended theory of emergence** from isomorphic composition to discontinuous compilation.

## Theory-building: what / how / where / when / why

Build from the **endogenous construct** outward. Specify the linking mechanism — top-down ([[Cross-Level Models|direct or moderating]]) and/or bottom-up ([[Emergence (Organizational)|emergence]]). Fix the focal **units** (formal vs informal) and the **[[Bond Strength and Coupling|bond strength]]** joining them. Make **time** explicit — [[Entrainment|boundary/moderator effects, differential time scales, entrainment]]. Argue **why** and **why not**. See [[Multilevel Theory in Organizations]].

## Model specification

The recurring failure is **[[Levels of Analysis Fallacies|misalignment]]** of construct level, measurement, and analysis. The fix: type every unit-level construct as **[[Global, Shared, and Configural Unit Properties|global, shared, or configural]]** (which dictates measurement), justify aggregation against the composition assumption, sample many units nested in higher units ([[Multilevel Research Design and Sampling]]), and match the analytic technique (HLM, WABA, contextual regression) to the question. **Theory takes precedence** over any statistical rule.

## The extension: elaborating emergence

The core original contribution. Emergence spans a continuum from **[[Composition vs Compilation|composition]]** (isomorphic → shared properties) to **compilation** (discontinuous → configural properties). Isomorphic emergence, long the default, "may well be the exception rather than the rule." A single phenomenon (team performance) can emerge either way depending on interaction dynamics and workflow interdependence.
