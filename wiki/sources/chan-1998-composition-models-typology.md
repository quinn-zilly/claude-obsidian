# Chan (1998) — Functional Relations Among Constructs in the Same Content Domain at Different Levels of Analysis: A Typology of Composition Models

> [!abstract] One-line
> Proposes **five ideal-type composition models** — additive, direct consensus, referent-shift consensus, dispersion, process — that specify *how* a lower-level construct functionally relates to "the same" construct at a higher level. Explicit composition models are the fix for the proliferation of vaguely-defined multilevel constructs.

Nav: [[index]] | [[Multilevel Theory & Methods MOC]]

## The problem

Multilevel research spawns new constructs faster than it defines them, threatening the **principle of parsimony** — a "multitude of labels" with no incremental explanatory value. A composition model makes explicit the **transformation across levels**, providing conceptual precision, testable implications, and a basis for reconciling "apples-and-oranges" contradictions.

## The five forms

Using a running **organizational climate** example (see [[Chan's Typology of Composition Models]] for the full table):

- **Additive** — higher = sum/average, ignoring within-unit variance (Glick's organizational climate).
- **Direct consensus** — meaning is in within-group **consensus**; agreement ($r_{wg}$) justifies aggregation.
- **[[Referent-Shift Consensus]]** — shift the item referent (self → team) *before* consensus; yields a conceptually distinct construct. **Team efficacy ≠ mean self-efficacy.**
- **Dispersion** — the within-group **variance itself is the construct** (e.g., climate strength). Requires **absence of multimodality** (multimodal = subgroups, not mere low consensus); watch **beta/gamma change**.
- **Process** — higher-level process parameters are **analogues** of lower-level ones (e.g., team self-regulation, team proceduralization). Parameters analogous; interrelations **homologous**; least developed form.

## Contributions & uses

The typology (a) **organizes** existing constructs — locate "collective efficacy" as direct- vs referent-shift consensus; (b) **develops** new constructs — compose climate strength (dispersion) or static vs dynamic team adaptability (consensus vs process). Person-organization fit "misfit"/"congruence" constructs are worked examples of cross-level **target** constructs.
