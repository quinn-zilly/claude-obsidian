# Thompson & Bunderson (2019) — Research on Work as a Calling

Nav: [[index]] | [[log]] | [[Work as a Calling MOC]]

Narrative review of ~two decades of organizational-psychology / organizational-behavior (OP/OB) research on **[[Work as a Calling]]**. The authors screened 203 PsycINFO articles (1997–2018) down to **84 high-impact articles** (SSCI impact factor ≥ 3.0, or ≥ 20 SSCI citations); 72 were empirical, covering 53,047 subjects. They argue calling is one of the oldest constructs in the study of work (traced to the Protestant Reformation via Weber 1930) yet has had limited mainstream OP/OB impact, and diagnose **four questions** blocking its progress: definition, differentiation, generalizability, and relevance.

## Review findings

Calling was the independent variable in 60% of empirical studies, dependent in 15%, moderator/mediator in 13%; 86% were quantitative-deductive. The authors found **no standard definition** — 14 distinct formal definitions across 84 studies — and **23 different survey instruments** (16 used only once). The best-established finding is a **positive calling–satisfaction relationship** (work, life, career), reportedly mediated by meaningfulness and career commitment. Calling also relates positively to attachment (commitment, engagement, identification), lower withdrawal (burnout, turnover intentions), efficacy, occupational clarity, and self-reported well-being.

**"Dark side" evidence:** calling is a double-edged sword. Bunderson & Thompson (2009) found zookeepers' calling produced heightened moral duty, acceptance of sacrifice, and hypercriticism of the organization; Schabram & Maitlis (2017) documented sacrifice paths (two involving leaving the org); Dobrow & Tosti-Kharas (2012) found calling reduced accuracy of self-perceived talent and willingness to heed negative career advice; unmet callings produce regret and lower life satisfaction (Berg et al. 2010; Duffy et al. 2016b).

## The two definitional camps

The authors map the balkanized definitions onto two "ideal types" (see [[Neoclassical Calling]], [[Modern Calling]]):

- **Neoclassical / outside-in** — anchors calling in destiny, duty, and discovery (an external summons or societal obligation). Secularized descendant of the Reformation view.
- **Modern / inside-out** — anchors calling in internal passion, self-expression, and self-fulfillment; meaningful because significant to the individual, not necessarily to society.

## The integrative move: transcendent calling

Rather than a single neoclassical–modern continuum, the authors follow **Maslow (1967)** in treating **[[Inner and Outer Requiredness|inner requiredness]]** (passion, "I want to") and **outer requiredness** (duty, "I must") as two orthogonal dimensions. The 2×2 (Figure 3): low/low = job or career; high-outer/low-inner = neoclassical calling; high-inner/low-outer = modern calling; **high/high = [[Transcendent Calling]]** — the state Maslow called self-transcendence, marked by a sense of harmony, fit, or destiny.

> Proposed definition: "A transcendent calling is a conviction — often felt as a sense of destiny or fit — that a particular domain of work leverages one's particular gifts and consuming passions in service of a cause or purpose beyond self-interest."

The authors argue this integration resolves the other three questions: **differentiation** (calling as a strong form of person–job fit — matching inner requiredness to needs in the world — see [[Calling as Person-Job Fit]]); **generalizability** (six Asian studies suggest calling-as-inner/outer-match may be a universal human experience, not merely a Protestant legacy; extends to mainstream occupations and possibly the working poor with reframed requiredness); **relevance** (calls for non-self-report outcomes, especially **resilience** and **rigidity** as uniquely calling-sensitive DVs).

## Why it matters for this vault

The canonical taxonomy paper for the calling literature. Supplies the vault's organizing frame (inner × outer requiredness 2×2) and the critical-methods lens (definitional fragmentation, discriminant/incremental-validity gaps, self-report over-reliance) that connects calling to the vault's broader [[Criterion Problem|criterion]] and construct-validity spine. Pairs directly with [[Dik-Duffy-2009-Calling-and-Vocation|Dik & Duffy (2009)]], whose definition it classifies as the rare one straddling both requiredness dimensions.
