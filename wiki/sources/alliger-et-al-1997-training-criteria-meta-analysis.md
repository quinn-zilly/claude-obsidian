# Alliger et al. 1997 — Training Criteria Meta-Analysis

Nav: [[index]] | [[wiki/sources]]

## One-Line Summary

Meta-analysis of 34 studies (115 correlations) augmenting Kirkpatrick's model shows affective reactions are unrelated to learning or transfer, while **utility reactions** predict transfer better than learning measures.

## Core Argument

Kirkpatrick's four-level taxonomy (Reactions → Learning → Behavior → Results) is too coarse. The authors propose an **augmented framework** splitting:
- Reactions → *affective reactions* vs. *utility judgments*
- Learning → *immediate knowledge*, *knowledge retention*, *behavior/skill demonstration*
- Behavior → renamed **Transfer** (on-the-job only)
- Results → retained (too few studies to analyze)

Key findings upend the implicit assumption that reaction data proxies learning:
> "Affective reactions alone correlated on average just about zero with immediate learning."

## Methods

- Search: Journal of Applied Psychology, Personnel Psychology, Academy of Management Journal + 10 others, PsycLIT, ERIC, Dissertation Abstracts
- N = 34 studies, 115 correlations (vs. 12 studies / 26 correlations in Alliger & Janak 1989)
- Meta-analytic method: Hedges & Olkin (1985) — corrects for bias, uses inverse-variance weighting
- Moderator analysis: content overlap (close vs. divergent to training content)

## Key Results

| Criterion Pair | Mean r | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Affective reactions ↔ Immediate learning | ~.00 | Replicates Alliger & Janak 1989 |
| Utility reactions ↔ Immediate learning | Small positive | Better than affective |
| Utility reactions ↔ Transfer | Moderate | **Higher than learning → transfer** |
| Affective reactions ↔ Transfer | Weak | Lower than utility |
| Immediate learning ↔ Retention | Moderate positive | Expected convergence |
| Immediate learning ↔ Behavior/skill demo | Moderate positive | |
| Immediate learning ↔ Results | .52 (only 2 studies) | Tentative |

Within-level convergence (affective ↔ utility r = .34; immediate ↔ retained learning r = .35) suggests discriminant validity across levels but convergent validity within.

Moderator: close-close content overlap r = .16 vs. divergent-close r = .04 (p < .001), but confounded with criterion level.

## Reliabilities (diagonal)

All criterion types averaged α > .58; six of seven > .75. Immediate measures slightly more reliable than delayed. Learning measures somewhat lower (broader content).

## Implications

1. **Don't use affective reaction ("smile sheets") as learning surrogates** — no empirical basis.
2. **Use utility reactions** ("Will this help you do your job?") if you want any predictive power over transfer.
3. Collect utility and affective reactions **separately**; do not collapse into a single scale.
4. Consider collecting utility reactions *after* trainees return to job (better hindsight).
5. Content validity of criteria matters — mismatched content deflates correlations artifactually.

## Limitations

- Few Level 4 (Results) correlations — inherently hard to study, not reporting bias
- Still anchored to Kirkpatrick framework despite known shortcomings
- Many cells sparse (few studies)
