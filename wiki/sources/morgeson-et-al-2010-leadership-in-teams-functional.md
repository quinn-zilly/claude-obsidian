# Morgeson, DeRue & Karam (2010): Leadership in Teams — A Functional Approach

> [!abstract] TL;DR
> A team-centric, **functional** theory of leadership: leadership exists to *satisfy critical team needs*. Leadership can come from **[[Sources of Team Leadership (2x2)|four sources]]** (internal/external × formal/informal), and the authors taxonomize **[[15 Team Leadership Functions|15 leadership functions]]** across the transition and action phases of the team performance cycle.

## The core move: from leader-centric to team-centric

Traditional leadership models focus on leader–subordinate dyads and formal, appointed leaders. Morgeson et al. argue this yields an incomplete account of how leadership actually helps teams. Grounding leadership in **team performance cycles** ([[Teamwork Processes (Marks et al. Taxonomy)|Marks, Mathieu & Zaccaro 2001]]) reframes leadership as *whatever satisfies the needs that arise as teams cycle through transition and action phases*.

## Four sources of team leadership

See [[Sources of Team Leadership (2x2)]]. Two dimensions — **locus** (internal vs external to the team) and **formality** (formal vs informal) — cross to yield four sources:

| | Formal | Informal |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Formally appointed team leader | Shared / distributed leadership among members |
| **External** | Sponsor, coach, manager outside the team | Mentors, informal external influencers |

Any given team likely has *multiple* sources operating at once — a key link to [[Shared Leadership]] and [[Vertical vs Shared Leadership]].

## 15 leadership functions

See [[15 Team Leadership Functions]]. Derived taxonomically from **517 behavioral items** (76% → 100% inter-coder agreement), split by performance phase:

- **Transition phase (7):** Compose team · Define mission · Establish expectations & goals · Structure & plan · Train & develop team · Sensemaking · Provide feedback
- **Action phase (8):** Monitor team · Manage team boundaries · Challenge team · Perform team task · Solve problems · Provide resources · Encourage team self-management · Support social climate

Each source is rated as good (+) / better (++) / best (+++) positioned per function; empty cells mean a source is ill-suited. The **Team Leadership Questionnaire (TLQ)** operationalizes the functions.

> [!key-insight] Diagnostic value
> Because functions map to team needs and to sources, the framework is diagnostic: a team missing "manage boundaries" or "support social climate" points to *which source* should fill the gap — rather than assuming the formal leader must do everything.

> [!example] Vault fit
> Anchors the "team leadership" wing of the [[Leadership MOC]] and connects to the [[Teams & Org Behavior MOC]]. Complements [[Shared Leadership]] (Wang et al. 2014; Zhu et al. 2018) by supplying the *content* (what leadership does) to shared leadership's *structure* (who does it).
