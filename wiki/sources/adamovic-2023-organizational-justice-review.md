# Adamovic (2023) — Organizational Justice Research: A Review, Synthesis, and Research Agenda

> [!key-insight]
> A **semi-systematic review** (state-of-the-art, not exhaustive) organizing the whole field into four buckets: **(1) conceptualization** of justice, **(2) importance** (why employees care + effects), **(3) team-level** justice (climate + peer justice), and **(4) a research agenda** (cultural sensitivity, diversity, justice–conflict, changing nature of work). Reminds the field its true goal is **fighting injustice and creating just workplaces**, not accumulating frameworks.

## 1. Conceptualization

- **Definition** — org justice is *subjective/descriptive* (justice in the eye of the beholder), opposed to philosophy's *objective/prescriptive* justice (Aristotle onward). Goal: measure/analyze effects of subjective justice perceptions.
- **[[Four Justice Dimensions|Four dimensions]]** — distributive (Adams equity + Deutsch/Leventhal equality & need), procedural (Thibaut & Walker process control; Leventhal's 6 norms), interpersonal + informational (Bies & Moag → Greenberg 1993 split). Construct-validity worries have faded given support for the four-factor taxonomy (Colquitt 2001).
- **Overall justice** — high dimension intercorrelations (r ≈ .37–.74) motivate a global justice construct (Ambrose & Schminke 2009): often the more **proximal** predictor, and stabilizes effect sizes. Measured directly ("just") or as a higher-order latent variable. See [[Justice Measurement Taxonomy]].
- **[[Justice–Injustice Asymmetry|Justice vs. injustice]]** — are they opposite ends of one continuum, or distinct? Colquitt et al. (2015): injustice evokes more nuanced/differentiated reactions ("bad is stronger than good," negativity bias), yet justice sometimes more salient (self-esteem, reciprocation, OCB). Injustice construct-validity vs. incivility/bullying/abusive supervision needs work.

## 2. Importance — theoretical perspectives (why employees care)

- **Instrumental / control model** (Thibaut & Walker) — justice → favorable future outcomes; self-interest.
- **Relational** (Lind & Tyler group-value) — justice satisfies identity + belonging needs.
- **[[Social Exchange Theory|Social exchange]]** — dominant lens; classic (economic, short-term) vs. contemporary (relationship-quality, identity). Justice reciprocated via extra-role behavior + commitment.
- **Uncertainty** — [[Fairness Heuristic Theory|fairness heuristic theory]] + uncertainty management theory: justice reduces uncertainty; primacy effect forms a stable heuristic.
- **[[Deontic Justice Perspective|Deontic / moral]]** (Folger) — justice as an end in itself; violations trigger "moral outrage"/deontic anger.
- **[[Multifoci Justice & Target Similarity|Multifoci]]** — source-based justice + target similarity.
- **Integration** — no single theory explains all; motives complement rather than compete. Cropanzano et al.'s **multiple needs model** (control, belonging, self-esteem, meaningful existence) can subsume instrumental, relational, and moral perspectives (+ uncertainty).

### Effects on outcomes
Justice predicts positive affect, job satisfaction, commitment, OCB, task performance, trust, identification; reduces CWB, turnover intentions, revenge/retaliation. Concrete business stakes: layoff lawsuits ~20% (unjust) vs. ~1% (just) (Lind et al. 2000).

## 3. Team-level justice

- **[[Justice Climate]]** — "distinct unit-level cognition regarding shared justice perceptions of treatment by organizational authorities" (Whitman et al. 2012). Referent shifts from "how am I treated?" to "how is my *team* treated?". Justice **climate level** × **climate strength** (agreement / within-team variance); high strength amplifies level effects. First study Mossholder et al. (1998).
- **Peer justice** — coworkers/teammates as a *source* of justice (neglected). Salient in self-managed teams where members collectively assign tasks, workload, rewards.

## 4. Research agenda

- **Cultural sensitivity** — most measures US-based (Colquitt 2001); justice a universal need but its definition/interpretation differs across cultures; study the **moderating** role of cultural values, not just main effects.
- **Diversity** — justice as a mechanism to include dissimilar members; consistency + bias-suppression rules underpin equal opportunity; note majority (white male) backlash concerns.
- **Justice–conflict** — "all social conflicts are basically justice conflicts" (Montada); under-integrated because justice research is vertical (leader–member) and conflict research horizontal (peer). Combine Colquitt (2001) × Jehn (1995) taxonomies; conflict-management style matters.
- **Changing nature of work** — telework/virtual inclusion, gig workers (no security, algorithmic supervision), migrant/refugee integration. Adopt a justice lens; run intervention studies with organizations.
