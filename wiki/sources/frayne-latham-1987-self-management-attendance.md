# Frayne & Latham (1987) — Application of Social Learning Theory to Employee Self-Management of Attendance

Nav: [[index]] | [[Gary Latham]] | [[Self-Management Training]]

*Journal of Applied Psychology*, 72(3), 387–392.

## Core Argument

Self-management training — rooted in Kanfer's (1970) self-regulation framework and Bandura's (1977) social learning theory — causally increases employee job attendance by raising perceived self-efficacy to overcome personal and social obstacles to attendance.

## Study Design

**Sample:** 40 unionized state government employees (carpenters, electricians, painters) who had used ≥50% of sick leave. Mean age 44.33; 70% male; mean tenure 7.41 years.

**Design:** Random assignment — experimental (*n* = 20) vs. waitlist control (*n* = 20). Both groups exposed to identical organizational sanctions and sick-leave incentives throughout.

**Training:** 8 weekly 1-hr group sessions + 8 weekly 30-min one-on-one sessions.

| Week | Content |
|------|---------|
| 1 | Orientation: principles of self-management |
| 2 | Self-assessment: classify attendance obstacles (9 categories; top = family, supervisor conflict, transportation) |
| 3 | Goal setting: distal (attendance target) + proximal (specific enabling behaviors) |
| 4 | Self-monitoring: charts and diaries, daily feedback |
| 5 | Self-reinforcement: identify reinforcers and punishers, specify contingencies |
| 6 | Behavioral contract: written specification of goals, timeframe, consequences |
| 7 | Relapse prevention: anticipate, plan, cope |
| 8 | Review / one-on-one tailoring |

**Dependent variables:** Attendance (hours present/40 scheduled), sick leave (hours taken/week).

**Intervening variables:** Perceived self-efficacy (15-item scale, 0–100 strength rating), outcome expectancies (15-item, 100-point probability scale). Measured at pre, post, and 3-month follow-up.

**Learning measure:** 12-item situational test scored blind by two judges.

## Results

**Attendance:** Training group *M* = 458.4 hrs (*SD* = 32.7) vs. control *M* = 403.2 (*SD* = 22.8), *F*(1,38) = 5.52, *p* < .05, ω² = .10.

**Sick leave:** Training *M* = 69.6 hrs vs. control *M* = 83.1 hrs; only marginally significant (*p* < .10) — attributed to criterion contamination (self-report, low reliability = .42).

**Learning test:** Training *M* = 29.95 vs. control *M* = 16.4, *F*(1,38) = 6.30, *p* < .02, ω² = .10.

**Self-efficacy:** Significant Group × Time interaction, *F*(2,119) = 46.02, *p* < .05, ω² = .26. Training group *M* = 102.3 vs. control *M* = 81.1. SE post-training correlated *r* = .49 with subsequent attendance.

**Outcome expectancies:** No significant findings — ceiling effect (uniformly high pre-study).

**Skill application (3-month follow-up):** Goal-setting, chart-keeping, and self-reinforcement correlated *r* = .48, .45, .47 with attendance.

## Theoretical Interpretation

- Low attendees judge themselves **inefficacious** in overcoming obstacles (family, supervisor, transportation) — not primarily unmotivated by outcomes
- Training raises SE → employees focus effort on situational demands rather than imagining obstacles as insurmountable (Bandura 1982)
- High outcome expectancies alone insufficient if SE is low — replicates Barling & Abel (1983), Godding & Glasgow (1985)
- Outcome expectancies = external rewards/punishers; SLT also emphasizes **self-evaluative outcomes** (goal–performance discrepancy → self-dissatisfaction → effort)

## Practical Significance (authors' five points)

1. External validity: extends self-management from clinical/educational → unionized government workers
2. New DV: employee attendance not previously targeted with self-management
3. Efficient: 12 hrs total training; behavioral contract is the unique element
4. Stringent control: both groups had desire to improve and faced same sanctions — rules out demand/attention effects
5. Measurement: attendance superior to sick-leave records (reliability .90 vs. .38–.42; absenteeism records = categorization behavior of recorders)

## Limitations

- Voluntary/involuntary absenteeism not distinguished
- Self-report sick leave = contaminated criterion
- No interobserver reliability on absence observation (first-ever suggestion in literature)
- Single organization, single occupation cluster
