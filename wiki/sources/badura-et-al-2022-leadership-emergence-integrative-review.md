# Badura, Galvin & Lee (2022): Leadership Emergence — An Integrative Review

> [!abstract] TL;DR
> An integrative conceptual review of **270 primary studies** across seven decades and multiple disciplines. Puts forward a **[[Distal-Proximal Framework of Leader Emergence|distal–proximal framework]]** and, crucially, integrates **[[Formal vs Informal Leader Emergence|formal and informal]]** leader emergence — where past reviews treated emergence as an informal-only, leaderless-group phenomenon.

## What is leader emergence?

The authors define **leader emergence** as *the process through which an individual becomes influential to relevant others in a manner that involves the implicit or explicit granting of the leader role* (building on [[Leadership Identity Construction|DeRue & Ashford 2010]]). Emergence is inherently **relational** — it entails others acknowledging the prospective leader — and can occur without every follower granting leadership.

- **Informal emergence**: the informal granting of the leader role by prospective followers, with *no change* in formal status or authority.
- **Formal emergence**: a more visible granting from followers or superiors that results in a legitimately *designated* leader with formal status/authority (e.g., promotion to CEO).

Formal and informal emergence are interrelated and reciprocal. Treating emergence as informal-only (leaderless-group) is a longstanding scope error.

## Four review questions

1. What do we know about the **phenomenon** of emergence itself? *(Least mature — the social-interaction process is a relative black box.)*
2. What are the **antecedents**?
3. What **outcomes** are associated with emergence?
4. What are the **boundary conditions** (moderators)?

## The distal–proximal framework

See [[Distal-Proximal Framework of Leader Emergence]]. Distal antecedents (demographics, stable traits) predict emergence largely *through* proximal antecedents (behaviors, transient states). Studied antecedents cluster on a subset of traits — [[Five-Factor Model|Big Five]] (esp. extraversion), dominance, narcissism-adjacent traits, and self-monitoring recur — while **mediators and moderators of the *why/when* remain under-theorized**.

> [!key-insight] Maturity is lopsided
> The field is mature on a *narrow subset of antecedents* but nascent on (a) the emergence process itself, (b) mediating mechanisms, and (c) outcomes (whether emergents are actually effective or satisfied). [[Gender and Leadership|gender]] is a recurring moderator throughout.

## Method

Expansive multi-database search (PsycINFO + Web of Science), plus dissertations/theses and conference programs; screening yielded **270 empirical primary studies** of informal and formal emergence, double-coded for accuracy. Findings summarized across Tables 1–8.
