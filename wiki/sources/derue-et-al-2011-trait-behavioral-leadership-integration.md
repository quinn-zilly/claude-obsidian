# DeRue et al. 2011 — Trait and Behavioral Theories of Leadership: An Integration

Landmark *Personnel Psychology* meta-analysis that answers Avolio's (2007) complaint about leadership's chronic lack of integration. Rather than testing one trait or one behavior in isolation, DeRue and colleagues build a single [[Integrated Trait-Behavioral Model of Leadership|integrative model]] and run all predictors against one another across four effectiveness criteria — the first direct relative-validity contest between the trait paradigm and the behavior paradigm.

## Core Argument

Leadership research fragmented because scholars kept inventing new theories without comparing them to existing ones. Traits and behaviors had been meta-analyzed separately (Judge et al. 2002, 2004 for traits; Judge & Piccolo 2004 for behaviors) but never simultaneously, so no one knew whether their effects were independent or which mattered more. DeRue et al. organize predictors into three trait categories (demographics, task competence, interpersonal attributes) and four behavior categories (task-, relational-, change-oriented, and passive), then test relative importance via relative-weights analysis and a mediation model.

## Method

59 studies (13 existing meta-analyses + 46 primary studies) feed a pooled correlation matrix (Table 2). 90 of 143 bivariate cells came from prior meta-analyses; 53 were newly estimated. Relative importance computed with Johnson's epsilon (relative weights that sum to model R²). A completely-mediated path model tests whether behaviors carry trait effects to effectiveness.

## Key Findings

Four effectiveness criteria: **leader effectiveness, group performance, follower job satisfaction, satisfaction with leader.**

> [!quote] Headline
> Traits and behaviors combined explain **31%–92%** of variance in leadership effectiveness. Behaviors account for **62–94% of the total explained R²** in every criterion.

Combined trait+behavior total R²: leader effectiveness **.58**, group performance **.31**, follower job satisfaction **.56**, satisfaction with leader **.92**. Behaviors' share of explained variance: 74.5% (leader effectiveness), 62.4% (group performance), 93.7% (job satisfaction), 85.0% (satisfaction with leader).

**Traits (relative validity by criterion):**

- Task-competence traits dominate task-performance criteria — Conscientiousness accounts for ~77.7% of trait-explained R² in group performance (H1 supported).
- Interpersonal traits dominate affective criteria — Agreeableness/Extraversion account for ~81.6% of trait-explained R² in satisfaction with leader (H2 supported).
- For overall leader effectiveness, task-competence + interpersonal traits explain 98.6% of trait R² (H3 supported).

**Behaviors:**

- Transformational leadership is the strongest behavioral predictor across almost all criteria (task-performance %R² up to ~19.6; leader effectiveness path β = **.497** in the mediated model).
- Contingent reward is the dominant predictor of follower job satisfaction (38.7% of R²).
- Consideration dominates satisfaction with leader (41.7% of R²).
- Passive leadership (laissez-faire, MBE-passive) is negatively related and matters most where the leader is the target of evaluation (H7 supported).

**Behaviors beat traits (H8 supported):** behaviors predict more variance than traits for every criterion.

**Mediation (H9–H10 partial support):** behaviors mediate a substantial part of trait→effectiveness. Intelligence and Conscientiousness effects run through initiating structure + transformational; Extraversion/Agreeableness run through consideration (and, for Extraversion, transformational). H11 (attribution/identification mediators) untestable — insufficient data.

## Why Behaviors Out-Predict Traits

Two theoretical reasons: (1) behaviors are more *proximal* to the act of leadership than distal traits; (2) per [[Trait Activation Theory]], traits only manifest as behavior when the situation makes them relevant, so trait→outcome links are attenuated by situational variability, whereas observed behavior has already manifested.

## Significance

Provides the integrative backbone later work builds on — [[hoch-et-al-2018-ethical-authentic-servant-vs-transformational|Hoch et al. (2018)]] explicitly answers DeRue et al.'s call for integration and construct-redundancy testing when evaluating newer leadership forms.
