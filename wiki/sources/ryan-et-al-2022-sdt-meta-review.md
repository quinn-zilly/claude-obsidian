# Ryan et al. 2022 — SDT Meta-Review

Meta-review of 60+ meta-analyses (2000–2022) evaluating SDT across all six mini-theories. *Psychological Bulletin* 148(11-12), 813–842.

## Scope

60 meta-analyses organized by SDT mini-theory. Domains: education, health, work, sport/exercise, parenting, relationships. Focuses on what is robustly established and where gaps remain.

## CET Findings

- **Choice → intrinsic motivation**: r = .15 (Patall et al. 2008; 41 studies)
- **Positive feedback → intrinsic motivation**: positive vs. negative, r = −.18 for negative (Fong et al. 2019)
- **Performance-contingent rewards undermine IM**: r = −.14 (Deci, Koestner & Ryan 1999; 128 studies)
- **Intrinsic motivation → performance**: r = .21, incremental over ability (Cerasoli et al. 2014)
- Reward taxonomy confirmed: task-noncontingent < task-contingent < completion-contingent < performance-contingent for undermining
- Verbal rewards (positive feedback) enhance IM when informationally delivered

## OIT Findings

- **Simplex structure confirmed**: Howard et al. 2017 (N > 200,000); regulations correlate per predicted order
- Integrated and identified not reliably differentiated psychometrically — measurement challenge
- **Autonomy support (PE) → intrinsic motivation**: r = .54 (Lochbaum et al. 2016)
- Autonomous motivation mediates most SDT intervention effects

## COT Findings (Causality Orientations)

- **Autonomy orientation → autonomous motivation**: confirmed across meta-analyses
- Priming autonomy vs. controlled orientations: evidentially sound (Murphy & Steel 2021)
- COT predicts regulatory style above and beyond personality traits
- See: [[Causality Orientations Theory]]

## BPNT Findings (Basic Needs)

- **Competence satisfaction → positive affect**: r = .45 (Stanley et al. 2021)
- **Autonomy → subjective well-being**: r = .46 cross-cultural (Yu et al. 2018)
- **Relatedness → well-being**: consistent across cultures
- Need satisfaction vs. frustration = distinct constructs (not merely opposites); frustration predicts ill-being uniquely
- **Need frustration NOT yet meta-analyzed** — major gap identified
- See: [[Basic Psychological Needs Theory]], [[Need Frustration (SDT)]]

## GCT Findings (Goal Contents)

- **Intrinsic aspirations → well-being**: r = .24 (Bradshaw et al. 2022)
- **Extrinsic relative centrality → well-being**: r = −.22 (Bradshaw et al. 2022)
- **Mediation by need satisfaction**: confirmed (Dittmar et al. 2014)
- Autonomy ≠ independence: moderation analyses confirm cultural universality of need for autonomy
- See: [[Goal Contents Theory]]

## RMT Findings (Relationships Motivation)

- Newest mini-theory; **zero meta-analyses to date** — major empirical gap
- Core predictions: high-quality relationships characterized by autonomy support, need satisfaction
- See: [[Relationships Motivation Theory]]

## Domain-Specific Findings

### Work
- Intrinsic motivation → job satisfaction: r = .57; engagement: r = .67 (Van den Broeck et al. 2021)
- Autonomy support → autonomous work motivation: r = .54

### Health
- SDT-based interventions: avg r = .11 (Sheeran et al. 2020; across 29 meta-analyses)
- Autonomous motivation → health behavior change: consistent small-to-medium effects

### Sport / Exercise
- Coach autonomy support → athlete well-being: r = .41
- Athlete burnout: autonomy frustration r = −.50, competence frustration r = −.52 (Li et al. 2013)

### Parenting
- Parental autonomy support → child well-being: r = .28 (Bradshaw et al. 2021)
- Key finding: autonomy support ≠ permissiveness or lack of structure

### Education
- Autonomous motivation → engagement, performance, persistence — consistent; see [[ryan-deci-2000-sdt]] for foundational studies

## Key Gaps Identified

1. Need frustration lacks meta-analytic coverage
2. RMT has zero meta-analyses
3. Integrated vs. identified regulation not reliably differentiated by current measures
4. Cross-cultural tests underrepresent Global South
5. Longitudinal designs underrepresented relative to cross-sectional

## Conclusion

SDT's core propositions — need satisfaction → well-being, autonomy support → autonomous motivation, intrinsic motivation → quality outcomes — are robustly supported across domains. Effect sizes typically small-to-medium (r = .15–.54) but consistent and theoretically coherent.
