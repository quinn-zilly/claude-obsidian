# Transactive Memory Systems 1985–2010: An Integrative Framework

## Overview

Comprehensive literature review of transactive memory systems (TMS) research spanning 1985–2010. Integrates 76 papers across management, psychology, and communication disciplines. Presents antecedent-mediator-outcome framework for understanding how TMS develops, manifests, and impacts team performance.

## Key Contributions

### Core Definition
TMS: shared system groups develop for encoding, storing, and retrieving knowledge about different domains. Has two components:
- **Structural**: organized knowledge in individual memories + links to collective network
- **Transactive processes**: directory updating, information allocation, retrieval coordination

### Dimensions of TMS
Three behavioral indicators found across contexts:
- **Memory Differentiation**: members specialize in different knowledge domains
- **Task Credibility**: mutual trust in expertise of group members
- **Task Coordination**: ability to work together smoothly and coordinate activities

### Antecedents Framework (Three Levels)

**Member/Team Composition Inputs:**
- Demographics (gender, age, ethnicity)
- Technical competence
- Personality traits (assertiveness)
- Member diversity

**Team-Level Inputs:**
- Task/reward interdependence
- Group training & shared experience
- Prior relationships & familiarity
- Communication (face-to-face > CMC in early stages)
- Imposed knowledge structures (role assignment)
- Team environment (stress, time pressure)

**Organizational-Level Inputs:**
- Acute stress
- Geographic distribution

### Consequences

**Team Performance Behaviors:**
- Team learning
- Creativity
- Team reflectivity

**Team Performance Outcomes:**
- Effectiveness (work quality, meeting goals)
- Efficiency (time to completion, cost)
- Recall accuracy
- Decision quality

**Member Affective Outcomes:**
- Job satisfaction
- Team identification
- Commitment

### Moderators of TMS-Performance Link
- Group size (smaller groups show stronger TMS effects)
- Task complexity (more complex tasks benefit more from TMS)
- Task type & task change
- Environmental turbulence (mixed effects)
- Relationship conflict (negatively moderates)
- Trust (unclear moderating role)
- Geographic distribution in virtual teams
- Group membership change

## Measurement Issues

Five established measurement approaches identified:

**For Dyads:**
- Hollingshead (1998a, 1998b) and Wegner et al. (1991) questionnaires

**For Small Groups:**
- Liang et al. (1995) - behavioral coding + direct expertise measures
- Austin (2003) - four components (knowledge stock, consensus, specialization, accuracy)
- Lewis (2003) - 15-item scale (specialization, credibility, coordination) *most widely adopted*
- Faraj & Sproull (2000) - expertise location & expertise utilization

**Recommendation:** Use standard measures (especially Lewis 2003) to accumulate knowledge. Compare new measures against standards.

## TMS as Multidimensional Construct

Dimensions not always highly correlated. In mature, established teams, correlations vary from zero to moderate. Dimensions may follow different evolutionary paths. Different dimensions may have different performance impacts.

**Key finding:** Need both specialization AND coordination for functional TMS. Specialization = structural component. Coordination = transactive processes.

**Role of trust:** Relationship between trust and TMS unclear. Trust has been conceptualized as antecedent, dimension, and moderator. Task credibility (knowledge-based trust) most closely related to TMS.

## Organizational-Level TMS

**Challenges to generalization:**
- Organizations larger than work groups
- Multiple subgroups with limited cross-boundary communication
- Geographic distribution
- Tacit knowledge harder to access across distances

**Technological approaches:**
- Expertise directories & repositories
- Web 2.0 technologies (wikis, blogs, social networks)
- Email/messaging mining for expertise inference
- Small-world networks for connecting expertise

**Interpersonal approaches:**
- Locating specific person with needed knowledge
- Member rotation across groups
- Accessibility & trust in experts

## Four Key Research Directions

### 1. Consistency in Measurement
- Adopt standard scales across contexts
- Report inter-correlations of dimensions
- Allow for separate and interactive effects of dimensions

### 2. Multidimensional Nature
- Clarify relationships among dimensions
- Understand evolutionary paths
- Investigate when each dimension matters most

### 3. Organizational-Level TMS
- How organizational TMS functions with technology & social networks
- Relationship between group-level and org-level TMS
- When to prioritize each level

### 4. Experience & New Ventures
- TMS as mechanism explaining benefits of experience in established firms
- Task-Expertise-Person links as seeds for entrepreneurial ventures
- Performance benefits of prior team experience in new firms

## Notable Findings by Context

### Communication & Virtuality
- Face-to-face communication crucial for TMS development & utilization
- Early-stage teams: phone/F2F work; email/CMC ineffective
- Mature teams: CMC becomes viable if properly used
- Virtual teams: totally dispersed teams develop stronger TMS than subgrouped teams

### Membership Change
- Total turnover completely disables team TMS
- Partial turnover: structure stable but processes less efficient
- Similar expertise in replacement member minimizes disruption

### Task Transfer
- TMS generalizes to similar tasks
- Members with prior TMS show better understanding of underlying principles
- Most beneficial when expertise stability high across task transfer

### Stress Effects
- Acute stress: negatively affects all TMS processes
- Challenge stressors (time pressure): improve TMS & performance
- Hindrance stressors (role ambiguity): degrade TMS & performance

## Critical Gaps Identified

- Small number of personality/trait studies
- Limited interdependence research
- Minimal understanding of motivational factors in TMS development
- Insufficient research on social networks & opportunity to develop TMS
- Forgetting & decay of TMS poorly understood
- Dynamic evolution of TMS under-studied
- TMS in virtual teams under-researched
- TMS in entrepreneurial ventures largely unexplored
- Organizational-level TMS very limited
