# Tett et al. 2021 — Trait Activation Theory Review

Nav: [[index]] | [[sources/_index]]

## Core Argument

Trait activation theory (TAT) posits personality traits are *latent potentials* expressed as valued work behavior in response to **trait-relevant situational cues**. Traits ≠ behavior by default; they require activation. Situations matter — not just as main effects on behavior but as moderators that expose *individual differences*. TAT targets situational specificity of trait–performance linkages.

## Review Scope

- 99 primary/secondary sources citing TAT, 2011–2019 (extending Tett et al. 2013)
- 75 empirical studies; 60 reported 262 TAT-based moderator effects
- **Overall: 60% significance rate** — far above 5% chance baseline
- Builds on Tett & Guterman (2000) and Tett & Burnett (2003) foundational articles

## Key TAT Propositions

### Situation Trait Relevance
A situation is relevant to a trait to the degree it offers *opportunity for that trait to be expressed*. Trait-relevant situations both (a) have main effects on behavior and (b) expose individual differences on that trait. Without trait-relevant situations, the trait cannot be observed or predict outcomes.

### Six Functional Cue Types
See [[Situational Cue Types (TAT)]] for full definitions.

| Cue Type | Function |
|---|---|
| Demand | Activates trait; positive trait–outcome relationship |
| Distracter | Activates trait; negative trait–outcome relationship |
| Constraint | Weakens trait–outcome relationship |
| Releaser | Counters a constraint; intermittent activator |
| Facilitator | Multiplies salience/potency of existing cues |
| Discretionary | Untethered to valued outcomes; intrinsic motivation |

**Demands = 60% of tested effects** (most common). Distracters = 11%, constraints = 13%, releasers = 1% (rarely studied), facilitators = 8%, discretionary = 8%.

### Three Operational Levels
- **Task level** (60% of tested effects): day-to-day demands, job description features
- **Social level** (30%): peer/supervisor behaviors, norms, team culture
- **Organization level** (6%): culture, policies, reward systems
- **National culture** (4%): extended by Choi et al. 2015 and Holtschlag et al. 2013

### Behavior/Performance Distinction
Trait activation → behavior. Behavior → performance only when judged against work demands by observers. Separating these explains **bidirectionality**: same trait can correlate positively or negatively with performance depending on whether its expression meets or undermines demands. Ignoring bidirectionality → meta-analytic underestimation of personality validity.

### Trait-Based Motivation (4 Sources)
1. **Intrinsic need satisfaction** — trait expression is pleasurable (Murray 1938; Smillie 2013 on extraversion)
2. **Knowledge of performance results** — salient to achievement-oriented workers
3. **Extrinsic reinforcement** — salary, praise for positive; demotion for negative expressions
4. **Differential reward preference** — extrinsic motivation elevated when reward type aligns with trait

## TAT and Personality Dynamics

Tett & Fisher (2020) identify five lines of personality dynamics research; paper applies TAT's five key issues (cross-situational consistency/variability, behavior/performance distinction, interpersonal compatibility, dynamic interaction, need-based motivation) as a 5×5 matrix. See [[Personality Dynamics (TAT)]].

Five dynamics domains:
1. **Dispositional change** (self-monitoring, adaptability, proactive personality)
2. **Person–situation interactions** (situational specificity over time)
3. **Personality states** (Fleeson's whole-trait theory; states = proximate mediators of trait effects)
4. **Personality and career development** (promotion, turnover, Peter principle, RIASEC)
5. **Self-regulation** (SR) models of motivation

Key TAT contribution to SR: TAT provides *motivational rationale* for trait engagement (need satisfaction) that SR models lack — they invoke traits as moderators but not as needs. SR motivation (goal–performance discrepancy) and TAT motivation (need satisfaction) are compatible; both reduce to affect as fundamental motivator.

## Five Priority Research Directions

1. **Personality-oriented work analysis** — use trait-specific job analysis instruments (Raymark et al. 1997); most studies still use O*NET/job titles
2. **Longitudinal studies** — bidirectional person–situation dynamics over minutes to careers; cue density, stability, modulation
3. **Within-job bidirectionality** — same trait can be + for one performance dimension, − for another within a job
4. **Trait activation in teams** — 4 TAT opportunities: team orientation, task type, member roles, mutual activation (chemistry)
5. **PE fit and situational relevance** — situational relevance as unifying principle across KSA, value, and trait fit variants
