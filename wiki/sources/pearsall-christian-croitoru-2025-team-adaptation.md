# Pearsall, Christian & Croitoru (2025) — Team Adaptation Stimulus-Based Model

**Source:** *Journal of Applied Psychology*, 110(4), 459–480. Published Online First October 7, 2024. Designated a **Monograph** by the Action Editor.

## Core Argument

Prior team adaptation research has been **behavior-driven** and **response-focused**, collapsing across diverse adaptive demands and relying on input–process–outcome frameworks (Hackman, 1987; McGrath, 1984). This has caused three critical oversights: (1) piecemeal, context-specific findings that don't generalize across stimulus types; (2) neglect of earlier phases (detection, assessment) and the role of uncertainty; (3) treating team attributes as antecedents rather than phase-specific moderators.

The paper proposes a **stimulus-based phase model** where the adaptation process begins with the nature of the stimulus itself, not the team's response.

## The 6-Phase Model

| Phase | Name | Key Focus |
|-------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Stimulus Detection | Noticing that an adaptive demand exists |
| 2 | Urgency Assessment | Prioritizing stimuli by **proximity** × **magnitude** |
| 3 | Duration Assessment | Temporary vs. sustained — shapes response type |
| 4 | Adaptive Response & Performance | Duration-matched behavioral response |
| 5 | Reflexive Learning | Recursive updating during the performance cycle |
| 6 | Knowledge & Skill Acquisition | Post-episode learning → future adaptability |

Phases 2–5 form a **recursive cycle**; Phase 6 feeds back to Phase 1 for future episodes.

## Key Definitions

- **Adaptive stimulus**: "a demand, problem, disruption, threat, or opportunity that requires the team to purposefully and collectively change its behaviors and/or processes to either mitigate, overcome, or benefit from the situation"
- **Urgency**: perceived proximity × magnitude → overall prioritization level
- **Duration**: expectation of whether situation will revert vs. become the "new normal" (continuous, not binary)
- **Reflexive learning**: "within episode" cycle of reflection, feedback-seeking, updating

## Adaptive Response: Duration-Contingent

**More temporary stimuli** → closed-loop communication, reallocation of assets, implicit coordination, mental model updating, bricolage

**More sustained stimuli** → plan formulation, role discussion, information sharing, action identification, team reflection

## Contextual Factors (Phase-Specific Moderators)

**Team Attributes & Structure:**
- Regulatory focus (promotion vs. prevention) — strongest in Phases 1–2
- Goal orientation (learning vs. performance) — strongest in Phases 5–6
- Cognitive ability, conscientiousness, emotional stability
- Psychological safety, collective efficacy
- Team maturity; workload distribution; structural coupling

**Leadership & Training:**
- Directive leadership effective for high-urgency stimuli (Phases 2–4)
- Empowering leadership effective pre-detection and in learning phases
- Shared leadership: inconclusive evidence for adaptation
- Adaptive training, perturbation training, concept mapping cross-training all show benefits

## Methodological Agenda

Five priority issues: (1) measuring successful adaptation (behaviors vs. outcomes); (2) temporal focus and duration assessment; (3) detecting stimuli in the field; (4) team maturity/development; (5) baseline performance confounds.

## Key Insight

The stimulus-based perspective shifts analytical focus from "what did teams do?" to "what did they face, how did they categorize it, and did the response fit?" This allows stimulus-specific actions to be isolated and compared across varied demands.
