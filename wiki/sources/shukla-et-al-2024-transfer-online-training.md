# Shukla et al. (2024) — Transfer of Online Training: Taxonomy

> [!key-insight]
> First systematic taxonomy of factors affecting transfer specifically in the **online/digital** training medium. Identifies a novel category — **computer (digital) literacy and attitudes** — absent from all offline training transfer frameworks.

## Core Contribution

Systematic literature review (PRISMA; 33 articles from 502 initial hits; April 2001–May 2021) proposing a four-category taxonomy of online training transfer factors. Fills gap: offline transfer taxonomies (Baldwin & Ford, 1988; Burke & Hutchins, 2008) don't address online-specific factors.

## The Taxonomy (4 Categories, ~42 Factors)

### 1. Computer (Digital) Literacy & Attitudes *(online-unique)*
Factors specific to digital medium — absent from offline frameworks:
- Computer anxiety, computer confidence, computer liking
- Computer self-efficacy, computer literacy
- Technology readiness / knowledge of digital devices

**Implication**: Before online training, organizations should assess and address these. Sessions to reduce computer anxiety and build digital confidence may be prerequisite.

### 2. Learner Characteristics *(~14 factors; most-studied category)*
Stable learner attributes affecting online transfer:
- Self-regulation, self-efficacy, motivation to transfer, motivation to learn
- Internal values, participation/engagement in training
- Trainee reactions, learner control, enjoyment
- Satisfaction, cognitive skills, perceived usefulness of online training, previous experience

### 3. Online Learning Design & Planning
Instructional design variables:
- Training content usefulness, learning strategy and design
- Course learnability, ease of learning, learning needs analysis
- Goal setting, learning flow, teaching methodology/pedagogy
- Teaching models, evaluations built into design

### 4. Learning Environment & Organizational Support
Context/climate variables (parallels offline literature):
- Technology infrastructure, organizational culture
- Organizational support, supervisor support, peer support
- Interaction opportunities (learner–trainer, learner–learner)
- Opportunity to use, organizational climate, organizational policies
- Organizational readiness, learning environment

## Sample & Method

- Databases: Google Scholar, Web of Science, EBSCO
- Keywords: "transfer of e-learning", "transfer of online learning", "transfer of computer-based learning", "transfer of mobile learning", "transfer of online education"
- PRISMA selection: 502 → 91 (full text) → 33 included
- Study types: 18 quantitative, 6 qualitative, 8 literature reviews, 1 mixed-method
- Time coverage: 2001–2021; peak activity 2006–2010 and 2016–2021

## Contextual Analysis (RQ2)

**By training purpose (KSA framework):**
- Skill-focused studies (n=5): computer literacy/attitude factors prominent
- Knowledge-focused studies (n=8): learner characteristics (motivation, self-efficacy) prominent
- **Gap**: majority of studies don't specify training purpose — no study examined context as moderator

**By participant profile:**
- 11 industry studies (oil/gas, banking, electronics)
- 13 education/university studies
- Computer literacy/attitude category: more studied in industry; other categories studied across both

## Comparison with Offline Transfer Taxonomies

| Feature | Offline (Baldwin & Ford 1988; Burke & Hutchins 2008) | Online (Shukla et al. 2024) |
|---|---|---|
| Learner characteristics | Yes | Yes (expanded) |
| Training design | Yes | Yes (adapted for digital) |
| Work environment/org support | Yes | Yes |
| Computer/digital literacy | **No** | **Yes — novel** |

Common factors (both online + offline): self-efficacy, peer support, learning strategy, training usefulness, organizational support.

## Research Gaps Identified

1. Computer literacy/attitudes underresearched relative to its importance as online-unique category
2. No studies examine context (purpose, industry) as *moderator* of factor efficacy
3. Cross-sectional designs dominate — causal relationships untested
4. Digital medium sophistication (mobile vs. VR vs. browser LMS) treated uniformly
5. Measurement: no consensus definition or validated scale for "transfer of online training"
