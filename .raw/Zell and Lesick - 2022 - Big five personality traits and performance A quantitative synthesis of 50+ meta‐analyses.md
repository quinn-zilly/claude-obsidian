Results: Whereas conscientiousness yielded the strongest effect (ρ = 0.19), the
remaining Big Five traits yielded comparable effects (ρ = 0.10, 0.10, −0.12, and
0.13 for extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism, and openness). These asso-
ciations varied dramatically by performance category. Whereas conscientious-
ness was more strongly associated with academic than job performance (0.28 vs
0.20), extraversion (−0.01 vs 0.14) and neuroticism (−0.03 vs −0.15) were less
strongly associated with academic performance. Finally, associations of person-
ality with specific performance outcomes largely replicated across independent
meta- analyses.
Conclusions: Our comprehensive synthesis demonstrates that Big Five traits
have robust associations with performance and documents how these associa-
tions fluctuate across personality and performance dimensions.
