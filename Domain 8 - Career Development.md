# Q1 — Supporting protean and boundaryless employees (Lumen Digital)

> Prompt: A ~800-person marketing agency keeps losing mid-career creatives who say there is "nowhere to go" and move laterally between agencies. Give five specific, evidence-based ways to support protean/boundaryless employees, justify each with theory and research, and address how recommendations differ for women, early-career creatives, and underrepresented minorities.

## Contemporary career orientations

- A **[[Protean Career Orientation|protean career]]** is a self-directed, values-driven orientation in which the person, not the organization, manages the career in pursuit of psychological success ([[hall-yip-doiron-2018-protean-careers-at-work|Hall et al., 2018]]). A **[[Boundaryless Career|boundaryless career]]** is pursued across organizational boundaries rather than up one firm's ladder; its psychological mobility (openness to new work) is distinct from physical mobility (actually changing employers), which behaves as a separate construct ([[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal, 2019]]).
- Meta-analytically these orientations collapse into a single **[[Proactive Career Orientation|proactive career orientation]]** that strongly predicts [[Career Self-Management Behavior|career self-management]] and subjective (career-satisfaction) success but only weakly predicts [[Objective Career Success|objective success]] such as salary and promotions ([[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal, 2019]]; [[ng-eby-sorensen-feldman-2005-career-success-meta-analysis|Ng et al., 2005]]).
- Implications for Lumen: (a) mid-career creatives value growth, meaning, and mobility more than rungs, so a linear ladder is the wrong retention lever; (b) proactive orientation is a **malleable characteristic adaptation** the firm can develop, not just select for ([[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal, 2019]]).

## Five evidence-based ways to support these employees

1. **Replace the ladder with a career lattice** — cross-studio rotations, an internal project/gig marketplace, dual IC/managerial tracks. Boundaryless employees satisfy psychological mobility by moving across roles/projects/clients; orientation only weakly predicts actually leaving the employer ([[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal, 2019]]), so internal lateral opportunity captures the mobility desire and reduces the inter-agency churn.
2. **Use developmental stretch assignments** — new accounts, pitch leadership, client-facing roles without a promotion. Human-capital predictors drive objective success ([[ng-eby-sorensen-feldman-2005-career-success-meta-analysis|Ng et al., 2005]]), and stretch assignments build the adaptability and agency that convert a protean orientation into success ([[hall-yip-doiron-2018-protean-careers-at-work|Hall et al., 2018]]).
3. **Support career self-management via counseling and job crafting.** Proactive orientation is more malleable than broad traits and is best developed through career counseling ([[wiernik-kostal-2019-pbco-meta-analysis|Wiernik & Kostal, 2019]]) and strongly predicts [[Career Self-Management Behavior|career self-management behavior]]. Job crafting lets creatives build intrinsic values and meaning (identity awareness) into current roles ([[hall-yip-doiron-2018-protean-careers-at-work|Hall et al., 2018]]).
4. **Formalize mentoring, sponsorship, and developmental networks.** Sponsorship and supervisor support are the strongest predictors of [[Subjective Career Success|subjective career success]] (career satisfaction ρ ≈ .44–.46), far exceeding pay ([[ng-eby-sorensen-feldman-2005-career-success-meta-analysis|Ng et al., 2005]]). Formalize it, because informal sponsorship is unevenly distributed ([[beigi-2025-career-success-minority|Beigi et al., 2025]]).
5. **Broaden how success is defined, measured, and rewarded.** Track both objective and subjective success and recognize meaning, self-development, and work-life dimensions ([[seibert-akkermans-liu-2024-contemporary-career-success|Seibert et al., 2024]]). Reward outcomes over face time and close pay inequities — women close the performance gap but not the reward gap ([[seibert-akkermans-liu-2024-contemporary-career-success|Seibert et al., 2024]]) and gaps widen for racial/ethnic and intersectional minorities ([[beigi-2025-career-success-minority|Beigi et al., 2025]]). Frame around [[Sustainable Career|sustainable careers]] (happiness × health × productivity; [[seibert-akkermans-liu-2024-contemporary-career-success|Seibert et al., 2024]]), acknowledging creative work as a potential calling while guarding against its dark side of overwork ([[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson, 2019]]).

## Tailoring to groups facing distinct challenges

- **Women** — structural reward barriers: glass ceiling, glass cliff, maternal wall, and a persistent pay gap despite comparable performance ([[beigi-2025-career-success-minority|Beigi et al., 2025]]; [[seibert-akkermans-liu-2024-contemporary-career-success|Seibert et al., 2024]]). Weight Rec 1 (outcome-based flexible roles, internal lateral moves) and Rec 5 (reward outcomes not face time, close pay gaps, hold managers accountable for advancement).
- **Early-career creatives** — prioritize Rec 2 (stretch assignments) and identity-awareness counseling toward a clear values-based identity, the meta-competency that converts a protean orientation into psychological success ([[hall-yip-doiron-2018-protean-careers-at-work|Hall et al., 2018]]). Because callings are strongest early and can reduce heeding of career advice and invite self-exploitation ([[Thompson-Bunderson-2019-Work-as-a-Calling|Thompson & Bunderson, 2019]]), pair challenge with mentoring and sustainable workloads.
- **Underrepresented minorities** — minority status operates through **complex visibility** (hyper-, in-, and managed visibility) to constrain access to networking, mentoring, role models, and stretch assignments ([[beigi-2025-career-success-minority|Beigi et al., 2025]]). Deliver Recs 2 and 4 equitably: audit who gets stretch roles and sponsorship, provide same-group mentors/role models, monitor for glass-cliff placements. Retain objective-success metrics, because pay and rank are matters of equity ([[seibert-akkermans-liu-2024-contemporary-career-success|Seibert et al., 2024]]).
